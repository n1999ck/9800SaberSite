Bagley receives award

by Jennifer Dickerson / Sports Editor for 1990 Saber Staff

This article is taken from the January 24, 1990 edition of the Saber.
Earl Bagley, coach of the men’s golf team at Columbus College, has been named the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division II national golf coach of the year.

The annual “Dave Williams Award given by the Golf Coaches Association of America (GCAA), culminates a season in which the Columbus College men’s golf team won the NCAA Division 11 national championship, and one golfer won the national individual title.

“I’m extremely pleased to be selected by my peers for this award,” said Dr. Bagley. “It makes it even more rewarding.”

Dr. Bagley’s six year record as golf coach includes a national championship, two second place finishes, two third place finishes, and a fifth place finish in the NCAA Division 11 rankings.

“I inherited an outstanding golfing tradition,” said Dr. Bagley. “We attract good players to Columbus College because we offer a quality program.

“We provide them with sound academics, a challenging golf course at Bull Creek, and a strong golfing tradition. I think that’s the key to our national prominence,” said Dr. Bagley.

Over the past six years, the golf team accumulated individual awards for 10 players who received All-America honors, one was named as national player of the year, and two won individual titles in the national championship tournament.

The 1989-90 Columbus College golf team is currently ranked number one in the NCAA Division 11 by the GCAA poll.

Dr. Bagley serves as the golf chairman for NCAA Division II, District 3, and as vice chairman of the Bull Creek Golf Course Authority.

He also serves as associate dean of students and associate professor of social science education at the college in addition to coaching the men’s golf team.

A 1961 graduate of LaGrange High School, he earned his baccalaureate, master’s, and doctoral degrees in education from the University of Georgia, and has been on the Columbus College faculty since 1973.
