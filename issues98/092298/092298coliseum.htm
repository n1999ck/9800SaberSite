Coliseum would benefit Columbus College

by Jami Norris / Editor-in-Chief of 1984 Saber Staff

This article was taken from the November 14, 1984 edition of the Saber.
A proposed coliseum that would seat between 11,500 - 15,000 people could become a new home for the Columbus College Cougar basketball team.

“A coliseum would help us in recruiting,” said athletic director and head basketball coach Herbert Greene. “It would attract good clubs and attract more people.”

The Cougars are playing Auburn this year, talking to Georgia Tech, and have tentatively scheduled games with the University of Florida and Florida State, said Greene.

“One of our biggest problems is the size of our gym,” said Greene. “We could attract collegiate teams on a repeat basis if we have the facilities.”

A subcommittee has been formed to review marketing and financial data compiled by consultant Burt Cohen. The subcommittee will make a recommendation on November 15.

Six sizes are being considered, ranging from 10,000 to 15,000 seats.

“Columbus College has been successful in recruiting athletes because people who come here like Columbus,” said Greene. “A new coliseum would be a definite plus.”

No definite site has been considered, but the committee is looking at land along I-185 in the vicinity of Columbus College and the downtown area.

“A location close to the campus would be advantageous for the College,” said Greene. “Anywhere you build it, we want to make use of it.”

“I would like to sit on the sidelines and coach a Columbus College team in front of a capacity crowd and have people feel that the team is their team,” said Greene. “My dream is to have athletic teams on the floor, in the field, that everyone in Columbus wants to be associated with.

“It can be done.”

Editor's Note: This dream will be realized in October 1998 when ground is broken for our new Physical Education Complex.
