Looking Back: 1961-1962

by James W. England / Editor-in-Chief of 1987 Saber Staff

This article was taken from the October 28, 1987 edition of the Saber.
“State of the College” announced, with hope voiced that the new campus facilities would be ready by Fall ‘63. Construction was expected to begin during October 196 1. The college was quickly becoming overcrowded.

Fall ‘61 enrollment reflected a drop of thirty students over Fall ‘60, with an enrollment of only 740. There were 464 freshmen, 201 sophomores, and 45 unclassified. All applications were not approved, but the number was not reported.

School spirit, or lack of it, drew fire in student editorials, along with a lack of parking. Removal of parking lot “speedbreakers” hailed - how did they get back?

The annual “Harvest Hop” dance was packed to overflowing - maybe the school spirit editorials did some good. The ‘63 Homecoming featured a basketball game, bonfire, and dance. Susan Copeland was crowned queen, but the Rebels went down to defeat against the Middle Georgia Wolverines, 113- 10 1.

A literary magazine, Spectrum, was to be published for 1961-62, with Miss Irene Roach and Mr. Robert Watkins as advisors.

The basketball team finished the season 16-6-0, losing to Armstrong College in the first round of the Junior College Tournament in Statesboro.

The 1962 Miss Columbus crown was captured by CC freshman Glenda Davis Hall.

SGA elections required a run-off election for the office of president, with Guerry Thode, Baker High School graduate taking the honors.

John L. Quick was named the 1962 Faculty Cup winner. John had been the 1960-61 SGA president and played on the basketball team for two years. The class of ‘62 graduated 30 students June 10, 1962.

The Rebels baseball team finished their season 9-2-0 overall, and 9-2-0 for the conference. The Golf team won two of three matches for the year, but placed fourth overall in the Junior College Golf tournament.
