Letters to the Editor
These days the only time you read or hear about Fraternities it is for excessive partying or unsafe Initiation practices, we think it's only fair to look at the other side of the picture - the good we do for the community.

701 - that is an awesome number, and what makes it more impressive is that it is the number of hours Tau Kappa Epsilon put forth for community service this last year. That is over a month's worth of time!! At last week's Greek Banquet Sigma Nu walked away with the award for winning Greek Week Congrats! But...Tau Kappa Epsilon walked away with the award that we feel Is the real prize the award for philanthropy. The award for most community service hours. Tau Kappa Epsilon is very very proud to win this award and show our critics that Fraternities do more than drink and fight.

Tau Kappa Epsilon picks up hours throughout the year, but there are a couple of events that we are especially proud of-, for the last 10 years we have worked hand in hand with the Roosevelt Rehab Hospital in war-in springs. Once a year the have black tie charities ball to raise money for the hospital. We will show up on Friday afternoon and help with anything we can. Then on Saturday we all dress in shirt and tie and we serve as car valets, bar tenders, and general gophers. The other event Tau Kappa Epsilon would like to mention is for the first time this year we worked with the Miss Wheelchair Georgia Pageant here at the Liberty Theatre. It was our first year associated with the pageant, but definitely not our last. We even received a letter of recognition from the mayor.

Tau Kappa Epsilon is very proud of what we are and how we achieved that status. We will continue to be In the news sharing with the public our good works in the community and the surrounding areas. As for upcoming events we have been asked to work with the Roosevelt Hospital this summer at the Special Olympic trials to be held in Warm springs this July.

- Michael Newlove, CSU Student
