Cottonmouths bring home the cup

by Tommy Peavy / Associate Editor
After a long season of bumps and bruises, both emotionally and physically, the Columbus Cottonmouths won the Central Hockey League championship and brought to Columbus it’s first hockey championship.

After winning the first two championship series games in the Snake Pit, Columbus travelled to Witchita, Kansas where they systematically tore down the Thunder and went on to sweep Witchita to claim the title.

The Cottonmouths organization prides themselves as having the best fans in the CHL and their beliefs were made solid as steel when the team returned home from Kansas. After being flown in two separate flights to Atlanta, the team loaded into vans provided by the Columbus Parks & Recreation and began their journey home.

As the team team approached Columbus, they were met by a police escort. While the team approached, a large contingent of fans had gathered on the side of the Columbus Civic Center and were already breaking into cheers as reports of the Cottonmouths location was announced over the PA system. “I knew we would have fans because that’s just the way they are, but this is incredible,” stated an enthusiastic head coach Bruce Garber. What was incredible was a mob of nearly 1000 crazed fans armed with bags of confetti and silly string. As the players arrived they made they way down a narrow path and were showered with all sorts of fun party favors.

Once on the stage, the players looked out across the screaming fans and many had a look of utter amazement on their faces. When asked what he thought of the huge crowd, goalie Frankie Oulette said, “Unbelievable, never in a million years, it’s just unbelievable.”

As the crowd settled down, each player gave a quick speech, beginning with team captain Jerome “Boom Boom” Bechard, who carried the Levins Cup trophy proudly. As the team went down the numerical roster, some danced, some thanked the fans, and some spoke of former Cottonmouths owner Charlie Morrow who died of cancer earlier in the year. One of the most emotional speeches came from General Manager Phil Roberto. Roberto, fighting off tears and a quivering voice, hoisted the Levens Cup skyward, looked up and simply said, “Charlie, we did it.”

When all the speeches were said and done, the players were given a bottle of champagne each and they promptly began spraying the fans with the cold liquid. They then left the stage and walked directly into the new celebrity status that each individual has acquired.

The celebration showed just how much the Cottonmouths mean to the fans of Columbus and if the team has their way, there will be more celebrations like that one in the future. This thought was made apparent as Roberto led the fans in a chant of Repeat, Repeat, Repeat.
