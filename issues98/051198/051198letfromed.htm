Letter from the Editor

by Tommy Peavy / Associate Editor
Ya gotta watch out!

A recent accusation against one of Columbus State's fraternities has brought to light the thoughts that hazing is talking place on campus. Although the event passed without any serious effects on that chapter or the CSU Greek system, the controversy had the potential to form a black cloud over all of CSU's Greek organizations.

The event in question was reported about two weeks ago when several CSU students say they witnessed members of the Sigma Nu fraternity forcing one of it's pledges to drink large amounts of alcohol at a local establishment. All sources wish to remain anonymous. The fraternity vehemently denied the accusations against them. I'm not one to pass judgement, especially since I didn't witness the event and facts tend to get mixed and jumbled as they pass around from person to person. However, the event, whether it happened or not, should serve as a strong warning to that fraternity and all other greek organizations at CSU.

All organizations should be aware of the consequences against them and other campus groups when a controversial situation like this takes place.

Should this event have become a serious situation where the news groups of the area made it public, the reputation of the university would be tarnished, along with the other greek organizations on campus.

All fraternities and sororities would be examined for hazing, whether they've been accused or not. A serious situation could also cause a decline in all groups new members.

Students coming to CSU who were informed of the hazing would steer clear of greek organizations in fear of being hazed themselves.

The point of this whole editorial is to say, WATCH OUT. When you are out in the community as a member of a fraternity or sorority, people see nearly everything you do. Many citizens and students already have the stereotype of fraternities and sororities as being violent hazers. Let's not give people any excuse to prove these stereotypes to be true.

Take care of your brother's and sister's and think of the reputation of the university, your organization, and the entire CSU greek system when considering taking part in any kind of dangerous activity.
