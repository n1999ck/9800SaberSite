Guest artists join opera cast

- Office of Public Relations
CSU to present Puccini's Suor Angelica and Gianni Schicci Guest artists join local talent to perform two one-act operas.

Sandra Walker of New York City's Metropolitan Opera Joins Columbus State University's Schwob Department of Music in its presentation of Suor Angelica and Gianni Schicci, two of the three one-act operas that comprise Puccini's Il Trittico. Performances are scheduled for Friday, May 29 at 8 p.m. and Sunday, May 31 at 4 p.m. in Fine Arts Hall.

Local opera buffs will enjoy both comic and tragic opera in one performance. According to Joseph Golden, professor of opera at CSU, Gianni Schicci depicts the evils of greed and is one of the "finest examples of Italian comic opera ever written." This production will be sung in English and set in modern day Florence. Suor Angelica is "traditional, heart-breakingly beautiful, and tragic," says Golden. It tells the story of a young, unwed girl who gives up her son to live in a convent in the 15th century. Both operas present life lessons that are as current as this morning's news.

Walker, a world famous Contralto who is currently appearing at the Metropolitan Opera in New York City, will sing the part of La Zia Principessa in Suor Angehca. Walker and her husband reside in Augusta where she has one of the largest private voice studios in the Southeast. Walker will be on campus May 22-31. CSU students will have the opportunity to talk with her and gain some first-hand knowledge of her experiences on the major opera stages of Western Europe and the United States. Joining her on stage is tenor Scott Berry, guest artist from New York City, who sings the part of Rinuccio in Gianni Schicci.

Local performers are represented in both casts. CSU graduates Kimberly Humphries and Bart Eysel sing the parts of Nella and Simone in Gianni Schicci. Student Erin Chancellor appears as Suor Genovieffa in Suor Angelica. They join a cast that includes CSU faculty members Shirley Brumbaugh as Zita and Robert Peavler in the title role of Gianni Schicci. Patricio Cobos conducts the orchestra for Gianni Schicci; Robert Rumbelow conducts for Suor Angelica.

According to Golden, the operas have wonderful music, allowing the "orchestra to fit seamlessly with the plot." In addition, "every word sung is essential and serves to move the plot straight through to the moral of the story." Golden is impressed with his cast, which includes a number of freshmen. "Often freshmen have a tendency to be afraid their first time on an opera stage, but this cast exhibits enthusiasm in all corners.

Sets are designed by Peter Cranton and built by Rich Scenery and Lighting, who also does the lighting for the production. Costumes are coordinated by Steven Graver, professor in the CSU theatre department. CSU presents one operatic production each year which is "well attended and enjoys a tremendous amount of community support," says Golden, a faculty member since 1990.

Admission for all performances is by reserved seating only: $12 adults, $10 senior citizens, $6 ages 18 and under, $8 group (8 or more), $15 at the door. For more information, call the CSU Box Office, (706) 569-3179.
