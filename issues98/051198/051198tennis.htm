CSU Tennis: flying high

by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor
How tough is it to win in tennis this year in the Peach Belt Athletic Conference? Extremely tough, considering 8 of the men’s teams and 5 of the women’s teams are ranked in the top 25 in Division II.

Columbus State is among these teams, the women are ranked 10th in the country and the men are ranked 24th nationally.

Gearing up for the the regional tournament, the men faced a tough non-conference contender in Abraham Baldwin College out of Tifton, GA.

Although, ABAC is a Junior College they are a regular opponent of many four year schools. Rightfully so, after having won the Junior College National Championship in 1984 and defeating the University of Georgia in 1990. However, the Cougars came out on top with a 5 - 4 victory.

The results were not as positive this past week as the men were soundly defeated by Auburn University at Montgomery 7-2 and the women lost to Brenau by the same margin.

Regardless, both are in the regional championship which begins on Friday and runs through Sunday. The men will play the University of North Florida in Jacksonville and the women will play Mississippi University for Women at Georgia College in Milledgeville.
