 Cheerleaders go to national

by Heather Warren / Associate Editor

The Columbus State University cheerleaders won a national title earlier this month. The team competed against 23 other division two teams. CSU cheerleaders placed sixth in the nation. That was four places higher than last year.

Jimbo Davis, Cheerleading Coach, said the degree of difficulty was much higher than they had tried before. Davis said the cheerleaders were “eighteen hardworking, dedicated athletes that believe in what they do and believe in the institution they represent.” Davis also said the cheerleading program has gone from one of the worst programs in the country to one of the best.

The 1997-1998 Cheerlead-ing Team consists of eighteen people but during competition only sixteen are allowed on the matt. The routine the team used had ten males and six females on the mat. There were two alternates.

The competition was invitational only. Teams had to send in tapes with specific criteria to be invited. After viewing the tapes, the judges evaluated and ranked from the tape. The CSU team placed 7th from the tape,but achieved a higher rank in competition.

The competition was in Orlando, Florida at the Indiana Jones Theater. The team left on Thursday, January 8 and came back on Sunday, January 12.

During the semifinals around 10,000 spectators showed up to watch the performances by the cheerleading teams. If you were not one of the 10,000 in Florida, donít worry, beginning in May you can watch the Cheerleading competition yourself on ESPN 2.

Jimbo Davis said in the month of December the team practiced seven days a week from 8:00 a.m. until 12:00 p.m. The only day off for the cheerleaders were Christmas day and two days after that. Sandy Riley, a member of the team, said they practiced twenty eight hours a week for six weeks. “Sometimes you wonder if it’s worth it, but when you get off the floor and you know you did your best, that’s where it pays off,” Riley said.

Davis said “we’ve come a long way in a short time.” Sandy Riley said “I feel like next years squad is going to be even better, Jimbo won’t settle for less he wants to go all the way. Both of them expect big things in the future of cheerleading at Columbus State University.
