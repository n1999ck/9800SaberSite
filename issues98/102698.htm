Monday, October 5, 1998

Barnes for H.O.P.E. Scholarship

by Helen Godwin / Associate Editor


Roy Barnes is the Democratic hopeful for Georgia's next Governor. When asked what he intended to do for the students of the Georgia University System, he stated, "I am going to make sure that the university system continues, that H.O.P.E. is safe, that they have a state that is economically prosperous, and is not going to make a radical turn from left to right."

Many students are concerned that the H.O.P.E. scholarship is in jeopardy, but you need not fear if Mr. Barnes is elected. He wrote and sponsored Constitutional Amendment #2 that will appear on the ballot on November 3. This amendment states that the lottery money can only be used for H.O.P.E and for pre-kindergarten.

Mr. Barnes would also like to see a raise in standards for higher education. He supports the Regents in their efforts in this area, including the 4-year requirement for math that the Regents is proposing.

Some other things that Mr. Barnes will work for if elected are: a tax elimination for the first 50,000 dollars of the home "so it helps young families get started and seniors who are on a fixed income," according to Mr. Barnes, parole for criminals to include a work program so that the cities can be cleaned up while criminals serve their time, and pro-choice efforts.

For all of the undecided voters out there, Roy Barnes had this to say, "If you are satisfied with Zell Miller, then vote for me."
