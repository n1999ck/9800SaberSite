Monday, March 9, 1998

Gore praises CSU during visit

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

“CSU is an appropriate place to talk about the number one priority of expanding access to education and training,” Vice President Al Gore said during a speech Monday given at Columbus State University (CSU) in the Woodruff Gymnasium.

Gore complimented CSU for pioneering ICAPP and congratulated Georgia on the success of the HOPE scholarship program. “The beauty of HOPE is it has the flexibility to serve the whole state,” Gore said. He also praised Georgia for leading the nation in creating high tech jobs and said he hopes the rest of the nation follows.

“You’ve got the kind of partnership here with Columbus State University, Total Systems, the private sector and government under Zell Miller that is exciting the whole nation,” Gore said.

During the speech Gore stressed the importance of education and knowledge. “We are in a new and different age, the information age and the strategic resource of the information age is knowledge,” Gore said.

Several audience members laughed and clapped when Gore shared that his first home was a trailer in Alabama.

The crowd also cheered when he commented that he thought Columbus did a great job of hosting the SEC tournament.

Many students who attended the speech were pleased with Gore’s comments. “He was more interesting than I thought he would be,” says CSU student, Lisa Rios. “I didn’t even know what ICAPP was exactly until now. I was suitably impressed.”

Rios also commented that she was pleased that the speech was clear and concise. “I was really glad he spoke a lot about CSU. I was glad he didn’t waste a lot of time talking about things I wasn’t interested in.”

Andy Manuel, CSU student, says, “I liked it. It was interesting to hear what the Vice President had to say about ICAPP. It was positive for everyone involved.”

The speech was given before a banner which read, “Columbus State University, 21st Century Education, 21st Century Jobs”. Gore addressed about 600 CSU faculty, students, business leaders and political guests.

Gore shared the stage with CSU President Frank Brown, Representative Sanford Bishop, Senator Max Cleland, Secretary of Education Richard Riley, Governor Zell Miller, Mayor Bobby Peters, State Senator Calvin Smyre and CSU student Kristen Fenty.

Brown welcomed the audience and said, “The eyes of the nation will be on Columbus today, not a bad situation to be in.”

Cleland began his remarks by saying, “We are gathering today at one of Georgia’s great institutions.”

Of the ICAPP tour, Riley said, “Partnership at work and anything that works well has the word partnership in it. “ Secretary Riley also said “ I am so delighted to see ICAPP at work . ICAPP is making a big difference in people lives.”

Shortly before the speech, Gore participated in a roundtable discussion with between 15 and 20 university, business and state leaders and 2 CSU students.

Fenty, who introduced Governor Zell Miller at the Vice President’s speech was present at the roundtable discussion. She says, “I really enjoyed myself. He was a lovely man. In fact, the entire panel enjoyed themselves.” A member of the first graduating ICAPP class, Fenty says she was surprised to hear that Gore was planning a visit to CSU.

Fenty is a graduate student at CSU and was chosen to attend the roundtable discussion through contacts at her job with Total Systems Services, Inc. (TSYS). She agrees with Gore’s praise of the program and says, “I could not have the job I have now without ICAPP.”

After the roundtable discussion Gore visited a class of Intellectual Capital Partnership Program (ICAPP) students.

ICAPP students waited for almost an hour for Gore’s arrival. While walking into the classroom, Gore shook hands and spoke to students. He sat with one student at a computer while she explained the program.

Before Gore’s approximately 3 hour visit, various people on campus were busy preparing for his arrival since last Thursday. Several Secret Service were on campus to scout for a suitable place to hold the event.

While Gore was here the Columbus Police Department, the Sheriff’s Department and the CSU Public Safety Office joined the Secret Service in helping to keep the Vice President safe.

Woodruff Gymnasium was searched by Secret Service agents and bomb detecting dogs before Gore entered. Extreme safety measures were taken and one student standing on a nearby tennis court was reportedly asked by a Service Agent to return a tennis ball to his athletic bag before the Vice President arrived.

Gore ended his speech by joking, “the nation has already stolen the HOPE program from Georgia and we are now laying the groundwork for stealing ICAPP”.
