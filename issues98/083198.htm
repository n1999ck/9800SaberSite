Monday, August 31, 1998

Courtyard II: A new
place to be

by Leslie Maxwell / Staff Writer

This semester welcomed the first residents of CSU’s new student housing, Courtyard II. The apartment complex, which was purchased and renovated by the University Foundation, helped fill the increasing demand for housing.

For the last several years there has been a waiting list for Courtyard I, and as the university has grown, so has the need for student housing.

Since out of town students were given priority, the university was often unable to meet the housing needs of Columbus area students. Now, with the addition of Courtyard II, CSU is capable of accommodating around 450 students.

Courtyard II is located at 3528 Gentian Blvd., just across University Avenue from the campus. The apartments were completely renovated with new carpet, paint, appliances, and furniture. They are set up in one, two and three bedroom units, and the master bedroom in each unit is shared.

In addition, the complex has a swimming pool and a club house. The club house is in the final stages of renovation, and it will be used as a laundry facility.

The resident manager, Karen Gutierrez, stated several advantages to living in Courtyard II. Residents live with friends in a college community, but they have the privacy and independence of living in an apartment.

Gutierrez said that the goal of the resident staff is for everyone at Courtyard II to be “one big happy family.”

Each building has a resident assistant to help with any problems that might occur, and the assistants are also on duty in the office at night. The residents include many incoming freshmen, music majors, athletes, and international students from countries such as Chile, Kenya, and Spain.

Even though things are off to a great start at Courtyard II, plans for improvement are already in the works according to Dana Schraufnagel, Director of Housing.

Next year, a new club house will be built to accommodate many activities for the residents. There are also plans to create more grassy areas around the apartments and possibly make room for volleyball courts or other recreational facilities. In addition, the CSU Housing Association is planning pool parties and cook outs for residents in the future.

As a resident at Courtyard II, I am very happy with my apartment. The units are much nicer than the accommodations at other schools that I have visited. A few kinks are still being worked out of the system, but check-in went smoothly and maintenance problems are addressed quickly.

I would encourage any CSU student to check out the housing options at Courtyard II. The community atmosphere is great, so come be a part of our family!
