Got Swing?

by Scott Meeks / Guest Writer
As you may or may not know, swing is coming back in a big way. Since the 1920’s and ‘30’s it has made changes some might say is for the better.

From BIG bands like the Royal Crown Revue, Big Bad Voodoo Daddies, Cherri Poppin Daddies, and the Brian Setzer Orchestra comes new bold sounds that are sure to keep the spirit of swing alive in the ‘90’s.

Swing just isn’t about music, however. It’s about dance and an overall frame of mind. You might ask, “How do I learn all about swing in a short time?” or even “Where do I pick up my zoot-suit daddio?” To answer your questions, I’ve compiled a list of how to become a swingin’ fool in the ‘90’s.

1. Start off by renting the movie “Swingers.” This will give you a good idea about the everyday lifestyle of a modern-day swinger. Besides, it’s a great movie and should be watched regardless.

2. Go ahead and pick up a CD of a modern swing band like the Big Bad Voodoo Daddies (they’re my favorite). Another option to get an overall taste of modern swing music is a compilation that recently hit stores called “Swing This Baby”.

3. Take swing lessons. There aren’t a whole lot of places in Columbus that teach swing, but the places that do are good in quality. Call around to area dance studios and find out how to enroll. A few places that you can check out here around town that can help you learn how to swing dance: the Rivertown Dance Theatre, Ballroom Dancing by Gloria Harris, and the Dance Centre of Cross Country Plaza.

Once you’ve followed these steps you might want to consider going to a club that offers a swing night. The Masquerade in Atlanta offers this delicacy every Sunday night. In fact I even saw a sign at Scooters announcing the possibility of a swing night.

Now that you have completed all of the steps, your on the way to becoming a very cool cat.

Don’t forget, “It don’t mean a thing if you ain’t got that swing!”
