RiverCenter construction begins

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
As dusk approached the downtown area of Columbus, children and adults began to come from miles around to witness one of the many advancements in Columbus State University history. The groundbreaking festivities for the new $60 million RiverCenter were like no other.

The entertainment brought joy to the many people who were gathered in the downtown area of 10th Street and Broadway. Some of the acts consisted of a great puppet show which provided entertainment for the children who were in attendance. There was without a doubt plenty of food and fun for everyone there.

The Columbus State University Wind Ensemble was also present and performed two works for the event. There were other events taking place as well which kept the people dancing until the big moment.

Finally, the festivities concluded with the groundbreaking ceremonies in which Dr. Frank Brown, President of CSU, participated along with the RiverCenter Board of Directors.

This was a great moment for CSU as it took yet another exciting step toward the future. The new facility will allow Columbus to host international acts, touring dance, children’s theatre and many other exciting events. It will also house both the Schwob Department of Music and the Columbus Symphony Orchestra.

This is an exciting time for Columbus State University as it not only builds for the present, but more importantly for the future.
