CSU Cross Country team competes

by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor
Columbus State competed in the Kennesaw State Cross Country Tri-meet this past weekend in Marietta and finished in third place.

Leading the way for the men was Geoffrey Rotich who finished in 2nd place with a time of 20:38, and Abraham Bitok who finished 4th place with a time of 20:50, in the 4 mile event. Both come to Columbus State via Kenya and are expected to make a big impact as the season progresses.

In the women’s competition the Cougars were led by two freshman, Megan Grinder and Dana Encarnancion who finished in 12th (21:49) and 15th (22:22) place respectively in the 3 mile event. The women look to have a strong year despite returning only one runner from last year’s team.

Both teams compete in the Georgia State Invitational on September 19th.
