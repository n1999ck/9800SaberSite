Music Bits: Love collaborates with Corrigan for new album

by Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor
>> Courtney Love and company arrived with their second album last week, and to be honest with you, it is awesome.

“Celebrity Skin” offers twelve tracks of music that is definitely a step up from their previous efforts. Billy Corgan, of Smashing Pumpkins, offers his assistance on several tracks of the album, but his touch is heard throughout the entire album.

The music leans toward more of a poppy-techno sound than the previous albums by the band. Hole is Courtney Love, Erik Erlandson, Melissa Auf Maur, and Patty Schemel.

The band performed at the MTV Video Music Awards last week and is planning a tour to promote their new project.

A standout song on the album is track number nine, “Boys On The Radio,” which is a reworking of the older Hole song called “Sugar Coma,” which the band played several years ago on MTV Unplugged.

>> Singer and songwriter Duncan Sheik will release his second album, “Humming,” on October 6.

A highlight of that album is sure to be a track dedicated to the late Jeff Buckley. Sheik wrote “A Body Goes Down,” a memorial for Buckley, who drowned while swimming in the Mississippi River in May 1997.

Matt Johnson of The The plays drums for that song, which is appropriate because Johnson played on Buckley’s first record, “Grace.”

>> U2 has signed a multi-million dollar deal with Polygram Records to put out a series of ‘best of’ albums, according to MTV News and several other internet sources.

The first two albums are scheduled to be released November 2. The first is tentatively titled “The Best Of U2: Volume One 1980-1990” and will have around 15 tracks. It will be accompanied by a separate B-sides set, which may appear as part of a limited edition double set or as a separate release all together.

“Best of Volume Two” will probably arrive either the next Christmas or sometime thereafter.
