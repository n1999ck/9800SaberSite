Campus Candid: Grant Salter

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

 

Grant Salter is one of the most active students on campus.

Originally from Thomaston, Georgia, Salter moved to Columbus and began attending CSU in the summer of 1996. He says he chose to attend CSU because it is a medium size college.

“I wanted to attend a college where I wouldn’t get lost in the crowd, but still have opportunities,” Salter says.

To gain experience as an educator Salter decided to pursue his B.A. in Early Childhood Development. He plans to get his Masters in Public Administration and eventually work in the education field. “Hopefully I will have a chance to work in the student development field at a University level, “ Salter says.

Aside from classes Salter is a leader on campus and a member of various organizations. He is an orientation leader, a Campus Organization Recruitment Excellence (CORE) member, Business Manager of the Saber and a member of Tau Kappa Epsilon. Salter also recently became the acting Vice President of Scholastic Affairs in SGA.

Salter says that working on camus is a very positive experience for him. “I feel working with different organizations on campus has helped me grow, become a more effective communicator and assume a greater leadership role,” he says.

Salter is presently working with the Leadership Curriculum Development Committee, which is attempting create leadership scholarships for CSU students. “I feel the experience of leadership is an asset to any student. It is a difficult role and deserves to be rewarded.”

Also, Salter is a member of the Ethics Week committee, which plans events, forums and panel discussions for CSU Ethics Week.

As for the future of CSU Salter says, “I really enjoyed seeing this school grow from a commuter college to a university and I’m looking forward to seeing it continue to grow.”
