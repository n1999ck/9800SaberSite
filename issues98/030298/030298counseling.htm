CSU counseling center earns accreditation

- Office of Public Relations
Columbus State University’s School Counseling master’s degree program has become one of three such programs in the state to earn national accreditation from the Council for Accreditation of Counseling and Related Educational Programs (CACREP).

The designation signifies the program meeting the nation’s highest preparation standards in the school counseling discipline. The criteria centers on leading-edge applications in technology, ethics and cultural diversity, says Lori Crutchfield, CSU assistant professor of counseling and clinical programs.

CSU President Frank Brown says the designation reflects a high level of commitment by faculty. “We are delighted that CSU’s school counseling program has been recognized for its quality. Our faculty set high standards and have worked hard to make them a reality.”

Joyce Hickson, department chair of counseling and clinical programs, says the accreditation stands to bring tremendous benefits. “We improve our ability to attract top faculty and students.” For graduates of the program, she adds, the status also means a reduction in the post-master’s degree supervision requirement for licensure, from four to three years.

Thomas Harrison, dean of the CSU College of Education, says “by adopting CACREP standards, the College of Education is stating to our students and the greater community that identifiable benchmarks of excellence are an integral part of our counseling program.”

Georgia State University and the University of Georgia house the state’s other such accredited programs.

CSU’s school counseling master’s program includes 40 students while CSU’s community counseling masters program, previously CACREP-accredited, serves 60 students.

“The College of Education is proud of the achievements of its counseling graduates who serve in various school and community agency positions,” Harrison says.
