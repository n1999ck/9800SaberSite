And the Grammy winners are...

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

 
The 1998 Grammy Awards took place this week. Some winners were not a surprise but the show was not without them. One of the surprises of the evening was Bob Dylan taking the Award for Best Contemporary Folk Album for his release “Time Out Of Mind.” Bob Dylan also took home the award for Best Male Rock/Vocal Performance for his song “Cold Irons Bound.”

Fiona Apple performed “Criminal”, and took the award for Best Female Rock/Vocal Performance. Jars of Clay, with their album “Much Afraid”, won the award for Best Pop/Rock Contemporary Gospel Album. Will Smith left with the award for Best Solo Rap Artist.

The award for Best Long Form Music Video went to Alanis Morissette for her “Jagged Little Pill, Live.” Sarah McLachlan came away with two awards. She took home the awards for Best Female Pop/Vocal Perfor-mance for her song “Building a Mystery” and Best Pop Instrumental for her song “Last Dance.” Radio-head and their album “OK Computer” came away with the award for Best Alternative Performance.

In an act that probably does not even deserve to be mentioned, Ol’ Dirty Bastard, of the Wu-Tang Clan pulled a stunt that was simply devised to call attention to himself and his group. As Shawn Colvin was about to receive her award for Song of the Year, ODB rushed the stage and took control of the microphone. He told the audience, “I went out and bought me an outfit today that cost me a lot of money, because I figured that Wu-Tang was gonna win.”

I am happy to report that Wu-Tang did not win and the award for Best Rap Album went to Puff Daddy.

Oasis is writing yet another chapter in their short, but volatile existence. The band is facing a possible ban from the Cathay Pacific Airline for several alleged reasons. Several of the things that the band is accused of doing on the eight hour flight include smoking, carrying on loud sing-alongs, using obscene language and gestures, and conducting food fights. Several witnesses claim that the band appeared to be drunk when they arrived for the flight.

Frontman Liam Gal-laugher responded, “I didn’t throw anything. I don’t know what they are talking about. Some panhead told me to shut up - some panhead who needs stabbing through the head with a f***ing pick-ax.” Guitar player and vocalist Noel Gallagher describes his younger brother, Liam, as having, “twenty-four hour turbulence in his head.”

Regardless of their actions off the stage, Oasis is at the top of their game when they step out on stage. The tour stopped in Atlanta several weeks ago and the band was phenomenal. The show included hits from all three of their albums and ended with a new song that might show up on a later album. The highlights of the show were a near ten minute version of “Champaign Super-nova”, a great version of the song “Don’t Go Away”, and an acoustic version of the Beatles classic song “Help.”
