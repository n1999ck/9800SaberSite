Letter from the Editor

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief
Seven years ago I was a new student at CSU. I was 18 years old, a new mother and at a very difficult point in my life. Personal circumstances left me insecure, alone and hopeless. Still, I wanted my college degree.

Many students here understand the difficulties of attending college while trying to raise a family. In my case, the problem was compounded by the fact that I was still a child myself. I wondered if things would ever get better in my life.

Something happened during that first year here that changed my life dramatically, and I remember it vividly. I was sitting in a History class in Howard Hall when I just broke down. I left the classroom in tears.

Before I could make it down the stairs I heard someone calling my name. It was my professor, Dr. Kayron M. Laska. I did not want her to see me crying, fearing I would be judged. I wanted to run away, but instead I turned to speak with her.

We talked for a half hour. She told me that one day things would be better, that I would succeed, that I would make it. She reassured me about the future. We hugged and I went on my way.

This professor and ones like her do more than give assignments, lecture and grade tests. They guide, console and relate to their students.

Dr. Laska’s advice and understanding helped me in a time of need. Although, I did drop out of school for a period until my son was old enough to start school himself. I returned as an adult, self assured, confident and ready to build a new life.

Of course, I still have bad days, even bad weeks. Everyone in school has those days when it seems impossible.

Last week my son was sick, my husband dropped four pages of notes I needed immediately for class behind the washer and I was late to class. Again, a professor came to my rescue. Dr. Alyce Cook, Professor of Language and Literature, comforted me and patted me on the back.

Professors do not get paid to be counselors, parents or psychologists to their students. However, many of them play one or all of those roles.

I have been fortunate to come across professors who go beyond the call of duty. When I leave Columbus State University I will have 4 years of college classes to carry with me and a lifetime of compassion.
