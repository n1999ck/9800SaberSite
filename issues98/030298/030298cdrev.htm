Three CD Reviews

by Josh Eller / Entertainment Editor
Marcy Playground: Self titled - Indiana trio Marcy Playground enjoys the top spot on the alternative chart for good reason. “Sex & Candy” is a prelude to an entire CD of laid back, and at times, depressing string of songs which are very effortless and enjoyable to hear. Standouts include #2 “Sex & Candy”, #4 “Saint Joe”, #6 “Sherry Fraser” , #9 “One more Suicide” and #11 “The Shadow of Seattle”. “Playground” is a definite investment buy, the band has real talent and should not have problems with being just a one hit wonder. A-

Crystal Method: Vegas - You may have heard the single release within the last year of “Busy Child”, but now D.J’s Scott Kirkland and Ken Jordan are enjoying well received reviews for there debut “Vegas”. Standouts are #2 “Busy Child”, #3 “Cherry Twist”, #4 High Roller, and #9 “Jaded”. Containing 10 tracks, all of which are high-output rave mixes, “Vegas” would be a definite starting CD for any techno impaired. B

Our Lady Peace: Clumsy - Canada’s very own Our Lady Peace is enjoying success with their third release “Clumsy”. They compare in sound to “Smashing Pumkins” and “Bush”, especially the lead singer, who sounds almost identical to Corgin in many of the vocals. One of the slower songs of the CD , #7 “Clumsy”, is the bands biggest single to date. Other standouts include #1 “Superman's Dead”, #3 “Carnival”, and #5 “4am”. All in all it has a few good songs but nothing to get real excited about. C
