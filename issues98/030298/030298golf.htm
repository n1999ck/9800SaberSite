Golf team on quest for number seven

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor
The Columbus State golf team began the defense of their sixth national title recently in a tournament in Puerto Rico. After returning four of the five starters from last years championship squad, the Cougars and head coach Scott Clark are eyeing their seventh national crown.

The Cougars began their quest in impressive fashion. Columbus State travelled to Rio Mar, Puerto Rico where they were the only Division II team in the field of 15.

Columbus had to face several of the nations top Division I programs including teams such as Georgia Tech, Florida, and North Carolina.

Columbus used an impressive showing from Puerto Rico native Wilfredo Morales to take fourth place in the 15 team field. Morales lost to Georgia Tech superstar Matt Kuchar in a one playoff to determine the tournaments individual champion.

Kuchar is the reigning U.S. Amateur champ and will participate in this year’s Masters.

Morales shot rounds of 72-67-and 69 to finish second overall. Other Cougar golfers were Jaco Rall who tied for 20th, Jon Drewery who tied for 27th, Terry Pilkadaris who tied for 55th, and Chad Glanton who tied for 66th.

Columbus State will next play in the Matlock Collegiate Classic on March 6-8.
