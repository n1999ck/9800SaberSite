Campus Candid: Dr. Frank Brown

by Evelyn Tolbert-Owens

 
Dr. Frank D. Brown, President of Columbus State University has been with the university for 16 years.

Since coming aboard in 1981 he has held the position of vice president for business and finance, and associate professor of management.

In January of 1988 Dr. Brown was picked, out of 100 applicants, by faculty, students, staff, other community members and the Board of Regents to be president of what was then Columbus College.

CSU has a major role in the Columbus community. In 1996, he was an essential part of the process that lead Columbus College to become Columbus State University.

After high school Dr. Brown was accepted to the University of Southern Mississippi on a music scholarship. He was active with USM’s marching band and aiming for a degree in business administration with plans to go into human resources.

Brown reported that he was off to a “slow start”, but quickly found his niche. He even made the Dean’s list a few times.

After receiving his degree in 1963 he opted for a career in computer programming instead of human resources management.

In 1969 he received his MBA from the University of Alabama and went to work for IBM. “At the end of a working day I had the feeling that I had not made a difference in peoples lives,” he says. He also says, “I really wanted to - that’s why I went back to school for my PhD.”

He received his PhD in higher education administration in 1974 from Florida State University. He joined the Alabama Commission on Higher Education teaching pupils at State level and moved on to become Chief Financial Officer at the University of Houston.

Finally, in 1981, he joined CSU. He is the link between the university and the Board of Regents and takes care of future administrative planning as well as budget planning.

His favorite parts of being president are being able to award scholarships, taking part in graduation ceremonies, and the dedication of buildings such as the clubhouse of the baseball stadium and the Japanese Garden given to CSU by Japan.

As for the future of CSU, Brown says, “The future looks extremely bright. So many positive things have happened and continue to happen. But it didn’t happen automatically. All success came about through hard work and planning.” He went on to explain that “CSU enjoys great support from its community. Without that support our ability to accomplish great things would be extremely diminished.”

Brown has been married to his wife Jo-Ann for 30 years. They have two children, April, who is a Junior at CSU, and Jay, who is a freshman enrolled at Carson Newman College in Tennessee. In his free time Dr. Brown loves to do yard work in his flower garden, spend quality time with his family, or read. He is also a genuine animal lover and a supporter of the Muscogee County Humane Society.

Brown says the best advice he can give to students at Columbus State University to be prepared for the “real world”, is “Concentrate on course work, do well, however, also be involved and active within the community. Grades are primary but they are only a part of the student’s experience in college.”
