Cougars stay in first

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
The Columbus State men’s basketball team recently recorded two key victories and a disappointing loss but still remain in the lead in the Peach Belt conference.

The Cougars first took on the Francis Marion Patriots in Florence, South Carolina. The Patriots entered the game as the first place team in the Peach Belt Conference North Division with a 14-7 record, 10-2 in the conference. Columbus jumped out to an early lead by holding the Patriots scoreless for close to seven minutes. By halftime the Cougar lead was sixteen. In the second half Francis Marion made a charge but their effort was foiled by an unlikely source. Quincey Richardson, who most defenses forget about, took charge and helped spread the Cougar lead by converting on three consecutive three-point plays. The Patriots continued to stay close but could get no closer than three points. Richardson scored a career high 16 points and nine rebounds. Senior Larry Coates continued his stellar season with a game-high 23 points.

After disposing of Francis Marion, the Cougars travelled to Aiken, South Carolina to face the USC-Aiken Pacers. Columbus took a two point lead on their very first possession of the game but that was the last time the scoreboard would show the Cougars in the lead. The Pacers stretched their lead to as many as 18 points in the second half. The Cougars could never get on track and fell 95-77. The fatal blow dealt by the Pacers was a combined 67 points and 33 rebounds by their top three players. Calvin Hill and Quinton Peterson led the Cougars with 17 points.

Clinging to an ever narrowing lead in the Peach Belt Conference, Columbus entered their next game against the Georgia College Bobcats looking for a confidence boosting victory and the chance to tighten up their conference lead. Behind the pinpoint shooting of Hill and Peterson, the Cougars got just what they wanted. Together, Hill and Peterson connected on 20-27 field goal attempts. For Hill and Peterson it was a story of two halves. Hill scored 20 of his game-high 31 points in the first half and Peterson followed that by collecting 21 of his 25 points in the second half. The Bobcats stayed close throughout the game and even held a slim lead at one point. The Cougars then went on a 12-0 run in the second half to pull far enough ahead to claim a 93-83 victory in front of 1,125 at Woodruff Gym.

The victory over Georgia College upped the Cougar’s record to 20-5 and 9-3 in the Peach Belt Conference. Columbus also held onto their slim one game conference lead over second place Kennesaw State.
