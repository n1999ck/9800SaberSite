Still Perfect

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
With victories over two of the top teams in the Peach Belt Conference, the Columbus State Lady Cougars remained unbeaten in conference play and moved up to number two in the South-Atlantic Region poll. The most impressive of the victories was a stunning victory over Francis Marion who, at 18-1 and 10-0 in the conference, was ranked at number 16 in the national polls and was the front runner in the Peach Belt’s North Division. The Lady Cougars also racked up victories at USC-Aiken and then defeated the Georgia College & St. University Lady Bobcats, who were the second place team behind Columbus in the PBAC South Division.

The Lady Cougars travelled to South Carolina to play two games in as many days, the first time this was attempted this season for CSU. The first stop was Florence, S.C. and the home of the nationally ranked Patriots from Francis Marion. The Lady Cougars took an early lead and held off a fierce comeback to down the Patriots 72-60. When Francis Marion pulled within one point late in the first half, sophomore guard Christina Iler took charge and went on a 6-0 run of her own. Iler finished with 12 points, all in the first half. After expanding their lead to as many as 19 in the second half, the Lady Cougars soon found themselves battling to stay in the lead. The Patriots went on a crucial 20-5 run but could get no closer than four points as the Lady Cougars pulled away in the games closing minutes. Senior Que Gilliam led the Lady Cougars with 18 points and pulled down a career high 19 rebounds.

The very next day Columbus took on the Lady Pacers from USC-Aiken. After being down for the majority of the first half, the Lady Cougars came storming back and eventually defeated Aiken by 14 points, 79-65. Robyn Davis led the Lady Cougars to victory with 15 points. Tocshia Campfield added 14.

After their success on the road, the Lady Cougars returned home to face off with the second place Lady Bobcats from Georgia College & St. University. Columbus appeared to be on the way to another one of their huge blowouts as an eight point halftime lead grew into a seemingly impossible wall of 20 in the second half. The 20 point lead soon disappeared and the Lady Bobcats were breathing down Columbus’ neck. The Lady Cougars scored only two points in the games final 4:00 minutes allowing Georgia College to pull within six points. Fortunately for CSU the clock ran out on the Lady Bobcats and six was as close as they could get. Columbus survived a 33-point game from Georgia College forward Tamarra Brown and squeaked past the Lady Bobcats 96-89. Columbus was led by Gilliam and Campfield who both scored 18 points. The victory over Georgia College ran the Lady Cougars record to 18-4 and 12-0 in the conference. The win also lowered the Lady Cougar’s magic number to four for a conference championship.
