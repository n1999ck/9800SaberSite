Campus Candids: Anna Mion

by Matthew Young / Staff Writer

Back to Frontpage

Over the summer, Anna Mion, a History Major and Junior at Columbus State University, participated in a six week study abroad program in Metz, France. She was awarded the Board of Regents Study Abroad Scholarship to take classes in Art History, Business,and Modern French History. These classes were all taught by Georgia teachers to Georgia students.

Anna was born in Columbus and attended Hardaway High School before coming to Columbus State. Her father, Dr. Mario Mion, Chair of Political Science, stated that, "I wish more people from this University would apply for these scholarships." The Board of Regents handed out 100 scholarships last year, and are hoping to double that number this year.

While in France, Anna and her classmates made several field trips to nearby cities, including a medieval cathedral in Nancy. Anna stated, "The experience of visiting a cathedral, and having a class on it right outside of it is amazing." In addition to the trip to Nancy, Anna also visited the World War One fortifications around Verdun, France and visited the Maginot Line that separated France from Germany in World War II. She says, "The country is the classroom. That's what makes it so exciting."

On the weekends, she was able to take trips to other parts of France, and also to visit her relatives in Northern Italy. Anna is applying again this year for the Study Abroad scholarship and wants to go back to France, this time to study the French language. She said, "I found the majority of French people to be nice. If you try and speak their language, most of them will work with you. There are some rude people, but you run into rude people in every country."

Upon her graduation, Anna is considering a career in Law or Teaching.
