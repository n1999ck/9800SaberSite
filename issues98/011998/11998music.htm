"Great Expectations" from music this week

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

Back to Frontpage

Congratulations to Santana, the Mamas and the Papas, the Eagles, and Fleetwood Mac on their induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. The ceremonies took place early last week.

An all-star line-up was put together for the soundtrack to Great Expectations, a very modern take on the novel by Charles Dickens. The album includes new music by ex-Soundgarden vocalist Chris Cornell, The Verve Pipe, Stone Temple Pilots’ frontman Scott Weiland, and Tori Amos. Accompanying the soundtrack in stores is the classical score, composed by Patrick Doyle. The album, Great Expectations : The Score Music by Patrick Doyle, carries additional vocals by Tori Amos.

If you happen to be a fan of Elvis Presley and are computer literate, you might enjoy Graceland Interactive. In honor of Elvis’ January 8 birthday, the new multimedia distributor, Digital Ranch 101, is offering the CD-ROM set that will allow you to tour through eighteen rooms of the Graceland mansion.

The new multi-disc release by The Artist Formerly Known as Prince, Crystal Ball, is a compilation of rare tracks and b-sides. Crystal Ball is now available through Prince’s web site at love4oneanother.com, or the 1-800-NEWFUNK phone service. The exact date for a release to stores is still not known.

Fans of the band Heart might want to check out the debut album from a band that goes by the name The Lovemongers. The album, titled Whirlygig, is the new side-project of Heart vocalists Ann and Nancy Wilson.

On the local front, word on some of the possible performers at this years Uptown Jam has surfaced. Government Mule, a band made up of ex-Allman Brothers Band members Warren Haynes and Allen Woody, is a name being tossed around. Also thrown into the rumor mill of possible performers is Tonic, Cheap Trick, Veruca Salt, and Joan Jett. There will be more details on this situation at a later date.

A final note, local music store, Riff’s, will hold a release party for Pearl Jam’s upcoming album, Yield, at midnight on February 2. Riff’s, which is located at 2723 Manchester Expressway, will re-open at approximately 11:30 p.m. on February 2, and Yield will go on sale at midnight.
