Letter from the Editor

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
Students come to college expecting this great place where they can obtain a great education and have fun at the same time. However, students learn quickly that having fun is not as easy as it seems. I have been approached by several students who seem to have a hard time with the amount of attractions in Columbus.

There always seems to be a problem. Either the person is not of age to attend a particular place or there is no place to really go which attracts young people.

Recently, the main attractions around Columbus have been cracking down by making the premier audience 21 years of age and above. I realize the problems and risks of underage drinking, but the primary audience is freshmen and sophomores.

Columbus State University has begun to bring more student activities onto campus as it becomes covered with younger students. The Greek organizations are expanding as well due to the recent acquisition of fraternity houses near the campus of CSU. Small things such as these allow students to have fun on campus, but what is there to do off campus which is directed toward the crowd under the age of twenty-one?

Columbus needs to acquire a few new places in the area that caters to the younger atmosphere. This will benefit students tremendously by allowing them to stay close to home instead of traveling to Auburn or Atlanta.

As Columbus State University becomes a younger more traditional campus in the future, the city of Columbus needs to think of things for the younger crowd to do in town. This will allow students to have fun in a great environment which is most importantly close to home.
