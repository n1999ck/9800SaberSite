Monday, September 14, 1998

CSU Online: a virtual university

by Leslie Maxwell / Staff Writer

CSU doesn’t just have computers on campus anymore; it has a “campus” on the computers.

The faculty of the Computer Science Department teamed up with a company called Real Education to create a “virtual university” on the internet.

The first online class, a microcomputers course, was offered last Spring. Two more courses, Technological Solutions and Graphical User Interfaces, were offered this semester. Right now 45 students are enrolled in online courses.

Faculty and student assistants are working on the time-consuming task of creating an online curriculum. In Spring of 1999, one undergraduate and two graduate level computer classes will be offered online. Within the next two years the Computer Science Department plans to have its entire masters program online.

Although CSU Online currently offers only Computer Science courses, it will soon affect many students outside of that department.

Registration should be completely online by spring, and other departments, such as Communications, Education, Business, and Criminal Justice, have expressed an interest in offering online courses.

If you visit the web page now, you will find the initial framework that is being used to build CSU Online. It contains greetings from the President and basic information about CSU.

The Bookstore and the Career Center are already online, and there is an online Student Union where students can access guest lectures and sports news. Many additions, like an online library and financial aid office, are needed to create a true “virtual university.”

Soon, course syllabi and pictures of the professors should be posted on the web page. Professors that teach online courses post their material on the internet for students to access. Students can contact their professor about questions or problems by e-mail. Students can also interact with one another and their teacher in chat rooms. Having a class online also offers direct access to the vast resources available on the internet.

Dr. Leary Bell, Chair of the Computer Science Department, said, “CSU Online is an investment in the future. There will always be a place in education for campuses where young students can come to have the college experience, but for adults who are seeking to further their education, online courses seem to be direction of the future.”

Just think, one day students will be able to take classes, order books, deal with financial aid, go to the library, or chat with classmates without ever setting foot on the CSU campus. They’ll still have to pay regular tuition, though ... technology can’t solve everything!
