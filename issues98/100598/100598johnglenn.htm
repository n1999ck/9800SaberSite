John Glenn to visit space again
by Helen Godwin / Associate Editor

HOME

After 36 years, John Glenn will again be breaking new ground, on Oct. 29, as the oldest astronaut to travel into space.

Over three decades ago, John Glenn made history by being the first American to orbit the earth. In just 25 days, he will make history again. Glenn is now traveling into orbit to research the effects of space on the aging process.

Senate Democratic Party leader Tom Daschle, long time colleague of the former senator, said, “America is lucky that John Glenn went up there the first time and gained the perspective. And the country is very lucky that he is going up again.”

Though most are staunch supporters of this NASA project, there are a few skeptics. According to CNN Interactive writer John Christensen, Rick Tomlinson, president of the Space Frontier Foundation is one of these skeptics, is calling this mission a “PR gimmick” and a “political connivance.”
