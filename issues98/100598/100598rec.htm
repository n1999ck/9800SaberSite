Committee formed for recycling issues
by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief

HOME

Columbus State University has turned its attention towards the environment. Dr. Frank Brown has recently formed a new recycling project committee as of this year which will be chaired by Maria Holmes.
Holmes is the Director of Auxiliary Services and will be the head of a committee consisting of seven other members. These members are Dr. George Panos, Dr. Bill Birkhead, Dr. Kevin Peterson, Cindy Weeks, and Steven Schmidt.

“Right now our goal is to begin educating the faculty, staff and students about why we should recycle,” says Holmes.

The recycling effort is founded upon four basic principles which are known as the 4 R’s. The 4 R’s are Recycle, Reduce, Reuse and Respect using education as the force behind them.

Holmes explains, “We are also looking at what other universities in the State of Georgia are doing with their recycling projects.” Maria continues by stating, “as we begin to educate people, we will be working on more visible projects such as recycling bins and shredders, working with housing and food services and office areas to cut down on the use of items that can’t be recycled.”

The committee plans to educate the faculty, staff and students by putting an article in the Saber each week, placing interesting tidbits about recycling on the public notice board and having some programs that tie in with the National Recycling Organization.

Experience is a key factor as well since many of the committee members do their own recycling of paper they use while at work in their offices.

“I’m very pleased that the committee members are very interested in recycling”, says Holmes.

Education is both the lifeblood of this program that is being established here at CSU and the mission of this committee.

According to the committee, “Education is the life force. We can develop an elaborate recycling set-up, but without the education, the system will never reach its potential. Several universities, including Cornell, found that recycling increased significantly and that contamination decreased dramatically after an education program was introduced to complement the recycling system. This is what we hope to see at CSU.”
