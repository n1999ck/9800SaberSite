Monday, April 6, 1998

CSU hosts spring break celebration

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

 

The Columbus State University Alumni Association is hosting the fourth annual Spring Break celebration April 6-12 sponsored by Burger King, on the CSU campus.

Many attractions will return including, the James H. Drew exhibition, “America’s amusement park on tour,” providing more than 30 carnival rides, Farmyard Frolics on the Publix Children’s Stage, local and marquee-name musical acts, clowns, craft booths and carnival cuisine.

Several musical acts will be visiting the Spring Break festival including Lee Ann Womack, Drivin’ n’ Cryin’ and Swingin’ Medallions.

Also, Wednesday, April 8 will feature rap/R&B entertainment courtesy of WOKS/WFXE. WRBL and WVRK will present local rock talent in a Friday evening “Battle of the Bands,” and WPNX and WOKS/WFXE each will present sets of “Gospel Fest,” 1-3 p.m. and 4-7 p.m. on the final day of the event, Sunday.

New features will include, Tropical Rainforest Theater for all ages, Baboon Lagoon (several shows daily) displays the comical antics of nine African baboons and their jungle friends. Trainers Lee and Judy Stevens, with their sons Alexander and Sebastian, incorporate 25 years of animal training experience.

Directed by local curator and folk art enthusiast Fred Fussell, the Festival of Southern Culture (10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Saturday, April 11) brings together an assortment of traditional folk musicians, self taught artists and artisans, crafts people and scholars from west Georgia and east Alabama.

Activities include stage performances of traditional dance, storytelling and music; eccentric and traditional arts and crafts exhibits; and live demonstrations of crafting and other traditional skills, like blacksmithing, log hewing and traditional wood stove cooking.

Spring Break ‘98 proceeds benefit CSU student scholarships, faculty development and Alumni Association projects. Daily gate admission prices include $5 adults and $2 ages 18 and under. Free admission for CSU students with current ID, children under 4, and ages 4-12 with a pass available at all Phenix City and Columbus Burger King restaurants.

Tickets ($5) for reserved concert seating may be purchased in advance from the CSU Alumni office, Richards 101, 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday-Friday. Remaining tickets for reserved seating will be sold at the gate the day of the concert, in addition to the price of admission. In case of rain, concerts will be field in the Fine Arts Hall Auditorium.
