Student orgainzations on rise
by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief

HOME

The current rise in student organizations is raising a major question. Why? Currently, there are fifty-five registered organizations on the campus of CSU. Remarkably, forty-five of these fifty-five are registered through the Student Government Association making them able to participate in the SGA Forum as well as a campus recognized organization.

Joel Judah, president of the Student Government Association, mentioned that the increase in involvement can be seen over the last couple years. He says, “People come to the SGA forum who have no reason to come. They just want to see what is going on.”

The curiosity of students is overwhelming, and student involvement is on the rise. With more organizations comes more people to get involved and more students at Columbus State University.

For example, Mr. Judah explained how over twenty organizations registered for and actively participated in the Halloween Carnival hosted by Student Government last weekend.

“Many students attend SGA forums and ask to get involved, whether or not there are any offices available,” Judah added.

As Columbus State University continues to develop its active student body, it continues to grow. Eight new organizations have been approved by SGA this year alone. There are also many organizations that have not yet applied for recognition.

When asked the reason for such active participation and organizational growth, Mr. Judah replied,”It has been helpful to have more students living on campus as those students become more involved. The desire to be more involved is contagious and spreads to those students who are originally from Columbus.”
