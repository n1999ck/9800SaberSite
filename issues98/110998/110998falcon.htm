Falcons recover from loss
by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor

HOME

After a devastating 28-3 loss to the New York Jets a week ago, the Atlanta Falcons returned to their winning ways by defeating the St. Louis Rams, 35-17.

Chris Chandler may have returned, but Jamal Anderson was the workhorse. Anderson, who was named NFC player of the week, rushed for 172 yards and scored three touchdowns. He started his scoring with a 9 yard pass reception from Chandler, and finished off with a scoring run of 4 and 12 yards respectively.

Not to be outdone, Chandler finished the day with 236 yards passing, threw for two touchdowns and one interception. Much better than the 207 total yards gained against the Jets.

The Atlanta defense helped out by limiting the Rams to an average of 3.5 yards per rush and a total of 247 yards. Cornelius Bennett led the team with 7 tackles, followed by Keith Brooking and Columbus native Randy Fuller with 4.

The task does not get any easier as the Falcons travel to take on New England (5-3), who have suffered three recent losses to Denver, the New York Jets, and Miami. The Patriots are led by quarterback, Drew Bledsoe and former Georgia star, Robert Edwards.
