Band to play the Loft
by Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor

HOME

>> All I ever hear around this town is that there is nothing to do. Well, usually there isn’t, but on November 13 that will change, at least for one night. The John Scott Evans Band is going to be playing at The Loft on November 13 around 10:00 that evening. I spoke with Evans last week and he told me about several of the songs that are included in the show. A few of the highlights include a song he wrote for his mom called “Simple Gifts,” a song he wrote for his dad called “Missin’ You,” and a latin groove song that features band members on percussion called “Tema De La Banda.”

Evans says that his favorite songs to play are the two that are written for his parents. I have not gotten a chance to see the band play live yet, but I have heard the album, and you won’t be disappointed in the music. Drop by the Loft on the 13th and check them out.

The John Scott Evans Band features Sean Smith on drums, Dave Holland on percussion, Chris McGriff on keyboard, Raymond J. Blair on bass, David Ragsdale on violin, and John Scott Evans on guitar.

>> Just after piling in multi-platinum sales with their debut album, MatchBox20 has begun thinking about the second time around. This includes developing strategies about dealing with the pressure of following up such a successful record.

After finishing up their tour this week, frontman Rob Thomas told MTV news that the band would disappear for a period of time, in order to make sure that they did not experience any over-exposure.

Quite frankly, I think it is too late for that. MatchBox20’s debut, “Yourself Or Someone Like You,” has hung out for 86 weeks on the “Billboard” album charts, and is currently at number 35.

>> As I mentioned several weeks ago, George Michael was arrested on April 6 in a Beverly Hills park for engaging in a sexual act with an undercover police officer. Michael sat down with MTV last week to chat about this and life in general.

“What can I tell you,” Michael said, “I was stupid, it was a stupid thing to do. But I’ve never been able to turn down a free meal. [Laughs] People have said things to me like, ‘Surely you knew,’ or ‘How could you possibly have been so stupid?’”

“But I’m sorry,” he continued, “the fact is there was absolutely no one else in the vicinity; whatever the police arrest report says, there was no one else in the vicinity. It was just me. Well actually it was two undercover cops, only I couldn’t see one of them. Two undercover cops and a randy pop star, you know what I’m saying? So there was no one there. So if someone’s waving their genitalia at you, you don’t automatically assume they’re an officer of the law.”

Michael says his greatest regret about the incident was the pain he caused his boyfriend, which is larger than any sort of personal embarrassment the arrest caused. Actually, Michael’s new video, “Outside,” makes fun of the whole incident.

I say cut the poor guy some slack. Are you telling me you’ve never done that at Cooper Creek or Flat Rock Park? Give me a break. And undercover cops? What are the odds of that?

Well, regardless of his bad timing in the past, his new greatest hits collection, “Ladies & Gentlemen: The Best of George Michael,” is set to hit stores next week.
