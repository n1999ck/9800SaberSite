Nursing students brighten New Horizons
Office of Public Relations

HOME

The infant nursery of Columbus’ New Horizons Community Service Board Women’s Day Treatment Program, L-shaped with white-painted walls, had provided minimal visual stimulation, which is vital to the well-being of infants and toddlers. Students in Columbus State University’s Bachelor of Science nursing program identified the opportunity for enhancement and recently provided an extensive facelift for the room - walls painted brightly in primary colors and new toys provided through Warren’s Kiddie Shop in the Peachtree Mall. Other contributors included the March of Dimes ($1,000), Home Quarters (paint), Home Depot (painting supplies) and Melinda Newton (two “exersaucers”).

New Horizon’s Women’s Day Treatment Program is committed to helping pregnant or postpartum teens eliminate substance abuse and learn to cope and live in a high-risk environment. Significantly, these teens graduate from high school on schedule or attain a GED, and several progress to technical school or college. “These teens who are pregnant or already have babies need tremendous support as they pursue an education and workforce entry,” says Julie Miles, interim director of the women’s program. “We appreciate the work of the students who participated in the remodeling. “The project represents an extension of the “maternal child/community rotation” of the students’ nursing program.
