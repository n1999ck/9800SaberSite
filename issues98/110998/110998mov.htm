Entertainment woes: movies
by Wes Carroll / Guest Writer

HOME

Part four of our series on “What’s Wrong in Entertainment” will focus on movies. Now, I know that the number of flaws in movies are too numerous to count, so we will focus on what I think are the main five.

Flaw number one is simple - Brandy has no business acting. We tolerated it with the whole “Moesha” thing because that was on the WB and no one watches that anyway. But putting her in her first movie (and we’re not counting that “Cinderella” remake with Whitney Houston) “I Still Know What You Did Last Summer Part 2, the Search for Spock Lives On” is a huge mistake. This could lead to OTHER movies and that would simply be a bigger waste of film than my Senior Prom pictures.

Flaw number two (actually the biggest of the group, yet not more important than keeping Brandy off the big screen) is the repetitive story line for summer movies. I know that Hollywood thinks that we, the viewing audience, are stupid, but why release nine movies about volcanoes, six movies about floods, eight movies about tornadoes, and twelve movies about meteors/comets in just three years? It just doesn’t make any sense.

Flaw three has to do with why are we going to see all of these repetitive plot movies? I have a suggestion for you all- don’t go see them, and the studios will get the message. Plus, chances are that no matter how horrible the natural disaster may be, the star is going to save the day in every single one of the movies.

Flaw four: Wesley Snipes is a good actor. Why did he stop acting? At what point did he decide, “Well, I’m hailed by critics and fellow actors as a good dramatic actor, women seem to like me in those roles, the only other black leading man I’ll be competing with is Denzel Washington, so maybe I should stick with good dramatic roles instead of....nahhhh, I wanna be the black Rambo!”

Come on, Wesley, do us all a favor and act! It is a true waste of a fine actor (rent the movie “The Waterdance” if you don’t believe me) to see you shirtless and killing ninja vampires with Kenny Rogers (or whatever).

Finally, flaw number five is to re-stress my disapproval of Brandy in a movie. It never should have happened. I know that we all want to see Jennifer “Love” Hewitt in that tanning bed scene of “I Still Know Why Sting’s Real Name is Gordon Sumner”, but if we give in and see the movie, we risk having to see Brandy again. And quite frankly, it’s not worth the trade-off.
