CSU Baseball wraps up practice
by Patrick Collins / Sports Editor

HOME

The future looks bright for the Columbus State Baseball Team as they finished their fall session last Tuesday.

The NCAA allows a 6 week time period in which the entire team can combine and work together on areas such as hitting, base-running fielding, and other team oriented aspects of the game.

The Cougars boast a young, but very talented squad with the addition of an unheard of 15 members from the freshman class. This, coupled with the leadership of 8 seasoned seniors, should give this team the balance they need in the spring for a push at the Peach Belt conference title.

“At times we looked hungry out there this fall,” says head coach Greg Appleton, “we’ll have to play hungry in the spring to make up for our lack of experience.”

Appleton was quick to stress the importance of his senior leadership and their role in the influence of this team. “Those eight Seniors have to step up and lead this team in the right direction. Their influence will be key to the success of this team,” says Coach Appleton.

Stand-outs in the fall include Seniors Robert Cornett, Tony Pierce, and Brad Bender as well as Sophomores Luke Large and Rodney Kennedy. Also, every Freshman has a chance to see some playing time.

To end the fall session both Assistant Coaches Vince Massey and Patrick Collins drafted the CSU players and played the annual CSU World Series. The outcome was disappointing for the Massey’s red team as Coach Collins’ blue team completed a decisive three game sweep and got the right, at least for one day, to be called World Champions!

No playing position has been nailed down so this spring should be an exciting time to see where the players are placed. This year would be a great time for the CSU students to get involved and COME SEE SOME GAMES!!!
