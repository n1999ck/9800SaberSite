Students react to the recent elections

Susan Webb, Health Science

I wish Guy Millner had won because I am a Republican. I really got discouraged, though, because of all the mud-slinging. I think that there should be a rule that the candidates can only talk about themselves.

Brittany Allen, Communications

Even though I volunteered at the Republican headquarters, I am satisfied that Barnes will be our new governor. Throughout the election, there was talk of Millner taking away the H.O.P.E. scholarship if he was elected. I am a H.O.P.E. recipient and I’m glad the program is going to continue.

Jerry Barnes, Nursing

I am happy about the results of the election. I had more confidence in Barnes to continue the H.O.P.E. scholarship. In the last campaign with Zell Miller vs. Guy Millner, Millner said that there was no hope in H.O.P.E. The future of H.O.P.E. is very important to me because I also have two daughters in school. As far as the national election, I am glad that the Democrats gained more seats because they dealt with the real bread and butter issues like social security, education, and Medicare.

Ron Fuller, Computer Science

I am not happy with the results of either the state or national elections. I don’t think that the Democrats did a good job and I am not happy about the Democratic gain of seats. I think Clinton will escape much easier now. I am all for getting rid of him. He broke the law. I was hoping that the Republicans would make a really strong showing and set precedence for the next presidents. The gain for the Democrats is an indication that this country is tired of listening to impeachment talk and that people are falling for Clinton’s rhetoric. It’s disappointing. As far as the governor race, Millner had plans that fit better with what I think should happen in this state.
