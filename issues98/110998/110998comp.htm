Computer Center makes transition
by E'Lisha Williams / Guest Writer

HOME

During the Fall 1998 semester, Columbus State University’s (CSU) Computer Center is offering workshops designed to introduce CSU students to Windows 95 and Office 97.

Effective December 31, 1998, DOS programs and Windows 3. 11 software will no longer be available via the CSU networking system.

Bill Johnson, the executive director of Computer Information Network Systems, says “This shift is required for CSU to keep pace with technology in academic and administrative areas. Overall Windows 95 and Office 97 are easier to deal with.”

Johnson admits that this transition has been difficult for him. “The transition hasn’t been easy; knowing WordPerfect and now switching to Word [Microsoft Word].”

Johnson is concerned because since this transition has been difficult for him and some faculty, he is sure it is difficult for some students also.

Johnson says, “There are still some things I don’t know... and I have to go to classes myself.”

Although there are pamphlets and an e-mail on CSU’s noticeboard to inform students about CSU’s Windows 95 and Office 97 workshops., there has been little response.

According to Johnson, “there has been no good success” with the number of students signing up for classes. At the last computer class offered, “30 students signed up, but only 5 showed up.”

The next workshop offered to students is Thursday, December 1 from 6-9 p.m. Students can sign up in the Computer Center.

Johnson says, “We will continue to offer these workshops and will try to schedule them frequently to allow interested students to be prepared for 1999.”
