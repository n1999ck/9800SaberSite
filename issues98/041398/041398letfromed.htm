Letter from the Editor

by Heather Warren / Associate Editor
I was driving down the road the other day, half way listening to my radio, when I heard a scary statistic. Columbus is the highest market for the Jerry Springer show.

For the few of you who do not know what the Jerry Springer show is let me clue you in. It is an afternoon talk show that has very little talking (that you can hear due to the censors) and a lot of WWF action. Basically you judge the show by how many “good” fights were on that day. No fights means bad ratings for the day. It also appears that most guests have little in the common sense department. So in the end you have a lot of angry people speaking profanities and trying to get in the best punch.

Okay now that we have established what it is, let’s talk about ratings. The Columbus area is the highest market for the show. The Springer show is on NBC and runs up against the CBS and ABC local newscasts. It seems that people are not watching the news anymore.

One other funny thing about the show is that it is like a cult thing. People watch but everyone denies it. Ask anyone about the Jerry Springer show and they will know what you are talking about, but then ask if they watch it. Chances are they will say they saw one part one day while flipping channels. I know that for a fact because that is my line.

Yes, Yes I am admitting it. I do understand the attraction for the show but not when it runs against our local news cast. I suppose it could be that people get burned out by the news after the first commercial break and need a little laugh so they turn on Jerry.

The newest thing in town is the Jerry Springer hour at the Choctaw Grill. Okay this is taking things to an extreme for me. Not only let’s secretly watch the ridiculous panel of distinguished guest make idiots of themselves for our own amusement. Let’s go drink and watch them with others. It’s like a support group for those who are Jerry Springer addicts. What was once a source of embarrassment has become an afternoon event.

Okay let this be known, I am not passing judgement on those who enjoy a slight laugh with Jerry Springer. Let’s just not revel in our own disfunctionality by doing it as a hobby.

I am not an extremist who thinks this is a sign of the deterioration of our society. I only hope people do not watch for intellectual stimulation or for matters on their love life.

One more item of interest is that Jerry Springer show has signed an agreement to stop the violence. Well this makes me wonder. Will the show live or will another wild and crazy tactic be used to attract all of us with dull lives. The answer to that will have to be a mystery for now.
