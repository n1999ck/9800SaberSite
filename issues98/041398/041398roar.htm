Everyone knows the common stereotype, “Greeks are only good for one thing and that is a party.” People could not be more wrong. As a member of a Greek organization, I have personal experience to prove these rumors to be false. Research has also contradicted the ridiculous rumors conjured up by people as well.

Greek organizations obtain these standards in several ways. According to Jim Morris, Director of Student Counseling Services at the University of Idaho, Greek organizations serve as positive influences. Morris says, “ I think there is a psychological factor feeling part of something supported, the identification within a group. He also comments that, “ I think that contributes to their willingness to stay and progress through school, and there is also a fair emphasis in fraternities and sororities on academics; an interest in house grade performance starting with their freshman year working with pledges.”

To further prove that Greek organizations excel in academics, the Xi-Iota Chapter of Kappa Sigma at Columbus State University has shown what diligence and commitment can do. This past quarter, Winter ‘98, Kappa Sigma compiled a total cumulative grade point average of 3.11 as a chapter. This is the highest GPA the chapter has had and better things lie ahead in the future. Brannon Kuykendall, Kappa Sigma president comments, “ Our current academic performance speaks highly of the ideals of our fraternity. It is our belief that the primary purpose of the collegiate experience is to better oneself academically and socially. The diligence and commitment the brothers of our chapter have displayed proves that the Kappa Sigma Fraternity truly is excellence is action.” Jon Gray, Scholarship Chairman, also felt strongly about the fraternities progress as well. He states, “ Hard work and dedication is the key to getting ahead in life, no matter what you are doing. As a Kappa Sigma, we strive for excellence in and out of the classroom. It is for this reason we take such pride in our academic achievement.”

Kappa Sigma certainly strives for excellence both in and out of the classroom. With twenty-two active brothers who pride themselves with both active community projects and high academic standards, the Xi-Iota Chapter of Kappa Sigma proves that Greek organizations are worth more than just a party!

Michael Cox, CSU Student
