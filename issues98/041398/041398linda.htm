Campus Candid: Linda Hurst

by Grant Salter / Guest Writer
Linda Hurst started CSU through the adult re-entry program in 1993. Before returning to school Hurst was a professional mom as well as a career woman.

Hurst has crossed over all the lines typically drawn for non-traditional students. After a representative from the Student Government Association spoke to her about the SGA, she decided to make the best of the time she was going to spend in college.

Hurst began to get involved and take an active role in student life on campus. She has served in every executive office for SGA and is now serving as President.

Hurst says, “If I had not had some of my friends behind me I could not do all that I do. My friend Tracie [Price] started school with me and served in SGA with me and I could not have done it with out her”. Hurst has served on numerous committees, as well as an orientation leader through her years at CSU.

Graduating with her Bachelors Degree in Criminal Justice, Hurst is almost ready to finished here at CSU. After graduation she plans to work as a Juvenile Probation Specialist. Hurst chose this career after having problems with her son in the Juvenile Court System.

Hurst is not just a student who goes to class and hangs out on campus. She has a family of three that demands much of her time. Her son is currently applying to colleges himself and says he would love to follow in his mothers footsteps. Hurst’s daughter is finishing her Freshman year at Shaw High School.

Hurst says “my family means a lot to me, it’s not always easy but I try to be the best mom that I can.”
