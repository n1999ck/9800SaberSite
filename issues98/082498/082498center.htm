New education center on campus

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
Columbus State University will expand it’s educational opportunities in the fall semester with the addition of a new center on campus.

The Center for International Education will begin services in the fall semester with study abroad, foreign exchange and internship opportunities for students. Dr. Neal McCrillis, Director, has a list of goals which he would like to see the center accomplish in the upcoming year.

According to the Mission Statement of the Center for International Education, the center exists primarily to enrich and broaden the understanding of both students and faculty about the world.

The new center was created as a result of faculty-initiatives in the past. However, in the past there have been no coordinated programs for internship or study abroad according to Dr. McCrillis.

The Center will function on three main objectives. The Center will focus on support coordination and dissemination. This will entail the coordinating of international programs developed by Columbus State University faculty and the distribution of information regarding these opportunities.

A few of the existing programs include the Kiryu English Language Institute, study abroad programs in Mexico, Spain and St. Petersburg, Russia and the Oxford, England summer course. The second objective involves initiating new programs.

Dr. McCrillis would like to begin a Latin American Iminent Scholars program which is an Endowed program and an Irish Studies program, which is very probable.

The third and final objective is to internationalize the curriculum by cultivating a more international curriculum across disciplines.

Dr. McCrillis states that he would like to see the Center create an identity on campus as well as in the community as a leader.

Dr. McCrillis came to CSU in July and is a member of the History faculty as well as being the Director of the Center for International Education and Chairman of the Mildred Miller Fort Chair of International Education.
He states, “ I am really impressed with what the program has done so far with funding and the level of activity of the faculty with their participation.”

The Center for International Education hopes to enlighten people about their opportunities and provide them with these opportunities as well.

Dr. McCrillis concludes by saying, “ In five years I hope the Center for International Education has created a solid identity on campus and that people will know that the office exists.”
