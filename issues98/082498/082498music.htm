What's new in music - Liz Phair releases new CD

by Brian Hale / Editor-in-Chief
Liz Phair hit the stores last week with her first album in four years.

The new album, “WhiteChocolate-SpaceEgg”, is not much of a departure from the other Liz Phair material, but shows a bit more variety and growth than her previous albums.

I think this is a good album, and worth picking up next time you go shopping.

The set list is as follows: “White Chocolate Space Egg,” “Big Tall Man,” “Perfect World, “Johnny Feelgood,” “ Polyester Bride,” “ Love Is Nothing,” “Baby Got Going,” “Uncle Alvarez,” “Only Son,” “Go On Ahead,” “Headache,” “Ride,” “What Makes You Happy,” “Fantasize,” “Shitloads Of Money,” and “Girls’ Room.”

Dishwalla is back with their follow-up album to their 1995 release, “Pet Your Friends.” The new album is called “And You Think You Know What Life’s About.”

Dishwalla is Scott Alexander, Rodney Browning-Cravens, George Pendergast, J.R. Richards, and Jim Wood.

This album has several potential hits and has an overall better sound to it than their previous projects.

The set list for the album is as follows: “Stay Awake,” “Once In a While,” “Bottom of the Floor,” “Healing Star,” “Until I Wake Up,” “5 Star Day,” “Truth Serum,” “So Blind,” “Gone Upside Down,” “So Much Time,” “The Bridge Song,” and “Pop Guru.”

The new Culture Club album is out is stores and is excellent. It is a double album, featuring their VH1 Storytellers performance and a “Greatest Moments” cd.

I will have a more detailed description of this album, the story behind it, and the band next week.
I would also recommend to anyone that has not already done so, to pick up Third Eye Blind’s album and anything by Our Lady Peace.

I had a chance to catch their show at The Fox in Atlanta last week and it was awesome. Eve6 was the first band out, but I was running late and I missed them.

It was my second time watching Our Lady Peace and they were excellent. They ran through most of their hits, including “Starseed,” “Superman’s Dead,” “4am,” and “Clumsy.” They closed out their set with the late Jeff Buckley song “Eternal Life.”

Third Eye Blind was very impressive. I had not wanted to stay and watch their show, but the guy I was with convinced me to stay for a few songs, and I was hooked.

We stayed the entire show, and they were great. Running down a highlight list is impossible, because they played everything they had, a new song called “Gorgeous,” and even played The Who’s “Baba O’Riley.”

Our Lady Peace has dropped off the tour to take a break and then begin working on a new album, but Third Eye Blind and Eve6 are still on the road. If you get a chance to catch this show, I highly recommend it.
