Campus Candid: Joel Judah

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
Joel Judah is one of the most successful students at Columbus State University. Through hard work and dedication, Joel has made his way to the top and takes pride in every one of his endeavors at Columbus State University. Joel Judah has achieved many great things in his time at Columbus State University.

A graduate of Columbus High School, Joel came to CSU in the Fall Quarter of 1995. When asked why he chose CSU, Joel replied, “I chose CSU because I had been around it a lot and I knew that it was a good school.”

Joel is a Biology- Pre-Med student who is currently applying to Medical Schools. Joel was awarded the Faculty Cup Award this past year which was one of his many achievements. Joel is involved both academically and socially on campus.

A few organizations that Joel is a member of include Student Government Association, of which he is President, an tow honors programs, Beta Beta Beta and Phi Kappa Phi. He is also a member of the Baptist Student Union at Columbus State.

Outside the campus, Joel expands his involvement into the community by being a member of the Board of Columbus Kiryu Club, Sunday School Teacher, and a volunteer in the Emergency Room at the Medical Center.

Joel states, “CSU has given me a variety of study abroad experiences.”

He traveled to Kiryu, Japan during his freshman year and attended Oxford University as well.

Joel says, “Columbus State University has given me a foundation for success. The educational experience has taught me facts and information as well as the desire and ability to learn on my own in life.”
