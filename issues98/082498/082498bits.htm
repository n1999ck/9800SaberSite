Music Bits - Sammy Hagar in acting debut

by Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor
Former Van Halen frontman Sammy Hagar has just finished taping an episode of the television show “Nash Bridges” in San Francisco, which according to several sources, is his acting debut. He played a bartender, a role he plays frequently at his club, the Cabo Wabo Cantina in Cabo San Lucas, Baja California.

Hagar held a live show at his Cantina late last month to test some of his new material and to finalize the cover for his upcoming new album, “Red Voodoo.” The album will be his second solo project since leaving Van Halen, and is due out in January.

Marilyn Manson will show the world his new sound, and his new glam look at this year’s MTV Video Music Awards on September 10 in Los Angeles.

Manson’s new album, the much anticipated “Mechanical Animals”, is set to hit stores on September 15. Manson has finished shooting the first video from the album, for the song “Dope Show.”

Regardless that the release date is set and plans are being finalized, the project has hit a few bumps in the road. Retail stores Wal-Mart and K-Mart have refused to sell the album as long as it has its current cover art, which depicts Marilyn Manson dressed up to look like a stripped-down doll with prosthetic breasts. Manson’s record label, Interscope Records, is currently working on an alternative cover that would cover Manson’s breasts.

The set list for the album is as follows: “Great Big White World”, “The Dope Show”, “Mechanical Animals”, “Rock Is Dead”, “Disassociative”, “The Speed Of Pain”, “Posthuman”, “I Want To Disappear”, “I Don’t Like The Drugs (But The Drugs Like Me)”, “New Model no. 15”, “User Friendly”, “Fundamentally Loathsome”, “The Last Day On Earth” and “Coma White.”

A new video is in the works for Pearl Jam. The band confirmed that they had collaborated on a new video for “Do The Evolution,” its first since 1992’s “Jeremy,” with Todd McFarlane, artist and creator of the “Spawn” comic book series.

“We are challenged to expand the meaning of our work, and by utilizing this visual medium and working with a visionary like Todd, we were able to further explore some of the themes we depicted in the song, ‘Do the Evolution.’ Basically, we’ve tried to make a good stoner video,” said the bands official statement.

Two weeks ago Pearl Jam released a full-length home video, “Single Video Theory,” taken from footage of the band rehearsing for the Yield Tour.

A spokesperson for Beck’s record label, Geffen Records, confirmed a “Los Angeles Times” report that the artist has laid down several tracks for his next album. “Mutations” will hit stores on October 20.

The possible set list for the album is as follows: “Cold Brains,” “Nobody’s Fault But My Own,” “Lazy Flies,” “Canceled Check,” “We Live Again,” “Tropicalia,” “Dead Melodies,” “Bottle of Blues,” “O Maria,” “Sing It Again,” and “Static.”

Ol’ Dirty Bastard has done it again. The Wu-Tang Clan rapper missed another court date in Virginia Beach, Virginia, so Judge Robert L. Simpson, Jr. issued an order for his arrest without bond.

When caught by the police, ODB will be kept in jail until his next scheduled court appearance to make sure that he actually shows up. He was scheduled to appear in court on Thursday August 13 on shoplifting charges after missing his date on Tuesday August 11, allegedly because of bad weather conditions.

As I reported several weeks ago, Ol’ Dirty Bastard, a.k.a. Big Baby Jesus, a.k.a. Russell Jones, is accused of walking out of a shoe store on July 4 with a pair of $50 Nike shoes. He was then ordered to appear on July 28 in court, but failed to show up for that date as well. It seems that the only thing that ODB can show up on time for is a robbery.
