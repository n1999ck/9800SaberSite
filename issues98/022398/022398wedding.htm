A "Wedding Singer" review

by Josh Eller / Entertainment Editor

 
The Wedding Singer is a hilarious comedy finding Sandler, although very funny, playing a more toned down serious part. The story is set in the mid-eighties and if the clothes and hair styles aren’t funny enough, Sandler makes a living as a wedding singer living in his sister’s basement.

On the day Sandler’s character Robbie Hart is to be married he is left at the alter which in turns effects his motivation to perform at weddings. Along the way Robbie meets Julie (Drew Barrymore) and they fast become friends. Julie consoles Robbie and asks if he would mind helping out for her wedding, which is slated to take place in three months. Her fiancée is a womanizing Miami Vice, Don Johnson-esque, jerk of a fiancée. As the story progresses Drew finds that she is in love with Robbie, and with the help of Billy Idol and Ann Landers Robbie is able to intervene on Julie’s Vegas weekend wedding to save the day and live happily ever after with the girl of his dreams. ***
