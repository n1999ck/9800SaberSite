Enduring the stress factor

by Shannon Jackson / Staff Writer

 
Stress n. ...a state in which a strong demand is made on the nervous system......(per Webster).

There are many causes of stress for college students these days. There are the obvious ones, lack of time , workload, lack of money, professor demands and motivation. Time is a big factor. Taking a full load is exactly as it sounds; a full load.

A large number of students at Columbus State are attending full time and not only does the class time consume a large portion of students’ days, but finding time to study seems to be a problem for a number of students as well.

Shannon Morey, a communications major at CSU says that she has to consider her son when scheduling classes. She says that she has to take night classes often because her husband works during the day. The stressful aspect of this situation is that she does not get to spend the quality time in the evenings with her son that she would like.

Some students claim that the workload can place a great deal of stress on them. Shawanda Watkins is taking 23 hours this quarter and finding time and motivation to study seems difficult at times. Shawanda also says that her mother attends Columbus State and the two of them are quite competitive with their grades.

Money seems to be a very common stress factor for almost any college student, regardless of the workload. Any student receiving loans that must be paid back will have that lurking over their head until well after graduation. This realization places added pressure on top of the usual stresses of being a college student. Also, a large number of students are unable to work during college and often do not have the money it takes just to survive on a day to day basis.

There are other not so obvious factors that some may not consider when dealing with students. Some students feel that professors, do not seem to consider that students have other classes.

Also, the CSU student body is quite unique compared to other universities in our region. Many of the students here are local and the average age is 25-35. It is very hard to balance full time jobs, bills, spouses and children and obtain a college degree at the same time.

Buddy Bergy states that he has been out of high school for over ten years and he is having to “relearn” how to be a student.

Most students handle the stress of college life quite well. But for those students who cannot seem to deal, never fear. There is help. The Counseling Center, located in 146 Davidson Student Center, offers many services to the students at CSU. They provide a professional and confidential service to students.
