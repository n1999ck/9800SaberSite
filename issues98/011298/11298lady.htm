Lady Cougars Claim Victory

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

Back to Frontpage

For Kennesaw State women s basketball team, the Owls Nest has been a very comfortable home for the past year, however, the Nest was raided for the first time in 29 games by the CSU Lady Cougars. The Lady Cougars used a 20-point outburst by guard Reagan Housch to defeat the 21st ranked Lady Owls 89-87.

The Lady Cougars set the pace of the game by jumping out quickly to a 20-8 lead. Housch started out sizzling, hitting on her first four three-point attempts. Kennesaw then began looking like the same dominating team of the past, taking the lead and stretching it to as many as 11 points in the second half. The Lady Cougars kept fighting and clawing and used an 11-2 spurt to pull within two points. In the process of the comeback, CSU s All-Conference player Que Gilliam fouled out, leaving a big hole to be filled. Columbus reserves stepped up their play after Gilliam s departure and helped take the lead and hold on to upset the Lady Owls.

Along with Housch s 20 points, Tocshia Campfield added 18 and Carrie Long put in 10. With the victory, the Lady Cougars raised their record to 7-2, and 2-0 in Peach Belt play.
