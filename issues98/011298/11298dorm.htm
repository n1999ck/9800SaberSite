CSU Gets New Dorms

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

Back to Frontpage

Change is evident here on our campus of Columbus State University. That change brings about the development and growth of our school. Along with the growth of the university comes the growth of the student body. The next step was to find a place for these new students to live while attending CSU.

Columbus State University, in a joint effort from Student Housing, Auxiliary Services, and Plant Operations, recently acquired Peachtree Apartments for new student housing. These apartments are located across the street from the main entrance of the university, and will be called Courtyard 2. Current housing at Columbus State University holds approximately 230 students per quarter, with nearly 125 students on a waiting list for housing.

Maria Holmes, from Auxiliary services, said that the set-up for the Courtyard 2 would be the same as for Courtyard 1. Remodeling of the apartments has already begun and will continue right up until Fall semester in August.

Thirty-four apartments will have a one bedroom and one bathroom setup. Thirty-two apartments will have a two bedroom and one and a half bathroom setup. The final twelve apartments will have a three bedroom and two bathroom setup. The new courtyard will be equipped with an office and a clubhouse. There is no confirmation on if there will be a swimming pool or not .

Larry Kees, assistant Vice-President for Student Affairs , played a key role in the acquisition of the apartment complex. The purchase, which was made final in November of 1997, will nearly double the housing available to Columbus State University students. This new housing will not only benefit the students, but will offer much needed space to bring various camps and organizations to CSU, such as, basketball, cheerleading and volleyball camps.

The need for new student housing arose when the university had to turn students away, because of the lack of available housing. Larry Kees commented, turning people away became very frustrating and we do not want to do that. Purchasing the apartments was an, instantaneous solution, according to Mr. Kees.

When Courtyard 2 is fully functional, ten percent of the student body will live in student housing.

The apartments are open to Columbus State University students and are available on a first come-first serve basis. A signed contract and a $100 deposit will reserve you a room in the new Courtyard. The set date that Courtyard 2 will be available for use is in August 1998.
