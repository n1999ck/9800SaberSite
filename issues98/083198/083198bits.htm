Music Bits: Aerosmith soon to kick off tour

by Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor
>> Aerosmith is about to kick off their fall tour on September 9 in Scranton, Pennsylvania. The band has announced plans to release a new live album, its first live project since 1988’s “Classics Live 2,” on October 20.

“A Little South of Sanity,” features 23 songs taken from the 1993-94 world tour in support of “Get a Grip” and several tracks from the current “Nine Lives” tour.

The track listing for “A Little South of Sanity” is as follows: “Eat the Rich,” “Love in an Elevator,” “Falling in Love (Is Hard on the Knees),” “Same Old Song and Dance,” “Hole in My Soul,” “Monkey on My Back,” “Livin’ on the Edge,” “Cryin’,” “Rag Doll,” “Angel,” “Janie’s Got a Gun,” “Amazing,” “Back in the Saddle,” “Last Child,” “The Other Side,” “Walk on Down,” “Dream On,” “Crazy,” “Mama Kin,” “Walk this Way,” “Dude (Looks Like a Lady),” “What It Takes,” and “Sweet Emotion.”

>> Frontman for the band Days of the New, Travis Meeks, is acting up again. Actually, he is acting more like a child than he is a band leader.

Meeks has been telling fans and friends that he plans on continuing on without the help of the other players in the band. The record company has denied the reports, saying that, “Travis just talks.”

According to their official website last month, Days of the New were going to be making an announcement last week concerning their future. That announcement never happened. What did happen is that a message was posted that said, “Hey everybody... We’re one big #!@$%# happy family!!!”

Meeks appeared on a message board online recently and posted a message apologizing for “giving people the impression that Days of the New was a band.” He continued, “It wasn’t truly a band format, it is my fault for leading you on and setting you up to get attached to the prior members.”

Meeks tells his fans that he is going to play all the instruments himself and that only one other band member appears on the album, which is due out early next year.

I think the other band members, the record label, and the few fans they have acquired only by being an opening act for Metallica have waisted way too much time on this spoiled brat.

>> Speaking of brats, Oasis is putting the finishing strokes on a new album, “The Masterplan.” It is a collection of B-sides that have appeared on the group’s U.K. singles. The follow up release to their excellent 1997 album, “Be Here Now,” is due out in November.

The track listing for “The Masterplan” is as follows: “Acquiesce” - from “Some Might Say” single, “Underneath the Sky” - from “Don’t Look Back in Anger” single, “Talk Tonight” - from “Some Might Say” single, “Going Nowhere” - from “Stand By Me” single, “Fade Away” - from “Cigarettes & Alcohol” single ,”The Swamp Song” - from “Wonderwall” single, “I Am the Walrus” - from “Cigarettes & Alcohol” single, “Listen Up” - from “Cigarettes & Alcohol” single, “Rockin’ Chair” - from “Roll With It” single, “Half the World Away” - from “Whatever” single,”(It’s Good) To Be Free” - from “Whatever” single, “Stay Young” - from “D’You Know What I Mean?” single, “Headshrinker” - from “Some Might Say” single, and “The Masterplan” - from “Wonderwall” single.

>> The new solo project from the creator of White Zombie hit stores last week. Rob Zombie brings us his new project, “Hellbilly Deluxe,” equipped with Parental Advisory sticker, spooky cover art, even spookier art on the inside. I don’t recommend listening to this album alone - or at all. Don’t get me wrong, it stays true to Zombie form, and is still a talented version of Marilyn Manson. It’s loud, hard, and well put together - it’s just creepy.

>> On a lighter note, I mentioned last week that the Culture Club have released a new album. If you have not picked it up yet, you need to. It is a double album, with a greatest hits cd and their wonderful VH1 Storytellers performance. Both discs feature “Church of the Poison Mind,” “I’ll Tumble for Ya’,” “Do You Really Want To Hurt Me,” “The Crying Game,” “Karma Chameleon,” and many others. The liner notes to the album are done by Boy George, and they are reason enough to buy this album.
