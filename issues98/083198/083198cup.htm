World Cup 98 recap

by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor
>> United States falters

The World Cup of ‘98 was supposed to be a successful one for the United States. However it turned out to be a disaster. The U.S. lost all three of their games and forced the resignation of coach Steve Sampson.

Gearing up for the competition the team defeated Brazil, who at the time was ranked #1 in the world, but struggled with lesser known teams. Another blow came when John Harkes, who was captain of the ‘94 team was left off the roster. The troubles continued as they suffered a 2-0 loss at the hands of the Germans in the opening game.

Many players were upset at not getting adequate playing time throughout the tournament. Eric Wynalda, who started the majority of the games in the previous World Cup, saw only limited action against Yugoslavia, Alexi Lalas sat on the bench the entire tournament, and Marcela Balboa only played a few minutes in the final match.

So now upset and defeated the U.S. looks for a new coach and better opportunities. The selection has centered around Bora Militinovich, coach of Nigeria, and Bruce Arena who coaches D.C. United. The decision should be fairly soon and maybe 2002 World Cup will be better.

>> France wins

Imagine the Atlanta Falcons winning the Superbowl, well France did just that except in soccer this past summer. They defeated the reigning world champions Brazil 3-0 in the final match.

This feat has only been accomplished three previous times by the host country, and this one was possibly the biggest upset ever. Brazil boasts many of the top players in the world, and they play for the top professional teams around the globe. However, on July 12th none of that mattered as France controlled the entire game.

After the victory, fans flowed into the streets and celebrated until the early morning hours. Many people, as well as the French press, did not give the team any chance, but they were proven wrong.
