Letter to the Editor

I am writing this letter due to an incident that occurred on 19 August, 1998, while I was representing a group during the orientation day for new students. I WAS SHOCKED TO SEE ONE OF THE PROSPECTIVE STUDENTS, WEARING A SEXUALLY DESCRIPTIVE T-SHIRT, WHICH ON THE FRONT APPEARED THE SLOGAN “OFFICIAL MUFF DIVING INSTRUCTOR” AND ON THE BACK APPEARED A GRAPHIC PORTRAYAL OF TWO DARK SILHOUETTES ENGAGED IN A SEXUAL POSITION.

It may be unpopular in such a liberal institution such as this one to express disgust over this obvious “ABUSE” of the freedom of speech. Yet in an environment where mixed gender, parents of new students and people who may have religious values that compel them to avoid this type of material are sure to mingle the thoughtless action of those people who display such material is unacceptable. Having spanned a twenty year military career, this behavior is not totally new to me, but even our maligned service members have some scope of how to act in mixed company.

Since it is not expected that peer pressure would dissuade these people from this behavior, and since peer students have no official authority to address these people without enduring a fusillade of insults or possible physical altercations, it seems that the school faculty should actively work to protect an institution of higher learning.

After all, many of your students come from high schools were this type of activity is not tolerated. Furthermore the display of t-shirt slogans and other benign types of messages can be construed as sexually harassing, or perhaps religiously so. At any rate, these types of actions cannot make our students feel comfortable in a learning environment.

I am not naive in my beliefs concerning this matter, and am aware that to many young people, this type of material is inoffensive. I do believe however, that in attaining a higher academic level we should also strive to raise our moral and civic responsibilities. Our parents and many of our students due not send/attend this place of learning to have their children or themselves exposed to these childish immature (as opposed to “adult”) materials.

In my estimation, all faculty members should be required to identify such potentially, offensive material, and instruct such students to report to an assigned authority on such matters who can make the final determination on the acceptability of such material. In this manner the professors would not be the acting authority and any official protest could be handled through that authorities office, such as the Dean of Students.

It is well that universities are known as a place for open exchange of ideas, but it seems to me that those ideas should be tempered in such away as not to outright imply an insult, simply for the sake of an insult.

I appreciate your indulgence of time in considering this matter, and request that you discuss it with what ever responsible agent seems appropriate to you. I apologize for the graphic material that is the subject of this message, but if it is offensive or shocking to you perhaps it will relate my feelings on the matter.

Your Servant,
A.R. Johnson
