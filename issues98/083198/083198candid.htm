Campus Candid: Patricia Monteith

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
Patricia Monteith is a very successful student in many different ways at Columbus State University. Tricia is a native of LaGrange, Georgia where she attended Troup County High School and later graduated in 1996.

Monteith attended Columbus State University directly out of high school, wasting no time pursuing her college career.

“The reason I chose CSU was the friendly environment the campus contained and the outstanding student to teacher ratio,” Monteith stated.

Tricia is currently a junior with a double major, pursuing both Respiratory Therapy and Health Science. Tricia hopes to work as either a respiratory therapist or in health education following graduation.

Her success stretches beyond her academics as a result of her involvement in the Columbus State University C.O.R.E. and as a student assistant in the Admissions Office on campus.

Tricia says, “My involvement at CSU has allowed me to reach my personal best through communication skills and networking, both being essential needs for success in the business world today.”
