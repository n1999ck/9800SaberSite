Football just around corner

by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor
Are you ready for some football? Although the weather may not indicate it, fall is here. Now is the time when the leaves begin changing and the weekends are filled with non-stop football coverage. It is also a time when some teams surpass their goals and some remain in the doldrums.

The Atlanta Falcons fit well into this category. After a tough season last year, they look to rebound and actually be a contender. However, this is dependent on the health of quarterback Chris Chandler, who was constantly injured. They finished the season at 7-9 and lost six of those games when he was out. Dan Reeves has made some changes in the offensive line for better protection, but only time will tell.

On a local level how about the Auburn Tigers? Well, let’s just say last year was a great year. Dameyune Craig is gone, two starters have quit the team, and one player has been arrested. Terry Bowden will be happy for some good news, and it likely will not occur. They play LSU, Tennessee, and travel to Mississippi State and Florida. Without a proven quarterback, the tigers return to a ground attack and hope for better success.

Across the state Alabama looks to improve, but won’t contend for the SEC championship. The tide does return a starting running back, and a good corner back in Fernando Bryant, but the rest remains a big question mark. In order for the tide to be successful, many of the younger players must step up, and the quarterback must learn to throw. Despite the past success of running the ball, head coach Mike Dubose is determined to air it out.

Closer to home, Georgia should remain tough but will struggle to have the success that it did last year. The running game remains strong with Patrick Pass, Olandis Gary, and the addition of Columbus native Jasper Sanks. However, the quarterback position remains a question mark and will be a highly contested battle.

Georgia Tech has the best chance out of all the area colleges to have a very successful year. Despite the loss of key linebackers, the defense remains strong and Joe Hamilton returns at quarterback. The only weakness may be the receivers because they do not have a lot of experience, but the talent is there.

Let the season begin and we’ll see what happens.
