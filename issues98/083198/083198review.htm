CSU sports year in review

by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor
The 1997-98 athletic campaign was very successful with only men’s and women’s cross-country not going to post season competition.

The men’s basketball team finished the season with 26 wins and lost in the first round of the Division II playoffs to arch rival USC Aiken. Larry Coates was named to the the second team all-american.

The women were undefeated in regular season and lost to Francis Marion in the second round of the Division II playoffs. They finished the season with record of 22 and 4, their best ever.

Baseball was in transition under first year coach Larry Appleton, but the team finished strong with a trip to the playoffs. They defeated Georgia College and lost to #1 Kennesaw State.

The Lady Cougar softball team finished with a record of 18 and 2. They defeated #1 Kennesaw State before losing in the rematch. Julie Garcia was named to the first team all-america and now plays for the Virginia Roadsters of the Women’s Profession Fastpitch League.

Both the men and women tennis teams were ranked in the top 25. The women defeated Mississippi University for Women 5-0 and lost to Georgia College and State University in the following match of the Division II playoffs. The men finished the season with a 5-4 loss to the University of West Florida in the first round of playoffs.

The golf team had all the talent to repeat as national champs but came up short with a second place finish. Florida Southern made their move on the second day of competition and never looked back. John Drewery was named the Peach Belt player of the year.

The upcoming year looks to be just as competitive.
