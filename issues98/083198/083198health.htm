Health Weekly: Are you at risk of acquiring an STD?

by Jennifer Robinson / Staff Writer
Did you know that every day, 33,000 Americans get a STD? More than 12 million Americans get a STD each year. And, at least one in four Americans will have a STD at some time in their lives. There are more than 20 STDs out there. Of these, Genital Herpes affects more than 55 million Americans. Approximately 1/4 of all Americans over the age of 15 have genital herpes. The worst part is that 1/2 of all people infected with genital herpes do not even know it. The purpose of this article is to point out some common misinformation regarding this disease as well as the ease in acquiring it.

First of all, herpes is a problem for all who have ever been sexually active or plan to become sexually active (hmmm, could this mean you?). Yes, you! Herpes is not a disease that affects only the promiscuous or the individual who has had numerous sexual partners. A person can catch herpes even if one has only had sexual relations with one person and even if that person is your husband or wife!

So what are the classic symptoms of genital herpes? The symptoms vary in severity and type, from person to person, and from outbreak to outbreak. Herpes is one of those sexually transmitted diseases that does not follow any particular set of rules. The classic symptoms can include blister like sores on the genital areas as well as the upper thighs and rectal region, a red patch of skin, a tiny sore, an irritation around the anus, itching and burning of the genital areas, and pain during urination. Genital herpes can also result in swollen glands, fatigue and headache.

But I know what you’re saying to yourself right now. “Herpes happens to other people. I’m not going to get herpes.” Don’t kid yourself people. Remember, 1/2 of the people infected with herpes do not know they are infected.

Myth: You cannot get herpes if you always use a condom during sexual intercourse.

Fact: Using condoms does NOT provide 100% guarantee of protection from genital herpes. Abstinence is the only way to 100% guarantee your protection. But if you use condoms, realize that they may not cover all sites of exposure. Herpes can be present on the male’s scrotum or in the female’s vaginal secretions, which could reach skin not covered by condoms. Herpes infection can also exist on the upper thighs or rectal areas of either the man or woman.

Myth: Someone with herpes has noticeable sores or blemishes on his/her genitalia so if there is no visual evidence of herpes on my partner, then I can feel safe that he or she does not have herpes.

Fact: Herpes can be very subtle. It can go unnoticed for weeks, months, and even YEARS! As a result, herpes is often unknowingly and accidentally spread to a sexual partner. A person can acquire herpes without recognizing the first episode or even subsequent episodes because some of the milder symptoms resemble yeast infections, ingrown hair, heat bumps, bladder infections, the flu and hemorrhoids. Also, some people with herpes are symptom free all or most of their lives. But just because one shows no symptoms does not mean there is no herpes infection present. A person with herpes is contagious even when no outward symptoms are visible. Therein lies the danger of widespread transmission. To restate, 1/2 of all people infected with herpes do not know they are infected.

Finally, if one suspects he/she might have herpes, it is important to be seen by a physician or nurse practitioner and be tested. There is no cure for herpes. There are effective treatments and medications however. Herpes is not a death sentence. Herpes is an unfortunate fact of life for many more people than one might imagine. Herpes may be a part of your life or your future life. Know the facts. Protect yourself and your partner(s). Get tested. And be careful out there.

Resources: There are a number of organizations who provide confidential telephone counseling. There are also many web pages on the internet where one can find out more. Last, but certainly not least, the Student Health Clinic is available to students for information and testing.
