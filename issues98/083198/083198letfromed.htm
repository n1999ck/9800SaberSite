Letter from the Editor

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
The Fall Semester began with a bang as of August 24th when Columbus State University officially began the 1998-1999 school year.

The conversion to the semester system was not the only large event that hit the campus. Many organizations held events at the clock tower which attracted many students and at the same time helped these students to get involved on campus.

I was happy to see the increased support of the student organizations on campus attempting to encourage involvement on the campus of Columbus State University. Another noticeable occurrence on campus is the number of students out and about on campus. Columbus State University is slowly moving toward a more traditional campus instead of it’s past reputation of being a commuter school.

Overall, I am impressed with the interest of the incoming students this fall and strongly encourage every student at Columbus State University to get involved in any organization on campus. At the same time, I want to encourage every organization on campus to get these students involved and make their time at Columbus State University successful and enjoyable.

As a member of many organizations on campus, I know first hand the benefits of being involved. I, like many students on campus, came from somewhere other than Columbus. Getting involved solved my problem of both meeting new people and having fun. The effects of getting involved have been positive in my life both academically and socially.

Students, get involved and make CSU a positive aspect in your life.
