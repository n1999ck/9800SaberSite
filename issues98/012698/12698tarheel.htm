"Tarheel Tales" hits the road

by Josh Eller / Entertainment Editor

The CSU theatre department is currently involved in the well received production of Tom Behm’s “Tarheel Tales”. “Tarheel Tales” is a children’s theatre play based on five folk tales centered around the Carolina’s. Although very entertaining for children with its colorful costumes and audience participation, “Tarheel Tales” also presents children with the concept of right and wrong somewhat like that of Aesop’s Fables.

The six cast members who are all theatre majors find themselves engaged in a new but rewarding touring production. The set of “Tarheel Tales” is disassembled and reassembled for every production. Stage and tour manager Jeff Camp, who also designed the set is said to have had few problems with having to tote around an entire set in the mere confines of a van.

The cast members are Shaun Fuller, Kellie Lamb, Jennifer Todd, Jenny Newell, Melissa Walker, and William Enis. When speaking with William Enis he pointed out how much audience participation and pantamine are essential for keeping the children focused while entertaining. Enis also says how enjoyable “Tarheel Tales” is to perform with its complete ensemble having six cast members playing different parts for the average length of fifty-five minutes.

Tarheel’s first stop on the their city wide schedule of elementary schools was Blanchard Elementary where they were welcomed by hundreds of enthusiastic children ranging in grades from Pre-K through third grade. Mrs. Rochelle Jones, principal for Blanchard elementary comments, “Students and teachers alike really enjoyed the show. The audience was very attentive and responded very well to the presentation, and more importantly the children understood the messages behind the stories”. “Tarheel’s Tales” offers children exposure to semi-professional theatre that otherwise they may not know. “Tarheel Tales” will be touring throughout the month and will end production in early March.
