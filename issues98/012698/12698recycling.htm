Tri-Beta Recycling Campaign

by Evelyn Tolbert-Owens

Have you seen blue containers sitting around campus lately? Have you wondered what they are for as you stumbled across them at 7:45 one morning last week? The Tri Beta Honor Society of the CSU Biology Department is determined to keep our campus clean and do their part in preserving nature. They have launched a campus-wide recycling campaign for aluminum cans and covered all grounds to make access convenient for CSU students to dispose of their empty aluminum cans.

This project is not new but is an expansion of a project that has been ongoing in LeNoir Hall since 1992 when Tri Beta’s brainchild was first born. The project worked well and now has been expanded campus- wide. Members of the Honor Society are assigned to each of these barrels and are responsible for emptying the containers three times per week. The monies made with this effort has so far been used to finance the initiation ceremonies of the Society. However, if expansion results in greater income the money may even be used to finance student research equipment and for travel expenses to scientific meetings. The barrels were donated by Kinnett and painted blue by Tri Beta members before distributing them all over campus.

The members of the Tri Beta Honor Society preserving our campus are: Elizabeth Sikes, President; Dianne O’Shea, Vice-President; Elizabeth Lingo, Treasurer; Joel Judah, Historian; Farah Khan, Matt Martin, Nicole Griffith, Brad Hall, Dorana Vazquez-Hernandez, Ted Roever, Jason Harrison, Kim Hollingsworth, Thomas Hunter, Ron Patel, Sidney Harden, Jana Chalkley, Jimmy Sellers, Shaun Elliot, and Karen Biltz.
