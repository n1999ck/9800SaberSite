Gazebos on campus

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

In the last few months new gazebos have been spotted around campus. Some people have asked the purpose of these structures. Students have come to think of them as a smoking area. The Plant Operations and Landscaping Depart-ment say the gazebos serve several purposes.

Recently a new rule went into effect stating that no person can smoke within twenty feet of any building. This makes the new gazebos an alternative to standing in front of the buildings to have a cigarette. The gazebos were not a direct response to the smoking issue, but it just so happened their placement was at the same time of the placement of the new smoking rule.

Another reason was to help improve the look of the grounds on campus. Eddie Woodhouse, Assistant Director of Plant Operations, said, “Developing areas other than classrooms for students to get away from stress and pressure is one of our goals. We have a beautiful campus and we want everybody to enjoy it.”

There have been a number of problems with the gazebos as far students are concerned. Sophomore Jerri Lynn Hayes stated, “They leak, and need to be glassed in so it will be warmer.” She does, however, enjoy them and sits in them everyday. Hal Gibson, Director of Plant Operations, said that they had not received any complaints about the condition of the gazebos, but will look into it and see what can be done if any complaints arise.

The gazebos that were put up in several locations here on campus are just the beginning of the movement to beautify the grounds. This project is being overseen by the director of Plant Operations Hal Gibson, assistant director of Plant Operations Eddie Woodhouse, and Head of Landscaping Danella Lee.

The plan for landscaping the campus started in June and continues today. The plan is to develop more areas for students and professors to go to be able to relax and enjoy the outdoors. The clock tower and the area around Gentian Corner are the next areas to receive attention from the landscaping department.
