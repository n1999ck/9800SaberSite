Letter from the editor

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

 
Thursday the campus was crawling with men and women in black suits, wearing sunglasses and carrying briefcases. As they passed, people on campus turned and wondered who they were and what was going on.

Most everyone knows by now that the visitors where here in prelude to a visit from Vice President Al Gore. Gore did not arrive until Monday, but many of his crew arrived early and kept things busy on campus throughout the weekend.

It was interesting to watch what all goes on behind the scenes before an event such as this. Something about seeing the little tricks of the trade, the preparation, kept me more entertained than the speech itself.

There were little problems, such as the signs behind the Vice President in the gym. Gore’s people wanted the CSU Cougar signs on the wall to be removed before the speech. Diligent workers began removing the signs when it was noticed that the paint behind the signs were a strikingly different color than the rest of the wall. It was decided the signs would stay. I wonder if the painter was fired.

I learned a lot from his visit, but not about ICAPP. For instance, the seal displayed on the podium he stood behind is removable. It was originally a CSU emblem, then some man in a dark suit came by, snatched it off and replaced it with the Vice President’s very own, portable seal. I always thought he brought his own podium, I guess. Maybe I never really thought about it at all. It was just a small piece of embossed wood or plastic. The thought of this being carried around in the trunk of a car kind of takes the mystic out of the whole thing.

Also, while leafing through some photographs of the event, I noticed a couple of them caught the table just next to Gore’s feet. The table was hidden from the view of the audience by a cloth divider. On the table sat several containers of bottled water, some of them tipped over. Well, Vice Presidents and important political leaders get thirsty too. Just one more little secret.

I was also amused by the music played just before the speech. First there was the theme to Rocky, then the Vice President of the United States made his way to the podium while the song “ABC” by the Jackson Five played.

Although much of the behind the scenes sort of things I noticed were strictly comical, there were some incidents that were a little sobering.

I arrived early to cover the event and my stomach was a little queasy from the excitement. I headed downstairs to the cafeteria to get breakfast. When I stepped in the seating area many of the dark suited men were sitting there. They were accompanied by several very beautiful, man eating (or woman eating as the case may be) German Shepards. The dogs tilted their heads and eerily studied me as I crossed the floor. Their human companions did not seem any friendlier. I decided to eat upstairs.

On the whole the strangers wandering campus were friendly and only occasionally annoyed with the locals. Some students near the gym asked one Secret Service man if they could pet his dog. The Service man sneered, “I wouldn’t”. The students didn’t.

While there were some reports of the Secret Service men joking with students and administrators, they were all business when Gore showed up. Smiles faded to stern frowns and the spotlight was cast on only the Vice President.

I actually heard one of his people say “What’s your 1/20” into a mini microphone, which is something I thought only happened in movies.

The event was very high energy and it was exhausting covering it. But just seeing what went into it was enough to make it worth.
