Campus Candid: Barbara Hunt

by Sharon Z. Langford / Staff Writer
Dr. Barbara J. Hunt is an English professor at CSU’s Department of Language and Literature. She received her BA in English from Case Western Reserve University, her MA in Creative Writing from Indiana University and her Ph.D. in English from Peabody College of Vanderbilt University.

Other than teaching basic and advanced English and Literature courses, Dr. Hunt is also involved with several committees on campus. She is part of the Honors Program and the Advising Committee, which is producing a handbook for advisors. She is also in charge of the Semester Conversion Task Force.

Dr. Hunt has recently received the title of Assistant Dean of the New University College. She explained that CSU is one of two Georgia Universities that is undergoing an experimental program in which we will offer a two-year university within a four-year university starting next fall. The two-year university will be a type of junior college offered because there are none in our area. Dr. Hunt also explained that without this program, about 40% of the applicants would not be able to attend CSU due to poor high school transcripts or test scores. Even though she now has this administrative position, she will still be teaching full-time.

In her private time, Dr. Hunt enjoys “surfing the net”. She also likes to go hiking and bicycling with her 14-year-old son.

When asked about her hopes for CSU, Hunt replied,”I hope we can do more faculty and student exchanges within our country and abroad. With the amount summer study abroad programs growing each year at CSU, it seems her dream is already coming true.
