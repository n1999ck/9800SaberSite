Study abroad French style

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

 
Columbus State University (CSU) students have an opportunity to study abroad in France this summer.

Students participating in the program will spend four weeks attending classes with other students from around the world in Dijon, France.

The program will take place at the Center for French Studies, part of the University of Bourgogne in Dijon. Dijon is the capital of the Burgundy region, which is know for its medieval history.

2,000 students from about 60 countries participate in the program each year.

Students will live in university residence halls or single occupancy rooms, unless they request other housing arrangements. Two meals a day are included in the program price, but cooking facilities are available on each floor for students who prefer to cook their own meals.

The total cost for the program is $3,700, which includes CSU tuition, accommodations, two meals a day, round-trip airfare from Atlanta to Paris, and in-country travel to and from the host institution.

The cost does not include travel to Atlanta, passport and related expenses.

Typically, students should plan to have about $600 to $750 extra money. There may be entrance fees to various sites and students should plan to spend extra money on entertainment.

Students also have the opportunity to travel outside of Dijon at their own expense.

Full time or part time students are eligible to participate, but space is limited. Availability is on a first-come first-serve basis.

Aside from opportunities for students, the Dijon program offers a refresher course for teachers of French.

Those interested in the program should contact Jacqueline Konan, Associate Professor, Department of Language and Literature at 568-2054. Konan can also be reached by fax at 568-2123.
