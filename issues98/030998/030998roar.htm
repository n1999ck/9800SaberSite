What do You Say?

What are you supposed to say in certain situations? What do you call the “Native Americans” at the “Indian” Cultural Festival? What do you call African Americans who refer to themselves as something other than what they expect you to call them?

Why do we all bust our collective butts to be politically correct when it turns out to be a big joke? Here’s a good one: Black English, or ebonics. You know what I hear some blacks say about that? They say, “What are you talking about? Where I’m from, that means uneducated English’.” Personally, I don’t care if you’re the most politically pampered minority in the world, incorrect English is just that, incorrect. I use incorrect English, but I’m not gonna’ try to start a massive new movement to legitimize my linguistic laziness. How about this: Steve-phonics. So you want to speak differently? Go ahead. You’re even free to teach your kids to speak a different way, but English is still the accepted unifying language for the USA. No, not everyone likes that. But you know what? Change that if you like. Until you do, those who don’t know the basic rules of English will probably suffer when it comes to job interview time. Just a reality check, but ask employers of any race who worked hard in school if they are likely to be impressed by you if you sound uneducated.

Okay, who is in charge of inventing those so-called politically correct terms like Native American? The Native Americans like to call themselves Indians. Maybe not all of them, but just who exactly is actually offended by being called Indian? Oh yeah? Too bad. As long as you live on Indian Reservations and have Indian Cultural Festivals, I’m going to refer to you as Indians. If you come to me personally and say you are offended, then I might make the exception out of courtesy. But if you are a non-Indian and calling yourself offended on behalf of the Native Americans, I may tell you where to lodge your pretensions. That’s it for that pow-wow.

Here’s a good one, one of my favorites. Let’s get rid of the rebel flag because it was flown during some rough times, even though there is documentation showing that it originated for reasons other than racist. But let’s keep the good old U.S. of A stars and stripes which were flown during such high points as the near decimation of some great Indian Nation populations and the theft of their land. Can you think of some other bad things that Uncle Sam did? I can. Do you know how many times you would have to change flags because they got soiled? Show me the perfect nation or state that has an unblemished flag, and I’ll show you some damn fine propaganda.

Here’s one more. Teachers with a PhD who raise a fuss because you call them doctor instead of professor, even though they realize that you didn’t know better. “I worked hard to make this position and I expect to be addressed as professor. Ignorance is no excuse. When you get out there in the real world you may not get a second chance to get the proper honorific right” You know what I say? “What have you done for me lately?” I pay part of your salary and you get the better parking spot. So excuse me if I didn’t hear about your tenure (I must have missed that edition of Nightly News). And as for the real world, let’s not mention how many Professors need their hands held when it comes to technical skills like, “How do you turn this thing on?” Hello, you’re in the real world the moment you are brought in to it. Whether you are dependent upon your parents or the technical engineers, this is it. You are never really on your own.

Okay, if I didn’t answer the question “What do you say?” let me put it to you like this; use your good judgement. But then, you do that already anyway. Right? But try not to be so bogged down by so many political agendas which have nearly nothing to do with protecting the minorities which they claim to.
