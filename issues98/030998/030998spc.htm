Club build identity

by Shannon Real / Guest Writer

 
Have you ever considered joining a campus organization? Lisa Rios did and now she gets paid for her work on the Student Programming Council. She will also gain valuable experience to put on her resume when she graduates in 1999.

Lisa chose to join the SPC after hearing about it in a College 105 class. The Student Programming Council is made up of students who work hard to provide other students with more to do on campus than just attend classes.

The SPC is made up of four committees which specialize in bringing different types of social, cultural, educational and entertaining activities to the campus.

Lisa chairs the Public Relations Committee, which is responsible for promoting the organization and recruiting new members. She is majoring in Public Relations and has been able to put the techniques she learned in the classroom to work. After being with the SPC for only a month, Lisa was promoted to Chairperson.

Others in the SPC recognized her leadership abilities and suggested she interview for the position after the last person resigned. Lisa has found the experience to be rewarding and is assured her role in the organization is preparing her for a similar job after she graduates.

Although she is only required to work 5 hours a week, Lisa has been putting in a few more hours lately because the SPC is short on members. To correct this problem, Lisa has been working on a membership drive. The goal is to double the SPC’s membership and re-define the organization’s image on campus. In a recent survey of students, 67% said they had never heard of the SPC. However, 47% expressed an interest in learning more about the group.

Lisa believes doubling membership is an attainable goal if she could just get the word out about how the SPC benefits students. Lisa says, “Since joining the organization two quarters ago, I have had lots of opportunities to meet many different people, travel and have fun.”

Lisa also stresses the fact that the SPC does not ask a lot of its members. Each committee meets once a week for about 30 minutes and before each event. During the events, members are encouraged to participate and have fun. There are no dues to be paid because the SPC is supported through Student Activities Fees.

Recently, Lisa attended a convention in Indiana sponsored by the National Association for Campus Activities. There she was able to meet new musical acts and hire them to come to CSU.

In the past, the SPC has brought local acts like Cornbread, Beehive and the John Scott Evans Band to perform on campus. Past guest speakers have included Alex Hailey and Maya Angelou. The SPC also sponsors educational campaigns such as Alcohol Awareness Week and Black History Month.

Future events include a Monte Carlo Casino Night and more musical acts. Lisa and her committee will also be introducing a mascot to help build an identity for the SPC.
