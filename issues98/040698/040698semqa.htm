CSU semester conversion questions answered

by Evelyn Tolbert-Owens
Semester conversion is drawing near. In the fall of 1998, Columbus State University will begin the academic year on the semester system. The following list addresses some commonly asked questions about semester conversion and how it will effect students.

Q: What is the difference between a quarter and a semester system?
A: A semester system divides the school year into three 10-week periods, plus a 9 week summer session. The semester system divides the year into fall and spring semesters, each 15 weeks, plus a 7 week summer session.

Q: What are the advantages of a semester calendar for the student?
A: The main academic advantage is that the pace of a semester course is less hectic. The student will have more time to absorb course material and work on projects. About 65% of the colleges and universities in the United States use the semester system.

Q: Will the credit value of courses change on the semester system?
A: On the quarter system, most courses are five credit hours and meet five hours per week. On
the semester system most courses are three credit hours and meet three hours per week.

Q: What is a typical semester course load?
A: On the quarter system most students take three 5-credit-hour courses per quarter or 9
courses per year. On the semester system students usually take five 3-credit-hour courses per semester or about 10 courses per year.

Q: How many credits will be required for graduation?
A: Most majors will require 120 academic semester credits plus physical education; none will
require more than 128 semester credits plus physical education. To earn a bachelor’s degree on the quarter system, students must complete a minimum of 180 credits with at least a 2.0 grade point average. On semesters that requirement becomes a minimum of 120 academic credits with at least a 2.00 grade point average.

Q: Will it take longer to graduate?
A: Probably not. Current students will graduate as planned, provided they do not change majors.

Q: How will credit hours convert?
A: Quarter credits earned for a degree will be multiplied by two thirds to convert them to semester credit.

Q: How will the change to semester affect the students’ grade point average?
A: The student’s grade point average will not change because of the conversion to the semester system. Hours attempted and hours earned will be multiplied by two-thirds to convert them to semester credits.

Q: How many credits will be required for full-time status?
A: Full-time status will remain at 12 credit hours for undergraduate students. To graduate in four years a student needs to satisfactorily complete a minimum of 15 credit hours of degree
requirements each semester. Full-time status for graduate students will change to 9 credit hours.

Q: How will the number of credits determine classification on the semester system?
A: The new semester classifications require fewer credits:

  • Freshman: Fewer than 30 credits
  • Sophomore: 30-59 credits
  • Junior: 60-89 credits
  • Senior: 90 credits and above.

Q: How will the change to semesters affect tuition? Will it cost more to go to CSU?
A: The yearly tuition will not change as a result of the conversion to semesters. A student will be paying for two semesters instead of three quarters (two payments instead of three).

Q: How will the change to semesters affect financial aid?
A: Financial aid recipients should notice no change to the amount of financial aid awarded. Cost of attendance figures and yearly award amounts should remain the same, but will be divided into two semesters instead of three quarters.

Q: If a student begins on quarters and finishes on semesters, will they lose credits? How will their credits be converted?
A: Credits will convert on a two-thirds ratio. For example, if the student earned 45 credits on quarters, that will convert to 30 credits on semesters. The student will not lose any credits. Credits earned on the quarter system will be converted to semester credits. All advance-placement credits and transfer credits will be converted from quarters to the semester system on a two-thirds ratio.

Q: If a quarter system course is phased out or no longer required on the semester system, will the credits earned in that course still count toward graduation?
A: Yes. The student’s advisor will determine the semester functional equivalent of those courses that have been phased out.

Q: How can a student retake a course on the semester system after failing it on the quarter system?
A: If a student needs to repeat a course taken on the quarter system, an accepted semester substitute will be specified in the chart.

Q: If on the quarter system the student has started taking courses in their major program, do they have to change to the semester system degree requirements for their major?
A: That depends on when the student started and plans to finish:

>> If the student will complete graduation requirements after December 1997, he/she will be given the opportunity to satisfy Area II and Area III of the core as follows:

* Area II: Any two laboratory science courses in lieu of the laboratory science sequence
requirement. The student must still satisfy course prerequisites for other science courses.
* Area III: POS 105 and either HIS 105, 106, or 117, and any two social science courses approved for Area III.

>> If a student will complete graduation requirements Fall 1998 or after, he/she will have the
option of using semester courses to satisfy the quarter system degree requirements, or the option of switching to the new semester system requirements. Faculty advisors and Registrar personnel will assist the student in determining the best choice, given the requirements the student has left.

Q: If the student is in the middle of a two- or three-quarter sequence of courses, how will they complete it on the semester system?
A: Whenever possible, students should complete all sequences begun on the quarter system before the conversion to the semester system. For example, some three-course sequences on the quarter system will become two-course sequences on the semester system. There could be repetition or gaps of information if a student starts a sequence on the quarter system but finishes it on the semester system. If the student has started a sequence on the quarter system, he/she should try to finish it on the quarter system.

Q: What can the student do to plan for semester conversion?
A: First, if the student is close to graduation he/she should try to complete all requirements on the quarter system. Second, if the student cannot finish on quarters, complete all sequences on the quarter system.

Q: How can the student obtain more information about the semester conversion?
A: The student can ask their advisor or the chair of the department of their major. They can also visit the CSU website (www.colstate.edu), where new information will be posted regularly. Beginning this spring the student should see the schedule of all quarter system courses CSU plans to offer between now and Fall 1998.
