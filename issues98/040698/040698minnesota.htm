Visiting students endure tragedy

by Michael Cox / Guest Writer
We are all faced with challenges and obstacles everyday that could change our lives forever. This was recently discovered by a group of students from Gustavus Adolphus College in St. Peters, Minnesota.

After arriving to Columbus, Ga.to work on a Habitat for Humanity project, they were informed that their hometown and campus was damaged severely by a tornado.

Of the eight groups sent from Gustavus Adolphus College, this is the only group that stayed to finish their Habitat project.

The students were greatly appreciative of the response and attention they received after the devastation that both they and their hometown encountered.

The group of students spent their Spring Break helping with a Habitat for Humanity project. Seven other groups also helped with the project.

Bert Powell, Site Supervisor says, “The Collegiate Challenge gives groups a good program for spring break. As an alternative to a fling at the beach, I admire their sense of ambition for giving up the beach and they should be commended for sticking with it after the damage that has been done. It is good that our community has pitched in to help their morale by giving them entertainment as well as other stuff.”

One Minnesota student says, “We are not sure what we have left to go home to. Some of us found out that they have nothing left, some found out that everything is fine, and some know nothing.”

Columbus State University showed their Southern hospitality by opening up the use of their phones, internet services and televisions for the students. Other contributors in the Columbus community aided the Minnesota students as well.

Outdoor World, K-Mart, Williams Hair Salon, Toms Coke/Pepsi, Bookstore, Hollywood Connection, Health Clinic, Dental Hygiene, Student Government Association and other anonymous contributors helped the students by donating clothing, entertainment and meals.

Jared Hennen, a Gustavus Adolphus College student, says, “I can’t believe how much Columbus State University has done for us. We never expected anything like this.”

The students from Gustavus Adolphus college are very appreciative of the services that were offered to them. As a result of this incident, Columbus State University is in the process of building a program to welcome all habitat students in the future.
