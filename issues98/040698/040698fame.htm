Eight to be inducted into CSU Hall of Fame

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor
Columbus State University will induct eight new members into it's Athletic Hall of Fame this Saturday on campus.

The inductees are men who excelled in athletics for the then Columbus College or helped boost the athletic department to the status of today.

Entering the Hall this year are Columbus High School baseball coach and Columbus College alumni Bobby Howard, 30-year veteran of the Ledger Enquirer, Cecil Darby, radio broadcaster Jimmy Griggs, Joe (Butch) Bell Jr., Columbus College pitcher who teamed with Howard in the early 1970's, Don Andrae, a member of the Columbus College basketball team of the late 1960's who currently holds the school record for rebounds per game. Andrae is still active within the university. Also being inducted are golfer Sammy Rachels, golf coach Nelson Ross and William (Butch) Tillman.

Rachels won the school's first national championship in golf in 1971, Ross led every golf team he coached to the national tournament, and Tillman set the CSU basketball scoring record with 1,648 points in a career that stretched from 1966-1970.

The induction ceremony is scheduled for 3 p.m. Saturday in the Davidson Center Auditorium. The ceremony is free and open to the public.
