Letter from the editor

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief
The time is nearing for me to say good bye to the Saber. Although I hate to see it go, I know it is time to move on and pass the torch to the next unsuspecting victim.

The Saber has offered long hours and frustrating moments, but it has offered me many more good times than bad.

There is a certain satisfaction in this job. Developing an idea, putting on paper and watching it grow, is something everyone should experience at least once in life. At the Saber, I’ve had a chance to learn from my mistakes and keep going.

The Saber has afforded me the opportunity to meet many people on campus. The members of the Saber staff are lifelong friends. The memories I’ve gathered in this office are the ones I will carry with me and look back on as the good old days of my college career.

I covered a radical preacher’s sermon on campus, the relocation of several friendly gopher tortoises, the Georgia College Press Association awards in Macon, the Peachbelt Leadership Conference in Augusta, and Vice President Al Gore’s visit to campus over the last year.

Since the campus is growing, I’m sure the next editor will enjoy and endure even more exciting times.

The Saber is the epitome of a group of people coming together to create something useful and fun. Ever week is a new challenge, it is starting all over again. But every week is also a new accomplishment filled with congratulations and kudos for a job well done.

I know the next editor will share my enthusiasm for this paper and the school it represents.
