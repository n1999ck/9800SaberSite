Athletes: are they worth their pay?
Johnny Luse / Sports Editor

Why in the world do team owners pay ridiculous amounts of money to have a guy be on his team? Why do we pay athletes 25 million dollars for one season? Why are there such things as a league minimiums, salary caps, and etc. ? Why not just pay the guy an hourly wage and save yourself, as an owner, a hell of a lot of money?

Who in their right mind can honestly tell me that a Kevin Brown is worth 120 million dollars to pitch, a Michael Jordan worth 25 million for one season, a Jaromir Jagr worth 13 million a season? I do not think that in your wildest dreams you can justify these prices that athletes are requesting and getting from team owners who think, “… Hey if I sign the BIG free agent Mr. Superstar, then I will get a lot of crowd response…” 

What a joke.

Here is my point, why are we paying these people millions to just play a game? They are playing games that you and I used to play as kids, but did we get paid for it? Hell no, we just got dirty and tired from it. The money that these athletes are getting is ridiculous. Make them work for $5.15 and hour and see how many of them play for the game, instead of the money.

My favorite phrase of a big-shot athlete is, “I didn’t trade myself for the money, I just think that we have a better chance of winning here,” or “I would play this game even if I didn’t get paid.” 

Whatever, I do not think that half of these jokers would play their games if they were making the $5.15 per hour that a lot of us make.
