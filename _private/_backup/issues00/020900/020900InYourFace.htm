Looking for Love?

Well, Valentine’s Day is just around the corner. Have you bought your sweetheart anything yet? If you are like me, then you are probably waiting until the last minute.
Don’t look here for any ideas either. I have completely run out, but I know exactly what I would like to receive.

Valentine’s Day . . . a day for lovers. What a beautiful idea, right? Some people would disagree.

I personally am not a big fan of V-Day. Don’t get me wrong I like romance and all that mushy stuff, but something about having a mandatory day of love makes me uncomfortable.

I mean, wouldn’t it be nice to get a present on a random day of the year? Sure, guys do that sort of present giving for about the first six months you are dating, but after a couple of years the presents are few and far between.
And I don’t mean to sound like a greedy person. I just think it would be nice to get a card once in a while . . . am I right ladies? 

Being the independent woman that I am, I have been known to give my boyfriend random gifts. They may be small but they make him feel good.
So, what happens if you don’t get your sweetheart something for V-Day? You, my dear, should just head for the hills and hide. 

You see, one of my problems with V-Day is that people compare the gifts they receive. If you have a friend that gets a dozen roses, then you may casually mention to your significant other, “You know Susy got some roses from Jim today . . . it must be nice to get roses.”

Nine times out of ten this will result in a purchase of overly priced roses, and the motivation behind this purchase was mostly fear.

Boys, you better believe me when I say you will be in the dog house if you don’t deliver. I have listened to men moan about this day of love for several years.

Too bad! Just do it! That’s what most women will tell their man. 
And what about those friends of yours who don’t have a man? All they do is complain about how no one will be giving them a present this year.
How can you possibly enjoy this holiday with people around you making you feel guilty about being in a relationship? 

If you are single this V-Day then try and think about the things you do have in your life. Be thankful that you have true friends that love you, or that your parents are in good health. Try not to bring everyone down with you.
What is the true meaning of V-Day? It is not just the celebration of romantic love or lust. It should be a time when we all take some extra time to let the people around us know that they are appreciated and loved.

Isn’t that we all want anyway . . . to feel loved and adored? Many people search their whole lives and never find the right person.

It is so hard to find that one person who totally completes you. That one individual who shows you the tenderness and love that some can only read about.

We all know that real life is not like TV, and people usually don’t have this all encompassing passionate love lives that are so often portrayed in movies.

However, every once in a while we can see some real life magic. My grandparents were one of those couples that had this magic. They were married for fifty years.

It wasn’t that they were all over each other (not something a grandchild wants to see). It was simply the way they looked at each other. It was the way they sent a silent message to observers that they didn’t want to be without each other’s company.

It was the way my grandfather looked at my grandmother on the day of their fiftieth anniversay, three months before she died.

I want that kind of tenderness and feeling of completion, and I am sure that I am not alone.

That’s what Valentine’s day should be about, the celebration of never ending love and appreciation. Here’s hoping we all get some of that this year.
