University to celebrate Black History Month with variety of events
Jessica Trenchik / Features Editor

Columbus State University will be celebrating Black History Month all throughout the month of February with a variety of events sponsored many different organizations here on campus.

To kick off the celebration, the Student Programming Council sponsored a lecture given by Astronaut Guy Bluford, the first African-American astronaut. The talk was held in the Fine Arts Hall on February 3rd, at 12:30 p.m..

CSU’s Schwob Department of Music also participated in the celebration by sponsoring the Black Composers Convocation. The Convocation was held at 1:00 p.m. on February 4 in the Davidson Center Auditorium. 

A presentation entitled “African American Genealogy: An Introduction,” will be held in the Simon Schwob Memorial library. Callie McGinnis will be giving the presentations, which will be held on February 8 at 12:30 p.m. and again on February 17 at 4:00 p.m.
On February 10 at 12:30 p.m., CSU students can enjoy an African American Art Show. Gregory Raymond, the guest artist, will be displaying various pieces of his work, including oil paintings, wood carvings, and pencil drawings. 

The show, which is being co-sponsored by the Office of Minority Affairs and the State and Local Committees of The Life and History of Black Georgians, will be held in room 254 of the Davidson Center. Admission to the show is free.

Also being co-sponsored by the Office of Minority Affairs and the State and Local Committees of The Life and History of Black Georgians is a presentation entitled “Black Georgians in the Twenty-first Century: 2000 and Beyond.” 

The presentation is on February 12, from 10:00 a.m. to 12:00 noon in the Columbus Room in the Davidson Center. This presentation is also free.

At 12:30 p.m. on February 17 in the Davidson Center, students can participate in a contest called “Who Wants to be a Winner? A Black History Trivia Game.” The Office of Residential Life is sponsoring the contest.

The Seventeenth Annual Black Applause Banquet will be held on February 25 in the Columbus Convention and Trade Center. 
The guest speaker will be State Representative Maretta Taylor. The banquet begins at 6:30 p.m. and tickets cost $20.00. For more information, contact Mrs. Nancy Marino at 568-1653.

Wrapping up the celebration will be a candle light vigil and rededication ceremony at the Clock Tower on February 29 at 6:30 p.m. In case of inclement weather, the event will be held in the Columbus Room in the Davidson Center. 

Sponsored by the Office of Minority Affairs, the vigil will be a tribute to African Americans of the past.

In addition to the numerous events taking place around campus, there will also be a Black History Month essay contest. The subject for the essay is “What role can higher education play in enhancing an individual’s acceptance and tolerance of cultural differences?” Entry forms can be picked up in the Office of Minority Affairs. The contest is open to all students. A prize of one hundred dollars will go to the winner of the contest. February 18 is the deadline for entries.

Sharon Jamison of the Office of Minority Affairs encourages students of all cultural and ethnic diversities to attend the events, as they are intended to be a learning experience for everyone on campus.
