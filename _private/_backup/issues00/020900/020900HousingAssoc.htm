Columbus State’s housing association has new name
Tonya Euell / Staff Writer

If you read last week’s paper, you will have noticed that RHA is looking for a few good people. 

They are looking for building representatives for each building in Courtyard I and Courtyard II. Who or what is RHA you might ask? RHA, Residence Housing Association, formally known as the Columbus State University Housing Association, decided to review and restructure their constitution after attending a Housing Association conference recently at the University of Central Florida. 
Sheila Kennedy, the president of RHA stated that, “We attend conferences regionally and recently went to University of Central Florida. 

And all (the) other schools have their housing association named as Residence Housing Association, so we thought to be consistent when we go to other conferences, that we should also change. 

Basically it is (RHA) the same thing (as CSUHA), it’s just shorter.” 
Many changes have occurred in the constitution. 

Kennedy says that the previous constitution had the “basics, so we decided to make it more thorough, so we can really make official changes.” 

By changing the name, the Residence Hall Association is hoping that more people will know who they really are. 

RHA was created specifically to help those who resided in the dorms at CSU. So, it’s a bit of a privileged club for the students of Courtyard I and Courtyard II. 

One of the many things that RHA does for the residents in the dorms is to hold activities at both Courtyards.

Specifically, they throw a welcome back jam each semester and try to have activities on major holidays. 

Kennedy says, “Normally we try to plan events on major holidays like Halloween. 

“Our main goal is just to provide social entertainment because a lot of people complain there’s nothing to do, especially on weekends. And there are a lot of freshmen that live in the dorms and we want to get them more active. . ..

“We are thinking about planning a Valentine event, because that’s another major holiday. “Basically we base events on the feedback we get from residents, so if they say we want this, we see what we can do.” 

Because Columbus State University’s dorms have more of an apartment style atmosphere, Kennedy feels that most residents are somewhat isolated due to the extreme privacy that the apartment style provides, which is unlike traditional dorms.

So, when recreating the Residence Hall Association’s constitution, the members decided to provide a way for the residents of Courtyard I and II to get involved in our community. 

As you all know, volunteerism is not only a good way to build a great resume but is also fun while making you feel warm and fuzzy inside. 
Kennedy says that RHA is planning a few philanthropies for residents to get involved in sometime before the end of this semester. 

Along with philanthropies, the Residence Hall Association is hoping to do a couple of fundraisers. Though money is provided to RHA to sponsor events and put out flyers and packets, money does “get low when spring semester rolls around,” Kennedy says.

Kennedy also stated, “RHA wants to put on as many activities for the residents as they possibly can”. 

Kennedy is hoping that Building Representatives are soon established so that the changes in the constitution can be implemented.

If you would like to become more involved and active in RHA you must meet one requirement. 

You must reside in the dorms. To be a housing representative or an officer that person must be currently residing the dorms, and they must put in an application in the Residence Life office. 

The meetings are held bi-weekly and dates of meeting are usually posted at the dorms or emailed to residents. 

All residents are welcome to attend the meetings, but only building representatives have votes that count for each building. 

Please look for the RHA webpage being currently worked on and should be on web browsers soon. 

If you would like more information on how you can become involved in RHA, please contact the Residence Life Office in either Courtyard I or II, or contact Sheila Kennedy via email.
