Around the Corner Café, pharmacy-turned-diner with history
Chad Wayne / News Editor

A new diner just opened up in Phenix City. It’s the Around the Corner Café located on 16th street in Phenix City. 

The unique thing about this diner is that it used to be a location for the gangsters of the 1950’s to work out of. Part owner, Rick Biancardi showed me places in the café’s basement where there used to be doors and passages. 

An architect also told Rick that the building’s blueprints indicate that there ought to be rooms where it is now walled off. Tony has also been told that there used to be tunnels that ran from the building to the Columbus side of the river. 

The building itself used to be a pharmacy. There were even reams of old prescriptions in the basement that were left over from the 1950’s. There were also old Coke bottles and in their crates.

The part of the building that the café is in used to be a soda counter. Customers used to submit their prescription and then get a shake or soda while they waited for the prescription to be filled. 

Rick and Joni have kept the old bar where, at one time, a soda jerk would take orders of sodas and other drinks.

Now instead of a counter with sodas and refreshments being served by a soda jerk, there is a full kitchen. 

Here Rick makes breakfast and lunch from 7:00 am till 2:00 p.m. 
For breakfast, Rick makes French toast, breakfast sandwiches, pancakes, waffles, bacon, hash browns from freshly chopped potatoes and eggs. 

And the eggs, believe it or not, are the best I ever had. Did you know you can put other seasonings on eggs than just salt and pepper? 
Well Rick does, but he won’t tell me his secret. He just laughs when I ask him and remarks, “I’m glad that you like them.” 
Rick also stated, “If you want bland food, this is not the place to come.”

For lunch, Around the Corner Café serves a variety of subs, including Italian meatball subs, ham and cheese, grilled chicken subs, steak and cheese, turkey, Italian hoagie and Binky burger subs. 
They also serve salads, soup of the day, sandwiches and homemade chile. 

The Meatball sub is a must try at Around the Corner Café. And you know, with a name like “Biancardi” the Italian sub is going to be good.
Around the Corner Café also serves old-fashioned milk shakes in a dozen different flavors. They also serve homemade deserts like cake, cookies, cheesecake, brownies and muffins. 

Another item served is called a “Blast,” which is frozen cappuccino. Speaking of cappuccino, the Rick also serves hot cappuccino, espresso and coffee and feature a coffee “flavor of the day.”
Joni is the owner and manager of the Around the Corner Café, and Rick is the owner of the new Italian restaurant that will open right next door in the first week of June. 

The diner is open from 7:00 am till 2:00 p.m. and the restaurant, Red Tomatoes will be open from 4:00 p.m. until 10:00 p.m. It will be open until 11:00 on weekends. 

Red Tomatoes is located in the portion of the building that used to be the pharmacy. And the raised area were the pharmacist used to work and distribute the prescriptions has been converted into a designated smoking area called “The Upper Deck.” 

Around the Corner Café offers some discounts and specials. They give ten percent off for senior citizens and military personnel. 

Joni and Rick are not finished working on the diner and restaurant yet. They also plan on building outside benches and a deck with tables. 
They hope that Around The Corner Café will be a place where people can get a refreshment and relax and read a book from their “Give a Book, Take a Book” shelf. 

To get to Around the Corner Café, just take 14th street across the 14th street bridge to Phenix City. At the traffic light at the courthouse, take a right. Immediately after the first traffic light, Around the Corner Café is on your right.
