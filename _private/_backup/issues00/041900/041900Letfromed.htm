New Saber editor-in-chief to be elected Thursday night
A Letter from the editor
Clarissa Andrews / Co-Editor

Tonight, the Saber staff is getting together for our end of the year bash. It sounds great on the surface (a free meal at Malones), but in actuality, it’s going to be a sad evening. 

The Saber is losing a lot of it’s staff members, some to graduation, some for other reasons. So tonight is going to be a night of fond farewells, and maybe a few that aren’t so fond . . ..

Brian Hale, our entertainment editor for the last few years, is graduating. Stephanie Adams, the other co-editor, is graduating and moving to Texas. I’m going to miss her a lot--I’ve gotten so used to calling her when everything’s going nuts that I can’t figure out how I’m going to survive without her around.

I’ve also decided not to work for the paper next year, just so I can get a job that will hopefully have something to do with my future career. 

Next week, the editor who will take over the Saber in the summer will write this column, so I decided to give out a little bit of advice that Stephanie and I have learned from our short period running the paper.

We’ve discovered that if half of the staff doesn’t do what they’ve promised to do, we’re expected to do it. And we don’t get the money for doing it, no matter how much extra time it takes.
It’s impossible to please this entire campus. People get angry if we can’t cover an event. People get angry if we can’t fit their ads in. People get angry if we criticize, and people get angry if we don’t. 
If we have computer problems that stop us from being able to publish the paper on time, people don’t understand that it isn’t always our fault.

One thing our future editor will find out soon enough is that there aren’t always enough article ideas to go around. That’s why it’s wonderful to have people like our News Editor, Chad Wayne around--he can always be counted on to have at least a couple of ideas a week.

Another thing that everyone should know--you don’t get paid enough to make this job worth it. There are weeks that the paycheck averages out to around one dollar an hour. And complaining about that does no good.

But the thing you have to remember is that there aren’t many people on the Saber staff who are in it for the money. Sure, it’s nice to have a little bit of money tossed your way every two weeks, but that’s not what’s important. 

You’ve got to love this job with all your heart, or you’ll be miserable. It is really hard sometimes to remember why we’re here till the wee hours of the night--but no matter how late you’re here, no matter how stressed you are, you’re here because it’s what you wanted to do. Because I know Stephanie and I do love our jobs, however frustrating they can be.

You’ve got to suck it up and deal with it when you think you’ve put out a damn good paper, but all you hear are complaints. You’ve got to get over the fact that lots of people will tell you to your face what they think of the Saber, but maybe one percent of those people will actually take the time to write it down and send it in.
(By the way, thank you Lisa Grant, for always telling us what you think. We would rather get one of your letters that criticizes everything we do than get ten verbal compliments. And please don’t think I’m being sarcastic, because I really mean that.)
So, to our new editor, you’ve got a big burden to carry. For the most part, you’re going to have a brand new staff to work with, and I want to wish you all the luck in the world. Because, frankly, you’re going to need it.

Just remember that in spite of all the hard work, the long hours, and the slave wages, it’s a hell of a lot of fun. And if you need anything, feel free to call me (maybe that means Ben will finally stop getting frantic phone calls now, right?)

And now I get to end the last editorial/column that I will ever write for this paper, and it’s a little sad for me. But it’s also happy, because next year, someone else is going to be sitting in this chair, learning just how much fun working at the Saber can be. 
Plus, every once in a while, we get a free mean at Malones . . . : )
