XI THETA SORORITY

 

 

Spend the summer in beautiful St. Petersburg
Miguel A. Hernandez, Jr. / Staff Writer

Have you ever daydreamed yourself into another place or time to escape from the day to day routine that at times can become monotonous? 

I invite you to take a moment and daydream yourself taking a trip to a far away place. In this place, you are meeting a new kind of people, leaning a new kind of kind of culture, and seeing new kinds of things never seen before. In your daydream you are having the experience of a lifetime. Does this sound appealing to you? 

The CSU study abroad program can make your daydream reality! From June 27 – July 26, 2000 Dr. Alice Pate and several other CSU cougars will spend four weeks studying at St. Petersburg Technical University in Russia. 

You can be one of these cougar delegates to share in this adventure, while experiencing your class subjects coming to life all around you. 

This program will allow you to earn up to seven semester hours toward your undergraduate degree. Student can schedule “two three-hour courses and one one-hour course.” In addition, there are Fridays and weekend trips outside the St. Petersburg area. 
All of this sounds good so far, but I’m sure some students will say, “I don’t speak Russian, nor do I know anything about the country.” More good news! The ability to speak Russian I not a pre-requisite for this trip. 

There will be a “one-hour survival language and culture course” offered which will help students with basic phrases such as, where is the bathroom, and much more. Be assured others traveling with you will be able to lend a helping hand if needed. During an interview with Dr. Pate, she said, “Russian people like to practice their English and will be excited when they hear your accent.” 
Where will we live? Students will be assigned accommodations in modern dorms located on the campus of St. Petersburg Technical Institute. 

Will I like the food? Everyone is encouraged to try traditional meals, but Dr. Pate advised me there are fast food restaurants such as, “KFC, McDonald’s, and Pizza Hut” nearby.

How much will this adventure cost? The price students pay will vary based on the scholarships and financial aid currently being received. Please make an appointment with the Director of Financial Aid, Mr. Al Pinckney, to find out what assistance you are eligible for. You may be very surprised!

If you still have questions please take a few minutes and visit the International Education Center office located on the 1st floor of Howard Hall, or call (706) 568-2452. Dr. Alice Pate is also willing to answer questions via e-mail, pate_alice.facstaff.csu, or call for an appointment (706) 565-3632. 

You can make your daydreams become reality with the right guidance!
