XI THETA SORORITY

 

 

Within a Room
Leslie Maxwell / Columnist

A couple of weeks ago, I wrote a column about grammar. Well, think of this column as the second installment. But this week, instead of writing about the rules and oddities of English grammar, I would like to explore some of the weird things that we do with language. 

Since the way we speak and the way we write are usually quite different, interesting problems pop up in the papers we write for class. 
Some of these written errors are obviously the effects of spoken speech. 

For instance, one writer rendered “self esteem” onto paper as “self-of-steam” while another one wrote that “Money is the Ruth of all evil.” 

The Regents’ writing test is a gold mine of strange sentences because its time limit and often less-than-wonderful topics. As students try to whip out an impressive essay in an hour, sentences like these get scattered along the way:

— “Amidst all the spurious flapdoodle and bombastic and bruhaha of using a gas car.” (nice word choice!)

—“Extending someone’s life expectancy can really make someone’s day.” (brilliant observation!)

— “All men should be created equal and too many foreign diplomats have gotten off free.” (huh?)

— “In this paper I will give you some good reason for the more strict laws to some reasons why there bad.” (what?)

— “Americans need national parks because it gives them a place to pleasure animals.” (isn’t that illegal?!)

Well, English 1101 students and Regents’ test takers may be to blame for some of the linguistic insanity in the world, but they aren’t the only culprits. Public figures such as politicians and actors often either butcher the English language or make statements that are, well, less than profound. 
Check out these silly quotes I picked up from Quoteland.com: 

— “It’s no exaggeration to say that the undecideds could go one way or another.” —George Bush, US President

— “Not only is he ambidextrous, but he can throw with either hand.” —Duffy Daugherty, football coach and sports analyst

— “Hawaii is a unique state. It is a small state. It is a state that is by itself. It is a —it is different from the other 49 states. Well, all states are different, but it’s got a particularly unique situation.” —Dan Quayle, US VP

— “Be sure and put some of those neutrons on it.” —Mike Smith, Baseball pitcher, ordering a salad at a restaurant

— “I’ve read about foreign policy and studied — I know the number of continents.” —George Wallace, 1968 presidential campaign

— “We don’t necessarily discriminate. We simply exclude certain types of people.” —Colonel Gerald Wellman, ROTC Instructor

— “If we don’t succeed, we run the risk of failure.” —Dan Quayle

— “It isn’t pollution that’s harming the environment. It’s the impurities in our air and water that are doing it.” —Dan Quayle

— “The people in the Navy look on motherhood as being compatible with being a woman.” — Rear Admiral James R. Hogg

— “What a waste it is to lose one’s mind. Or not to have a mind is being very wasteful. How true that is.” —Dan Quayle

And finally, a few nonsensical utterances from George W. Bush, current presidential candidate:

— “What I am against is quotas. I am against hard quotas, quotas they basically delineate based upon whatever. However they delineate, quotas, I think vulcanize society. So I don’t know how that fits into what everybody else is saying, their relative positions, but that’s my position.” 

— “If the terriers and bariffs are torn down, this economy will grow!”
