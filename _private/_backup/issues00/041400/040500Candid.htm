Campus Candid: Ben Mallard
Jessica Trenchik / Features Editor

The always friendly and cheerful Ben Mallard is this week’s Campus Candid. Ben is in his sophomore year at CSU and is majoring in music education.

Ben is a percussionist in the CSU Wind Ensemble. He also performs with the La Grange Symphony, the Bob Bar community band, and the orchestra at Edgewood Baptist.

Ben has been a percussionist since he was in the seventh grade. He says he first got interested after attending a pep rally in the sixth grade where he “became amazed with the sounds of the drums.”
Ben comes to Columbus from a small town just south of Augusta called Sardis. According to Ben, Sardis is so small that it only has one red light.

“The music department attracted me,” Ben says when asked why he came to CSU. He also likes the small campus.

Ben participates in a number of activities here on campus. Since the fall of his freshman year, Ben has been a member of Phi Mu Alpha Sinfonia. 

“It’s a professional men’s music fraternity, sort-of like an honor fraternity,” Ben says.

CMENC, Collegiate Music Educator’s National Conference, is another organization to which Ben belongs. According to Ben, the group has monthly guest speakers who talk to the group about the fields of music and education. The group also attends the GMENA conference in Savannah each year.

Ben is also a TKE candidate for membership this semester. Ben says he decided to rush TKE because he “wanted to get more into the social aspect [of college].” “The guys in TKE, they’re a bunch of great guys,” Ben says. “It’s just fun. They’re a bunch of diverse people.”
As for hobbies and interests, Ben says, “I really enjoy musical theatre.” Les Miserables and Jekyl and Hyde are his two favorite musicals.

“I like to travel,” he also says. “I want to take a trip around the world one day.”

Ben also enjoys outdoor activities such as hiking and canoeing, as well as going to the movies and just hanging out with friends. Also on the weekends, Ben attends Community Christ church here in Columbus.

When asked about his future plans, Ben says, “After I graduate here with a BA in music education, I want to get a master’s in three different areas.” Those three areas are music history, composition, and conducting. 

Ben would like to begin his career as a high school band director and then work toward becoming a college professor. Ben says the five adjectives he would use to describe himself are dependable, compassionate, understanding, dedicated, and loyal.

Ben’s favorite quote is “Today is for dreaming…tomorrow’s for making dreams come true.”
