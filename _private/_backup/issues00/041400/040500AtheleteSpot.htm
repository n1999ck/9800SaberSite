Athlete Spotlight: Sylvia Allen
Johnny Luse / Sports Editor

# 34 * 5’11 * P * Jr 
Ellenwood, Georgia

1998-99: As a sophomore, Sylvia was second on the team in scoring and she led the team in rebounding. She averaged 15.5 points per game and 8.9 rebounds in PBAC contests. Her 17 blocked shots was a team high and she was tied for second on the team in steals. She started every game for the Lady Cougars as a sophomore. She notched 13 double-doubles including a career high of 34 points at Augusta State and she also grabbed a career best 18 boards at UNC Pembroke last season for CSU. She ranked fifth in the PBAC in rebounding and 14th in scoring. 

1997-98: She saw action in 29 of the team’s 31 games last season, starting three times. In just under 15 minutes per game, she averaged 5.3 points and 5.8 rebounds per game. She had a strong game at Georgia College & State University scoring 13 points and pulling down 15 rebounds. She also had a big game in the opening round of the NCAA South Atlantic Regional, scoring eight points, getting 10 rebounds, and getting five steals. 

High School: Sylvia is a 1997 graduate of Dunwoody High School where she lettered four years in basketball, two in track, and one in volleyball. In her senior season she poured in 20.2 points and pulled down 12 rebounds per game for coach Angela Nash. She was named to the All-Dekalb County first team and was an Honorable Mention All-State performer for her last two seasons as a player.

Personal: Sylvia is the daughter of Albert and Beverly Allen and has two sisters. She enjoys listening to music and watching the “Young and the Restless” in her free time. She is majoring in Criminal Justice at CSU. 

Career Bests:
Points: 34 vs. Augusta State, 1/9/99 
Rebounds: 18 vs. UNC Pembroke, 2/13/99
Steals: 7 vs. USC Spartanburg, 1/27/99 
Blocks: 3 vs. Delta State, 1/4/99 
Assists: 4 vs. USC Spartanburg, 1/27/99
