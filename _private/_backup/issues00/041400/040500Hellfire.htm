‘Hellfire’ ministers back at Columbus State
Stephanie Adams and Clarissa Andrews / Co-Editors

On Tues., March 23, Brother John Duncan once again visited the campus of Columbus State University.

Brother John is an Evangelist who travels to colleges all over the Southeast ministering to students, and he has been a regular visitor to our campus for several years. He will travel to at least 25 colleges and universities this year.

You may be wondering what exactly Brother John is preaching about on our campus. This past Tuesday, several students had the opportunity to see him in action. Duncan and fellow evangelist Joseph, who had no last name, stood in front of the Davidson center between 1:00 and 2:00.

For those of you who did not get a chance to hear their message, we will go over the high points. Duncan bases his theology on the King James Bible. From this source he uses the literal interpretation.

When asked what he hoped to achieve on Tuesday, Duncan stated, “Our main goal is to obey God . . . we come here today to worn this campus . . . that if people here don’t repent, then they will go to hell. We are here to debunk atheism and evolutionism . . . you will go to hell if you don’t repent.”

What are the students of CSU supposed to be repenting about? According to Brother John and Joseph, you must turn away from all forms of sin.

In order to be free from sin, one must first define exactly what sin is. According to Brother John you are sinful for a number of reasons that go beyond the basic Ten Commandments. 

Reasons for being sent to hell include, but are not limited to, fornicating, being a homosexual, a lesbian, masturbating, viewing pornography, smoking, drinking, having tattoos, and listening to 2-Pac.

Duncan acknowledges that the presentation of his message can turn people away from him. 

He stated, “Our goal here is to preach the truth. I know that people will harden to our message . . . they will harden because they do not want to repent. Most people are on a broad wide road to destruction and hate God . . . they are fighting God with sin. We expect unrepented sinners to be angry.”

Brother John said he knows that their style of ministry tends to bring out the worst in people. However, when asked if he planned on changing the way he ministered he stated, “The truth makes them hard or soft to God . . . I will not stop telling the truth. Most people love sin and hate truth . . . God loves you enough to tell the truth. You must turn away from your masturbation, lesbianism, and alcoholism in order to be forgiven.”

He continued by saying, “I appeal to the flesh--not the mind or conscience.”

Due to the nature of his message, Brother John is usually met with debate from the student body. Many students become extremely upset by his message and engage in open debate. 

During his ranting sermon which includes the infamous yelling of the word “Hellfire!” Brother John becomes what some students have said to be a “red faced religious fanatic,” who states that Donny and Marie Osmond are among the many sinners going to hell.

However, when speaking to Duncan on a one-on-one basis, he appears to be levelheaded and eloquent. 

One must wonder if the dramatic presentation is more for show than actual ministry. 

Regardless, Brother John and Joseph are dedicated evangelists who feel that their calling in life is to help the students in our region turn away from sin and repent. Religion has always been a controversial issue. Some students were asked about how they felt about the way Brother John delivered his message.

Most of the students gathered around listening to Duncan were amused rather than repentant. 

Chris Campos, a senior Communications major, said, “I think it’s kind of dumb--a good comedy scene.”

“He is a misguided child of God with delusional perceptions who is misinterpreting the Bible,” stated Christian Mettler, a senior Psychology major. Duncan, however, disagrees with this statement--he says that while others interpret the meaning of the Bible to mean what they want, he and Joseph do not do this. 
One anonymous student who heard this comment commented, “If he doesn’t interpret the Bible in twisted ways, I’d be interested in finding out where in the Bible it says that people who listen to 2Pac will go to hell. And why just 2Pac?”

Sophomore Jamie Williamson, a Computer Science major, said, “I wish he’d start reading the whole Bible instead of sections.”
Other students thought that Duncan was doing a good thing. Business Management junior Isha Hicks stated, “I applaud him for speaking about what he believes, and trying to persuade others to do the same.” She did, however, feel that some of his statements were “a little extreme.”

Kinesha Harden, a junior in Criminal Justice, said, “I just hope he’s real.” One of Harden’s biggest problems with Duncan was that she doesn’t think “he can tell someone whether [he or she] is going to hell.”

Rianne Cho stated, “He is ignorant . . . he is turning people away from God. He is not giving people a chance to speak.” 

Another female student, who asked to be called Amber, said, “I’m a Christian, and he is more likely to turn me away. He is saying God is judgmental and He is not. I don’t think he should be allowed to come out here.”

Does Brother John Duncan have a right to come onto our campus and preach his message, especially since it causes such uproar? 

Officer J. Boling of the CSU campus police stated, “Any expression of first amendment or any other freedom is all right with me. It doesn’t matter if I agree or not . . . he has a right to do it. I fought in three wars to protect his right to freedom of speech . . . so let him speak.”
Some students agreed with Officer Boling as far as freedom of speech. However, an overwhelming number of students disagreed. Many students felt that the display was inappropriate. 

One student, who wishes to remain anonymous, stated, “I feel that they have a right to say what they want. It may not be appropriate, and I certainly don’t agree with what they are saying, but I don’t have to stand out here and listen. If you don’t like their message then don’t add fuel to their fire.  

"It is true that the entire situation is fueled by angry comments and heated debate. Students skip class just to stay and try and make their point. One thing is for certain; we have not seen the last of Brother John Duncan and his fellow evangelist Joseph, who did not minister on Tuesday. 

Duncan assured us that he would be returning to our campus in a few months. Upon returning, he will continue to preach his beliefs and hopes to turn this campus away from sin. 

Unless you’ve “repented of your unholiness” by the time Brother John and Joseph are back on campus, remember these words of Duncan, “You’re one heartbeat away from hell, sinner!”

As a whole, most students were laughing at the tirades of Duncan. Nikos Papantoniou, a senior majoring in Finance, expressed this by saying, “I think he lost his mind.”

Robert DeVries, an SGA senator majoring in Computer Science, said, “I think if he wanted us to listen, he’d talk to us with respect instead of just yelling at us. By yelling . . . most people just think he’s a joke [but] he could have a really good message.
