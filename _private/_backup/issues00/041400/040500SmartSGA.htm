Getting smart about SGA
Heather Paulk / Staff Writer

I am going to be painfully honest here--a week ago today I knew nothing about the Student Government Association here at CSU. I basically assumed it was a group of want-to-be politicians who liked to hear the sound of their own voices more than anything. And now I admit that I couldn't have been farther from the truth! With the SGA elections approaching, I was assigned to write a report on what the SGA is all about and find out why we should vote. And quite frankly I was very impressed.

For those of you as politically clueless as I was, the SGA is meant to be the student voice here on campus. It is made up of basic offices and a senate. The current officer holders are Eddie Gordon (President), Vice President of Scholastic Affairs (Angela Williams), Vice President of Finance (Eric Stolpa), Chief Justice (Matthew Roberts), Speaker of the Senate (Heather Mendenhll), and Speaker of the Representatives (Tony Euell). Within the Senate are fifteen elected members.

I was amazed when I found out that most of these positions are paying jobs! According to Eddie Gordon, the President makes $1,100, VP of Scholastics gets $850, Chief Justice gets $450, and the two Speakers get $150. All of these payments are per semester.
So, what are these guys paid for anyway, right! The President is to oversee all comities, attend events, and serve as a contact for students. The VP of Scholastic Affairs is in charge of the forums (which anyone on campus may attend), assists with the advisor and educator of the year selection, and organizes the Halloween Carnival held on campus each year. 

The job of organizing the financial aspect of the SGA falls to the VP of Finance, who keeps records on all expenditures and how much money the association has. The Chief Justice deals with parking appeals and heads the Grievance Committee. The Speaker of the Senate keeps the senators together and relays messages to the Executive Counsel, while the Speaker of the Representative acts as a go-between for organizations and the Executive Counsel.

Besides holding nifty titles, what have these people accomplished lately? Eddie Gordon says quite a bit! Just recently, Gordon said, the SGA has made it possible for students graduating in the fall semester to not have to wait until spring. Before, if you finished all your classes in the fall, you would have to wait until the end of the spring semester to walk down the aisle. 

Thanks to the SGA, people don't have to wait anymore! They have just recently made a change we will all appreciate next year as well. Seeing that technology fees are going up next school year, the SGA pushed to have the computer labs opened 24 hours a day for our benefit. 

This brings up the other issue at hand. April 15 and 16 are the elections for SGA office out in front of Davidson. Last year 625 students voted and most of the current officers won by a landslide. 
To run, a person must have filled out an application in the SGA office by noon April 6; to run for office you must have a GPA of 2.5 or higher and have attended CSU for at least 30 hours. To run for Senator, you must have a 2.0 GPA or more with a minimum of 12 hours spent here. 
This year SGA urges even more student participation in these important elections. VP of Scholastic Affairs, Angela Williams, feels students should vote for those who are concerned with the well being of the students. 

"They need to vote for people who will be their voices," Williams stated.

Eddie Gordon agrees, adding, "I don't think students realize how much power SGA has . . . in order to have a good government you need to have people who get along and work well with a diverse group of people." 

When speaking with the current President of the SGA, he stated that the biggest challenge of working in the Student Government Association was dealing with officers who are elected without realizing the amount of time required in their jobs. 

"The Constitution requires us to put in five hours a week, but I am probably here at least nineteen hours every week," said Gordon. 
Angela Williams, whose name will be on the ballot for the upcoming election, felt the hardest part is having to deal with so much criticism as well as "making sure the students are taken care of in all aspects.” 
Each of the current officers have different ideas about what the most rewarding parts of their jobs are. Gordon loves the community recognition and meeting so many different people. Williams loves knowing she is making a difference on a daily basis. 

I think way to many of us, myself included, take the SGA for granted along with all their hard work. The least we can do is to take some responsibility and get to know the candidates running for office next year and vote on April 17 and 18. So, if you see an SGA officer on campus, give him or her a hearty “Thank you!” and I'll see you at the voting booth next week!
