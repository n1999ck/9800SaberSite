NEWS IN MUSIC

Pearl Jam is about to offer us their first release since 1998’s Yield and the Live On Two Legs concert album. The new album is called Binaural.

The cover for Binaural, which appears to depict a blue-green “eye” surrounded by several translucent circles, is actually an image of the Hourglass Planetary Nebula, located nearly 8,000 light years away from Earth. The scenery was digitally photographed by NASA’s Hubble Space Telescope in January 1996. The North American tour kicks off on August 3 and they roll into Atlanta on August 7. Yes, I will be there. 

Binaural is set to hit stores on May 23 and the setlist for the album is as follows: “Break Her Fall,” “Insignificance,” “Evacuation,” “Letter To The Dead,” “Rival,” “Grievance,” “Light Years,” “Of The Girl,” “Thin Air,” “Nothing As It Seems,” “Fatal,” “Sleight Of Hand,” “Soon Forget,” “In The Moonlight,” “Parting Ways,” and “Education.” 

In other new album news, Gavin Rossdale is busy working on new material while touring with Bush in support of their latest album, The Science of Things. He told MTV News, “I hope to be doing it by late summer. I think it’s important to keep momentum.” Gavin is also planning a solo LP, and said he’s working on “light sketches” of new songs, though he would not say what direction he thinks Bush’s next album will take. 

“The sound should be something elusive,” he said. “For us it’s about constantly evolving, trying to reflect things about your life.” 

Still, in other new album news …The Dave Matthews Band is playing hermit in a Virginia recording studio trying to complete the follow-up to its 1998 album, Before These Crowded Streets 

Boyd Tinsley, the bands violinist, told MTV News this week that the band is about halfway finished with the album, but they have yet to name it. 

“Right now we’re recording our fourth album in Charlottesville, Virginia,” Tinsley said. “We pretty much just rented a house and put a studio in it, and we’re just having a lot of fun. We’ve been at it since the middle of January and we’re going to finish up probably in the middle of June, and then go out on tour for the duration of the summer.” 

The tour he mentioned starts off on June 19 in Columbus, Ohio. He said that the band hopes to have the new album hit stores by late summer or early fall.
