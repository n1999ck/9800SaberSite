SGA elections only days away
Chad Wayne / News Editor

As you all know, SGA elections are coming up. On Monday and Tuesday, April 17 and 18, CSU students will vote for candidates running for various SGA positions. 

Students can vote on election days from 7:30am to 1:30pm in a tent in front of the Davidson center. From 5:30pm till 9:00 p.m., students may vote in the Davidson Lobby. 

Student ID’s are required in order to vote for SGA candidates. Thursday, April 7, was the last day that applications could be accepted, so no more candidates can run for this election.

Currently there are three candidates running for president of SGA. They are Eddie Gordon, Alan Akin and Jeff Barron. All three were at the clock tower on Wednesday at 5:00 p.m. for an informal get-together with the students. 

Each candidate made an informal presentation, describing their background and what they hope to accomplish in the future as president of SGA. After their individual presentation, students and candidates had refreshments and mingled – discussing the upcoming elections and CSU in general.

Eddie Gordon is the incumbent candidate for president of SGA. Before being elected as president, Gordon was speaker of the senate for one semester and vice president of finance for one semester. Gordon says that he is closely related to the Greeks ,being a member of AFA. He is also a member of the psychology club. 

Gordon said that he succeeded in getting the computer lab open 24 hours a day beginning this coming fall, and he has met with the University administration more than previous presidents. He has also brought back the fall graduation ceremony after it was not held from 1997 to 1999 due to a low turnout rate. He has also tried to inform students of all upcoming events.

Alan Akin is in his junior year majoring in pre-engineering. This is Akin’s second year as an SGA senator. Akin says that he will be here for another year if he gets the position as President of SGA, however, Akin plans to transfer to Auburn University if he is not elected as president of SGA. 

He says that he has been working in a machine shop for the last four years and has experience working with adults. Akin wants to bring a good work ethic to SGA and says that he is a hard worker. Akin has also been a tutor in the math lab, an orientation leader and a delegate to visiting Japanese students. He is also an active member of the BSU.

Jeff Barron is mathematics major in his junior year. Barron hopes to bring more organization to SGA and to improve the reputation of the organization. He would also like to incorporate ideas that would make SGA function more efficiently. Barron’s experience includes involvement in a number of organizations. He has been involved in the 4-H performing arts. He was president of the State Youth Assembly. Barrons has also been a summer camp counselor for the last four years and will be the manager of Camp Wahsega this coming summer working with approximately 200 children per week. 
Barron is also a member of the Max Club in the Math Department, as well as a math tutor.

Ironically enough, most other SGA candidates are running unopposed. Only one position other than President is being run for by more than one person. 

Robert DeVries and Alisha McLester are running for Vice President of finance. Everyone has seen both of their fliers on the walls. 

McLester’s color posters catch student’s attention as they walk through the halls of CSU buildings. McLester is currently on the Live Entertainment Committee for SPC. 

And of course it is almost impossible to miss DeVries’ posters with his photo and catchy sayings. DeVries is currently a senator for SGA.
Before voting, it may be a good idea to read the section of the student handbook on SGA. It is a brief section and will not take long to scan through and get some good questions from it. You may want to ask the candidates questions about their specific duties in order to gain a more full understanding of who understands their job. Think of some CSU campus issues to ask the candidates about and find out what they plan to do to make CSU a better University for you.
