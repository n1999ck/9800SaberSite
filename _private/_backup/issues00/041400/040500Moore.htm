Moore leaving CSU staff
Johnny Luse / Sports Editor

Columbus, GA—Columbus State University assistant basketball coach Robert Moore is leaving the Cougar staff to become the new head coach at Abraham Baldwin Agricultural College in Tifton, GA. Moore, a member of the CSU staff since the 1994-95 season, will officially take over at ABAC on August 1.

Moore, a native of Macon, has been an integral part of the success the Cougars have enjoyed over the last five seasons and will be taking over a program that is established at ABAC. He replaces Donnie Veal who retired after the 1999-2000 season. Veal had been at ABAC for 23 years.

The primary recruiter for CSU, Moore has been responsible for numerous players coming to CSU, including All-America player Larry Coates, and current players Travis Crutcher, Mike Collier, Maulvi Lewis, and Shawn Strong. ABAC will be the first-ever head coaching job for Moore, who has been an assistant at Virginia Intermont College in addition to Columbus State.

“It feels like a member of our family is leaving us,” said CSU head coach Herbert Greene. “Robert has been such a big part of our success here and has done an outstanding job. I am very happy and proud for

him, and I think he will do a wonderful job down there. I will miss him on the bench, but I will also miss him because we have become such good friends. He is as good as they come.”

Moore has been a part of one regular-season Peach Belt Athletic Conference championship and three PBAC tournament championships. He has helped the Cougars to a pair of NCAA Sweet Sixteen appearances -in 1996 and just this past season. He also helped lead the team to one other NCAA appearance in 1998.

“I have been fortunate to learn under one of the best coaches anywhere,” said Moore. “I have learned to do everything - recruit, win, and even lose - with class. I plan on using what I have learned at CSU in this new job and continue to build on the success coach Veal had there.”
