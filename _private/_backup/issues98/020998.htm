 Man wanted for having gun at dorms

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

Last Tuesday at approximately 3:15 a.m., CSU Public Safety and the Columbus Police Department responded to a call at the dormitories.

According to Jasper Cooke, Chief of Public Safety, Kveta Dalton, a resident at the dormitory, claimed a man broke into her dormitory and waved a gun in the air. The man, Anthony Dix, was allegedly accompanied by two unidentified men. None of the men are thought to be CSU students.

According to Cooke, Dalton knew the men and initially invited them into the dormitory. The men then left for a short time before allegedly returning with the gun.

Dalton and her cousin, Liz Thomas, who was also there, were unharmed. According to Cooke, Dalton’s three roommates slept through the incident. Neither Dalton nor Thomas could be reached for comment.

There is a warrant out for the arrest of Dix. Cooke says he hopes questioning Dix will provide some answers about the case.

Resident assistant, Ikeysha Fagan, commented that the incident only lasted about 10 minutes. Fagan called Public Safety, who responded to the call in a few minutes despite the fact that the only officer available was answering another call at the Coca Cola Space Science Center downtown.

Steve Schmidt, Housing Manager, says the men did damage the door frame of the dormitory attempting to get into the apartment.

Resident assistants who lived in an apartment nearby heard the noise and ran to Dalton’s apartment. However, according to Cook and Schmidt, no one claims to have seen the gun except Dalton.

Schmidt says Dalton’s story about what transpired that morning was not reliable. “Only one person claims to have seen the gun and she is not believable,” Schmidt says.

Despite the questions surrounding the incident, all but one resident in the dormitory was relocated to other apartments for safety reasons. According to Larry Kees, Associate Dean of Students, Director of Housing and Student Affairs, Dalton was removed from the dormitories because of an unrelated matter.

The dormitories have rules regarding visitors. According to Smith, because the men were first allowed into the dormitory by the students, all of the roommates were put on probation for breaking the visitor rules, which disallow visitors after midnight.
