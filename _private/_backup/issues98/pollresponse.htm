March 2, 1998

What are your feelings regarding the
campus smoking policy?

“If my smoking ever bothers anyone, I graciously accommodate. But there is no sense in people being outright rude to me. We need designated smoking areas out of the elements then everyone would be happy. The airport has special smoking rooms and everyone is happy.” - Lisa G.

"I can not stand the smell of cigarette smoke. I cringe every time I walk through the courtyard between Howard and Arnold Halls. I feel that if people chose to smoke they should respect the rights of people who chose not to smoke. They should not smoke near entrances. It may be cold and raining outside but, if they feel that they need a cigarette that bad, they have to deal with the cold and rain. The people who do not smoke should not be made to suffer because of someone else's nicotine addiction. Everyone knows smoking is bad for them, so if you chose to ruin your health do not try and ruin mine."

February 23, 1998

What are your thoughts regarding children being charged with sexual harassment?
(see February 23 Letter from the Editor)

“I feel this is a big mistake on behalf of the legal system because they need to remember a very important cause-affect outcome here. They after all are children and as everyone knows children are easily influenced because they are trying to figure out who they are as well as who the friend or foe is. Any child with this type of charge against them will endure extreme teasing and cruelty from other children and may eventually believe they are bad. They may even develop a personality complex that something must be wrong with them. The outcome will be that if the child believes he/she is a bad person they will do bad things. I believe the legal system will deal with them again at an older age all because of such a ridiculous charge. People really need to take a reality check here.” - Chassity R. Musick
