Sports Editorial

Hero lost, but never forgotten

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
The world of sports recently lost one of it’s greatest icons and I lost a hero. No, this person couldn’t slam dunk a basketball or hit towering home-runs. He couldn’t dart through a vicious defensive line like a burst of electricity. What this man could do though was get the attention of millions nearly every single day during a baseball season. This man was Harry Caray.

Caray was the type of individual who was known by people who never even watched a Chicago Cubs baseball game. Nearly every living soul who has ever heard of the game of baseball in the United States knew who Caray was. For those unfortunate few who didn’t know his name, they too even knew of the raspy voiced announcer with the thick black glasses who could grab the attention of millions with his rendition of “Take Me Out To The Ballgame” at every Cubs home game.

For me, Harry Caray was more than just an announcer. He was baseball. For as long as I can remember, I’ve enjoyed watching the Chicago Cubs play, not for the great players like Ryan Sandberg or Mark Grace, but to hear the passion and excitement of Caray’s broadcasting skills. He could take a simple grounder or pop fly and add just enough pizazz to it to make it exciting. His trademark exclamation of Holy Cow is what most will remember him for. One of his other greatest exclamations is one that I would always imitate while playing baseball. When the wood of the bat cracked with power and the ball went soaring into the air, Caray would proclaim, “It could be, It might be, IT IS! as the ball disappeared over the wall.

As a hopeful sports broadcaster, I always viewed Caray’s ability to call a game as an inspiration. His ability to bring excitement to a game was something to be treasured.

Yes, Harry Caray was a baseball icon and he will be truly missed. Hopefully, ol’ Harry is up there in baseball heaven watching over his beloved Cubs, sipping on his favorite beverage, beer, and exclaiming,”HOLY COW” for every remarkable play made by anyone wearing the red and blue of the Chicago Cubs.
