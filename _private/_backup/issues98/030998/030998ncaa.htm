Men and women head to NCAA

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
The Columbus State Lady Cougars Softball team recently swept a double header from West Georgia in a game held at CSU.

In the first game, Columbus gave up three quick runs in the first inning, but that was all West Georgia could manage. The Lady Cougars scored four runs in the fourth inning to take a 6-3 advantage and added a seventh run in the fifth inning. CSU was led by Britnie Wysong who went 2-4 at the plate and had 2 RBI’s, and Nathalie Fradette who went 2-3. Carrie Newton recorded her fourth win of the early season to run her record to 4-0.

The second game saw last year’s Freshman of the Year dominate West Georgia from the pitching circle. After coming off of her 2-3 performance at the plate in the opening game, Fradette continued to be impressive as she pitched a two hitter and gave up only one run in a 7-1 Lady Cougar route.

After the two teams went scoreless through the first two innings, CSU exploded for four runs in the third, one in the fifth, and two more runs in the sixth to cushion their lead. CSU was led at the plate by Merri Anne McCall who went 2-4 with 3 RBI’s and Kelly Murdock who went 2-3 with an RBI. With her victory, Fradette ran her record to 3-1 on the season.

Columbus raised their record to 3-1 for the season and prepare to travel to Valdosta, Georgia for the Valdosta State Univ. Tournament to be held March 6-8. The Lady Cougars next home game will be on March 13 when SIU-Edwardsville pays a visit to CSU.
