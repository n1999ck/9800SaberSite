Zepplin alumni back again

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

 
Led Zeppelin fans will be happy to know that the new album by Robert Pant and Jimmy Page is scheduled for release on April 21. “Walking Into Clarksdale” is the first new studio material the duo has recorded since Led Zeppelin’s 1979 “In Through The Out Door.”

Page and Plant recently wrapped up recording at Abbey Road Studios in London. The track listing for “Walking Into Clarksdale” is as follows: “Shining in the Light”, “When the World Was Young”, “Upon A Golden Horse”, “Blue Train”, “Please Read the Letter”, “Most High”, “Heart in Your Mind”, “Walking Into Clarksdale”, “Burning Up”, “When I was a Child”, “House of Love”, and “Sons of Freedom”.

The first single from the album is “Most High”. The standout track is “Please Read the Letter”, which boasts heavy guitar from Page and pleading vocals from Plant.

For those who think new material by the Led Zeppelin duo is just too much to handle right now, “Elmopalooza” is coming soon! Rock meets Sesame Street on this special compilation that is due to hit stores in April.

The album pairs Jimmy Buffett with Kermit the Frog, Shawn Colvin with Ernie, Gloria Estefan with Rosita and the Oinker Sisters, The Mighty Mighty Bosstones with The Count, Rosie O’Donnell and Elmo, and Celine Dion with Herry Monster. Other guests on the album include Steven Tyler of Aerosmith, Kenny Loggins, En Vogue, Fugees, and Big Bird.

Fans of the band Soul Asylum can track the progress of the making of their new album,”Candy From A Stranger” through the bands website. The address is www.soulasylum.com. The website showcases many pictures of the band at work and the finished product will available in late April. “Candy From A Stranger” will also include CD-Rom compatible footage of the band.

Presidents of the United States of America will release their final album on March 10.

The now defunct band is releasing “Pure Frosting”, a compilation of rarities and b-sides, plus three new songs. March 10 also brings new albums from Eric Clapton, Ben Harper, Natalie Imbruglia, and Spacehog. The Natalie Imbruglia album, “Left of the Middle”, is excellent and sure to be a huge success.

Scott Weiland, frontman for the band Stone Temple Pilots, debuts with his first solo album on March17. The album, “12 Bar Blues”, features special guests Peter DiStefano of Porno For Pyros and Sheryl Crow on the accordion. This effort by Weiland is better than anything that Stone Temple Pilots has done as of yet.

If you are interested in a new band, I think I’ve found a winner. The band is called Closer, and the album is called “Don’t Walk”. Closer is Harley DiNardo, Derrek Hawkins, Dave Cartategui, and Jonathan Nanberg. The band is currently on Revolution Records and are potential superstars. “Don’t Walk” is full of potential hit singles and this album will not disappoint. Closer is comparable to the sounds of Oasis and The Verve Pipe, but with their own spin and better lyrics.
