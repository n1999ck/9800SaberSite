Ladies sweep West Georgia

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
With their impressive runs in the Peach Belt tournament still fresh in their heads, the men and women’s basketball teams received word on where they would begin their NCAA tournament play earlier last week.

The men, who received an automatic NCAA birth with their PBAC tournament title, will enter the tournament as a number three seeded team and will travel to Spartanburg, SC to face PBAC rival USC-Aiken.

If Columbus (26-6) defeats the sixth seeded Pacers, they will go on to play the second seeded Catawba (24-5). If the Cougars are able to advance through the South Atlantic Regionals, they would move on the the Elite Eight in Louisville, KY.

Head Coach Jay Sparks and his Lady Cougars, coming off of a disappointing loss in the PBAC final to the Francis Marion Patriots, also received a third seed and and must travel to Florence, SC, home of Francis Marion, where they will first face the sixth-seeded team from Carson-Newman. Bowie State, at 26-1, took the number one seed and Peach Belt rival Francis Marion received the number two seed.
