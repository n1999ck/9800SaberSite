CSU students in production of Cinderella

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief
Columbus State University student, Brea Vaughan will be playing the wicked stepmother in the Academy of Fine Arts’ presentation of “Cinderella” on May 17 at 2:30 p.m. at the Three Arts Theatre.

Vaughan is a marketing student at CSU and has been a teacher at the Academy of Fine Arts’ for the past 6 years. She has been taking dance since she was four years old.

Vaughan says, “I was Snow White a couple of years ago. This time I’m playing the stepmother. It’s fun to be someone I’m not.”

The production is an adaptation by Patty Taylor, the director of the academy. Taylor will be directing the production, which will include a large cast of local children and adults from the community.

This adaptation of the Cinderella story is a combination of the Disney Cinderella story and the ballet version of the story.

The performance features a special portion devoted to popular modern jazz. There will also be a tribute to television and 50 teens and adults will dance to rock music in the production. CSU’s Brantley Arrington will also fill the role of the Prince.

The costumes, made by Linda Hall, are elaborate and fun, according to Vaughan.

Tickets are $7.00 and are available at the Academy now by calling 561-8085 or will be available at the box office before each performance. The play is for all ages.
