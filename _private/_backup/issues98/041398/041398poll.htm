Poll website for students

New York, NY - February 19

Network Event Theater and American Passage Media announced the official launch of an exciting Interactive Polling Network—Pulsefinder.com. This unique site offers college students the opportunity to vote via online polls on current events and specific issues that affect them. Pulsefinder.com is a dynamic site where questions are constantly changing and the polling results are instantaneously posted.

A recent survey conducted through Pulsefinder.com shows that 64 percent of polled students agree with the NBA one-year ban of Latrell Sprewell, Only 19 percent of students feel that Bill Clinton should resign the presidency. Students also state that they think Kramer should be the Seinfeld character to get his own show and that Panama City Beach is the hot place to go for Spring Break. These engaging questions are just a sample of what can be found on Pulsefinder.com.

College newspapers across the country will be sent weekly updates of new Pulsefinder.com results. Newspapers can utilize Pulsefinder.com in a variety of ways, such as quoting the national poll results in articles for students’ reactions to current events. Newspapers can also establish their own customized polling questions through Pulsefinder.com. Colleges can find out what their students think of a national or campus specific issue instantly.

Network Event Theater delivers entertainment and educational programming to its nationwide, satellitelinked, interactive network of college theaters serving more than 750,000 students. American Passage., a subsidiary of Network Event Theater, has been providing national advertising to college newspapers for 17 years.
