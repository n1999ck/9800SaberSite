Letter to the Editor
This letter is in response to the letter from “A Concerned Student” printed in your ROAR! Column April 6. This student voiced three complaints, which I would like to address.

The first complaint regards the quality of the lunches versus the dinners. Their complaint is that the lunches are better than the dinners because “there are less people of importance ... who eat dinner than lunch.” I agree that the lunches are better than the dinners, but it has nothing to do with the “importance” of the people who eat lunch versus dinner. The dinner menu, is comparable to those menus of other college foodservice programs, while the lunch menu at CSU far exceeds the quality (and cost) of many other college foodservice programs. This increase in quality is in effect to attract some of the students, Staff & Faculty who would otherwise go elsewhere for their lunch. Besides this, we already have updated the cycle menu for this spring quarter, and have made improvements to the dinner menu.

The second complaint was that there are more service lines for lunch than there are for dinner. Again this is correct. The fact is that we serve about 350 people each lunch and less than 200 each dinner. The service lines’ issue is more an issue of supply & demand. When we only have the Grill & Cafe Features line open, due to someone being out, we still offer the full Deli Corner menu at the grill. We have also increased the amount of pizzas that we are prepping so that we have more pizzas for the dinner meal.

The third complaint is a valid one. Before opening this quarter, my staff & I had a 6 hour employee meeting, with over 2 hours pertaining to Safety & Sanitation policies. We discussed the importance of wearing gloves, and I stressed that if anyone is handling food, they MUST have gloves on, or they will be written up. We have recently put in a surveillance system to be able to be more aware of these situations. I agree with this student that this is not acceptable behavior.

I appreciate the concerns showed by “A Concerned Student” as I am always trying to improve customer service. My door is always open, and I appreciate the candor. Any suggestions, comments, or complaints are welcome. This invitation goes out to all customers, meal plan or not.

Dan Silver
Food Service Director ARAMARK — Columbus State University
