Hang in there!
A Letter from the Editor
by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief

HOME

Class availability has become a major problem with many CSU students. However, many of the students are those who are approaching graduation and need certain classes to graduate.

What students need to realize is that the semester conversion is a new transition for the faculty as well. What some students do not realize is the fact that some faculty are trying to help students in the best way possible.

I am one of those students who is experiencing the same problem. But, the department I am associated with is helping students in the best way possible. The faculty and staff of the Communication Department are helping students in many ways due to the controversial semester conversion.

A more structured approach is being taken to allow for more time with students. This is just one of the many departments taking the initiative to help more students with their registration headaches.

Instead of getting stressed out over what classes to take, ask your advisor to take the time to help you manage your class schedule. The process is grueling, but adjustments are being made and help is being offered for students with class availability problems.

With cooperation between the faculty and student body at CSU, the problem of class availability will hopefully be a small problem in the future.
