H.O.P.E. questions answered
by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief

HOME

Many questions seem to arise when the words ”financial aid” are mentioned. One area that happens to create so much controversy is the H.O.P.E. scholarship and the eligibility constraints on this form of federal aid.

Questions that students may ask include, “What grade point average must I have?” “How long will it last?” or “How am I penalized if I drop or fail a class?” These are just a few of the questions raised by students. Director of Financial Aid, Mr. Al Pinckney, was able to provide some answers to these questions.

To be eligible for the H.O.P.E. scholarship as an undergraduate, you must fit into one of the following three categories. You must receive H.O.P.E. as a high school graduate, have a cumulative 3.0 GPA at 30 attempted semester hours, or have a cumulative 3.0 GPA at 60 attempted semester hours. A very important point to remember is that you must be a Georgia resident in order to receive H.O.P.E.

However, some students who receive an out-of-state fee waiver may qualify and should contact the financial aid office for specific requirements.

Mr. Pinckney pointed out that sometimes students don’t realize that all credit hours that they have attempted since completing high school will count in determining their eligibility for H.O.P.E. That means courses that were passed, failed, incomplete, or withdrawn from after the first day will be counted toward the maximum time limit for receiving the scholarship.

Mr. Pinckney suggests that students check with their academic advisor and the financial aid office before making a withdrawal decision.

There are check points to determine your continued eligibility. If a student has a 3.0 GPA at the 30th, 60th and 90th attempted semester hours, they will be able to receive the scholarship up to a maximum of 127 semester hours. Eligibility will be terminated if a 3.0 GPA is not obtained at the end of 30 semester hours for recipients out of high school.

However, a student does have a second chance to regain the scholarship at the end of 60 semester hours only.

The H.O.P.E. scholarship is a great asset to any student because it covers any actual tuition charges and mandatory fees charged to all students that are not covered by the Federal Pell Grant or Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant. Students enrolled in six or more hours will receive a $150 book allowance and those enrolled in five credit hours or less receive a $75 book allowance. There is no minimum number of hours per term of enrollment required for eligibility.

“We are receiving a machine that will allow students to fill out their FAFSA forms electronically for quicker submission and we hope to have this machine up and running next Fall,” Pinckney stated.

The office has undergone some changes and they now offer a “counselor of the day” to help students with any questions they may have.

Pinckney added, ”We experienced some rough waters in the fall, but we are looking for smoother sailing in the spring.”
