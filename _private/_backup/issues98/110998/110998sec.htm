SEC Football: a thrill for all
by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor

HOME

What is it like to attend an SEC football game? Well, let’s just say that there is nothing better.

People flow from all around into a small town that increases its population on Saturdays from 80 to 100 thousand depending on the size of the stadium. Local police and state troopers direct traffic and head off any violators. Fans have decorated cars to show their team support and fight songs can be heard resonating throughout the stands and the streets. The die hard fans have shown up a few days before game-day and lay claim to their parking space.

This is all going on and the game has not even started.

Not only is the atmosphere lively, but the stadiums are just as entertaining. There are T.V. sets in the concourses for those buying food or going to the bathroom. Likewise, the scoreboard shows instant replays and team highlights on a huge Sony screen. Also, for the hard of hearing , the speakers are loud enough to ring one’s ears.

Now for the ultimate highlight; the game itself. The teams begin To warm up about an hour to an hour and a half before game time, which is great if you are looking for a favorite player or want to pick out a coach. The band can also be heard streaming into the stadium as fans slowly file in.

The game starts and the fun continues. The coaches get into it with the referees, fans voice their opinion and the rest is history.
