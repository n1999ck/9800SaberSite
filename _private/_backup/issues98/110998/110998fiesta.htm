Fiesta promotes ethnic awareness
by Angela Nicholson / Guest Writer

HOME

On October 3, members of the International Network sponsored a Hispanic Fiesta in the Davidson Cafeteria that consisted of a guest speaker, dinner, and entertainment.

Members of the group, who often sponsor various ethnic events, hosted the event at no cost to those who attended. The event’s purpose was to celebrate Hispanic culture.

Members of the International Network and the Hispanic community performed dances and wore authentic outfits that represented 13 Hispanic countries. Some of the countries represented were Brazil, Colombia, and Mexico.

The Network invited Dr. Raymond Gonzalez, professor of political science at CSU, to speak about Hispanics in the United States. He spoke about the political influence of Hispanics in the country.

Gonzalez stated that while Hispanics make up about 10 percent of the country, many do not vote. Gonzalez also claimed that although many do not vote, many Hispanics involved in politics do well when they run for state and local offices. Gonzalez expressed the importance of voting among all Americans and encouraged everyone to vote.

The food provided an opportunity to experience different tastes from around the world. Although the event was dedicated to Hispanic culture, a buffet was provided by members of the Network that included food from other countries, such as China and Japan.

In her closing remarks, Merlina Salamanca, Executive Director of the Network and a native of the Philippines, expressed the importance of becoming a member of the Network. She said that the group provides an opportunity for members to gain new friends and experience different cultural activities.

The Network is a non-profit organization that began in 1995. The group sponsors other events such as the Asian Pacific Celebration, the International Picnic for the Japanese Exchange Students, and monthly cultural presentations.

The main goals of the group are to enhance cultural awareness, support improvements in education, and to promote fellowship through social activities for the members, their families, and friends.

The International Network is hosting a similar event, Polynesian Night, on Saturday, November 14.
