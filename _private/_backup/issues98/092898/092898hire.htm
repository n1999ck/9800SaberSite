"Georgia HIRE" on the way

The Office of Public Relations
The University System Committee on Placement and Cooperative Education (USC-PACE), chaired by CSU’s own Lesa Kerlin, director of the Career Center, hopes to have “Georgia HIRE” (Hiring Initiative for Recruitment Excellence) fully operational by January 1999.

"Georgia HIRE" is a web-based program that will provide employers with access to all of the intellectual capital of the University System of Georgia at one site,” Kerlin explains. In partnership with JobTrak Corporation, the site gives employers access to resumes of Georgia students and recent alumni, as well as providing them with a place to post their job openings. Browsing the resume base is free. For a reasonable fee, employers can post their job openings, which are then available to all units within the University System participating in the program. Students will be able to access “Georgia HIRE” without charge.

Both Kerlin and Annie Hunt Burriss of the Board of Regents, with whom USC-PACE is working closely, perceive “Georgia HIRE” as a marketing tool that chambers of commerce will use in recruiting employers to relocate to Georgia. No other state has such a program, partly due to Georgia’s unique, single university-system framework, according to Kerlin.

“Georgia HIRE” will be beneficial to smaller and rural schools in the system because it will, in effect, level the playing field. By piggybacking onto the name recognition of the University of Georgia and Georgia Tech, their students’ resumes will be available to the same pool of potential employers, since all resumes will be in the same database.

Although the committee’s work is “a huge undertaking,” Kerlin says it has been “very helpful in building cohesiveness within the University System of Georgia.” Proof of this is the fact that 29 of the 34 colleges and universities in the system have signed on to participate in “Georgia HIRE” the first year.

“Sometimes we forget that part of what we do is help students to a career,” Kerlin says. When that function is successfully accomplished, everyone benefits by producing “happy, contributing employees who in turn become the best possible salespeople for their school.”

Public service announcements are set to begin soon across the state. Look for more information about “Georgia HIRE” on campus soon.
