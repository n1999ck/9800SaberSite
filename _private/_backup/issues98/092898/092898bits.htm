Music Bits: A U2 "Best Of"

by Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor
>> As I mentioned two weeks ago, U2 is set to release a new greatest hits package, “U2 The Best of 1980-1990,” as part of a three-album, $50 million deal with Polygram Records. Well, more information has been released about the package in the last several days.

U2 plans to issue the compilation of b-side tracks as a giveaway disc to be sold along with the “Best Of” album. Here is the catch - you can only get the b-side album during its first week of release. The limited edition, double-disc set, called, “U2 The Best of 1980-1990”, is due out on November 3.

The track listing for the B-sides album is as follows: “The Three Sunrises” from “The Unforgettable Fire”, “Spanish Eyes” from “I Still Haven’t Found What I’m Looking For,” “Sweetest Thing” from “Where The Streets Have No Name,” “Love Comes Tumbling” from “The Unforgettable Fire,” “Bass Trap” from “The Unforgettable Fire,” “Dancing Barefoot” from “When Love Comes To Town,” “Everlasting Love” from “All I Want Is You,” “Unchained Melody” from “All I Want Is You,” “Walk to the Water” from “With or Without You,” “Luminous Times (Hold on to Love)” from “With or Without You,” “Hallelujah Here She Comes” from “Desire,” “Silver and Gold” from “Where the Streets Have No Name,” “Endless Deep” from “Two Hearts Beat as One,” “A Room at the Heartbreak Hotel” from “Angel of Harlem,” “Trash, Trampoline and the Party Girl” from “A Celebration.”

>> With Rob Zombie just now feeling some success from his new solo project, “Hellbilly Deluxe,” White Zombie has officially thrown in the towel.

The band called it quits on Wednesday of last week, announcing that they would not record or perform together again. According to band members, it was a peaceful split and just something that had to happen. According to bassist Sean Yseult, the band had “run its course.” White Zombie survived thirteen years together as a band.

The band had been planning to start recording another album in January of 1999. Yseult says that there are no unreleased White Zombie songs waiting to be released and that fans won’t be seeing the group reform for any special events.

Rob Zombie, the band’s frontman, is just about to launch his own tour to support “Hellbilly Deluxe.” Accompanying Zombie on the road will be White Zombie drummer, John Tempestra, who played drums on the album. Yseult is going to be giving most of her attention to her surf band, Famous Monsters, whose debut album, “In The Night”, will hit stores on October 20.

>> If you have not picked up the new album by Marilyn Manson, go get it now. This album is a masterpiece. Manson has made the switch from creepy guy to great artist with ease. Marilyn Manson’s “Mechanical Animals” will hit the top of “Billboard” charts this week after selling just about 223,000 copies in its first week in stores. A world tour is soon to follow, but not much has been finalized as of yet.
