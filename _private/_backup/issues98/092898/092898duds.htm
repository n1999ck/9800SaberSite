Summer Duds

by Scott Meeks / Guest Writer
In 1996 MTV Pictures made their first attempt at the big screen with a movie about talking roaches called “Joe’s Apartment”. Without a doubt, and without questioning why, MTV failed miserably.

However, this time around MTV and Paramount Pictures teamed up to put forth what seemed to be an all around better effort when releasing “Dead Man on Campus” this past month.

We were all hoping for the best when MTV dazzled us with fairly big names such as Mark-Paul Gosselaar who played TV’s Zack on “Saved by the Bell” and Tom Everett Scott who starred along side with Tom Hanks in “That Thing You Do.” But once again our hopes were crushed with a small plot and even less comedy.

Overall, movie patrons left the theater disappointed not only with the movie, but with MTV Pictures all together. Along with “Dead Man on Campus,” many other movies that were released over the summer weren’t really worth watching.

In case you missed the summer duds I’ve compiled a list of movies NOT to rent this fall when released on video. This list should save you a little money, a lot of time, and even more heartache.

1. “Lethal Weapon 4” - There was little more action this time around. In fact, Chris Rock was a nice touch to the movie, but he doesn’t make the movie any more enjoyable than the first three. I think it’s about time for the producers to know when to stop.

2. “Godzilla” - Just a little too much hype, and not enough destruction. A plot would have been helpful as well.

3. “Wrongfully Accused” - If you’ve seen one Leslie Nielsen movie, then you’ve seen them all. Enough said.

4. “Why Do Fools Fall in Love?” - First of all this is the story of Frankie Lymon. Who is Frankie Lymon, you might ask. Exactly my point. I know we can think of something better to base a movie about.

5. “How Stella Got Her Groove Back” - Alright, I actually heard this was a good movie based on an even better book. But if you’re like me, you don’t really care about Stella or her groove. I’d imagine the creators of this one could have thought a little longer about the title.
