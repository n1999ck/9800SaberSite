Letter to the Editor
A Breath of Fresh Air

On Wednesday Sept-ember 16th, I had a most uplifting experience here on campus. Arriving on campus at about 7:30 a.m., in my usual morning fog, I stopped by the men’s restroom in Arnold Hall on my way in to the office. Unknown to me, while there I dropped my checkbook. Sometime in the mid-afternoon my wife called me from home and asked me if I had our checkbook. As I told her that I did, I reached back instinctively and touched the pocket I had put it in that morning. I felt a knot grow in my stomach as I realized it was no longer there. When I then told her that it was missing, she informed me she had just received a call from a Columbus State University student who told her he had found it that morning and wanted to return it. I called the student and we made arrangements to meet so he could return the lost checkbook.

In these times we live in, this student’s behavior was not what I would have expected. I know it’s not fair or right to make assumptions about people you don’t know, but I think if I had discovered the checkbook missing prior to this student’s phone call, I would have already called the bank and put a stop payment on all those missing checks. My natural inclination would have been to protect myself because I would have expected the person who found my checkbook to have attempted to clean out my checking account, or at the very least to have helped themselves to an extravagant purchase. This was one of those times when it was nice to be proven wrong.

This incident has done much to restore my faith in mankind and confirm my faith in the student body here at CSU. I have always felt that we had a great group of students attending this university. Now I know that beyond a shadow of a doubt. Thank you Robert. Thank you very much, not only for the return of my checkbook, but also for making me look with pride at the people I associate with every day.

by Jay Kimbrough
