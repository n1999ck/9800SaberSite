Campus Candid: Greg Neises

by Dawn Salter / Proofreader
Greg Neises is an up and coming art major here at Columbus State University. He is a sophomore majoring in art with an emphasis in photography.

Neises was born outside of Boston, Massachusetts. So how did he end up at CSU?  He has been a member of a military family all of his life. He calls himself an “army brat.” His family moved to Columbus his senior year in high school, during which he attended Hardaway High. Neises was involved in soccer, cross country, and drama in high school. He also entered his artwork in exhibits. He had the honor of one of his photographs being purchased for the permanent collection of the high school.

Neises has been interested in photography for eight years and says art is his “form of expression.”  While at CSU, he has entered the Student Show exhibit and has even sold a few pieces. 

When asked what he thinks about Columbus State University, Neises said, “I like the people here, and CSU is not so big that it’s overwhelming but not so small it’s ridiculous.”

He plans to go to graduate school and pursue a career in art history, teaching, or working in an art gallery.
