CSU football: why not?

by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor
Imagine going to Memorial Stadium to see the Cougars trounce cross state rival Valdosta State or compete against perrenial power North Alabama. We have all seen the shirts that say “Columbus State football, still undefeated.” Why not make it a reality?

College football is a great selling point for an institution. Students, faculty, and the community as a whole would have another outlet to congregate and show support.

Not only is the game fun, but the atmosphere is electric. Supporters bring in their campers a few days before the game to just hang out and get a good parking space. During homecoming week, the schools host a parade through town and have additional events leading up to the game. All of the events are culminated with the actual game which at the time seems secondary.

Now for the more serious side, football brings in money for an athletic department. The majority of Division I schools are supported by their football program. The revenue that television, ticket sales, and bowl appearances bring in is astronomical. In turn, it allows for lesser known sports such as track and field or soccer to compete.

With that said, let the band fire up the fight song, the players enter the field, and Columbus State football become a reality.
