Innovations of the financially challenged

by Josh Eller / Entertainment Editor

Back to Frontpage

For most, eating out is hard on the budget, but there are places where you can get the most for the money. Several places offer cheap eating where you can actually get stuffed.

If you crave Chinese food “China Buffet” on Macon Road offers over eighty items for about five dollars until 5:00 p.m. What you want to do to take full advantage of the buffet is get there about 4:30 p.m., pay for lunch, and then eat slowly. Around 5:00 p.m. they will start putting out shrimp, snow crab legs, and sometimes crabcakes. Now the buffet is fair game, you may draw a few discouraging looks from the proprietor but not to worry I have yet to be asked to leave.

Now, if it is Italian you crave two restaurants and a pizza chain come to mind. Mazzio’s and Pizza Hut, both offer lunch buffets with Mazzio’s offering the same deal in the evening on Tuesday’s. With Little Ceasars you can sometimes go in and ask for canceled orders and barter them down to around five bucks for a large pizza. Some might question the legality of ordering pizzas and then showing up three hours later to purchase them at discounted rates, but I’ve heard from a few people that this actually works and Little Ceasars is always happy to sell rather than throw away pizza.

If it is Mexican you want, El Vaquero always has dirt cheap food which includes all the chips and salsa you can eat, for example, burrito ($2.30) + water + chips/salsa= fullness. Rio Bravo offers free, you heard right, free tacos, from 4-6 p.m. Monday through Friday, but may require the purchase of an alcoholic beverage. If it’s hot sandwiches or chicken fingers the cheapest in town is Red Rider. It’s somewhat of a Jurassic Park of a bar but, if you can get past the smoke and Patsy Cline songs you can get two grill cooked burgers and fries or chicken fingers for just over five dollars. The last and least advisable tactic for cheap food is done frequently by a CSU student who will be known only as Big A. Big A likes to enter the Magic Mart and prepare two hot dogs for purchase, he then stands with his back to the cashier and quickly devours two, sometimes three hot dogs. The grand total for such fine and underhanded dining comes to $1.89, drink included.
