January 19

On Martin Luther King, Jr.'s official birthday, the Historic Metropolitan Baptist Church will host an ecumenical service commemorating the life of an American legend. Luther Rice Seminary Professor and Pastor Green Forest Community Parish, Dr. George O. McCalep, Jr. will speak in his honor. All ethnic and religious groups are encourages to attend the 12:00 noon one-hour service. For more info call 322-1488.

January 21

All faiths are invited to celebrate the dream that Martin Luther King, Jr. envisioned for all people at the Unity Worship Service, 7:00 p.m. at the Columbus Civic Center, featuring dynamic guest speaker, Dr. Anthony Campolo. Professor of Sociology ad Director of the Urban Studies Program at Eastern College in St. Davids, Pennsylvania. Dr. Campolo also serves as the President of the Evangelical Association for the Promotion of Education. This best-selling author has twenty-five books currently in print and his weekly television program, "Hashing it Out," can be seen on cable in twenty-million homes. For more info, call 561-0343.

January 22

Sophomore and junior students from surrounding counties (Talbot, Harris, Russell, Muscogee, Marion, Schley, and Webster) are invited to convene at Friendship Baptist Church on Sixth Ave. from 8:00 a.m. - 2:30 p.m. Guest speaker Mike Gaymon, President of Greater Columbus Chamber of Commerce, among understanding among diverse groups, team building, conflict resolution, valuing structures, and career development and leadership. For more info, call 569-2636.

January 22 - February 7

The Springer Opera House is pleased to present "Joe Turner's Come and Gone," by playwright August Wilson, two-time Pulitzer Prize winner. The drama, staged in the early 1900s at a northeastern boarding house, unfolds as peculiar residents breeze in and out of the home. Evening performance are scheduled for 8:00 p.m., January 22, 23, 24, 29, 30, 31, and February 5, 6, 7 at Liberty Theatre. Matinee is January 25 only at 2:30 p.m. Tickets are $16/$24 for adults and senior citizens/active military, $12/$10 for full-time students and children. For more info, call the Springer Opera House, 324-5714.
