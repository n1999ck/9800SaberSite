Badk to Frontpage

The Greek Council of Columbus State University has started a tradition on campus that will hopefully grow in the years to come. The formation of the Homecoming event at Columbus State University is composed of many events and attractions that will hopefully spark an interest for students to become more involved in the future. I am excited to see such a great event occurring on the CSU campus. I feel as if the variety of events and involvement of organizations will prove to be an enormous success for not only the organizations participating, but for the students and campus of Columbus StateUniversity as a whole.

Homecoming will be a long week of activities that are sure to increase student involvement as well as school spirit. To see such an event taking place is truly phenomenal. Such events as a talent show, Island Fest, Pep Ralley and Cookout at the Clocktower, a Parade and a Band Party are a few examples of the events taking place throughout the week. The Greek Ball is the biggest event of all and will include such announcements as best overall organization for the week and let us not forget the Homecoming King and Queen.

But, this event is not all play and no work. Each Greekorganization as well as other organizations will have to apply a lot of time and effort for the week to go as planned. Each organization will be given responsibilities and tasks to perform in order for the week to be a success. These responsibilities will give organizations a chance to work together and increase student involvement on this campus. But, the campus and students are not the only people benefiting from this week. The kids of the 30th avenue school are getting a chance to get tutored by students of the organizations as well. This will give the kids a chance to meet new faces and the students to aid the community as well.

The Greek Council of Columbus State University along with other organizations on campus are making great strives to increase student involvement on campus. I feel as if Homecoming will prove to be a great success and pave the way for increased student involvement for the years to come. Hopefully, the Homecoming event will encourage more students on campus to get involved with organizations on the CSU campus.

 

Michael Cox, CSU Student
