Letter from the Editor

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

President Clinton is yet again caught in the midst of a sex scandal. This time he is accused of having an affair with a White House intern, Monica Lewinsky, who was due to testify in the Paula Jones case.

If people were having a difficult time believing accusations by Paula Jones, it is getting more difficult to believe President Clinton’s evasive answers to some very straight forward questions in the Lewinsky scandal. One thing that remains consistent is the President’s waning credibility.

As a woman, I wonder if Paula Jones is doing more harm than good for women. Her credibility is shaky if only on the grounds that she waited so long to come forward. Any collective feeling by the public or those in Washington, that Clinton is a faithful husband is history. However, the question remains, why did Jones wait so long to be so terribly offended by the President’s alleged actions. She took five years to be offended. It is possible that she is now motivated by greed.

If Jones was motivated by greed, why then should we believe Lewinsky’s allegations? After all, she didn’t even come forward herself, but was asked to give a deposition in the Jones case.

What makes Lewinsky’s allegations so believable is not what she’s saying, but what Clinton is not saying.

When asked by a PBS reporter on the program “NewsHour” shortly after the story came out, Clinton said, “There was no improper relationship”. When the interviewer asked him to further explain his statement he said, “There was no sexual-improper sexual relationship or any improper relationship at all.” He could have given the interviewer a flat, “No” or even told him it was none of his business. Instead, he choose a very carefully worded and slightly ambiguous response. After all, the term “improper” is relative and fairly subjective.

Clinton was not the only one giving answers that prompted more questions during the early hours of the scandal. White House Press Secretary, Mike McCurry, refused to comment beyond the statement that the President had no improper relationship with Lewinsky.

Chief White House Correspondent, Sam Donaldson, said on “Dateline” that he wondered why Clinton chose not to simply answer questions with yes or no. In other words, why choose words that do not flatly deny he had sex with Lewinsky?

If Clinton is innocent the matter would be less complicated. He could answer questions unequivocally. The problem with being woken up in the predawn hours to accusations of sexual misconduct is, there is no time to see the other player’s hand. Before deciding on answers to the press’ question. His advisors did not necessarily have to know the truth, but they had to know what evidence the other side had against the President to avoid later problems.

Clinton finally did deny any sexual contact with Lewinsky. His answers were shorter and more believable. Maybe because they were true, but maybe because he was confident the evidence was not available to prove otherwise.

The President has other evidence piling up against him. Including tapes which reportedly include conversation between Lewinsky and White House employee, Tripp, discussing the alleged affair. “Newsweek” reported that Lewinsky told Tripp the President had sent his long time friend, Jordan, to convince her to deny the affair.

Unlike Clinton’s wishy washy answers to allegations, Jordan blatantly denies he ever suggested to Lewinsky that she lie. This is an even more serious allegation that the original because it is a federal offense to tamper with witnesses due to testify in a federal investigation.

In light of the White House staff’s difficulty producing recorders that disprove allegations that Lewinsky entered the White House in the late hours of the night and lack of denial that Clinton presented Lewinsky with gifts, Clinton seems to be less and less credible with ever breaking news report.

His admission during the Jones deposition that he had an affair with Gennifer Flowers, which he had previously denied, did not help matters either. According to reports from “Dateline”, others in Washington, both Democrats and Republicans are keeping quiet about the scandal. Cokie Roberts said on the broadcast that she heard many people say they hoped it wasn’t true, but felt it was.

With power of the Presidency being what it is, in the end we may never know the whole truth of either the Jones or the Lewinsky allegations. Perhaps more importantly, we may never know the truth behind the accusations involving Jordan. Like the Kennedy assassination , Watergate and even the Whitewater scandal, it is doubtful that even in the light of new evidence, that everyone will ever agree on exactly what is true and what is fiction in this case.
