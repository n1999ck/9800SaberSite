CSU Host Career Expo

by Heather Warren / Associate Editor

Career Expo ‘98 is coming to Columbus State University. It will be on January 30 in Woodruff Gymnasium. This year the expo will have over ninety five employers from the surrounding region. CSU is co hosting the expo with Georgia Southwestern University and Columbus Technical Institute. Several companies from around town help sponsor the event including Tom’s, Powertel, and many more.

The employers scheduled to come are a very diverse group. Some of the employers coming are WTVM Channel 9, AFLAC, Coco-Cola, Quad/ Graphics, and Synovus.

The day will begin at 7:30 a.m. with early registration and a continental breakfast. At 8:15 Dr. Robert Johnson, the Dean of the Abbott Turner College of Business, will give a speech on teamwork. After the speech from Dr. Johnson there will be an hour for businesses to set up booths. Beginning at ten the expo will let students, alumni, staff and people from the community visit tables.

Everyone attending the expo must register. Christi Dixon said that it is not necessary to be there at 10:00 a.m. to participate. If you have an early morning class you have time to meet the employers. They will be interviewing until 1:00 p.m.

If you think that meeting employers is all Career Expo is about you are wrong. After meeting employers there are workshops held in the afternoon. Beginning at 2:00 there is a Panel Presentation in Davidson Auditorium where representatives from large organizations discuss what they look for in interviews. Then there will be a one-on-one mock interview with one of the employers.

The Career Center is expecting about 1500 participants. Christi Dixon from the Career Center said “It’s open to all students, and all students are encouraged to attend to begin a job search.” Dixon wanted to encourage all students to attend not just Seniors. “Freshman, Sophomore, and Juniors are encouraged to come to ask questions and to network. It helps to see what opportunities are open for that field,” said Dixon.

Dixon also strongly encourages those almost ready to graduate. Dixon said “students getting ready to graduate are expected to dress professionally and bring a lot of resumes.” Dixon maintains that this is a great opportunity for the students to get to know what is available in our area. “Never again will you ever have that many employers in one room,” said Dixon.

Dixon summed up the expo by saying it’s a “huge, exciting, great event, and everyone should come.”
