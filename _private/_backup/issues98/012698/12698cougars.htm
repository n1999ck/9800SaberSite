Green gets 300 and Cougars claim first

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

The Columbus State men’s basketball team continued it’s dominance over it’s competitors and in the process, took over first place and helped it’s coach reach another milestone in his legendary career.

With a first place showdown on the horizon, the Columbus State Cougars knew they had to keep their heads straight in order to prevent being upset by the 7-9 Armstrong Atlantic Pirates. The win would have to come amid the excitement of head coach Herbert Greene gunning for his 300th victory at CSU and in front a season-high crowd of 1,415 at Woodruff Gym. The Cougars were not to be denied. Columbus was led by senior Larry Coates who scored a game high 21 points. CSU also got key playing from Calvin Hill who returned to the lineup after a one game suspension. The win gave Columbus State it’s best 17 game start in school history at 14-3.

The Cougars weren’t through however. After disposing of Armstrong Atlantic and non conference Morehouse, Columbus took dead aim on the Bobcats from Georgia College & State University.

The Cougar vs. Bobcat matchup pitted the first and second place teams in the Peach Belt Conference against each other for the first time this season. At 9-6, Georgia College promised to be a stiff challenge for the first place Cougars. After being down by six at halftime, Columbus State used the ice cold shooting of Georgia College as a spring board to leap in front of the Bobcats and cruise to a 70-61 victory.

The Columbus victory brought the Cougars record to 16-3, 6-1 in the Peach Belt. Columbus was ranked 20th in the national poll and now has a two game lead in the Peach Belt Conference.
