New allegations against Clinton

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

President Bill Clinton was once again accused of sexual misconduct Wednesday.

Whitewater Independent counsel Kenneth Starr requested to expand his probe to include the allegations that Clinton conspired to coerce a White House intern, Monica Lewinsky, to lie in the Paula Jones trial coming up in May.

A three judge appeals court panel authorized Starr to expand his investigation.

Starr was prompted to expand the investigation after a series of tapes made over a period of 18 months by another former White House aide and friend of Lewinsky, Linda Tripp, were brought to his attention.

On Tuesday, January 13 FBI agents working for Starr wired Tripp before she met Lewinsky for drinks at the Ritz.

According to reports from ABC and the Washington Post, the tapes included detailed descriptions of an on going affair between Lewinsky and the president and allegations that Clinton asked her to lie about the affair.

One source who has listened to the tapes told CNN that Lewinsky told Tripp the president said to her, “There is no evidence, so you can deny, deny, deny.”

Reporters who have listened to the tapes say they also include comments by Lewinsky that Vernon Jordan, close friend to the president, asked her to lie about her relationship with president Clinton in the Jones trial.

Jordan adamantly denied the accusations at a press conference Wednesday.

On January 7, before the tapes surfaced, Lewinsky gave a sworn affidavit in connection with Jones case. The Post reported its sources said Lewinsky denied the affair in the affidavit.

During an interview on the PBS program “NewsHour” Clinton said, “There was no improper relationship . . .” When asked to explain what he meant by “improper relationship” he answered, “I think you know what it means. It means there was no sexual-improper sexual relationship or any improper relationship . . .”

Late Wednesday afternoon Starr and Jones’ attorneys subpoenaed White House documents including custodial records, logs, employment records and documents relating to Lewinsky.

Starr also asked for White House logs that would show how often Lewinsky visited the West Wing of the White House and telephone records to show if Lewinsky called the president or if he called her.

Lewinsky began her unpaid internship in the White House at age 21 in the summer 1995. She was hired in December 1995 as a staff assistant in the Office of Legislature Affairs, where she sometimes delivered correspondence to the Oval Office.
