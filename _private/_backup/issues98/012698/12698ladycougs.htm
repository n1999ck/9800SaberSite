Chalk up another one for the Ladies

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

It’s starting become the same old story for the Columbus State Lady Cougars, face a tough opponent, stomp them into the ground, face an even tougher opponent, stomp them into the ground a little worse. In short, the Lady Cougars are consistently defeating quality opponents by huge point margins, margins that are causing many around the Peach Belt Conference to shutter at the thought of having to face off with Columbus State.

The latest fatality on the Lady Cougars hit list was the Georgia College & State University Lady Bobcats. Georgia College entered the game in Milledgeville in second place behind Columbus with an impressive 11-3 record. The Lady Cougars had an equally impressive 12-3 record and were undefeated in Peach Belt play. The game promised to be a closely fought battle, and it was, that is until the Lady Cougars exploded on a 21-0 run in the opening minutes of the game. The score was 10-11 when Columbus shot their play into high gear. Once the nets had cooled down the score was 39-12. From their the Lady Cougars never looked back as they defeated Georgia College 107-86. Columbus was led by Toschia Campfield’s 26 points and Que Gilliam’s 25 points.

The Lady Cougars raised their record to 13-3 and 7-0 in the Peach Belt Conference. Earlier in the week Columbus State defeated Armstrong Atlantic 90-52. The victory over Georgia College marked the fifth game in a row that Columbus has won by 20 points or more.
