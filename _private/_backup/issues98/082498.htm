Monday, August 24, 1998 - Welcome Back to School!!

Public Safety
on the Rise

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief

The Fall Semester of 1998 is bringing about changes throughout the entire campus at Columbus State University.

If you notice campus safety on bikes instead of in a car it is no mistake, it is just the new transition. Of the many departments undergoing changes on campus, the Public Safety Office at CSU is enduring changes of it’s own that will hope to better the campus.

The biggest change that has occurred is the transition from car to bike patrol. The transition however does not change the authoritative aspect of the Public Safety Office. These officers are not only security, but real cops who handle moving violations or any other events at Columbus State University that may be of their concern.

The Public Safety Office will be experiencing expansion in the future as they move toward patrolling the Performing Arts Center, Oxbow Meadows, and the Space Science Center.

Public Safety plans to increase the bike patrol by two members in September and all officers will attend bike training.

Another change involves the acquisition of the new parking decals for the Fall Semester. Students can pick their new decals up at the Public Safety Office. Students need to remember, however, that their fees for parking decals were figured in with their tuition.

Public Safety will be adding another addition to their security features on campus.

At the moment, Columbus State University holds hours around the clock 365 days a year and has emergency phones in the parking areas on campus for extra security purposes. However, a new addition will make it’s way to the department in the Fall when the Public Safety Office begins their new Escort Service. This will entail golf carts which will usher students to different areas on campus.

Experience can never be replaced and the Public Safety Office at CSU has no problem with an experienced staff. Of the officers on staff, three-fourths hold a Bachelor of Science Degree and two Sergeants have obtained a Masters of Arts Degree in Criminal Justice.

Sergeant Wilson states, “ This is a highly educated, highly trained department that is continually receiving new specialized training for crisis prevention.”
