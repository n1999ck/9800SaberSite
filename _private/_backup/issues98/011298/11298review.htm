Tomorrow Never Dies

The 18th film in Ean Flemming’s James Bond series had no problem delivering non stop action with yet another way 007 manages to save the world from the brink of destruction. For those that have followed the James Bond series, there seems to always be a global threat ignited by a main evil doer who makes it a point to find time to do Bond in. In “Tomorrow Never Dies,” the overall plot stays true to form but is refreshed by teaming Bond up with a sexy Chinese agent who adds martial arts to the films arsenal of explosions and gunplay.

Coming off a successful debut in “Golden Eye,” Brosnan has no problem playing a cool debonair bond reminiscent to that of Sean Connery. With the addition of Michelle Yeoh who also starred in the Jackie Chan movie “Super Cop”, “Tomorrow Never Dies” never hits a lull and has no problem selling its plot, which makes “Tomorrow Never Dies” one to watch. Three Stars.

Jackie Brown

The characters and plot are what made this a good movie. Based on the 1995 novel Rum Punch by Elmore Leonard. “ Jackie Brown” reminded you of “Pulp Fiction” in a lot of ways Quentin Tarrintino writes the plot and dialogue among the characters in his movies. The story although taken out of adaptation of a novel never gives you a clue of what happens next. Like many of Tarantino’s other films, “Resevoir Dogs”, “True Romance” & “Pulp Fiction”, the characters are all unique and has the story just following what they might normally do in a day. Samuel L. Jackson has the best character as gun-runner “Ordell Robi” who plays a role somewhat like that of the hitman in “Pulp Fiction”. “Jackie Brown” was a welcomed relief from “Four Rooms” and “Dusk till Dawn” and shows that former video rental clerk, Tarrintino, has made a movie befitting his talent. Three Stars.

Titanic

I know everyone says they loved it and it was the best movie this year or they would see it again but this was the longest movie I have ever seen in one day. Three hours and forty five minutes, if you include previews, of romance was hard to handle. I will give Leonardo credit for some descent acting, but his co-star was not that bright and there was a obvious difference in talent between the two. The story was historically accurate but the dialogue and plot were very average, and even sometimes boring. I don’t want to seem morbid but my favorite part of the movie is when the boat finally sank. There were some very realistic stunts especially when that guy fell off the back and hit the propeller which kind of made me wake up for a few minutes. Guys if you plan to take a date to this movie bring a thermos of coffee and maybe a tent. This is not to say I have anything against romance or anything like that, I think “Braveheart” is a great romance and would gladly watch it twice in a row before seeing this again. “Titanic” gets Four Torpedoes - please send it back to the bottom.
