Letter to the Editor

 
In the Feb. 2 issue, the Saber editor implies that a woman with a college degree will earn 75% of what a man with the same qualifications and experience would. This is a fallacy. If at one time the female population of the U.S. as a whole earned 75% of what the male population did, it was because fewer women had completed their post-secondary education and many would eventually sacrifice their careers to raise a family. Today, women constitute the majority of college graduates and are free to choose their own destiny. It has become socially acceptable to remain childless or develop some kind of alternative child-rearing arrangement if natalist urges must be fulfilled. Think about it - what company would risk pissing off half the consumers of the U.S. by overtly discriminating against women? But go ahead and keep believing the “75%” statistic and you’ll contribute further to the very divisiveness the editorial laments about. Go ahead and choose to be a victim.

- Modern Man
