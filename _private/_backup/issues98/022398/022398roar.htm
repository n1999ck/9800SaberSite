There are over 70 organizations on the Campus of Columbus State University. These organizations are, overall, the best of college life. These organizations allow students to get involved on campus and to have fun and actually learn something about the other students on campus. These organizations give students something to do other than just come to class and go home.

Student activities are also something else that allow students to be active. If a student is not a part of an organization, the campus organizations sponsor other events that are, best of all, free to all students. For example, there are concerts, fraternity and sorority parties during rush week, game shows in the cafe’, and coed sports in the evenings.

Students either don’t know about these events because of poor advertising or just don’t come because they are too lazy or their friends don’t come. I can understand not coming because of poor advertising, that’s the fault of the supporter, but not coming because your friends don’t want to or because you just don’t feel like it is the worst reason in the world. As students, you should want to go out into the world and try something new. Being lazy never got anyone anywhere, never discovered something new, or never passed a class. And your friends, if they jumped off a bridge, would you follow, of course you would, but only if it was a very low to the ground bridge. Your friend shouldn’t have control over what you do unless your drunk and you need a ride home.
I would like to see more students at event here on campus. If you want to express your feelings about events, go by the office you think sponsored the event and tell.them how you feel. If you want to get more involved, do the same thing. All 70 of the organizations on campus want more attendance in their meetings and also at their event. You becoming involved as a student looks good on a resume and also helps you to meet new people and new connections for later in life.
