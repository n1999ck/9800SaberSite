Letter from the Editor

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

 
Last week a 5 year old boy was charged with sexual harassment in Huntsville, Alabama.

The boy, Christian Mills, was initially suspended from school for one day after being charged with the offense.

Later, school officials changed the charge to a more generic violation, but the one day suspension stood.

The boy allegedly exposed himself to another student in the restroom. However, his mother says he has been having trouble using the toilet on his own, fell over, and stood up before covering himself.

The important issue here is not whether or not the boy intentionally exposed himself, but how the school administration handled the situation.

As a woman, I am fortunate to live in an era where I do not have to worry about being chased around a desk by my boss. It still happens, but at least now there are more channels to deal with this sort of harassment and stop it. I’m grateful that women before me stood up and demanded the right to work in harassment free environments, often sacrificing their own jobs or reputation in the process.

However, it has gone too far. We have reached a state of absolute paranoia.

The school administrations reaction to the common and normal act of this child, is not only overkill, but dangerous.

Children at 5 years old are just coming to understand their bodies. Most of us remember being curious about the body and remember wanting to share our new found knowledge with others. At 5 years old the body is a new frontier, not tool of power or domination.

Power and domination is what sexual harassment is about, not body parts and dirty jokes. It is the meaning behind the act, the intent. It is hard for me to believe that many kindergarten children are capable of such intent. It is possible that there are children who are malicious in these sort of acts, but there is no evidence of that here.

What there is evidence of here is a school’s attempt to appease an ever growing group of radicals who believe any act involving sex or sexuality is an overt attempt to harm someone else.

The school had an opportunity to do the right thing. The administration could have talked to the boy, ask him not to expose himself and left it at that . Instead, a statement had to be made at the expense of this child’s self esteem.

I wonder if the effects of this will follow this him throughout his life. It makes me sad that as a society we are so quick to label our children as perverted, bad or sexual deviants for engaging in things which for us as children were considered just a part of growing up.

This child will have to suffer through being tagged a sexual harasser for what we once simply referred to as, “I’ll show you mine, if you show me yours”.

The danger is not in the children, the danger is in pronouncing them criminals.
