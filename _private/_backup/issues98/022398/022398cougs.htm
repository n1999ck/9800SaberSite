Cougars spank UNF in season

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
Larry Coates poured in a game high 23 points including five three pointers as the CSU Cougars downed the North Florida Ospreys 85-77.

Coates began the game with a bang as he connected from three-point range to start the scoring. The first half stayed at a fast tempo as both teams consistently pushed the ball up the court quickly. The Cougars were clinging to a slim six point lead late in the half when a key 7-3 run put Columbus up by 10. The run was capped off by a thunderous one-handed dunk from Jermaine Taylor.

UNF came out in the second half and a flurry of baskets put the Ospreys down by only two 43-41. After a quick five point run by the Cougars, North Florida went on a huge 11-4 run of their own and tied the game at 52 a piece, much to the displeasure of head coach Herbert Greene. “We just didn’t combat their aggressive play very well at all,” stated Greene. A Coates three-pointer began the decisive rally as the Cougars responded to the Osprey run and went on a 25-8 outburst. “Larry (Coates) really made them (three-pointers) at big points in the game.” replied assistant coach Robert Moore, “He definitely knows when to hit them.” Columbus opened up a 10-point lead late in the game and stretched it to as many as seventeen as the Cougars cruised to a fifteen point victory.

With the victory, Columbus State clinched the PBAC South Division Championship and will be seeded 1st in the upcoming PBAC tournament. Also in the victory one record was broken and another tied. The victory over UNF gave CSU victory number 23 of the season, breaking the mark of 22 which was achieved several times throughout the Cougar’s history. Coates tied a thirteen year old record by handing out 242 assist this season. When asked how he felt about the record, Coates replied, “I’m happy about tying the record but I really wanted to break it.”

The Cougars now travel to Savannah, GA for the Peach Belt tournament which begins February 20th and finishes on the 25th.
