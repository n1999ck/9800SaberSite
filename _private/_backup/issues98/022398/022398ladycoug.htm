Perfection for the ladies

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
Entering the game against the University of North Florida, the Columbus State Lady Cougars had many records riding on the outcome. At stake was an out right conference championship, an undefeated conference record, a school record for victories in a season, and most importantly, the chance to have a huge momentum boost entering the upcoming PBAC tournament.

After the opening tip the two teams traded baskets with UNF maintaining a slight lead. While the Lady Ospreys moved the ball through CSU’s suffocating full court press, the rumblings of “Could this be the let down game for the Lady Cougars” began to circulate through Woodruff Gym. Columbus soon put those thoughts to rest as a 9-0 run at the 11:47 mark began the Lady Cougar route. A Reagan Housch three-pointer put CSU up by 10 and the full court press began to take effect as UNF was forced into 15 first half turnovers. Poor defense killed any chance of a UNF first half comeback. The Lady Ospreys committed 14 personal fouls, the majority of those coming in the waning minutes of the first half and CSU responded by connecting on 70% of their free-throws.

The second half opened with the Lady Ospreys looking like a completely different team. After being down by 16 at the half, UNF opened with five unanswered points. CSU began to look sloppy as turnovers and poor rebounding kept UNF close. What started as a Columbus blowout soon became a tense nail-biter as an 11-2 run pulled the Lady Ospreys to within six points with only 5:52 left in the game. “We were taking good shots, maybe a little too quick, (the shots) just weren’t dropping for us,” stated head coach Jay Sparks. The Lady Cougars needed a hero and they got it from an unlikely source. With the fans and players on the edge of their seats, senior point guard Holly Pearson took a pass and drained her only basket of the game, a key three-pointer from the left corner. The Pearson bomb started a crucial 10-0 run, capped by a three-point play by Que Gilliam. The Lady Cougars never looked back as they pulled away to take an 88-68 victory. Tocshia Campfield led all scorers with 21 points and Sylvia Allen pulled down a game high eight rebounds

The victory over North Florida finished a perfect conference season at 16-0. When asked how he felt about the victory and the undefeated season, Sparks simply replied, “Outstanding, It’s the greatest feeling ever.” Reagan Housch, who finished the game with five three-pointers stated, “At the first practice we knew we could go all the way.” The next challenge for the 22-4 Lady Cougars is the Peach Belt Conference tournament in Savannah, GA which runs from February 20th through the 25th.
