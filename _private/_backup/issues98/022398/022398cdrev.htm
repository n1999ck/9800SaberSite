Three CD Reviews

by Josh Eller / Entertainment Editor

 
DJ Shadow : Preemptive Strike

Contains eleven tracks ranging in sounds from mellow Beastie Boys blues to high-end techno riffs. Each track is very long, averaging over six minutes and is uniquely set apart from the one before, leaving you to think there is a different artist for each track. Track 10 “Highnoon” has had some airplay on MTV’s 120 minutes and look for it to go mainstream in the next few weeks. B+

NOFX : so long and thanks for all the shoes

NOFX has done a good deal to pick up the pace with there new release. The band is a lot livelier than their last effort and experiments with horns and Rancid style beats. All in all it can be considered one of their better releases. Track three “Monosylabic Girl, Track four “180 degrees” and Track seven “All Outta Angst” are the CD’s standouts. Sample the entire CD before venturing purchase, it’s different from any previous release. C+

Jane’s Addiction: Kettle Whistle

Fifteen tracks with four previously unreleased songs and includes live-demos with out-takes. Officially breaking up in 93’ Jane’s is now enjoying a sold out reunion tour schedule with Flea of the Red Hot Chile Peppers replacing bassist Eric Avery. The biggest standouts for the CD are the live performances of “Jane Says” and “Three Days”. No disappointments with this reunion compilation. A-
