Pearl Jam releases "Yield"

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

 
Pearl Jam stepped back into the spotlight again with their new release, “Yield”. The new album soared to number two in the top ten, settling in just behind the Titanic Soundtrack.

“Yield” sold just over 358,000 copies in just the first week of it’s release. Virgin Records representative’s describe sales as “phenomenal”. Rolling Stone Magazine gave the new album the excellent rating of four stars.
The good reviews “Yield” may very well be followed by more of the same. Sales show no sign of slowing, and the word about the album is spreading. This may be the best work that they have done as of yet.

Sales and reaction to the last two albums, “Vitalogy” and “No Code”, did not go over as well as the band had hoped they would. “Yield” shows us what the band was trying to do on the other two albums.

The album kicks off with the classic Pearl Jam roar on the opening track “Brain of J”, and moves into a series of songs that could all possibly be hit singles. The opening track is definitely the hardest song on the album, with most of the songs giving way to the newer, more mellow style the band has recently taken. Highlights of the album include “Faithful”, “Wishlist”, and “In Hiding”, which are evidence of success in the bands new attitude.

Pearl Jam is made up of Eddie Vedder, Stone Gossard, Mike McCready, and Jack Irons. The band recently sat down with MTV to chat about the new album and their new outlook on life, in general. “I’m getting older and I’m tired off being pissed off. I don’t think it’s cool anymore,” Vedder said. Gossard followed up by adding, “We are getting older. Thirties. Not so wound up anymore. Maybe we are a little better at letting stuff slide.”
The band does, in deed, seem to be enjoying themselves a little more lately. In January they once again took over the radio while putting together a broadcast they call “Monkey Wrench Radio”. It is basically a party that the band holds, inviting other bands to come and play with them. This years radio party may end up being captured in a documentary or home video collection.

Between the live music, Eddie Vedder plays disc jockey and spins some of his favorite records for everyone to hear. These records include Sonic Youth, Mudhoney, and Shudder To Think, all of which might end up opening for Pearl Jam on the upcoming tour. Eddie said, “It’s me being able to play the music I love and music that my friends love.”

Pearl Jam’s reluctance to tour or do videos has left fans with many questions about the bands future. It seems as though these questions will receive answers in the near future, because Pearl Jam is ready to step out into the public once again. In preparing for recording “Yield”, the band went separate ways for a short period of time before coming together to record. Each member brought his own demos and ideas with him, thus “Yield” was created.

Jeff Ament said, “In terms of a creative breakthrough in music, that’s the biggest one I’ve made in years.” The first single from the album was “Given to Fly”. This song caused a few people to take notice of the bands that influence Pearl Jam in their music making process. If you listen closely, “Given to Fly” is strangely similar to Led Zeppelin’s 1971 hit “Going To California.”

Mike McCready admitted, “Sure there’s some influences that I might have stolen, but I just don’t know.” Any Led Zeppelin fans that take offense to this borrowing of sound should remember that Muddy Waters never complained about Led Zeppelin borrowing his sound.

The thirty-three date American tour for “Yield” kicks off in Montana on June 20 and ends in Washington D.C. on September 18. “I’m really anxious about the music, you know, that’s great. But we are going to Australia and Hawaii first, so it’s kind of built around surfing,” Veddersaid.

Eddie Vedder and company will hit Atlanta on the tour at Lakewood Amphitheater on September 1. Tickets for some shows are actually being sold through Ticketmaster.

Pearl Jam may also play on the David Letterman show on May 1. In another showing of the bands new found happiness, Eddie Vedder shared the bands new motto, “If it ain’t fun, it’s funny. If something goes horribly wrong, we’re just gonna have a laugh.”
