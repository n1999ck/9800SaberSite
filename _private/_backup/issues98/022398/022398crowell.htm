Professor celebrates 31 years at CSU

by Evelyn Tolbert-Owens / Staff Writer

 
When he was a little boy, William J. Crowell was not quite sure what his future would bring. He was diagnosed with post-polio syndrome in 1946 and a big part of his childhood was spent in and out of hospitals and fighting the disease.

This year, 52 years later, Crowell, faculty member of the Communication Department, celebrates his 31st anniversary at CSU.

Lyndon Johnson was President of the U.S. and Dr. Thomas Y. Whitley was President of Columbus College. The Communications Department as we know it today did not exist.

“A lot has happened since I first came here on June 10th, 1967,” he says.

Professor Crowell went to work for the Department of Theater and Liberal Arts. Soon the Communications Department was born and he became the first faculty member.

Crowell taught one course for 15 months, a course called “Basic Speaking.” This course is well known today among students as COM 105, Public Speaking. Gradually, he added on more courses such as Group Discussion, Persuasive Speaking and Interpersonal Communications. He moved the Department forward and in the early 70’s communication studies were offered as a minor first, and in the later 70’s also as a major.

“I love to teach”, he says. “It’s always been what I like to do best.” Crowell’s enthusiasm and dedication shows. To this day he has accumulated 280 hours of sick leave.

A former student of Crowell’s, Wanda Newsom, remembers, “When I took Professor Crowell’s Interpersonal Communication Course in the fall of 1981, I really enjoyed him as a teacher. It seemed like he really enjoyed teaching us, and that, in turn, made me enjoy taking his class. I received a certificate for being “the best improved communicator” and I was very proud of that. I always felt like I could not communicate well, however, with his encouragement everything seemed possible.”

Crowell’s determination to push the development of the Communication Department forward was evident when he applied for a grant to establish a speech lab for the Communications Department at CSU. His brainchild became reality when the grant was approved, the equipment was ordered, and room 005 in the basement of Schwob Library was made available to set the lab up. “We put a lot of work and effort into the development of this lab,” he says. “Today this speech lab has earned the reputation for being the best in the region. People come to us to get advice on setting up their labs.”

Crowell holds an undergraduate degree in history and speech from Bowling Green State University, Ohio. He earned his masters degree in speech and theater at the State University in St. Cloud, Minnesota. He also took 25 courses toward his doctorate at Louisiana State University and 22 more courses at Auburn University. Crowell then switched to Georgia State University to write his dissertation. However, because of political problems and the closure of the Georgia State University Communications Department Professor Crowell never completed his dissertation and therefore never receive his doctorate.

When asked how he felt about his historical impact here at CSU he told us, “I feel like I have three histories here at CSU: The first ten years I developed the Columbus College Choral Readers, a body of students who would travel all through the Southeast reading aloud to various audiences. My second ten years I was involved with the direction of television and news production. For the last ten years I have devoted my time to writing and revising my book and developing speech labs.”

Crowell’s book, “Successful Public Speaking” has been published four times and is used here at CSU for COM 105. He is currently working on the fifth edition - his last one, he says.

“Been there, done that, moving on.” he comments, “I look forward to retirement in 1999. It will give me the opportunity to travel the U.S. with my wife in a recreational vehicle, buy some more land and horses, and do all those things. I also enjoy doing, but never had a lot of time for.”
