Campus Candid: Sophia Karlssen

by Brigett Brumbaugh / Staff Writer

 
One of many outstanding students attending Columbus State University is freshman Sophia Karlsson.

She is currently on a sports scholarship that pays for room, board, and tuition.

Karlsson, 20, arrived here in September after graduating from a Swedish high school in June.

Ranked eighth in her country for her age group, she is participating in tennis at CSU and will play doubles in her first match with a fellow Swede.

“Playing with another Swede is great. We can talk to each other about the plays and no one else understands us,” Karlsson says.

Karlsson is in her second quarter at CSU. She is taking a math class, English for international students, and theater appreciation. She is undecided in her major, but is considering psychology.

She enjoys attending CSU but does not understand why people are often astonished that of all places in the United States, she chose here.

Being the last child to leave home, her parents miss her a lot. Sophia commented that they are very happy and proud of her for trying to accomplish her goals even though they want her home.

Karlsson e-mails her parents often and is able to talk on the phone with them once a week. Christmas break was the last time she saw her family and friends in Sweden. In March, her parents plan to visit her in Columbus.

Karlsson has had to adjust to some changes in the states as well. One thing is the climate. She said,”In my country it is winter now and here it is nice out.”

Food is another big change. At home she always ate a healthy meal prepared by her mother. Karlsson says,”Here everybody eats fast food and nachos. There are fast-food restaurants everywhere.”

Another difference that Karlsson has dealt with is people saying “hi” even if they do not know you. In her country this is not common.

Karlsson can remain at CSU on scholarship until her degree is completed. After graduation, she wants to travel around the world.
