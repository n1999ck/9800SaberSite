Monday, March 2, 1998

Vice President Gore visits CSU campus

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

Vice President Al Gore is due to visit the Columbus State University campus at 11:15 today in the Woodruff gym. He is reportedly going to speak about the Intellectual Capital Partner-ship Program (ICAPP).

The campus was alive with anticipation and excitement Thursday when news hit that Gore was planning to come to campus. Several secret service officers were seen around campus with Public Relations Director, Cheryl Gaston and Dr. Earl Bagley, Dean of Students and Vice President of Student Affairs.

According to Gaston and Dr. Bagley the visitors were scouting for a place to hold the speech. Not much else was said about the visit.

Details about the speech, its time and exact location were not available Thursday.

U.S. Secretary of Education, Richard Riley, is expected to accompany Gore on the trip.

The audience for the program is by invitation only and will be attended by several CSU administrators, the SGA members and others.

ICAPP, reportedly the main focus of the speech, is a program designed to provide education for future employees of local companies such as Total System Services Inc. (TSYS). The program, which is state funded, promotes computer training for adults which will help in securing jobs for them in the future.

Unlike originally reported in the Columbus Ledger Enquirer, Gore will not be visiting the local Hannan Academy elementary school.

Gore will arrive at Lawson airfield at 9:40 a.m. today and will leave Lawson at around 1:30 p.m. He is due to speak in Atlanta, Georgia later today.

Cougars celebrate Peachbelt title

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

After clinching the Peach Belt’s regular season title, the Columbus State Cougars romped through the conference tournament to claim their third tournament championship and in the process, gained an automatic birth in the NCAA Div.II tournament.

Columbus State traveled to Savannah, Georgia with high spirits and high expectations. As the number one seeded team, the Cougars received a bye to start off.

The first team to face the Cougars were the Clayton State Lakers. The Lakers, coming off an opening win against Lander, had a mere 12-15 record and were swept by the Cougars in the regular season.

In the game, senior Calvin Hill scored 24 points, nineteen of those in the second half as the Cougars took the quarter final matchup 81-76. Clayton State kept the game close in the first half and were down by only four at the break. Hill took charge in the second half as the Cougars jumped out to a twelve point lead and never looked back. Larry Coates added 16 points and Quinton Peterson scored 12.

With the win Columbus advanced to face the third seeded Kennesaw State Owls. In that semi-final game the Cougars held Kennesaw to a pathetic 17 percent (6 of 34) shooting performance in the first half and took a very easy victory, 82-69, and advanced to the tournament final. Columbus had five players in double figures in scoring, led by Coates and Jermaine Taylor, each with 17.

The final barrier in the way of a CSU championship were the second seeded Armstrong Atlantic Pirates, who had an immediate advantage by playing on their court in front of their students and fans. The Pirates led by as many as eight in the second half but Columbus State fought their way back by firing a barrage of three pointers and taking a 74-60 come from behind victory.

Coates, who was held scoreless in the first half, hit several key three pointers and finished the game with 14 points. Hill scored 17 points and was named the PBAC tournament MVP.
