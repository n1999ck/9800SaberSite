HAPPY 40th BIRTHDAY CSU!!! - September 22, 1998

Columbus State University:
then and now

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief

In honor of 40 years of diligence and hard work by both the community and faculty, the Saber would like to present a special 40th Anniversary Issue. This issue will entail primarily articles, pictures, and ads from 1958 up until the present.

The Saber is the only organization on campus to maintain the same name since 1958. In this special issue, we hope to educate as well as recall memories from the past and look into the future.

Since 1958, Columbus State University, formerly known as Columbus College, has grown tremendously and accomplished great things in the process. The tradition lives on today and Columbus State University is surely “Building Something Great Since 1958.”

On May 14, 1958, the Board of Regents voted to establish a junior college in Columbus. This would be the 17th unit to the University System of Georgia and would be headed by President Thomas Y. Whitley. Whitley was a special assistant to the Board of Regents in coordinating the new junior college program.

Then on June 10, 1958, the Board of Regents officially names the junior college Columbus College according to the recommendation of the Board of Education of the Muscogee County School System.

On July 8th, the Board of Education of the Muscogee County School District used a $101,000 contract for the renovation and conversion of the Shannon Hosiery Mill into Columbus College. As time progressed, both growth and change began to occur regarding Columbus College.

On January 6, 1963, the formal opening of the ceremonies for the new campus were held following the beginning of classes on the new campus on January 3rd.

Other changes in the coming years included the change of mascot from a rebel to a cougar in 1970 and the erection of departmental buildings.

Today, Columbus State University is larger than ever containing 93 academic programs ranging from Associate to Masters degree programs. Campus life has flourished tremendously and the campus looks better than ever.

Columbus State University is certainly “Forging a New Era of Excellence!”
