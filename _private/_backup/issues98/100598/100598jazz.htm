CSU Jazz band takes the stage

The CSU Jazz Band will perform 3 p.m. Sunday, October 18 in the Fine Arts Hall auditorium. Performance numbers will be selected from “Dark Side of the Blues,” “I’m Beginning to see the Light,” “Girl Talk,” “Isfahan,” “The Bark of the Aardvark,” “Brazil,” “Count ‘Em,” “Gone but Not Forgotten” and “Blue Skies.”

In its 27th year, the band is the cornerstone of the jazz studies program in the Schwob Department of Music. Performances have reached more than 700 audiences in the Southeast and Midwest including honors at I I regional and national jazz festivals. Recent performances include the North Sea Jazz Festival (Netherlands), the Eurodisney Music Days Festival (France), and the Montreux and Brienz Jazz Festivals (Switzerland). The band repertoire covers the traditional literature of jazz pioneers such as Count Basic, Duke Ellington and Stan Kenton, as well as contemporary composers.

The musicians:

• saxophone - Alonzo Ward 11, David Hardegree, Nick Williams, John Sims, Jessica Haynes,
• trombone - Derrick White, John Kelley, Gerald Yarbray Jr., Mike Holt
• trumpet - Marci Patrick, James McLeod, Michael Gray, Melissa Hammonds
• piano - Tom Chadwick
• guitar - Matt Jenkins
• bass - Esar Simo
• drums - Eric Buchanan, Tommy Smith

Founder and director Paul Vander Gheynst has performed and recorded with Henry Mancini, Woody Herman, Nancy Wilson, The Temptations, Les Elgart, Buddy Greco, The Four Tops, Warren Covington, Gerry Mulligan and others. Vander Gheynst, professor and dean of the College of Arts and Letters, has been active locally as well, serving as the Springer Theatre musical director, Muscogee Sartoma Club vice president, and as a member of several boards.

Admission includes $5 adults and $3 ages 18 and under. Call the CSU Box Office at (706)569-3179.
