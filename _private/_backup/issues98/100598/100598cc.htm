CSU Cross Country: On the Move
by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor

HOME

Columbus State cross country teams gained more ground as the men finished 7th and the women finished 11th in the Georgia State Invitational on September 19th. The field was divided up between Division I, Division II, and had local track club members competing.

Geoffrey Rotich finished first out of all the colleges with a time of (25:21). Other top runners included: Abraham Bitok (25:56) who finished 15th and Titus Rugut who finished 39th with a time of (27:56). The meet was won by Kennessaw State who leads the Peach Belt.

In the women’s meet, the Lady Cougars were led by Danerys Encarnacion (22:26) who finished 58th, followed closely by Megan Grinder (22:36) in 61st, and Cheryl Mitchell (22:56) in 65th. Pia Ruotsalainen of Kennesaw State won individual medallist and helped her team capture first place.

The teams will compete October 3rd at the Georgia Collegiate Championship.
