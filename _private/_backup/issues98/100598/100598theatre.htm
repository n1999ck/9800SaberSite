Columbus State University’s Theatre Department presents Eric Overmyer’s On the Verge, or as the playbook subtitles it, “The Geography of Yearning.” The dinner theatre will run October 1-4 and 8-11 in the experimental theatre in Fine Arts Hall.

Hazel Hall, chair of the department of theatre, directs this all-CSU production. Actors include Columbus senior Jennifer Todd as Mary; Jacquelynne Jordan, a freshman from Lawrenceville, playing Alex; and Molly Henderson, a senior from Eufaula, Ala., playing Fanny. Jason Home, a senior from Columbus, and freshman Gerald Gibson from Alpharetta, play multiple roles in the show.

In 1888, three female adventurers set out to explore the Antipodes. They have made treks before as part of an exploring club, but always with an entire ensemble of assistants. This time they go out on their own. No sooner are they under way than strange things begin to happen, such as finding things that none of them packed. But this is only the beginning. Encountering strange characters and unusual situations, they ultimately find themselves in the 1950’s. Overmyer’s play is a celebration of discovery laced with liberal doses of one-ups-manship as the explorers try to outdo each other. The time travel scenario lends itself to a delightful script full of amusing word play.

Alan King, assistant professor, designed the set and lighting for the production. The action takes place on varying levels of a platform. As the adventurers visit new locales, projection screens provide background scenes.

Assistant professor Steven Graver’s costuming may well steal the show. From bustled, khaki safari costumes to Marilyn Monroe gowns of the 50’s, he dresses the cast in period clothing that creatively plays to the dialogue.
