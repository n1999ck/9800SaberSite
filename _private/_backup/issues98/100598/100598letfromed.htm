Letter from the Editor
by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief

HOME

Student involvement is a major issue on this campus as far as I can see.

As a junior, and having experienced a lot in my last three years here at Columbus State University, I have seen the importance of involvement. I cannot stress enough how involvement has changed the way I have enjoyed my college experience.

A student is more likely to stay in school if he or she gets involved in an organization and stays active within it. There are plenty of activities to take part in if students will just take the initiative to get involved.

There are several key reasons why a person should get involved in student organizations. The main reason is the networking advantages as well as the responsibility and experience one obtains.
I would like to encourage every student on campus to get involved in at least one organization on campus. It has made a huge difference in my college experience and I have benefited from my involvement in many ways.

As Editor of the Saber, I would like to encourage more students to get involved with the school newspaper. There are a couple of staff writer positions open as well as a position for a columnist. If you have any experience or would like to voice your opinion, feel free to stop by the office.

I would also like to see more responses to the paper. Take a chance to voice your opinion in our reader poll or write a letter to the editor. Both the staff and I would love to hear your opinions regarding issues faced on the campus of Columbus State University.

Take a stand and voice your opinion!
