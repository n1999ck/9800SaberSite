Pinhead - Daring to be different
by Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor

HOME

His objective is clear. He wants to make a connection with a major label in order to get national distribution of his music. Why not? He’s already made a connection with a large fan base and Columbus, in general. I predict that the major label connection will come soon.

He goes by the name of Pinhead, and is backed up by the musicians known as The Hellraisers. The Hellraisers are Esab Da Insain, Johnny Baps, and Galifea.

Pinhead, born Johnny Wesley II, attended high school at Kendrick here in Columbus. He became interested in rapping in 1985 at a local talent show.

“It was just a hobby. I became more serious about music in the last three years,” says Wesley. He has taken his creativity and stretched over into several other aspects of the entertainment business.

Pinhead has developed his own record label and clothing line. The record label, Flipnflava Records is backed by Pinhead and other people from the Columbus area. Flipnflava simply means versatile, having many styles, original and different.

The rapp style that Pinhead performs is called “Split Personality”, and quite frankly, I like it. I never thought those words would come from me about a rapp song or artist, but I like it.

The album is scheduled to be called “Skillz Dat Killz,” and has no official release date as of yet. The single “Fire And Funk” is available in several local stores already.

The style of rapp sounds as if there are several people singing at once, but it is all Pinhead. It’s like having two or three artists in one person.

If the album is anything like the single, then it is going to be good. The song is catchy. After I first heard it last week, I caught myself singing it all day - well, most of it, because I’ll never remember all of the words.

You can pick the single up at Blockbuster Music on Macon Road or at World Music on St. Mary’s Road. And hey, lets all help out and call the local radio stations and request Pinhead’s “Fire And Funk.” It’s about time these stations play something good anyway.

One other thing caught my eye about this guy. When he came in to Powertel last week to conduct other business (I work there), he gave me a press kit with some information I had asked him for.

It is what appears to be his motto, of sorts, or something like that. It is printed on every sheet in the press kit- “Daring to be different is the key to longevity.”

I’m not sure exactly how far Pinhead will make it in the music business, but with the overflow of gangster rapp and out right thug, shoot everybody rapp crapp, it is nice to see somebody like Johnny “Pinhead” Wesley II arrive on the scene. His music is a breath of fresh air for the rapp scene and personally I hope he gets heard.
