REM returns to previous style
by Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor

HOME


>> REM will be hitting stores with their new album on October 27. The new effort, titled “Up”, is their first project since the departure of drummer Bill Berry.

The album, the band’s eleventh, has fourteen tracks, and is said to be a departure from the style of their 1996 release, “New Adventures in Hi-Fi.” Inside word on the internet is that “Up” picks up where “Automatic for the People” left off in 1992.

I have mixed feelings about this, because I think the “Hi-Fi” album is excellent and one of the band’s best. Of course, album sales reflected that most fans have a different opinion than mine, but who cares. It is still a great album.

Don’t get me wrong, if “Up” picks up where the band left off in 1992, then we are in store for another great album. Think about it though, when was the last time that REM put out a bad album? Hint - the answer is never.

Michael Stipe, Mike Mills, and Peter Buck are not planning on touring to support the new album, which is probably a good idea considering the hectic way the last tour went.

>> Alanis Morissette’s new album, “Supposed Former Infatuation Junkie” is set to be released on November 3. The follow-up to her 1995 debut, “Jagged Little Pill,” has some big shoes to fill, considering that “JLP” sold over 15 million copies.

She worked with producer, co-writer and musician Glen Ballard again on this album, and the final project is expected to be as good, if not a step up from her previous effort. I’ll believe it when I hear it.

>> There are several albums hitting stores a little sooner than REM and Morissette that are worth checking out. On October 6, we will see new stuff from Cake, Enuff Z’Nuff, Less Than Jake, Depeche Mode Greatest Hits, and a new video from Widespread Panic.

The Widespread video comes with a four song live EP, and I don’t know why I mentioned Less Than Jake - like anybody cares.

October 20 brings us “A Little South of Sanity,” which is the new live double album from Aerosmith. Also being released that day are the double album from Ozzy Osbourne Black Sabbath Reunion, the Frankie Goes To Hollywood Greatest Hits package, and a new album by Vanilla Ice. Yes, I said Vanilla Ice.

October 27 rolls in a new album from Bryan Adams, “Up” from REM, a greatest hits package with a few new tracks from Motley Crue, and what appears to be a Christmas album from Cyndi Lauper.

Anything from Cyndi Lauper will be good, and personally I’m looking forward to the new stuff from Motley Crue.

Along with Morissette on November 3, we get a live album from 311, the b-sides album from Oasis, and a greatest hits from Skid Row. It’s almost like being in the 80’s again.

>> I picked up the album by Eagle-Eye Cherry, “Desireless,” last week and I must say, I was impressed. I thought he would be just another one hit wonder, but apparently the talent runs in the family.
Eagle-Eye is the son of the jazz great Don Cherry and you 80’s/early 90’s music fans will remember his sister, Neneh Cherry.

Eagle-Eye talked to MTV about his new hit single, “Save Tonight” last week: “It’s about a bittersweet time, it’s really about the perfect evening, with the candlelight and the wine and the fireplace. And in turn if you have this perfect evening you can probably survive a period of being apart. Savoring the moment, and maybe a miracle will happen and tomorrow won’t even come. It’s also about one of those nights where you don’t want the break of dawn to arrive.” How depressing. It’s a good album anyway.

>> Sheryl Crow released her new album, “The Globe Sessions,” last week, but I haven’t listened to all of it yet to tell if I really like it. It sounds more like a blues/country album than a rock album so far.

>> This is the part of the article I’ve been waiting to write all week. Guess who screwed up again? You guessed it - Ol’ Dirty Bastard got himself kicked out of a Berlin hotel last week.

What is being described by officials as “lewd behavior” is translated to mean “hanging off the balcony naked” by witnesses. These reports were taken off the internet, so lets remember he’s innocent until proven guilty, but how many times can this idiot hide under that escape clause?

I think he’s just plain out guilty, but at least this time he didn’t steal anything. Two weeks ago ODB was arrested after threatening security officers at Hollywood’s House of Blues. Just add that to the list of crimes.

Look at the bright side, if he stays in jail, then he can’t put out albums. If this is the best he can behave, I say let him, and maybe we will all get lucky and next time he will fall.
