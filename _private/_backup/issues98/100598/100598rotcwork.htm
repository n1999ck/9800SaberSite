ROTC - all work and play
by Angel Jackson / Staff Writer

HOME

The ROTC program has been on the Columbus College Campus for over twenty five years. The purpose of the program is to train students to become officers in the U.S. Army.

Lieutenant Kernel Gery Truitt, Professor of Military Science, is responsible for the ROTC program here at Columbus State University. Truitt explains the many benefits of becoming a part of the ROTC program. “The program gives the Cadets a higher sense of responsibility. It’s the only program that guarantees the students a job right out of college in a field of their choice.”

Truitt is proud to be a part of something that gives students something to look forward to.

This past summer at Advanced Camp, the CSU Cadets finished third of all the Cadets from the Universities in Florida, Georgia, Panama and Puerto Rico. Cadet Dan Wood achieved the highest score of over 3,000 in the regime. There are 49 Cadets currently in the ROTC program and the plan is to double in size within the next two years.

The ROTC program also offers scholarships to the students, and for students who sign on in the program as Juniors and Seniors, receive monthly pay.

Master Sergeant Davis, Principle Drill Instructor and Operations NCO, oversees all physical training. He teaches a physical fitness class for the Cadets. His job is to develop and train the Cadets.

Davis stated, “the class is open to any student at Columbus State University. There is no obligation to join the program. Some people just come out for the physical fitness.”

The newest member to the ROTC staff, Captain Irvin Pete, is responsible for recruiting ROTC participants. He also recruits CSU students from the Fort Benning Army station through a program called Green to Gold. People who are currently on active duty in the Army and want to become officer can come to CSU and join the
ROTC program and also receive a college education.

Although ROTC may not be for everybody, it is very beneficial. Unlike most students may think, ROTC is not all work. Besides learning quality leadership skills like first aid, land navigation skills, and water survival skills, the ROTC program has a host of other activities that allows the students to take it easy and become more family oriented. Some of these activities include a party once a month, fund raising, a Spring formal, and visits to the Civil War battlefields.
