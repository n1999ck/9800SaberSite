Monday, September 28, 1998

40th Anniversary
a success

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief

As Columbus State University celebrated it’s 40th Birthday last Tuesday, many gathered to engage in this momentous occasion.

The celebration was held at the clocktower and both students, faculty and members of the community joined in the party. The celebration was definitely one to remember and Columbus State University certainly made this a historic event.

Dr. Frank Brown, President of Columbus State University, and Mayor Bobby Peters both shared speeches regarding the University and the 40th Anniversary Celebration. The atmosphere was excellent. Both the campus and community gathered to partake in the events that were taking place on the lawn in front of the clocktower.

The entertainment was excellent as well. Everyone in attendance was able to engage in many activities such as sumo wrestling, boxing, and rock climbing. Others enjoyed the antics of Jeff Greenfield, who made his second appearance here at Columbus State University. Jeff is a chiropractor turned magician who entertained the crowd in every way possible.

The Columbus State University Cheerleaders led the crowd in cheers while the CSU Dance Team baffled the crowd with their talent and beauty. The afternoon event was three hours of togetherness and excitement for both Columbus State University and the community as well.

As nightfall approached the campus the celebration continued. The festivities began at six o’clock and continued until ten. The atmosphere was just as great if not greater. The prices of food and drinks were set at 1958 prices and the music was turned toward the same time period. The crowd became very involved with the dancing scene and before long everyone was involved in the party.

The party continued into the night with an appearance by the Village People, but the main event had not appeared.

Finally, the Swingin’ Medallions led the crowd in a great show to finish off the 40th Anniversary Celebration. Fireworks concluded this historic day and managed to light up the faces of the crowd and campus at Columbus State University.
