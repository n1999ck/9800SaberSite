Campus Candid: First College President, Dr. T.Y. Whitley

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
Dr. Thomas Y. Whitley, first President of Columbus College, became widely known as one who fostered the warm and effective relationship between the college and the surrounding community.

Whitley began his long and productive 21- year tenure on May 14, 1958. In this time he changed the somewhat junior college into a complex senior college which offered graduate courses.

Dr. Whitley was a native of Irwin County, Georgia and received his A.B. and M.E.d. degrees from the University of Georgia. He later received his Ed.D. from the University of Texas.

Prior to becoming the first President of Columbus College, he achieved many great things. He was a Teacher-Coach at Tate, Georgia, Dean of Students at South Georgia College, Co-ordinator of the Junior College development program for the Regents of the University System of Georgia, and Vocational Advisor for the U.S. Veterans Administration.

His hobby was known as Columbus College and he was known as "A Man on the Job!" His hard work and efforts exemplify what Columbus State University stands for today!
