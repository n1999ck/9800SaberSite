A look at the last 40 years in athletics

by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor
The Columbus State athletic program has come a long way in forty years and the future looks even brighter.

When the basketball team began playing at then Columbus College, there was not even a gym on campus. The team would practice at Jordan High School and play games at Columbus High School. The mascot and colors were also different. The team was known as the Rebels and the colors were gold and gray.

Changes started taking place on April 14, 1965, when the college was made a four year institution. The Rebels were coached by Frank “Sonny” Clements and the team finished the season with record of 15-8.The next major change did not occur until April 17, 1970, when the students voted for the current nickname.

Over the years the program has made many division changes. After becoming a four year school the team competed in the NAIA, then moved up to Division II in 1973, and even had a one year stint in Division I. However, because of a decline in student enrollment the Cougars were forced to drop out and become independent. In 1990 the team joined the Peach Belt Conference where they continue today.

Currently the team is coached by Herbert Greene who has been at CSU since the early eighties and has led the team to 286 wins against 159 losses.

Although a majority of the early athletic history centered around the basketball program, there were other sports. Baseball began about the same time as basketball and has seen prominent years. The golf team has been competing since the 1960’s and has won six National Championships. The softball team has been competing since the early eighties and is among the top teams in Division II. Tennis and cross country are other sports that have been around for a number of years.

With a strong basis, the athletic programs are headed in the right direction for the future. The next big step will be the construction of the multi-purpose field house which will provide first class facilities for athletes and students alike.
