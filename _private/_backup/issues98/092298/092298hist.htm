History professor recalls last 40 years at CSU

by Angel Jackson / Staff Writer
Mrs. Mary Jane Watkins, History Professor, had her first affiliation with Columbus State University in 1958, when she came to watch her sweetheart play basketball. Watkins began her education at CSU in 1960. She received her Bachelors in 1970 and started teaching at CSU in 1971.

Watkins describes Columbus State University as liberating. “Thinking back over my years of teaching, the CSU experience has changed the lives of many students and opened many doors.”

Professor Watkins recalls the many changes she’s seen come about at CSU. She remembers when all women had to wear dresses or skirts and blouses, registration was done on cards and by hand, and the CSU mascot was a Rebel. She also states that the campus has become more diverse with# global students as opposed to an all white college with only Georgia and Alabama students.

Other than being in the first graduating class at Columbus State University, Watkins’ achievements include the Educator of the Year Award in 1986 and the Thomas L. Willey Award in 1993 for the most distinguished alumni.

When asked how she feels about the 40th Anniversary of CSU, Watkins pauses, lets out a sigh and says, “This 40th anniversary is one of the many milestones, and I’m proud to be a part of it.”
