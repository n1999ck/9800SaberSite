The new and improved cafeteria

by Pedro Lopez, Jr. / Photo Editor of 1992 Saber Staff

This article was taken from the February 20, 1992 edition of the Saber.
During the last two or three quarters, since the new cafeteria management took over, they have been getting a lot of flack, a lot of negative fed that could be construed as throwing stones at the sinner, “Dave the Brave,” for causing such upheaval and despair amongst our talented, intelligent, hard working college students, staff and faculty, and especially our athletic department, those wild and crazy guys with ferocious appetites. “Burn him at the stake,” the people yelled.

Being David Meyers, the cafeteria manager after two or three quarters, and having to take all kinds of abuse, being called names I wouldn’t call my enemies, he has survived through it all. There were quite a few attempts—meetings, forums, open hearings—to help resolve the problem of the cafeteria in general. The main complaints were: not enough food, variety, taste, time of business, and the way they operate the cafeteria. I’ve been through most of the hearing, meetings, etc., but, due to my position here, I speak and listen to hundreds of students daily and I hear the good, bad, and the ugly.

To say the least of you’ve come a long way, baby. “Dave the Brave,” I just knew you could do it. it took a lot of guts. Patients and understanding to deal with all of us and come up with a compromise for the best interest of all of us, especially those obligated to eat here. I feel that since there isn’t any mobs or frantic college students trying to burn down the cafeteria, and no complaints, David always has an open-door policy. He says it’s been quiet in the front!

This battle is practically over as far as I am concerned. Believe me, I know about “food.” The changes that David Meyers has brought due to recommendations- on our behalf, or what the company allows him to do and what he does by himself, has increased the questions and increased the stillness of the pond.

“Dave the Brave” would like to thank everyone who helped make your cafeteria a better place of service to you all. I am going to list the changes and ideas that have been implemented and that are going to be implemented. So, listen up and then you can’t say I didn’t know, or nobody told me."

New students or clients can choose within specific guidelines. Pick one entree and three side items. (e.g., Pick one hot dog, fries, desert, salad, vegetables. You can pick two hot dogs, one fries, desert, or salad. You can choose two of the same item if you order one entree and something else [that’s freedom of choice]).

• Before, you were forced to eat what they told you or gave you. Now you can eat a balanced meal. The regular meal they may offer, mix it, or eat junk food, tried stuff, or whatever you choose.

• The lift of restriction on what to eat or how has spun a more self- conscious and positive attitude towards the cafeteria, especially for the next incoming students.

• The external changes in the cafeteria and face life has made a more pleasant place to work in, not dinky and dingy. Believe me, they put a lot of work into control of bugs and keeping health standards to a very, very high level.

• They sell a cup in the book store, that whatever you put in it is 39 cents. Get it. It’s a good deal if you drink a lot of coffee.

• The salad bar by the ounce has as much as you want and you pay by the twenty ounce.

• He’s working on getting a new stock of ice creams, yogurt, and frozen deserts to sell by cups, half pints, or pints.

• They sell cakes made fresh while you wait. $8.50 for a plain and $9.50 for a decorated one. I am quite sure there is someone out there you want to celebrate with.

• Howard Hall’s improvements are to include hot dog, chili, and similar programs.

• Restructuring of prices-some went up a little and some came down a little.

• They are now opened from 7:00 in the morning to 7:00 in the evening. You have twelve hours to eat something.

• For all you lazy people, they stopped serving breakfast between 10:00 - 10:30. Be there or be square.

• The biscuits, waffles, pancakes, and pizza are made fresh every day.

Well, these thirteen reasons seem to be enough for now. Don’t you think so? The athletes are the biggest chow hounds and they are not complaining. We, the students, appreciate your help and concern for our health and sanity. Thanks to everybody who had anything to do with the improvements albeit there are still improvements to be made. Be patient and work with David. Talk to him. Tell him what you feel. That’s what he’s there for.
