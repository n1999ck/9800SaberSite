 A Summer in Spain

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

Columbus State University students have a chance to visit Spain. Similar to the successful trip last summer to Mexico, the Spain trip gives CSU students a chance to learn from and enjoy another culture.

One student who went on the Mexico trip, Sharon Langford, says, “It was the most intensive learning experience of my life, which I will not soon forget.” Langford says she came away with a lot from the trip. “You have no choice but to learn the language. You’re calling taxies, ordering in restaurants and shopping in markets,” she says.

Dr. Alyce Cook, CSU Spanish professor says the Mexico trip was a success and she expects the same success in Spain. “The group of students that went to Mexico learned as much as I’ve ever seen any group learn from an experience like this,” Cook said in a Spanish 102 class last week. Dr. Cook will be traveling with the students to Spain.

The objective of the trip, according to literature available through the CSU Language and Literature Department, is to provide quality education in the classroom as well as outside the classroom. The philosophy is to go outside the classroom.

Students who go on the trip will spend the month in Salamanca, Spain, a historical city where Christopher Columbus studied. The city is a small university city with a mid- evil feel and is called the cradle of Spanish education. Salamanca is one of the oldest university cities in Europe and its Plaza Mayor has been recognized by many as one of the most beautiful in Spain and Europe.

The cost of the trip is $3700.00. The cost includes room and board with a Spanish family, airfare, CSU course payment, orientation and three meals a day. Also included in the cost of the trip are several day trips to nearby cities and three night hotel accommodations in Madrid. Other cities to be visited are Escorial, Avila, Segovia and La Aberca.

A limited amount of scholarships are available. Also, other loans and financial aid is available. Eligible students can apply financial aid such as Pell grants, HOPE scholarships and others to the courses taken Spain. Students who pass the course will receive 12 credit hours.

Cook says to raise money for last year’s trip, students held a car wash which proved unsuccessful. However, she also says that one student who attended the Mexico trip actually raised over $800.00 selling candy bars. Cook warns students not to get caught up in deciding you can not go because you are married, have children or a job.

“Some employers actually encouraged students to go,” Cook says. According to Cook some employers offered time off and worked around the trip schedule. Cook says to make up your mind that you are going to go, and the money can be found somehow.

The trip will be from June 29 until July 30 of this year. Classes are held from 9 a.m. to 1 p.m. daily. Between classes students are free to travel on their own. Passports should be made well in advance. Anyone interested going one the trip should contact Dr. Cook at the Language and Literature Department.
