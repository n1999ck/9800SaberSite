Kappa Sigma plays huge role in community

by Greg Price / Staff Writer
This year, the Kappa Sigma Fraternity at CSU has taken on the lofty challenge of assisting the city of Columbus in as many ways as possible.

Kappa Sigma has long been a leader on campus and in the Columbus community for providing activities for the citizens of Columbus, but now, more than ever, these men are showing the people of Columbus, Georgia what they are made of.

Since fall quarter of 97’, the members of Kappa Sigma have put in well over 140 hours of community service helping organizations such as the Ronald McDonald House, St. Mark United Methodist Church, Columbus State’s Halloween Carnival, Help the Hooch, and projects at 30th Avenue School, just to name a few.

Recently, several brothers spent a Saturday morning cleaning up a one mile stretch of road along Manchester Expressway through the adopt-a-mile program. The program is sponsored through the Keep Columbus Beautiful Commission, a bureau who’s efforts are designed to

keep the streets and highways of Columbus clean. The brothers of Kappa Sigma have a designated area on Manchester Expressway, just past the Warm Springs Connector.

Philantropy Chairman, Kevin Breemes, stated, “We take great pride in helping the community. So many people have a false impression of what fraternities are all about, and we are hoping that through our efforts the community can have a chance to see that Kappa Sigma is very community oriented, and our activities go far beyond that of others.”

Along with it’s activities in the community, the brothers of Kappa Sigma still manage to find the time to take part in campus activities and several members hold high positions in other organizations on campus. Just over a year old, the Kappa Sigma fraternity is well on it’s way to a successful future at Columbus State University.
