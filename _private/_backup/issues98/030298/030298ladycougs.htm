Lady Cougars fall just short

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor
The Columbus State Lady Cougars rolled into Savannah as the team to beat in the Peach Belt Conference Tournament. After all, they were 16-0 against Peach Belt teams during the season, had easily taken the regular season title, and were the number one seed team. The Lady Cougars had a great big bulls-eye on their backs and everyone was gunning for them.

The Lady Cougars faced off with the Armstrong Atlantic Lady Pirates to begin their tournament run. CSU played sluggishly for most of the first half and shot an icy 1-12 over a six minute stretch.

Columbus went on a crucial 9-2 run late in the half and took a 35-34 lead into the locker room. The Lady Cougars dominated the second half as their pressure defense began to wear down Armstrong. Columbus forced the Lady Pirates into 31 turnovers in the game.

Sylvia Allen took charge in the second half and scored nine of her game high 14 points in the second half. Senior Que Gilliam added 12 points in the 81-71 victory.

The Lady Cougars moved on to face the Augusta State Lady Jaguars in the semi-final matchup, a game which turned into one of the most amazing comeback victories in school history.

The Lady Cougars could never get their game on track and appeared on their way to a heartbreaking loss. Columbus was held to 36 percent shooting and found themselves down by nine, 42-33, at the half.

The second half opened with the Lady Jaguars still dominating and leading by 21 points at one point. Augusta led by 17 points with just over four minutes to play, and that’s when all hell broke loose for the Lady Jaguars. Augusta found several of it’s top offensive weapons in serious foul trouble and they had to be benched.

While the Lady Jaguars were falling apart at the seams, Columbus State was fighting back. After realizing the threes weren’t falling, the Lady Cougars went to the inside.

Gilliam scored 10 points in only a minute to cut the Lady Jaguar lead to eight with two minutes to play. Columbus’ defense held Augusta scoreless for the games final 2:40.

After cutting the lead to six, Reagan Housch nailed a three pointer to make the score 86-83. CSU forced a turnover and pushed the ball down the court. A three point attempt by Housch was partially blocked but the defender was called for a foul with just 31 ticks remaining on the clock. Housch found herself at the free throw line shooting three free throws.

After converting on the first two to pull CSU within one point, Housch, after withstanding an Augusta timeout, calmly sank the game tying bucket to send the game into overtime. The overtime period saw Columbus State complete it’s unbelievable comeback by taking the lead and holding on for an improbable 97-96 victory.

The shocking win advanced CSU into the championship game against the 11th ranked Patriots of Francis Marion.

Francis Marion boasted a 26-2 record and only one loss in the conference, that loss came at the hands of Columbus State. Francis Marion led the whole game and held off another Lady Cougar rally to take a 71-67 victory.

Toshcia Campfield was held to a season-low two points and the Patriots mastered a full court press which caused an average of 25 turnovers.

The loss ended the Lady Cougars 18-game conference unbeaten streak. Despite the loss the Lady Cougars are still hopeful of an NCAA tournament bid.
