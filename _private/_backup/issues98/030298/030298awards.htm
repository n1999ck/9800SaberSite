Awards earned and records fall

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor
The entire Peach Belt Athletic Conference tournament was a success for Columbus State University (CSU). Along with the success of both men and women’s basketball teams, two other “teams” were rewarded in Savannah.

In the cheerleading competition, Columbus State finished second behind Georgia College & State University who clinched their eighth title.

Also finishing second was the CSU Pep Band. The band, who is seen at most CSU basketball games, were also defeated by GC&SU. The band performed a rather amusing segment where one member ran around shaking a tambourine, and flailing his arms, imitating a televangelist as the band played energetic gospel music.

In winning the regular season and tournament championships, several CSU Cougars claimed awards. Senior point-guard Larry Coates was named the Peach Belt Athletic Conference Player of the Year, becoming the first Cougar to earn such an honor, and was named to the first All-PBAC team. Coates averaged 16.5 points a game and tied the CSU record for assists in a season with 242.

Also receiving honors for the Cougars was senior Calvin Hill, who was named PBAC tournament MVP. Hill scored 53 points in the three Cougar games including 24 against Clayton State.

On the women’s side, after leading his team to a 22-4 record, 16-0 in the conference, head coach Jay Sparks was named as the Peach Belt Athletic Conference Coach of the Year. Sparks, who is in his ninth season at CSU also won the award in 1994. Also for the Lady Cougars,

seniors Que Gilliam and Toschia Campfield were named the to the first team All-PBAC squad.
