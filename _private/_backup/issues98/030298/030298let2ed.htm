Letter to the Editor
Dear Editor,

I would like to bring to your attention and to the attention of the entire student body a question, this is Georgia, Where is the southern hospitality? I am a new student here this quarter and I realize there are over 60 organizations to get involved with but some have rules and regulations. Also, not everyone feels comfortable joining an organization to get involved due to other school responsibilities. Therefore, where do the lonely people go? I realize many students have local friends or mates that they go out with but some have none.

I met a young lady the other day and she was sitting alone in the Davidson Center. I talked with her enough to know that she often sits there alone and depressed. She said that she tried to get involved with others but she had very little response. She also said she was a shy type person. This girl is in counseling and suffers with very low self-esteem. This is the most shameful thing I have ever heard. In a city this large, full of possibilities, and a person must sit alone. I know that some of the organizations here try to have entertainment such as dating games but not all people will participate because they are shy or reserved. Should these people remain alone, sad, and depressed their entire college life? Here at college, classmates do not get the chance to really know each other and it is easy to see why this cycle takes place. But, it needs attention because it does not have to be this way. I have an idea that I would like to see take effect here. Some of the organizations of the school or a new one perhaps should create a “make-a-friend night” so that all the students could gather together for a movie and refreshments at least once a week in the Davidson center. I also feel that someone such as a “mixer” should see that even the shy people join in. People could make friends and possible dates. What do you think?

It is a shame to see anyone alone in a would full of people. There is also no excuse in it. People who are happy should share in their happiness to help make other people happy. Please see to it that the campus is made aware of the lonely, somewhere out there!
