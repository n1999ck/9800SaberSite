Smoking debate continues

by Heather Warren / Associate Editor

 
Smoking is a hot topic on campus these days. The issue is not whether people should smoke or not, it is the matter of the regulations regarding smoking and the controversy it causes when some do not know the rules.

There is a relatively new rule on campus that requires persons to be at least 20 feet from any building to smoke. The rule was signed into effect by Dr. Brown around five months ago, according to Public Safety officials.

The rule is new and not well known around campus. There are signs regarding smoking. One warning on various signs posted reads “please extinguish smoking materials here. “

The Davidson building has a similar sign about five feet away from the building. The Davidson building and other buildings have ashtrays about five feet away from the door.

Howard Hall and the Fine Arts Hall both have signs which discourage smoking near entrances, but ashtrays are available within 20 feet.

The smoking policy in the 1997-1998 Columbus State University Handbook states that “Smoking is prohibited in all buildings on campus.” Student Government Association and the Public Safety say the rule is no smoking in campus buildings and no smoking within twenty feet of a campus building.

Officer Enrique Degracia says he has only had to ask one or two students to step away from the doorway. “I feel that most everyone complies,” says Degracia.

Linda Hurst, president of Student Government Association, says that she feels very discriminated against, “If the law is written down it should be enforced and it is not being enforce.” However, Hurst feels the policy should not exist.

“I can understand them not wanting us to smoke inside,” Hurst says, but she feels restricting outside smoking is not necessary. “It is bad enough we have to stand in the cold or the heat. I don’t see how they can say we have to stand away from the building,” says Hurst.

This brings up the issue of the gazebos. Many smokers complain that the gazebos, which reportedly cost around $5000.00 each, leak. Hurst says the gazebos were put in place primarily for the use of smokers but not exclusively so.

Hurst says that Public Safety can not enforce the rule. She says that all they can do is ask people to step away from the door.

One student who wishes not to be named says, “I feel one person’s rights end where another’s begin. It is uncomfortable to have to walk through a cloud of smoke. I understand that smokers feel discriminated against but some people, like myself, have allergies or asthma and could avoid a great deal of discomfort by smokers simply stepping away from the doors that everyone must enter.”

Despite the controversy about smoking on campus, one thing is clear: the rules are difficult to sort out and confusing to both smokers and non-smokers.
