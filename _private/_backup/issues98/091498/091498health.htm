What is Health Weekly and why
are we running it?

by Jennifer Robinson / Staff Writer

Hopefully in the last issue everyone noticed “Health Weekly,” a brand new column of the Saber. This column will be a regular feature addressing health and wellness issues of interest to students. “Health Weekly” will be written by different members of the Student Nursing Association, a CSU student organization of nursing (Associate Degree and Bachelor Degree) and pre-nursing majors.

These members will be writing about health topics that relate specifically to students and young adults, general health topics, as well as the “happenings” of the Student Nursing Association. The purpose of this column is to inform readers about issues which can improve and protect your physical health. We also hope to educate students about the Student Nursing Association and what activities we participate and are involved in both on campus and in the Columbus community at large.

We are excited about writing health articles that interest CSU students, columns that provide you with information you find valuable. The Student Nursing Association would like to hear from you about future articles you would like to see in “Health Weekly”. E-mail your ideas to Jennifer Robinson through campus e-mail or call 569-1506. We want your input!

In the next issue James Underwood, the President of the Student Nursing Association, writes about a recent set of models the Senior Baccalaureate class bought for the Maternal/Child nursing department. These models are not like anything that you have seen in the Biology lab-this I promise you! To give you a hint, these models will assist nursing students in learning to determine how close to delivery a woman in labor is. Look for it in the next issue of The Saber.
