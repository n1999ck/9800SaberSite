Letter from the Editor

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
As the world awaits the decision upon whether the President should be impeached or not, what is the President thinking?

What started out as a straight out denial has turned out to be a nightmare for both the President's family and White House officials. If things could not get worse, the denial became a lie as Monica Lewinsky proved the affair with evidence of her own. DNA samples proved to match President Bill Clinton which forced him to admit on national television that he did have sexual relations with Miss Lewinsky.

However, Clinton states that these accusations were wrong. The President said the two did not have sex, but merely made sweet love the short time they were together. If you ask me, I feel as if he has begun to dig a deeper grave for himself. This not only made him look like he strayed, but made him seem as if he had no feelings for his family all together.

Ken Starr has submitted his report to the Judiciary House Committee which outlines a case for impeaching President Clinton on 11 grounds.

The first 445 pages of the Starr Report are being reviewed with an estimated 2,600 pages of supporting material still waiting to be reviewed. The biggest question is, What will be the final call? Will the President be impeached? Should the President be impeached? These are all answers that will eventually arise after the review of the Starr Report.
