College football kicks off

by Chris Anderson / Sports Editor
The opening week of college football brought some surprises and big disappointments for area schools.

Auburn was drilled 19-0 in a Thursday night game on ESPN. Quarterback Len Baird was given little opportunity to throw the ball and the running game was not much better. Virginia led at the half 6-0, and continued to add points in the second half in part to Auburn mistakes. The season does not get any easier as the Tigers face Ole Miss in Oxford.

The Georgia Bulldogs on the other hand had a much easier task in facing Kent. Freshman quarterback Quincy Carter threw for three touchdowns and finished with 256 yards as the Dogs cruised to a 56-3 victory. Georgia should face a tougher task this weekend when they travel to South Carolina.

Well, so much for making predictions as Georgia Tech suffered a 41-31 loss to Boston College. The defense had too many holes and the offense could not keep up with the pace. The Yellow Jackets will have one more tune up game against New Mexico State before opening ACC action against North Carolina.

Unlike Georgia Tech, Alabama had a successful campaign against BYU winning 38-31. The Tide was led by Shaun Alexander who ran for five touchdowns and was named SEC offensive player of the week. Another surprise was the passing of John David Phillips who appeared calm under pressure and had no interceptions. The Tide will play Vanderbilt in Birmingham.
