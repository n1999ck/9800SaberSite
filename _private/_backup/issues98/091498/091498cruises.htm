"Courtyard Cruises" offers increased safety

by Bridgett Brumbaugh / Staff Writer
Many students can relate to the uncomfortable feeling of walking to the dorms or to the parking lot late at night. A new escort service, which went into affect on campus September 8th, is aimed at helping students arrive safely to their dorm rooms and vehicles.

Known as “Courtyard Cruises”, the service provides students a ride, via golf cart, to Courtyard I or to any of the parking lots on campus from 7:00pm - 11:30pm Monday through Thursday. Students simply call 568-2022 and inform security of their location and the escort will pick them up. Those who reside at Courtyard 11 will be able to use the service once the city puts the sidewalk in.

Before this service, Campus Police would normally receive 4 or 5 escort requests a night. According to Jasper Cook, Chief of Public Safety, students would sometimes have to wait 10- 15 minutes for a ride. Cook continued by stating that since a student assistant has been hired solely for escorting students, the officers can now concentrate on their main job and students are not inconvenienced. However, the officers will continue to provide escorts when needed.

This is the first time a service like this has been in place at CSU as far as Mr. Cook knows. He and the Residence Life staff decided the escorting was greatly needed on campus with the increase of the student body as well as the many dorm students without vehicles. Improvements or changes to the program will be made as needed.
