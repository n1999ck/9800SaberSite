Men win key Homecoming match-up

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
A record crowd of 1,612 screaming fans saw the Columbus State men’s basketball team avenge an earlier loss to the second place Kennesaw State Owls recently.

The Cougars found themselves trailing the rival Owls 33-25 at halftime but came out of the locker room firing on all cylinders. Senior Larry Coates scored 17 of his game-high 23 points in the second half. Coates seemed to take over the game in the second half as he hit from every possible spot on the floor, including several monster three-point shots. Columbus was also boosted by a stellar performance from senior forward Will Dickerson. Dickerson pulled down 19 rebounds and scored ten points. Dickerson also set the Woodruff Gym crowd to its feet with a thunderous rejection of a potential dunk by Kennesaw. The victory extended Columbus’s home winning streak to 15.

Unfortunately for the Cougars, 15 games was as high as it would go. After a road victory over non-conference Morris Brown, the Cougars lost their second conference game of the season as the USC-Spartanburg Rifles spanked Columbus in front of a Woodruff Gym crowd of 879. Spartanburg jumped on the Cougars early as Columbus shot only 39 percent in the first half. The Rifles led by as many as 20 points in the second half before the Cougars began to make a charge. With 5:13 left in the game, Columbus cut the Spartanburg lead to only four on a Calvin Hill steal and layup. Spartanburg then responded with several three-point baskets to extend their lead. Columbus never folded but could never quite catch up as the Rifles took the game 68-64. With the loss, the 18th ranked Cougars fell to 18-4 overall and 7-2 in the Peach Belt.
