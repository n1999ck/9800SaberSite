I would like to say a few words about this school. I love CSU very much and I only wish the best for the University. However I would like to point out a concern. I hear many students that are frustrated because of the lack of support from their professors. It seems that some professors feel that students are here only for an education, period. That seems to be a very cold attitude towards the students and life. Sure life is cold sometimes, but if that is ones way of living then my frigid friend, you chose the wrong path in life. Maybe that professor should have picked a career where hope and happiness could be far from them.

Shouldn’t college be a way of expanding your mind as well as your state of being? Why do some professors become upset and resentful when the students have an event the sometimes interferes with their class? I totally support going to class on time and consecutively, but when the SPC, Greeks, SGA, or any other organization plans an event, professors sometimes refuse any leniency what-so-ever. Have we become so dis-attached to the students that we don’t remember when we are in college? There is a time and a place for work, and a time for “School Spirit”. It seems that we don’t have much school spirit. I really enjoyed the talent show the other night, but did you notice that their were only three or four faculty and staff members present? I think that they were asked to judge. It’s a shame to me. I love this school and I would hate to see it to just fade into just another place to get a degree. Now I realize that the problem is not with all the instructors, there are students who have other things to attend to like work; but it would be so much more enjoyable to teach a student that is happy to be here. These organizations are trying desperately to get things started on this campus for everyone to enjoy. So please lets work together to make this a school that’s fun to attend and educational; not just a job and a classroom. Let’s get to know each other as people that have something to offer each other. That’s where the learning begins...

-Friend of Columbus State University &The Student Body
