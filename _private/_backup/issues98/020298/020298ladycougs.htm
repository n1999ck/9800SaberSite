Loss doesn't slow Lady Cougars down

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
If anyone questioned the championship mentality of the Columbus State women’s basketball team, one need not look further than what transpired recently between Atlanta and Columbus.

The Lady Cougars dominance this season continued as Columbus took an 89-81 homecoming victory over the defending Peach Belt champions, Kennesaw State. Columbus used superior speed and skill to outplay a team that boasted some of the tallest players in the conference. CSU saw a 12-point lead dissappear but the Lady Cougars kept fighting and eventually took the lead back and coasted to victory. In the win, Columbus set two new school records. Going 38-45 at the free-throw line, the Lady Cougars broke the school record for free-throws made in a game and tied the record for free-throws attempted in a game. The win over Kennesaw only began the saga for the Lady Cougars.

After the win over Kennesaw, Columbus State, with a 14-3 record, traveled to Atlanta to face non-conference Morris Brown. In the game, the Lady Cougars found themselves not only down in score but facing major controversy as well. During the game Que Gilliam was benched after a flagrant foul and had to be disciplined by head coach Jay Sparks for her actions on the sideline. The Lady Cougars could not get their game on track and eventually were upset by the Wolverettes 86-81. For many teams who are used to success, an upset loss marred by controversy usually starts a downhill spiral that ends with great disappointment. The Lady Cougars proved, however, that they are not like most teams.

With Gilliam sidelined for the entire first half for her actions against Morris Brown, Columbus State rebounded from it’s loss to destroy the USC-Spartanburg Lady Rifles 104-49 at Woodruff Gym. The 55-point win broke the Peach Belt Conference record for largest margin of victory. The Lady Cougars used a vicious full-court press to hold the Lady Rifles to a meager 17 points at halftime. Gilliam came off the bench in the second half to lead the Lady Cougars with 15 points. Center Carrie Long and forward Tocshia Campfield each added 11 points. The defensive press that Columbus State applied produced 38 Lady Rifle turnovers, 28 of those on steals. The victory enabled the Lady Cougars to stay perfect in the Peach Belt Conference at 9-0, and raise their overall record to 15-4.
