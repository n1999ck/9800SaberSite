It's about time

by Tommy Peavy / Sports Editor

 
Throughout my days here at Columbus State I have had the pleasure of attending many a sporting event on campus. Although fun and entertaining, I was always bothered by the lack of support shown by the student body at these games. The trend has continued this year as only a handful of students attend basketball games. Even when they do occupy Woodruff Gym, they tend to sit quietly and watch the game, cheering only for a monster slam-dunk or an arm-breaking rejection.

At the recent homecoming game against Kennesaw State, I finally got to see what I’ve been waiting for: someone to get obnoxious.

The members of the Sigma Nu fraternity brought to Woodruff Gym many of the sights and sounds of a large university sporting event. With members decked out in traditional shirt and tie and hovering around a grill at a tailgate party, it almost seemed to bring the same pre-game atmosphere one would find at a University of Georgia or Auburn football game. During the games, the members of Sigma Nu made their presence known in a big way with their bead filled aluminum cans. The Sigma Nu members got so loud at one point during the men’s game that the Kennesaw State coach protested to the referee. After being asked to not use the artificial noise makers, the Sigma Nu members migrated across the court and sat directly behind the Kennesaw bench where they used their natural noise makers to throw taunts and insults at the rival Owls. I don’t know how much of an affect the loud cheering had on the game, but when an opposing team walks over to their bench and has their attention focused on the crowd, it’s bound to have some impact.

Now I’m not promoting the use of artificial noise makers, they are against NCAA rules, but the school spirit shown by these students was classic. The kind of cheering section formed by Sigma Nu at the game was on a smaller scale to the “Cameron Crazies” at Duke or the “Cliff Dwellers” at Auburn. It was obvious that these men were genuinely having fun cheering on the Cougars and taunting the opponent. It’s really ashamed that more students and student organizations won’t pull together to support the teams here at CSU. One thing is for sure, the Sigma Nu fraternity did a stand up job at the Kennesaw State game supporting the team and more groups need to follow their example in the future.
