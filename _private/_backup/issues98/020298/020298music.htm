Music Bits: Video big news in music this week

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

 
The American Music Awards took place last week. Babyface claimed the Best Male Artist prize, with Celine Dion taking the award for Best Female Artist. The Best Alternative act went to Bush.

As if we didn’t have enough scandal going on already, Bret Micheals and Pamela Anderson Lee are causing some more. The Poison frontman is going to court this week to try to put a stop to the release of a sex video of himself and Pamela Anderson Lee.
A copy of the video is in the hands of an organization called the Internet Entertainment Group, and they plan on releasing it to the public as early as this week.

IEG claims to have retrieved the copy from a friend of Bret Micheals, but they are not giving out any names. Micheals claims that the only other copy other than the one he has is owned by Pamela Anderson Lee.

Micheals has spent nearly $100,000 so far to keep the tape off the market, and threatens to sue IEG for $90,000,000 in damages, if they release the tape.

A sex video of Pamela Anderson Lee and Motley Crue drummer, Tommy Lee, has grossed over $1,000,000 on the home video market and internet. In the midst of this court battle, Poison will go back into the studio to record a new album this spring. Vocalist Bret Micheals, bassist Bobby Dall, and drummer Rikki Rocket will be joined once again by original guitar player C.C. Deville.

Matt Cameron, former drummer for Soundgarden, has recorded seven new songs with the Smashing Pumpkins. Cameron recently told fans on an internet chat that , “I have no plans of joining the Smashing Pumpkins.”
He says the recordings were just on a friendly basis and he has no idea which ones or if any will show up on the next Smashing Pumpkins album. He went on to announce plans to complete second albums with both his side projects, Wellwater Conspiracy and Hater.

Oasis has made it three weeks into there United States tour with virtually no fighting or arguing between the two Gallagher brothers.

According to fans that recently attended shows, Liam Gallagher has been joking and playing with both the crowds and his bandmates. After closing the show in San Francisco with a ten minute version of the song “Champaign Supernova,” Noel Gallagher told fans, “Thanks for listening.”

Oasis will perform at the Fox Theater in Atlanta on February 8.

KMFDM looks to be the only big release this week, with there remix versions of Megalomaniac.

If you are looking for new music to listen to that is already in stores you might want to check out a band called Babybird. Fans of the band U2 should enjoy Babybird, because there album “Ugly Beautiful” is very much in the U2 style of music. Another excellent new album is “Monsoon” by Caroline’s Spine.

Everclear has announced that they may be adding a Spring tour to there already year long tour. I highly recommend catching their show if at all possible. Everclear performed at the Cotton Club in Atlanta several months ago, and showcased their new album, “So Much For The Afterglow” flawlessly.

On the local front, LeAnn Rimes will be performing at the Civic Center here in Columbus on February 7.

Pearl Jam fans take note: On Monday February 2 at 11:45, local music store Riff’s will re-open for a midnight sale of Pearl Jam’s new album, “Yield”. Riff’s is located on 2723 Manchester Expressway.
