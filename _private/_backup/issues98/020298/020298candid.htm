Campus Candid: Tommy Peavy

by Heather Warren/ Associate Editor

 
Columbus State University Sophomore Tommy Peavy spends his summers traveling all over North America. He is a member of a drum and bugle corps. The last four summers he was a member of the Southwind Drum and Bugle Corps. This year he is a part of the Madison Scouts Drum and Bugle Corps which finished 4th in the world last summer. Madison Scouts Drum and Bugle Corps is based in Madison, Wisconsin.

Peavy had to audition to become a member of the Madison Scouts Drum and Bugle Corps. He was selected out of about thirty people. He has to fly to Wisconsin one weekend a month for rehearsal. During that weekend they practice thirteen hours on Saturday and seven hours on Sunday before flying home. In May he will have to move to Wisconsin to participate in daily rehearsals.

There are many corps in the United States and Canada and the groups travel all over competing against one another. At the end of the summer the Drum Corps International (DCI) World Championships are held in Orlando, Florida.

Peavy is a well known member of campus through his activities with Kappa Sigma and his work with the campus newspaper. Lei Paulding the secretary of the Student Government Association said “Tommy is a really great guy who will get far in life.”

Peavy graduated from Shaw high school in 1995. In high school he played golf for four years and wrote on the high school newspaper. He began Columbus State in Fall 1995. His major is undeclared but Fall 1998 he is hoping to transfer and declare a major of Broadcast Journalism.

Peavy hopes to one day anchor for ESPN or Sports South. Peavy said “I want to go into a field that relates to sports in some way.” When he was in high school he realized he could write so he wrote about what he loved and that has always been sports.

Peavy is the Sports Editor for the Saber and is a founding father of the Columbus State University Chapter of Kappa Sigma Fraternity. Peavy also teaches drums at Harris County High School and works at Mable Ridge.
