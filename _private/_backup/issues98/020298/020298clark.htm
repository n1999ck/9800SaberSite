Joe Clark visits CSU

by Elisa Williams / Guest Writer

 
Joe Clark will speak at CSU on February 5, in the Fine Arts Hall auditorium at 12:30 p.m. The event, which is part of CSU’s Black History Month celebration, is free and open to the public.

Best known as the principle with the bullhorn and a baseball bat, Clark turned a troubled inner city high school into an institution of serious learning and immeasurable pride.

In September 1981, on the first day of classes at Eastside High, in Paterson, New Jersey, a student was stabbed. By 1982, Clark transformed the school into a model learning environment.

Clark’s disciplinary actions and determination earned him national recognition. He was named a model educator by President Reagan and was offered a White House post.

Clark held high expectations for his students, and challenged them to develop habits for success. During his first week at Eastside High, Clark expelled 300 students for vandalism, fighting, drug possession, profanity or abusing teachers. Clark says, “If there is no discipline, no learning can take place. Without discipline there is anarchy. Good citizenship demands attention to responsibilities as well as rights.”

“60 Minutes,” “Night-line” and “Donahue” profiled Clark and his story was portrayed in the motion picture “Lean on Me,” starring Morgan Freeman.

While attending high school Clark worked to help support his mother, brother and sisters. He received a BA from William Patterson College and received his master’s degree at Seton Hall University.

Today, Joe Clark is Director of Essex County Youth House, a juvenile detention center in Newark, New Jersey.
