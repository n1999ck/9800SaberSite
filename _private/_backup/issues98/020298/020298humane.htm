A voice for those who cannot speak

by Evelyn Tolbert-Owens

 
Do you own one of those critters that has been man’s best friend since the beginning of time? Do you pamper and spoil him just a little? If you do, your animal is one lucky pet. There are many homeless animals packed away in shelters. One hundred-thirty seven of them are housed at the Muscogee County Humane Society right here in Columbus off Schatulga Road, founded by Colonel Sydney Sacerdote in 1973.

The Muscogee County Humane Society is launching an awareness and fundraising campaign in the Greater Columbus area to make people aware of the identity and needs of the Society and their animals as well as to help support our area’s homeless animals housed at the shelter. Their facility is in dire need of renovation and expansion. The number of homeless pets is growing - but unfortunately the facilities and therefore accommodation possibilities at the Society are not.

Upkeep of the animals as well as expansion and renovation of facilities are crucial issues. At this time, the Muscogee County Humane Society can only house 137 animals at a time. From January 1997 to the end of October 1997 almost 2,000 animals passed through the Society’s doors. They were able to find homes for 657 of them. Sadly, the rest had to be destroyed because of insufficient room and insufficient funds to carry and provide for the animals for a longer period of time.

The Society takes in homeless animals from Muscogee, Talbot, Meriwether, Harris, Taylor, Lee, and Russell Counties, as well as from Phenix City. Contrary to popular belief, the Society does not receive funding from the State of Georgia nor the City of Columbus. They are not associated with the Columbus Dog Pound. The Humane Society solely relies on volunteers and private or company contributions from the greater Columbus area’s animal lovers. They are barely breaking even at this time and could use YOUR help in getting improvements underway.

The big question: What can you do to help? The answer is: A lot of different things: You, your fraternity, sorority, association, honor society, or any other organization you may belong to could help in several different ways:

1. Actively promote neutering and spaying of animals with family and friends to cut down on the animal overpopulation.

2. Promote adoptions of pets from the Muscogee County Humane Society with family and friends or anyone you know who is considering to enrich their lives with a pet.

3. Shop at the Humane Society Thrift Shop. They have everything from dishes to clothes to furniture and electronics. You can offer your support by buying from them instead of other stores.

4. Hold fundraisers such as car-washes, yard sales, raffles, bake sales, recycling campaigns and donate the proceeds to the Humane Society.

5. Place Human Society donation banks around town and pledge to maintain them.

6. Volunteer your time at one of the special events the Humane Society holds throughout the year.

7. Donate unwanted items that may be hidden in your closets, cupboards and sheds at home to the Humane Society Thrift Shop for resale. (multi-purpose cleaners, bleach, rags, old newspapers, etc.)

8. Donate towels (all sizes), blankets, food bowls, pet toys, pet carriers, pet food. They can even use empty toilet paper roles for the animals to play with.

9. Become sponsors or even members of the Muscogee County Humane Society and bless the animals with monthly pledges.

10. Make a one time contribution only.

Take your pick - the Muscogee County Humane Society appreciates any help you can provide them with.

Please, be “a voice for those who cannot speak”. Help the Humane Society succeed in making Columbus a better place to live for our homeless, four-legged friends. If you would like to volunteer your services, have questions or want more information about how to help please call Evelyn Tolbert-Owens at (706) 568-3235.
