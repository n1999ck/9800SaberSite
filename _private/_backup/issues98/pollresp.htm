October 12, 1998

Tell us how your professors are doing this semester. Are they adapting well to the semester transition?

I feel that some professors are confused about the semester system. They are trying to jam too much into the days we spend with them. I have one professor in the language department that is trying to adjust to the semester system, but it is very difficult for her and her students to do this in such a short amount of time. The sad thing is, for people who have never taken a foreign language, they are having to jump right in instead of the small steps that they could take on the quarter system. Both professors and students this semester are guinea pigs in this whole semester system experiment. As a Student Government representative, I have had other students express these same concerns about foreign language.

-Eddie Gordon, Sophomore

I think that there is a period of adjustment. The students are having to adjust as well as the faculty. All of us need to get used to the pace before we can really judge how well our teachers are doing.

-Joel Judah, Senior, Biology Department

October 5, 1998

Do you think that professors are overloading classes with too much work for students to handle?

The semester system is supposed to help the students absorb the materials better and provide plenty of time to work on assignments. Yet, professors think that since the students have a longer term, they can bombard them with projects. Most of the time these projects are due during the semester and not at the end. I’m a business major and I am taking six classes. One of my classes is requiring eight group projects during this semester. If the University System is expecting the students to take at least five classes per semester, I’m asking: How do they think we should manage this with this much work for students to handle? I can understand that CSU professors are inexperienced with the semester system, but it is insane to overload students with projects.

September 28, 1998

Give us your thoughts on the Clinton-Lewinsky issue. Is it appropriate to spend the nation's time on this?

I think it’s great that American women have modernized to the point where they can accept a man’s little infidelities. It’s only natural, from a biological perspective, for a man who has fought his way to the head of the pack (the Alpha male, so to speak) to want to breed with as many eligible females as possible. The fact that the current President of the United States of America is a bald-faced liar is somewhat harder to accept. This tendency for fabrication has been a Clinton character flaw since his early days in Arkansas politics. Trustworthiness should be one of the foremost attributes of the leader of the most important nation in the world but Clinton views the truth as a liability. Now he’s the liability.

- Ted Roever, Senior, Biology Dept.

As far as I’m concerned, what they do or did is none of my business. There are bigger tragedies in THIS country alone to be concerned about. Why focus on an affair someone else has or had if it was in a room without a view or video camera? Besides, if Monica has lied before what makes anyone think she isn’t now? Does the government own the Sun, we do not know it? Okay, so he’s the president. And? His morals? Those hold true only to himself. Everyone defines moral differently in every case. All I can say is either fire the man so we can all go on with our lives or leave him alone, so he can go on running the country that he has thus far done well doing. If I actually had time to give my full attention to this situation, I would say “Hey! Stop wasting my time and money, show a cartoon!”

- Sarra C.
