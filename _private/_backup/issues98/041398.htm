Monday, April 13, 1998

Drivin' n' Cryin' rocks CSU

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief

The rock band Drivin’ N’ Cryin’ performed at the CSU Spring Break Festival Thursday, April 9.

About 200 people attended the concert, which started out without much audience participation. The atmosphere improved as people from the back rows moved in front of the stage to dance and mosh.

Drivin’ N’ Cryin’, famous for songs like “Straight to Hell” and “Fly Me Courageous” has toured with such acts as R.E.M., Soul Asylum, Neil young and Sonic Youth.

The band, originally from Atlanta, has visited Columbus many times and played at CSU before.

Band drummer Jeff Sullivan says playing colleges is great except for the no alcohol rules on many campuses.

“The equipment at colleges is usually really good. They’re usually good places to play. The only problem is that most campuses don’t allow drinking at the show. Drinking makes things more festive, “ Sullivan says.

The crowd was more diverse than Sullivan predicted with more children and less traditional college students. However, while the diversity of the crowd surprised him, he was pleased with the turn out.

“I can’t believe how many families are here. That’s okay, though. We’re going to jam,” Sullivan says.

Sullivan says he doesn’t mind playing familiar songs for the fans, although, playing newer songs is sometimes more fun.

“The fans have been loyal. They’re opened to new songs,” Sullivan says. He also says that the fans have shown great support for the band’s newer music as well as old favorites.

The band played an ensemble version of “Fly Me Courageous” and ended with “Straight to Hell”.

The band’s latest album, self titled, has been well received.

Sullivan says, “We wrote the songs for this album over a period of 8 months. I think it made it better than if we’d done the whole thing in a recording studio.”

According to Sullivan, the new album, the first on the band’s independent label, is more about what the band wants and less about big business. He says more of the band’s personal opinions and creativity went into this album and they are happy with the result.

Drivin’ N’ Cryin’ can be seen in Atlanta May 2 at the Midtown Music Festival.
