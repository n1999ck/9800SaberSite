CSU combats homelessness

by Athena Curry / Editor-in-Chief
Habitat for Humanity and Columbus State University are looking for volunteers to participate in building homes for the homeless in our area. Volunteers will be needed on May 2 to work one of two shifts.

There is a $5.00 fee to participate, which includes lunch and a t-shirt. Participation is opened to anyone and those wishing to help can sign up at the Career Center in Davidson 147.

Habitat for Humanity is a nonprofit, international, Christian organization, which uses volunteer labor and supplies to build homes for the homeless. The group’s goal is to eliminate substandard housing and provide affordable homes for the homeless worldwide.

A three-bedroom Habitat house currently costs an average of $38,000 in the United States. The average length of a Habitat mortgage in the United States is about 20 years.

Those who receive Habitat homes participate in the building of their house and the building of other Habitat homes. Usually homeowners invest between 300 and 500 hours per family.

Habitat for Humanity accepts donations, which are used as designated by the donor. Gifts are also accepted and distributed according to the wishes of the contributor.

The organization’s work is accomplished by independent nonprofit corporations that operate within a specific service area and within the framework of the Habitat Affiliate Covenant. There are currently are more than 1,300 affiliates in the United States.

According to Habitat for Humanity literature, nearly 30 million U.S. households face housing problems. These problems include, cost burdens such as, paying over 30% of monthly income for rent and utilities. According to Habitat, over 14 million households pay over 50% of their income for rent and utilities.

Other problems, that Habitat works on overcoming are overcrowding and the physical inadequacy of homes such as having no hot water, no electricity or no toilets.
