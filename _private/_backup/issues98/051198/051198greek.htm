Greek participation increases at CSU

by Michael Cox / Staff Writer
Columbus State University is five weeks into the Spring Quarter and Greek Council wasted no time in it’s pursuit of excitement on campus.

Last week nine different greek organizations participated in what proved to be a competitive, yet successful Greek Week. The greek system at Columbus State has grown a little since last year’s greek week which introduced a few new faces to the scene this year.

In the past, the greek system at Columbus State University has consisted of eight organizations, with everyone of them bringing their own aspects to the campus.

With the addition of a new sorority on campus, Phi Mu Kappa, the greek system is making an effort to increase it’s status at Columbus State University.

Greek Week ‘98 allowed the members of every greek organization to have fun, and at the same time, learn a little about themselves and other organizations as well.

There were six greek organizations actively involved in this year’s Greek Week. These organizations participated extensively by sponsoring specific events on certain days throughout the week.

Greek Week ‘98 began on Sunday, April 19 and finished up on Saturday, April 25.

Every member of a greek organization was able to participate in events and become actively involved in the Columbus community as well.

Some of the events that were presented during Greek Week this year were a scavenger hunt, greek olympics, basketball tournament, mud volleyball, tug of war and a danceathon.

Other events included a parent’s picnic, 30th Avenue School tutoring, cafeteria day and CSU day at the Red Stixx Game.

The Greeks closed out the week with a banquet which honored specific achievements.

Greek Week ‘98 proved to be an interesting time of fun and active involvement.
