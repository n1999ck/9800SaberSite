CSU students to compete in Miss GA

by Bridgett Brumbaugh / Staff Writer
Three CSU students will be competing this summer for the title of Miss Georgia with 47 other contestants. The competition begins the last Saturday in June and will be held at the Three Arts Theatre in Columbus.

April Brinson is one of the contestants that will compete in the Miss Georgia Pageant and is currently attending CSU.

Brinson is originally from Bainbridge, GA and has been attending CSU for two years. She is a sophomore with a grade point average of 3.23. She is majoring in Choral Music Education and plans to attend Florida State University to receive a Ph.D. in Choral Conducting in the future.

Brinson won Miss Greater Southwest Georgia in order to compete in the Miss Georgia Pageant. This was her first win in the Miss Georgia Preliminary Contest.

Brinson has been competing since she was fifteen years old.

Although she was awarded $600 for her most recent win, it was not her biggest. In 1996, Brinson won Miss Georgia Forestry and was awarded $2,500.

Brinson has had the advantage of preparing for the pageant since July. She says a lot of work goes into preparing for the competition like working out at the gym for 2 to 2 1/2 hours a day, finding a choreographer for the talent, and choosing the best outfits. She also has to find time to manage 20 quarter hours as well as teaching 2nd graders math and music.

Brinson's platform is rather unique compared to what most contestants emphasize. Her title is "Music for Sound Education." She wants to help people learn through music. Brinson's platform is based on the idea that certain types of music enhance learning.

Brinson also enjoys many hobbies when she has time. She enjoys fishing, jogging, and many water sports. She is also a Star Trek fan.

Shana Teel is another contestant in the race for Miss Georgia. She is working on her Masters Degree at CSU. She won Miss Presidential Pathways as her preliminary pageant to advance to the Miss Georgia Pageant and was awarded $1250.

Teel is a Florida native. She earned her Bachelor's Degree from a university in Tennessee and graduated with honors. While residing in Tennessee, Teel placed eighth in the Miss Tennessee Pageant.

Since moving to the Columbus area, Teel has been involved on campus. At one point she worked with the Greek organizations and now she works with the Counseling Center to help those with eating disorders.

Teel's platform deals with eating disorders. In her teen years she suffered from an eating disorder and weighed only 67 pounds.

According for Charles Eason, Director Planned Giving for University Advancement, "You couldn't even recognize her. If they hadn't put her in the hospital when they did, she would have died in three days."

Now Teel is busy trying to prevent this from happening to others. She recently spoke to the females at Hardaway High School about preventing eating disorders.

The third contestant from CSU is Amy Swanson. She is the current Miss Columbus and she received a scholarship for her prize.

Swanson has an Associate Degree in Journalism and is a Communications Major with a concentration in Public Relations. Swanson is the host of News Briefs on Fox 54. Swanson competed in the Columbus Pageant with a cracked bone in her foot.

In an interview with the Saber shortly after winning the Miss Columbus title Swanson said, "I had to compete with a cracked bone in my foot. Usually I exercise more just before a competition, but since I just got a cast off the day before I couldn't prepare much as far as that goes."

A former Miss Presidential Pathways, Swanson has also won Miss Tifton in the past.

Along with other community work Swanson works to help raise awareness and support for the Special Olympics.

The Miss Georgia Competition is a week long event and the winner goes on to compete in the Miss America pageant.
