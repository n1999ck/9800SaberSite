Campus Candid: Robert Oliver

by Sharon Z. Langford
William Robert Oliver (II) is a 22-year-old graduate of the Computer Science Department. He is currently completing his Masters in Computer Science and works as a software development Consultant at CSU. There he develops online courseware software, specifically for the CSU Online initiative, with Dr. Jim Leonard. He also provides software and networking solutions for several professors on campus.

Robert specializes in Windows NT software development, the Internet, web application development, Windows NT and Internet server networking. He also writes technical books about Windows NT and Microsoft Certification for Macmillan Publishing and recently for a foreign publisher in Singapore. He has earned the Competitive Edge 4-Year Science Scholarship, the Callaway SAT Scholarship, the Governor's Scholarship for STAR Students and the HOPE Scholarship.

Organizational Communications (COMM445) is lucky enough to have Robert as its Chief Information Officer. "I'm developing and testing a web-based application with components that enable students to view live or stored audio/video lectures and view slide shows," Robert explained. "Students can learn and interact with the class, view the same material and hear the same lectures as their in-class classmates." This is known as remote learning.

When asked how he became interested in computers, Robert thinks his sister got him hooked. "I decided that Ôcomputer geek' sounded a little better than just Ôgeek'," he said. He likes playing blues guitar and target shooting. "I just bought a new ESP electric guitar and a Remington 700 rifle and I'm not sure which one is more dangerous." He enjoys listening to the Doors and says he spends too much money on Star Wars toys because he never had them as a kid.

Robert's goals are to own a Jaguar XK8 by November and be a millionaire by age 25. He'd like to improve his shooting aim, travel the world, and publish something besides computer books.
