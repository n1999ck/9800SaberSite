I am writing this document so that it may serve as a wakeup call for CSU’s cafeteria service. I am currently a junior who has lived in the College Courtyard for 8 quarters. For those of you who do not know, the students who live in the Courtyard are required to be on a meal plan with the cafeteria. Students have a choice between a two or three meal-a-day plan. We pay a housing fee along with tuition, which includes the cost of the meal plan that is chosen. The point of me writing this document is to inform the cafeteria managers, students, faculty and staff of the many complaints that several of the meal plan students have about our cafeteria services:

The first complaint is that the lunches that are served are much better than the dinners that are served. I am sure that many of the Courtyard residents notice this, because we are the ones who eat both lunch and dinner. in this statement, I am trying to convey the fact that there are less people of “importance” on the campus who eat dinner than lunch. It is my belief that because there are fewer members of the faculty and staff who eat dinner, better meals are prepared for lunch. I do not think that this is fair to the students who pay their money and expect quality for payments that have been made. I will not go into the details of what is actually served, but I can say that many of the meals served for dinner have a lot to be desired. Many times, students walk out of the cafeteria with nothing but a drink in their hands because of the lack of quality of the meals that are prepared.

The next complaint that I have is about the service lines. Why are there only two of them open for dinner? People who eat dinner in the cafeteria can only get a meal from the main meal line, the grill, or sometimes the sandwich line. It would be much better for the students if all of the lines were open. This would give us a variety of items to choose from.

My final complaint is about one of the cafeteria employees. This particular employee has very close contact with our food when she prepares it. For some reason, she does not wear gloves and I think that it is disgusting. I have walked over to her several times to be served, and have seen her eating in between serving customers. I do not think that this is sanitary or acceptable behavior.

I hope that these issues can be resolved or at least improved. If they are not resolved, at least more people are now aware of the complaints that frequent the minds of many students, especially those of us who are on the meal plan.

Sincerely,
A Concerned Student
