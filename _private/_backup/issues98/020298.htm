 Homecoming tradition begins

by Heather Warren / Associate Editor

Columbus State University begins what some students hope will become a tradition. Winterfest was the title of the week which ran from Tuesday, January 20 until Saturday, January 24.

Lei Paulding, Secretary of Student Government Assoc-iation says, “I think it was a great success and will get better every year.”

The Winterfest basketball homecoming celebration began with a Pep Rally and cookout that was sponsored by Student Government Association (SGA) and the cheerleaders. There were free hot dogs and the cheerleaders were there get everyone in the spirit.

All week long there was a “find the cougar contest”, in which students received clues via e-mail regarding the location of the cougar. The cougar was found on Thursday by CSU Housing Authority. The hiding place was in the Tower Center in an office stuffed under a lamp. Linda Hurst, president of the SGA, hid the cougar.

Part of the festivites included a homecoming court with king and queen to be crowned at the end of the week. The court consisted of representatives from the organizations that were participating in the homecoming activities. The couple that volunteered the most at 30th Avenue Elementary and who raised the most money for the Special Olympics were awarded the titles King and Queen. The King and Queen of homecoming week were Jeff Battles and Jamie Smith who represented Sigma Nu.

On Wednesday the Talent Show, which was sponsored by Student Programming Council (SPC), was held in the Davidson Auditorium. The winner was Kelli Howard and second place went to Andy Manuel.

Thursday was Island Fest at the Gym Annex. The Island Fest consisted of what one student referred to as “goofy” games. Participants played games such as, the balloon relay and bat spin.

As the end of the week approached more activities were scheduled for the two remaining days. On Friday, the Winterfest Parade was held. F ollowing the parade was the homecoming pep rally and band party. The band present at the party was DSOS.

The final day was Saturday, which consisted of an Old Timers Game, CSU women vs Kennesaw State University game and CSU Men vs Kennesaw State University game. The evening concluded with a Winterfest Ball at the Bradley Theater.

The homecoming court raised $1696.49 to go to the Special Olympics. The money was raised in various ways ranging from producing t-shirts to raffling off televisions. The check was presented to Public Safety and they will send representatives to Atlanta for the 1998 Law Enforcement Torch Run.

Although, most students thought homecoming was an overall success a few students had some suggestions to give. A student who wished to remain unnamed says, “It’s a great idea, I’m glad we’re starting traditions on campus but it needs more organization and student support.”

Another student who wished to remain anonymous says, “although, bringing group organizations together was the overall theme the organizers may want to revamp the program to better include many of the smaller organizations.”
