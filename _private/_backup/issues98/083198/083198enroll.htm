Enrollment at CSU Rises

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
Columbus State University has undergone several changes, but never suspected the drastic increase in incoming students this year. CSU is slowly loosing it’s reputation as a local and commuter school.

Columbus State University moved into the 1998-1999 school year with many changes in hand with the conversion to both Banner and the Semester System. Little did anyone know that the enrollment numbers would change as well.

The number of incoming freshmen at CSU has increased by 100 this year. This is a 15% rise from last years numbers. This increase is due to a number of factors says Shannon Wilson, Acting Director of Admissions at Columbus State University.

“The increase is due to mainly four factors; the recognition of Columbus State University in Atlanta and other surrounding areas, new offerings by CSU such as the Space Science Center, the addition of another recruiter, Susan Webb, to the Admissions Staff and the CSU C.O.R.E.”, says Wilson.

Last Fall, roughly 660 new freshmen attended Columbus State University and this Fall the numbers are up around 760 students.

Wilson states, “This can definitely be a plus for student life since the freshman this year seem interested to get involved more.”

Dr. Carl Wallman, Dean of Admissions and Registrar, feels positive about the transition to the semester as a whole even though the school did experience things that were not anticipated. Columbus State University successfully completed the conversion to the semester system this fall.

Dr. Wallman says, “We are excited about finally being able to get through what we have anticipated for so long. Even though we have gone over the hump, there is still lots of work to be done.”

Kim Padgett, an Admissions Counselor in the Admission Office, is excited as well about the increase in numbers.

Padgett states, “When I came to CSU, the school was more of a commuter college, but I feel as if we are moving away from that reputation as we move toward the future.”

The freshman class this year seems more enthusiastic and more willing to get involved that in the past. Student Activities has played a large part in that with the display of events this past week.

“Getting the students here is half the battle, but retaining them is a different issue. Everyone needs to work together to give the students the best services we can provide,” added Padgett.

As Columbus State University moves toward higher standards, so to does it move toward larger numbers on campus. Everyone on campus plays a large part in these change of events and it will take a team effort to keep this type of environment on campus for the years to come.
