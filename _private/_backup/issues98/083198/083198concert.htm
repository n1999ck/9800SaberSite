The current concert scene

by Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor
The Dave Matthews Band played at the Lakewood Amphitheater in Atlanta, Georgia, on August 20. Dave and company were as impressive as ever, and the music was given a twist by the addition of guests Tim Reynolds and Herbie Hancock.

The show was the band’s second appearance in Atlanta this summer, with the first being the MTV 10 Spot show from the Tabernacle.

The setlist for the show was as follows: “Seek Up,” “Jimi Thing,” “Don’t Drink The Water,” “Rapunzel,” “Tripping Billies,” “Lover Lay Down,” “Warehouse,” “Stay,” “#41,” “Crush,” “So Much To Say,” “Too Much,” “Crash,” “All Along The Watchtower,” and the encore included “Granny” and “Ants Marching.”

In other concert news, Pearl Jam roles into Atlanta Tuesday, September 1 and then hits Birmingham the following Thursday.

It is no secret that Eddie Vedder and crew put on an awesome show, but this tour seems to be shaping up even better than ever. Internet sites are reporting that the band appears to be having a blast and are even trying out a few new songs at various shows. Vedder has even been singing Madonna’s song “Ray of Light” at the end of the song “Daughter.”

I am going to catch both shows and will get back to you on how everything went next week. Adios.
