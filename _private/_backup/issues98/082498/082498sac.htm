CSU Students go to SAC conference

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
The Student Government Association was recently represented at this summer’s Student Advisory Council conference.

The Student Advisory Council holds four conferences a year. Jekyll Island hosts the first conference of the year and three other institutions host the others.

SAC is made up of representatives from each institution within the University System of Georgia. These representatives play an extremely important role in the system as they make recommendations on behalf of 260,000 students within the University System of Georgia.

Student Advisory Council focuses on three main components which are Student Life, Academic Affairs and Legislative Affairs.

Representatives are divided into committees where they will then deal with issues affecting one of these areas. For example, the Student Life committee is currently focusing on three different areas. These areas are a system wide healthcare program, childcare facilities and computer technology equalization. A few accomplishments in the past include the Hope Scholarship Program and Semester Conversion.

In the Academic Affairs committee, some other issues were addressed as well. Raising the HOPE book allowance to meet the rate of inflation, updating school education curriculums to adequately prepare prospective teachers for their required exams, and finding ways to curb the growing number of teachers who will raise a students grade with the sole purpose of making them eligible for the HOPE Scholarship were among the issues discussed.

The main function for the meeting is to handle concerns, and a few were announced at this year’s meeting in Jekyll Island.

The main concern dealt with the Banner System and the question of whether or not the system was compliant with the year 2000 situation. This issue is still in discussion and will be solved at a later date.

This new computer software is much of the reason why registration and handling financial aid has been difficult this year.

Grant Salter, Vice-President of Scholastic Affairs, says that the summer conference is a brainstorming period to gather problems and issues to cover at conferences later in the year.

Grant also stated, “SAC is a great way to voice concerns on issues that affect the University System. If you have any concerns that need to be addressed on a state or local level, please come by the Student Government Association Office for more information.”

Other representatives of CSU at the SAC conference were SGA Senators Alin Aiken and Ben Wells.
