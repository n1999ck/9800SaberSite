Letter from the Editor

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
The new transitions occurring at Columbus State University are receiving raved reviews which tend to be both positive and negative.

The biggest complaint seems to be with the newly acquired Banner system and the ever so popular Financial Aid Office. Students seem to be running into Admissions problems for many different reasons.

At the same time people are having trouble with both receiving and keeping the money they are already eligible for. Lines also tend to be longer which results in tension between faculty and students.

As a former employee in the Admissions Office, I can honestly say that I understand the problems with the transition entirely. Despite all of the controversy, Columbus State University proved to be very successful at Orientation during early registration.

An article in the Thomaston Times earlier this month mentioned the good and bad aspects of the early registration process under the new system. But, many of the problems faced by the new students were handled as a result of both courteous students and faculty.

Everyone just needs to bare with the new system for the first year. Nothing in life is ever perfect, especially when a system as large as Banner is going through. If everyone will show some patience and understanding about the whole situation, everything will go a lot smoother.

If you have any doubts about your acceptance process or financial aid just ask yourself this question. If I am under this kind of stress, what kind of stress is the faculty and staff at Columbus State University having to overcome? Because, these people are the ones that are having to manage the transition problems, not the students.
