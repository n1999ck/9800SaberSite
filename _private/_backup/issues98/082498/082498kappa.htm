Kappa Sigma receives award

by Michael Cox / Editor-in-Chief
Kappa Sigma Fraternity forged it’s way into the history books in early August at the 1998 Area II Leadership Conference held at the University of Tennessee at Knoxville.

The Xi-Iota Chapter of Kappa Sigma at Columbus State University was represented by ten brothers who embarked on the trip to receive one major award, but the chapter returned home with several.

The three day trip proved to be successful with the brothers attending workshops and engaging in a community service project which was organized to aid a local elementary school in the community.

The highlight of the trip occurred on August 1st at the closing banquet. The Xi-Iota Chapter received several awards for being #1 on the campus of CSU academically for the Fall, Winter, and Spring Quarter of 1997. The chapter also received an award for being #1 academically above the all mens’ average as well.

The final award given was the F.A.C.E. Award also known as the Founders Award of Chapter Excellence. The F.A.C.E. Award is the highest achievement given in the Kappa Sigma Fraternity both nationally and internationally. Of the 27 F.A.C.E. recipients at the 1998 Leadership Conference, the Xi-Iota Chapter of Kappa Sigma received this outstanding achievement.

The award is based on numerous categories involving scholarship, community service, publicity, and many more areas.

The Xi-Iota Chapter is one of the youngest chapters to receive the award by receiving this accomplishment in under two years.

Brannon Kuykendall, President, said, “Being a recipient of the Founders Award of Chapter Excellence has changed our chapter in many ways. In receiving this award we have become one of the top 28 chapters of the 210 chapters that compose our brotherhood. This spotlight has motivated our chapter to make 1998-1999 the best year we have ever experienced. We will continue to focus on scholarship, community service and campus leadership throughout the upcoming school year.”
