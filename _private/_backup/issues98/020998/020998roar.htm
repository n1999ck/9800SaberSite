ROAR Column

by David Daniels / CSU Student

 
I was trying to breathe outside one of my classes the other day but was unable to due to the blue-gray cloud of cancer-laden smoke drifting through the air.

The smoking policy states that smoking outside entrances to the buildings is not allowed, yet no one, especially public SAFETY is interested in enforcing this policy. As a matter of fact, a friend of mine was personally enforcing this by using a water-gun when told by public SAFETY that she had to stop bothering the smokers.

Bothering the smokers. What a wild concept. When I went through new student orientation in September I was amazed to hear that the new gazebos located in the FOB / Howard courtyard were for the comfort and enjoyment of the smokers! Nothing like spending taxpayer money on something for the comfort of the smokers. I’ve also been told that the smoking policy can’t be enforces until the new signs are posted on the buildings apprising smokers of the policy. This poses two more questions: who will enforce the policy then and where are the signs?

It appears to me, and I am only a senior, that there is a definite conflict of interest here. 1.) CSU is a state school funded by taxpayer dollars as well as our fees. 2.) The state of Georgia has a current civil lawsuit against the tobacco giants. 3.) State money is being used to benefit the smokers and in turn punish those who choose not to smoke by bombarding us with the more deadly second-hand smoke.

Why anyone attending school in this day and age has taken up smoking is beyond me. Yes, you have the right to smoke, it is not illegal. But when it has been proven for over thirty years to kill you it makes me wonder.

A rule or law that is not enforced may as well not exist.
