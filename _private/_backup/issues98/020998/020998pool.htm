New pool to cost 1/4 million

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

 
The Columbus State University pool has been closed since the second week of this quarter. It has been on campus for over thirty years, but has had trouble holding water in the last several months.

The problems became more serious early this quarter when the pool started losing nearly ten inches of water each day. Losing that much water is not only expensive, but dangerous. The deteriorating pipes were leaking the water under the building. The excess water could damage the foundation of the entire building. The problem started with only the main pipes leaking, but the leaks are now effecting the secondary pipes as well.

Hal Gibson, director of Plan Operations here at Columbus State University, is overseeing the repair process of the pool. He says that there will not be any repairs, but a new pool will be installed.

This project is going to cost nearly a quarter of a million dollars. A request is being presented funds. If this plan works out, the money will be approved and the removal of the old pool will begin in the next several weeks.

If the emergency funds are denied, the money will come from the Major Repair and Renovation funds, which is an annual allotment of money each year to the University.

The new indoor pool should be open close to summer quarter.
