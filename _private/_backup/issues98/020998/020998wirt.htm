Professor to be featured in recital

- CSU Public Relations

 
Columbus State University Associate Professor Ronald Wirt, bassoonist, will be featured in a CSU faculty recital 8 p.m. Thursday, February 12 at the Davidson Center Recital Hall.

Wirt teaches applied bassoon, music theory, music appreciation, and performs with the CSU Faculty Woodwind Quintet.

Accompanied by CSU Professor and pianist Betty Anne Diaz, Wirt will perform selections from Philipp Friedrich Boddecker’s La Monica, Michel Corrette’s Le Phenix, Samuel Adler’s Canto XII, P.D.Q. Bach’s Abbassoonata, Heitor Villa-Lobos’ Ciranda das Sete Notas, and Ermanno Wolf-Ferrari’s Suite-Concertino.

He has performed twice at Carnegie Recital Hall as a member of the New York-based Borealis Chamber Ensemble. The Providence Journal has called him “outstanding. . . a very subtle practitioner of his specialty.”

Diaz has performed throughout the United States and in South America. She has taught at Georgia State University, Kennesaw State and The Georgia Academy of Music.

Tickets purchased in advance include $8 adults, $6 senior citizens (60-plus), $4 children (under 18) and $5 per person in groups of eight or more. Admission at he door is $10. Call the CSU Box office at (706) 569-3179.
