Music Bits

Musicians take a turn at writing

by Brian Hale / Staff Writer

 
Marilyn Manson is set to release his new autobiography this month. Manson titled his new venture “The Long Hard Road Out of Hell.” One can only guess as to what mysteries will be contained in this new project of Marilyn Manson’s. The release of the book will be kicked off by a book signing at midnight on Valentine’s Day at Tower Books in New York City. The book signing tour will last several weeks, and span across the nation. Marilyn Manson will also be performing on MTV Live on, appropriately, the upcoming Friday the thirteenth.

Steven Adler is also dropping the instruments and picking up a pen for the next several months. The former Guns’N’Roses drummer has written a book and is currently looking for the best publishing deal before he signs to have it printed. “No Bed of Roses” documents Adler’s experiences before, during, and after his stint with Guns’N’Roses. Adler was publicly fired from the band by frontmen Axl Rose and Slash at the 1990 MTV Video Music Awards. The band sited Adler’s drug abuse as the reason for the firing. Adler admits that his seven year addiction to heroin and cocaine ruined his relationship with the band. He also admits that all his money, including the $2.3 million he received for being fired from Guns’N’Roses, was dwindled away by drug abuse. Deanne Adler, Steven’s mother, also tells her side of the story in the book. The story documents thirty-one trips to the emergency room, one heart attack, and one stroke, all due to drug abuse. Along with the book, Adler says he has a new song to accompany it , and is in negotiations with several organizations about either signing or creating his own music label.

The British anarchist group, Chumbawamba, are out to prove that they are not just another one hit wonder. Nevermind the fact that it took the band fifteen years to obtain their first hit single, they seem to think they can do it again with their next single. The upcoming release for Chumbawamba is called “Amnesia.” The band issued a statement about the new song, “People forget what Bill Clinton says before he gets elected is not what Bill Clinton will do when he is in office. The song is not about Bill Clinton, but all politicians.” The bands distrust of politics and dislike for authority has become very well known lately. Last week the band issued a statement to fans urging them to obtain their album any way possible, including stealing it. That is probably not the best marketing technique available, but when hit singles come once every fifteen years, they are probably just happy that somebody is still listening.

Former Judas Priest frontman Rob Halford has signed a new deal with Nothing Records. Nothing Records is owned by Nine Inch Nails mastermind Trent Reznor. Reznor has been recruited as executive producer for the new Halford project. Halford’s new band is called Two, and the upcoming album is titled, “Voyeurs.” A United States tour is expected to begin in April. Cypress Hill members Sen Dog, B-Real and DJ Muggs are back in the studio working on their new album together. An official release date has not been set.

Radiohead is hitting the United States one more time on their “OK Computer” tour. This time around they are bringing a few special guests with them. Bjork and Spiritualized will be joining Radiohead on tour starting March 28. Radiohead are also putting together footage for a home video release, but that will not be complete until later this year.
