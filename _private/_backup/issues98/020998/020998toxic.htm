Professor discusses "Toxic Media"

by Sharon Z. Langford / Staff Writer

 
What is “Toxic Media”? Do you think you’re exposed to it? Is it harmful? The Unitarian Universalists (U.U.) are hosting a series during the month of March pertaining to the infiltration of media into our lives. WORLD magazine will the source of the information discussed with U.U. members in a one session lecture provided by CSU Psychology/Sociology professor, Dr. Cecile Doroff.

The U.U. has no nation wide formal creed or test of membership for members. According to several websites from fellowships across the country, parishioners are not required to submit to any particular set of beliefs. Instead, it is a place for anyone regardless of specific spiritual beliefs.

The series “Toxic Media” refers to the ever-present leakage of the media into our lives via television, newspapers, magazines, electronic media, radio, and other sources.

The series will also include information about the harm found in the way media targets people, particularly the young. Small children want televisions in their rooms and Sony PlayStations. According to material to be presented in the series, teens are the hardest hit by feeling they have to have Tommy Hilfiger clothing and Nikes to be accepted. Adults want bigger houses and $40,000 cars. People believe they have to get second jobs in order to buy these things.

March 1 is the first in the series, which will be an open forum, led by member Chris Lancaster. Toxic Media and how to regain control over our lives is the topic for this discussion.

March 15 is a lecture led by Dr. Doroff entitled “Woman and the Media; Our Sexual Contradictions of Womanhood”. Doroff will discuss the “commonalties of several stereotypes accepted by both men and women”. She stated that many women are victims to anorexia due to the pressure to look like “the ideal woman.” The lecture will include such examples as the new expanded Barbie with the wider hips and smaller bust. Will society still want to purchase the doll?

March 22 will be the discussion of “The Beauty Myth and Self-Esteem”, led by member Jennifer Harriman. This will focus on how the media-influenced ideas of beauty, success and power relate to our self-esteem. Also discussed will be the truisms and what is financially acquired in order to feel self worth and success.

All meetings will be at 10:30 a.m. at the Unitarian Universalist Fellowship of Columbus located at 1442 Double Churches Road, Columbus.
