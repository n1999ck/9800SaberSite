Monday, May 11, 1998

Ethics Week proves successful

by Heather Warren / Associate Editor

Columbus State University hosted Ethics Week May 2 through May 11, 1998. Ethics Week was enacted by Governor Zell Miller. The week officially began on May 3, but CSU kicked the week off a day early with a Habitat for Humanity project.

The Habitat for Humanity project began Volunteer Week as well as Ethics Week and was a celebration of the formation of “Cougar P.A.W.S.”, Partners At Work and Service. Cougar P.A.W.S. is a new organization on campus to promote volunteerism in the Columbus area.

Over 60 students, faculty and staff gathered on North Lumpkin Road to assist in building a house for families in need. There was a large enough force to plan a second day to work on the Habitat project.

Dr. Frank Brown, President of Columbus State University, helped with construction of the Habitat House.

On Tuesday, May 5 Glenn Davis spoke in the Davidson Center Columbus Room. Davis is a Major League Baseball player and founder of Carpenters Way visited campus and spoke to students.

Carpenters Way Ranch for Boys is a foster home for under-privledged young people. A Ranch just outside Columbus is near completion and construction on a Ranch for girls will begin soon.

Davis spoke on the importance of maintaining a positive image and discussed ethical dilemmas faced in his own life.

On Thursday, May 7 a panel discussion was held in the Azalea Room. The topic was ethics in the media. Andy Craig, CSU student, was the panel moderator. The speakers were Tim Chitwood, columnist at the Columbus Ledger-Enquirer; Athena Curry, Editor in Chief at the CSU Saber; Mark McGee, News Director at WTVM News; and William Crowell, CSU Associate Professor of Communication.
After each speaker presented comments on the origin of ethics codes in the media and individual experiences on the topic, the panel answered questions from the audience.

Cecilia Buffkin, Coordinator of Student Development, attended the discussion and says the topic was very worthwhile and interesting. Buffkin says, “I liked it. It was very thought provoking. I liked the discussion comparing [the coverage] of Princess Diana and Mother Teresa.”

On Friday , May 8 the film, “Absence of Malice”, starring Paul Newman and Sally Fields was presented in the Azaela room. The film illistrates the ramifications of lack of ethics in media.

Neil Thomson, Assistant Professor of Business Administration; Dr. Mario Mion, Chair Professor of Political Science; and Dr. Art Joy, Assistant Professor of Accounting, Finance, and Computer Information Systems, led the discussion.

The final event in honor of Ethics Week will the Phi Kappa Phi “A House Divided”: Truth and Reconciliation in South Africa: Implications for America and the World Forum on May 11 in the Davidson Azalea Room at 12:30. Race relations in South Africa and America will be the topic discussed.

Dr. Cordelia Kirk, Professor of Sociology at Savannah State University and Ms. Kailas de Voux, a student in the Doctorate in Counseling Program at Auburn University spoke at the “A House Divided” forum. Both Dr. Kirk and Ms. Kailas de Voux lived in South Africa, where they were academic leaders.

‘98 proved to be an interesting time of fun and active involvement.
