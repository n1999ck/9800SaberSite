 New technology at CSU

by Athena Curry & Heather Warren / Editor-in-Chief & Associate Editor

Columbus State University is breaking new ground in technology. One new application of the internet technology has been given the name Desktop Broadcasting.

Desktop Broadcasting is an innovation that was conceptualized by Victoria Wise and Dr. Ann Rosenthal. The basic concept is streaming multimedia. Microsoft Netshow is being used to create streaming audio and video over the Internet. Streaming multimedia downloads steadily in small enough increments for standard home users to view video or hear audio smoothly. It can also broadcast live audio and eventually video on the web. Other applications for the technology besides Desktop Broadcasting are being used.

Dr. James Leonard from the Computer Science Department says within a couple of quarters the technology will be a reality. He has been working with Microsoft for the last few years. He began to work with Microsoft after he won a nation wide award for Innovators in Higher Education in 1995. Only five of the awards are given out each year.

Robert Oliver is a graduate student at CSU. Oliver is a software developer and is working with Dr. Leonard. He has been one of Leonard’s students for the past three years and is helping develop online technologies such as the streaming multimedia, video conferencing, and the Windows NT networking. He said “this could be used for online learning, presentations, lectures, speeches, etc.” He said the problem is finding people who are skilled enough to run the Internet servers and to develop the software used. Oliver is a Microsoft Certified Professional, a Windows NT specialist, and has co-authored three or four books on Windows NT and the Internet, he says “I’ve been roped into the whole desktop broadcasting concept by my favorite professor - Dr. Rosenthal.”

Thursday night some students from Dr. Ann Rosenthal’s Persuasion class produced the first presentation of Desktop Broadcasting. Dr. Rosenthal said “It is Max Headroom come to life.” The group in Dr. Ann Rosenthal’s class were representing the Communication Club. Rosenthal said “This is the first desktop broadcasting. With traditional media the media is owned and operated by a limited number of people . But we now have the capacity to broadcast to anyone on the internet.” She also says “It is a whole new avenue of mass media. There are no gatekeepers anymore.”

The technology is also being used in more places than Dr. Rosenthals’s class. Dr. Charlotte Stephens, a professor of accounting, finance, and computer information systems, is using online slide presentations, discussion groups and will soon be using online lecture recordings to her MBA graduate class. Dr. Leary Bell, head of the computer science department, is teaching Computer Science 108 with some of the third party online courses from NETg. NETg is an online training service. Dr. Leonard is using sources on his website to supplement several of his computer science classes.

Oliver says he can see the application bringing about a standard for online learning and publishing class material and content online. He said “not just static web pages that everyone’s used to, but the entire classroom with all it’s benefits, right on the Internet. “ Oliver also said real-time audio/video conferencing opens up the possibilities of the “virtual classroom.” “Publishing lecture recordings online means professors can offer supplemental material that students are more likely to listen to. There are enough new technologies and new uses for them, to accommodate a majority of the needs of the instructors we’ve talked to. There’s a lot of room for creativity and innovation here, and we come up with new ideas and new applications for our stuff every couple of days.”
