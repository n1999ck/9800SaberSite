Cougar golfers finish ninth in Missouri
Johnny Luse / Sports Editor

(Columbia, MO) The Columbus State University golf team, in fifth place after two rounds of the Purina / Missouri Classic in Columbia, MO, fell to ninth place after Tuesday’s final round. This is despite shooting its second best round of the tournament, a 288 (+4) on the par-71, 7,047-yard Missouri Bluffs Golf Club course.

The Cougars were victims of some good play by other squads. Wake Forest, Colorado, UNC Charlotte, and Arkansas State – all even with or behind CSU after two rounds – jumped in front with good play in the final round. The University of Kansas fired a two-under-par round of 282 to edge second-place Southwest Louisiana by three shots at 852.

Anton Haakman continued his fine play for CSU. In by far his best outing of the fall, Haakman finished in a tie for fourth place after carding rounds of 71, 69, and 69 to finish at four under-par. Nate Blauer made the biggest move for the Cougars in the final round. His 69 jumped him from 26th position after round two to finish in a tie for 11th.

David Lea shot a final-round 75, followed by a pair of 76’s from John Campbell and Stephen Hansen. Campbell finished in 47th position, Hansen in 60th, and Lea finished in 64th.

The Cougars’ next action will come at the College Golf Foundation / Rolex Division II Match Play Championships at Jekyll Island on October 24-25.
