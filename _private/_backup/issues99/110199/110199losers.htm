The following column, entitled “Luse’s Losers,” will deal with the picking of the losing team in the upcoming week’s SEC football games. It will be a cynical twist to the way everyone calls the sport. Practically no one ever picks the losing teams, so I will take that challenge upon myself--to successfully pick the losing team each week in the SEC and try and predict the score as well. 

In dealing with the last four weeks worth of games, I currently have a record of 45-28. So I am not too bad at picking the losing team. So here is this week’s LUSE’S LOSERS.

Game # 1
Alabama vs. LSU
The running game here will be the key for the Elephants of Alabama if they plan on winning this game. But without the team’s key superstar Alexander, the running game looks very dismal. Not to mention the fact that Zow, the Bama QB is not 100% either, these two factors just might be the downfall of this game. If the LSU defense can pressure Zow out of the pocket and get a few good hits on him, look for the Roll Tide express to just stop all together. I look for LSU to surprise everyone by winning this one. 
Alabama 24 LSU 28

Game # 2
Arkansas vs. Ole Miss
This game is perhaps a complete toss up as to who will win. Nevertheless, if the Rebels can squeeze out a victory here, the Razorbacks are pretty much out of contention for any bowl appearance. The Rebels are looking good this year with a solid 4 man front defense and they are stopping the run efficiently. Close game here.
Arkansas 21 Ole Miss 24

Game # 3
Auburn vs. Central Florida
Even though the Knights are not in a strong division, they are perhaps the best team out of Florida to just surprise you. The Knights always put up a good fight when they have the opportunity to play a big time school in football. Usually they can spoil your season as well. Noting that, with the return of starter Ben Leard to the offense, I look for Auburn to get in a groove and go on a 3 game winning streak and perhaps, by luck, make it to a bowl game. Look for the A-TRAIN to pull into the station with a victory this weekend by 10.
Auburn 24 Central Florida 14

Game # 4
Florida vs. Vanderbilt
“Welcome to the Swamp” is what the Gator fans will be chanting as Vandie walks into the stadium this weekend for the butt whipping they are about to receive. Look for this game to merely be a warm up for the big dance the Gators have coming for them in two weeks when they host Florida State. Vandie is no match for the fun and gun offense of Mr. Spurrier. Look for the Gators to light up the scoreboard several times and early.
Florida 58 Vandie 27

Game # 5
Kentucky vs. Mississippi State
With the rare Thursday night game here, the Wildcats hope that they can rebound from the whipping they got at Georgia two weeks ago. However, the other Bulldogs in the SEC look to turn the Cats into kitty litter as they just wear them out. The State team has been very confident in their abilities so far this season with posting a top 25 ranking in the country. The Good news for Kentucky here is that basketball starts up in 4 weeks.
Kentucky 21 State 35

Game # 6
Tennessee vs. Notre Dame
If Tennessee wants to have any shot at winning the National title again this year this is a game where they have got to win and have to win BIG. The Volunteers must come out and just completely dominate the Fighting Irish from start to finish. If they can do this then they have a shot at moving back into the top 3. Now in order for them to win the title, they need the help of Florida in two weeks to beat the Noles. If these two key items happen, coupled with a Virginia Tech loss and Tennessee winning the rest of their games big, you could very well see Penn State vs. Tennessee for the championship at the end of the season. But first they have got to get by Notre Dame.
Tennessee 42 Notre Dame 21

For those of you keeping up with the schedules, the following team has the week off: South Carolina
