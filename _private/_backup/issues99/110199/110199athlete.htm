6’4 * 220 * F * Senior * Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

Maulvi is another of the five seniors on the 1999-2000 basketball squad. He is coming off a very good junior season for the Cougars which saw him lead the team in scoring (15.1 ppg) and rebounding (8.6 rpg). He was second on the team in assists even though he spent the year as primarily a post player. He posted 12 double-doubles on the year, with his best performance coming against Ft. Valley State when he put up 24 points and grabbed 14 rebounds. His best scoring day came against Augusta State on January 9th when he tallied 27 points.

Juco: Maulvi actually attended two junior colleges prior to coming to CSU. As a freshman, he played at Jacksonville(TX) Junior College where he helped his team to a 25-5 record and a number five national ranking. Last season he played at Jones County (MS) Junior College where he averaged 13.8 points, 7.2 rebounds, and 3.5 assists.


High School: Maulvi graduated in 1993 from Southern High in Philadelphia where he also participated in boxing and football. He helped lead the Rams to a record of 18-2 in his senior year. 

Personal: Maulvi is the son of William B. Harrison and Willie Mae Harrison. He has three brothers and two sisters. He is majoring in criminal justice at CSU and enjoys church and fishing outside of basketball. He also enjoys listening to music and working with youth on the playgrounds in his hometown of Philadelphia.
