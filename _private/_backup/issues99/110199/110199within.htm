This week has been crazy . . . and it’s only Tuesday! I have often stopped to ask myself “Why does everything always have to happen at once?” I’ve accomplished plenty already, but every time I think I can stop, I remember one more thing I have to do (like write this column).

By the time you read this, the most imminent of my tasks will be over and I will be sane once more (or as close to sane as I’ve ever been, at any rate). In short, I will be over the midterm funk. But, since I am currently drowning in academia, I have decided to get some help on my column this week. I asked all of my friends to give me some of the profound questions that they face in life (well, on second thought, maybe they’re not so profound). 

So, in honor of all the times we sit back during midterm and ask “Why?” about our crammed schedules and our endless work, here is a list of random inquiries that might jog your exhausted brain or even make you laugh. 

--Why do “Dumb Blonde Jokes” always apply to women? Aren’t there enough dumb blonde men out there? 

--Why does the air-conditioning run constantly in Howard Hall when it is 40 degrees outside? Is a frigid room conducive to learning?

--Why can’t all doors open both ways? (Thus avoiding the embarrassment caused by pushing a door that should be pulled, or vice versa . . .)

--Why do biscuits and hoecakes taste differently? (You’ll have to check with Ms. Fussell for an explanation of that one ;o)

--Why do parents always ask, “Why did you do that?” Do they expect a rational answer?

--Why do some professors wait until the end of class to return tests? Isn’t the notion of impending doom an impediment to concentration?

--Why are professors always on time on the days that we are late, and late on the days that we are on time?

--Why do you get sick if you drink beer then liquor but not liquor then beer?

--Why are most statues of Buddha fat and jolly when he was really emaciated for most of his life?

--Why is cafeteria food always too salty or not salty enough?

--Why are more people afraid of public speaking than of death? (Personally, I would rather suffer total embarrassment than die in a flaming car crash, but that’s just me.)

--Why does a house burn down while a piece of paper burns up?

--Is it possible to be “gruntled” before you are disgruntled?

--Why does the nameless guy in the red suit, who gets beamed down with the crew of the Starship Enterprise, never get beamed back up? Those poor guys must have the life expectancies of fruit flies . . .

--Why are dough, bough, and tough pronounced differently while you, blew, rue, and through rhyme? 

--Why do we sing “Rock-a-bye baby in the treetop . . . when the bough breaks the cradle will fall, down will come baby, cradle and all” to children? Maybe that’s why we all have those “falling” dreams . . . in our infancy, we were cruelly implanted with a fear of falling out of trees.

So, I hope you have enjoyed this short trip into the meaningless queries of life. I’ll be back next week with something a little more coherent to tickle your brain, but until then, have a good week and don’t let all the “Whys” get to you.
