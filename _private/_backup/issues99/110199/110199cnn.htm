CNN Espanol President Rolando Santos will address “The Presentation of Hispanics in the Media” 12:30 p.m. Thurs., Nov.11, in the Davidson Student Center Columbus Room. 

The lecture is presented through Columbus State University’s Center for International Education (CIE). Admission is free. 

Call Neal McCrillis at (706) 565-4036 for more information.
As president of CNN’s 24-hour Spanish language news network, Santos oversees all daily operations and editorial content of CNN en Espanol and CNNRadio Noticias. 

In addition, he supervises Spanish-language newsgathering operations for both services, including a fully bilingual staff in Atlanta, a world-wide network of Spanish-speaking correspondents and eight full-service bureaus in Miami, New York, Washington, D.C., Los Angeles, Jerusalem, London, Mexico City and Buenos Aires.

Santos has been involved in newsgatherings for both radio and television throughout his career. Most recently he was executive producer for network news for Telemundo. 

Prior to this, he was news director at KVEA Channel 52, a Telemundo owned and operated station in Los Angeles. 

In this position, he oversaw international news coverage for the Telemundo news stations throughout the United States. 

During his tenure, the station won the 1992 Emmy award for best newscast marking, the first time a Spanish-language newscast won such an award.

Prior to joining KVEA, Santos worked for KTTV-FOX News, also in Los Angeles, as an assistant news director where his duties included managing the daily editorial process, story selection and content of the newscast while overseeing a 91-person staff. 

Other posts held by Santos within the news arena are news anchor, reporter, bureau chief, and producer in stations such as KPIX-TV, San Francisco; KSL-TV, Salt Lake City, and KMOL-TV, San Antonio among others.

Born in Eagle Pass, Texas, Santos is fluent in both Spanish and English. He earned a bachelor of arts degree in journalism from Texas A&M University. 

He was UPI Regional Winner for writing and has served numerous awards for live reporting.
