Hey honey... you want a drink?

Women always look out for each other, right? We walk each other to our cars after a late class, or we call to let each other know that we got home from the club okay.

But one thing we rarely do is discourage our good friends from accepting drinks from men we don’t know.

I know what you are thinking... hold on girlfriend, don’t tell me you want me to tell that fine man he can’t buy me a drink. Girl, I came here tonight to drink for free and I am looking good--so back off!

Well, that is exactly what I am saying. We take care of each other in every aspect of our lives except when we go out. For some reason it is acceptable for some random guy to try and liquor us up.

I was at the Loft last night, and a comedian made a really good point. When a man is so drunk that he falls off the stool, women avoid him like the plague. However, if a women was to start to teeter...there would be a fight over her before she even hit the ground. “Do you want another drink, darlin’?”

Maybe it is because some men don’t stand a chance in picking a girl up unless she is totally blitzed out of her mind, but men like to pick up on drunk women. Don’t we have an obligation to each other to prevent something tragic from happening?

I am not saying that every guy at every bar is a potential rapist or psychopath. . . I am just saying that we should all be more careful.
We have all heard about the date rape drug known as Rohypnol, also known as “Roofies.” I know . . . I know . . . that will never happen to me, right?

Actually, the odds of it happening are greater than you think. Think about the last time you were out. Who bought your drinks? Did you watch your drink the entire time (was it left unattended)? Odds are you probably were not even thinking about it.

That’s the problem, and that is how bad things happen to good people. Did you know that 91% of female college students that are acquaintance raped don’t report it. Would you report it?

My point is this . . . when we are out together we should do everything possible to protect each other. Don’t go crazy and start seeing problems where there are none, but we need to be more cautious.

We all have that mentality that it will never happen to us . . . maybe because we are young we think that we are invincible. Reality tells us that this is not true, but we are bound and determined to hold on to that belief.

The law of averages tells us that the more we put ourselves at risk the greater the odds that something bad will happen. 

When I go out with my friends I try and be aware of what is going on around us. It only takes one time for your life to be changed forever. It only takes one drink . . . and you will never be the same.

Don’t get paranoid and refuse to enjoy your life, but please be cautious. I know that it is flattering when a handsome stranger waltzes up to you in a bar and wants to buy you a drink. 

Next time you say yes take some precautions: Go with him to the bar and watch the drink being made. Carry your own drink back to the table. Take your drink with you to the dance floor or to the ladies room. It is not being paranoid to take steps in preventing a disaster. 

Also, remember to never drink from someone else's drink . . . you may not know what is in there. If you are responsible enough to go out to a bar and have a drink, then be responsible enough not to put yourself in unnecessary situations. 

That goes for your friends too. So what if they get mad at you or think you are acting like an old woman? You have an obigation as their friend to help keep them out of danger. 

If a women suspects she has been affected by a date rape drug, she should get tested with in 4-8 hours, 12 for urine tests. It leaves the system very rapidly.

Have fun but be responsible...it only takes one.
