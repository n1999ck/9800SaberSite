ROAR!

Letters to the Editor

 

Okay, here it comes: this is my annual ALCOHOL message. I know, some of you will say that I’m repeating myself from last year, but it’s something that needs to be said over and over to get through to the greatest number of folks.

First and foremost, everyone needs to remember that alcohol is a very powerful DRUG. With that said, it also must be reiterated that, although controlled under most circumstances, it is a legal drug. Now, what “circumstances” are we talking about?

There are several. Possession and/or consumption of alcohol is legal for those twenty-one years old, except when they are operating a motor vehicle, on their job, or attending class. In other words, the only time they can use this drug is in their “off-campus” time, in their homes, or in licensed establishments. For those under twenty-one, there is no legal time or place to be “holding” or “using” alcohol.

Another “circumstance” in which alcohol is controlled is at any school-sanctioned event. Unless it is specifically authorized in the approval process for such events, alcohol is a no-no. That means any cook out, Greek function, picnic, or what-have-you must be “blessed” by the approving office before the first 12-ouncer is uncapped. Don’t get caught in the old “well, they’ve always let us do it before” trap — make sure your event is approved before you start.

All right, that’s about all that needs to be said about this — with one exception: NO DRINKING AND DRIVING! Get a designated driver, walk, take the bus, or just stay over, but don’t get behind the wheel after “partaking of the grape.” We want you around all semester, not just until the first party.
