TKE philanthropy a recording breaking success
Chris Rayner / Tau Kappa Epsilon

Eta Iota buckles up!
Ladonna Jones / Publicity Chair, Alpha Kappa Alpha

 

 

 

 

 

 


The brothers of Tau Kappa Epsilon devote much of their time to various philanthropies. On Friday November 12, 1999 the fraternity was involved in the can food drive sponsored by the local radio station B101 and the Salvation Army.

The fraternity started their efforts at six o’clock a.m. collecting food and loading trucks. The day ended around 7:30 p.m. By the end of the day they had helped collect an amazing 11,331 pieces of non-perishable food items.

The current record for any organization is only around 1,500 pieces. Tau Kappa Epsilon helped extend this number by more than 9,000 pieces. 

As one gentleman from the Salvation Army commented, “I have been doing this for twenty-two years and I have never seen anything like this before.”

TKE was glad for the opportunity to help out the community by collecting food for those who need it. The brothers were proud to be part of this record breaking effort.

Thanks to the brothers who were involved; Ricky Dumlao, Rob Piller, and Josh Horton for all your help. And a special thank you to all those who came out to donate food.
