Dogma

Ben Affleck , Matt Damon , Linda Fiorentino, Salma Hayek, Jason Mewes , Kevin Smith, and Jason Lee round out the cast of “Dogma.” Here is the scoop on the newest projec from Kevin Smith, better know as Silent Bob. Good and Evil are at it again, and this time the battlefield is New Jersey.

Dogma has been called a “comedy parable” and is sure to offend somebody--probably everybody. The film revolves around a woman who feels her prayers haven’t been answered. Then, out of nowhere, an angel appears in her bedroom and proclaims to be the voice of God. He then declares her the potential savior of humanity. Two angels were kicked out of heaven and have plotted a way to override God and creep back home. If they do this, then God is proven wrong and all humanity becomes no more. 

Affleck and Damon play the angels and Chris Rock appears as the thirteenth apostle. The thirteenth apostle was apparently left out of the Bible because he is black. Alanis Morissette makes an appearance as God. Add all this up and you have a good story, a great cast , and a decent movie that is absolutely going to offend most viewers. I loved it. 


End of Days

This movie revolves around the experiences of Jericho Cane, an ex-cop, who is the centerpiece of what has been called a “chilling, horrific thriller set at the cusp of the millennium.” The evil of all evils is setting up a worldwide takeover, and the countdown to the end of days is approaching. 

So what do we do? Arnold will handle it. End of Days stars Arnold Schwarzenegger, Robin Tunney, Gabriel Byrne, Kevin Pollak and Rod Steiger . The soundtrack includes a new track by a new Guns ‘N’ Roses called “Oh My God.” I think this movie will be better than this or any other review makes it out to be.
