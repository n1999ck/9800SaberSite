Public Safety means YOUR Safety
Sgt. Jim Boling / Public Safety Officer

This week, we are issuing an appeal for YOUR help.

We need to locate some property which came up missing from the Columbus Room in Davidson Center sometime on Friday, March 26.

Three carved wooden figures, representing three of the major gods in the Buddhist faith, disappeared from a display during the celebration of Diversity Week. These figures, each about eight-nine inches tall, are carved from a dark red wood, similar to mahogany. (See photograph.)

The religious significance of these statues, and their sentimental value to their owner, vastly outweigh their commercial value, which leads us to make this appeal. Since we assume that they were Inadvertently packed up with something else being moved (After all, who would deliberately steal another persons religious icons?), we are asking that any person who has these items, or knows their whereabouts, call, e-mail, or send a note to the Department of Public Safety, and let us know where these treasures can be found. We will make sure they are returned to their owner, no questions asked.

Look closely at the photograph included in this column, and - please - keep an eye out for these three holy objects. They really do need to be back with the others (there are more in the Buddhist pantheon), and not just decorating someone’s mantle or hall table. Consider how you would feel if you lost your own religious relics, and how glad and relieved you would be to have them returned. Thanks for your help.
