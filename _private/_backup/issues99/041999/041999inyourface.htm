In your face!
with Stephanie Adams

Show me the money!

When I began doing research for this article I had no idea that I would wind up in the middle of something so complicated. All I wanted to do was to find out how much of Student Government ‘s money could be used for organizational needs on this campus. Do yourself a favor- don’t go looking for any answers in the SGA office. They can barely agree on what kind of coffee to drink.

I have received conflicting information from people who are supposed to be working together to help the student body. For example, I went seeking information about the SGA budget. As a member of a social organization of this campus, I have always thought that SGA had some money set aside that organizations could request.

Want to know what I found out? SGA has a yearly budget of $30,000. After looking over a copy of the 1999-2000 Student Government budget it became clear that $14,000 of this budget is set aside for supplies, events, and organizational needs. Well, surprise, surprise. According to certain persons in the SGA office, this is simply not true. No amount of the budget was ever meant to be set aside for organizational needs.

Organizations have received some money as a courtesy over the past couple of years. I’m sorry... I must have been mistaken...poor little ole’ me, I must have forgotten how to read. Here is a direct quote for a copy of the 1999-2000 Student Government budget that I received from someone in the SGA office (don’t worry boys, it’s all about freedom of information).

“Account #95-30-1-31-101-000-71400” This account is for all supplies, events, and (this is a shock) ORGANIZATIONAL NEEDS. It started out with $14,000 for the school year of 1998-1999. Supplies have been purchased from places such as K-Mart, Wal-Mart, and Chapman’s. $3,000 of this money was used for the 1999 Homecoming Ball at the Bradley Theatre.

I have documentation of eight organizations that have requested money from Student Government this year. Of these eight, three organizations were denied and none of the organizations received the full amount they requested. Some of the organizations that received money even had their own budget. Less than $1100 has been given out, according to my documentation, since February 2, 1999. If this doesn’t upset you, it should.

So where did the rest of the money go? Well, I want you to go find out for yourself (be aware that you will probably be told everything is DYNAMITE, ain’t that right J.J.)?

First of all, I think campus organizations should make an effort to become aware of the services that are available to them. From what I understand the whole concept of the SGA forum has become a joke. For those of you that don’t know...SGA is made up of elected executive members, elected senators, and appointed representatives from the various organizations on campus. The executive council must pre-approve (or in many cases pre-disapprove) any document that will be voted upon before it can go before the forum. Once in the forum the executive council will make its recommendation to the forum and they vote. What is so sad is that the majority of the senators and representatives are in such a hurry to get out of there that they don’t even question the executive council’s recommendation.

You are probably thinking, wait a minute... I thought she was angry at the executive council. I am angry with all the parties involved. I put my trust in my representative to act in the best interest of my organization while in the SGA forum. How can she though, when questions are not encouraged and everyone seems to think that what the executive council says goes (Hey... that sounds good to me, you ready to go grab a beer).

According to one source, even if a person does question the recommendation of the executive council it may damage their ability to get further business passed (there’s no reason to hold a grudge...can’t we just kiss and make up). Even worse, the executive council, according to my sources, has never made an effort to educate the senators and representatives about the policies and procedures involved in these types of meetings. We are told that we must have a representative at each meeting. They will have one recognized vote and if they miss more than two meetings then that organization will not be recognized on this campus! Off with their heads!!!

This is a two way street. The student body has much more power than they realize. Stop complaining and go find out what exactly the rules are. Don’t take someone’s word for it just because they convinced 77 people to vote last year to elect them into office. That’s right, only 77 people voted in the election last year.

Speaking of those who are in office, don’t think I am not grateful for all the work that you do. There are tons of things that our executive members do behind the scenes that the average student is not aware of. We are grateful...extremely grateful. But...(oh you knew it was coming), I would much rather you be interested in me as a person. How many of you take the time to actually listen to the opinion of the ordinary student. We may be misinformed and un- involved, but when someone you have never met tries to tell you that there is a problem with your job you should listen to the underlying meaning. Bottom line... the students (those of us who give a damn) are unhappy with certain things. We need clarification on certain facts, not just your usual line of bull. I have no interest on engaging in a conversation with someone whose soul direction is to make me feel like I am a complete idiot. Enough said- I think I have made my point. I would just like to say that I wish the students were more interested in what happens on this campus, and I wish that the powers that be were more interested in the average student. We can’t complain about things on campus and not do anything about it. You have a voice... use it. For those to whom we give the power to work for us can also use the power to work against us. I will end by quoting the campaign speech I received from a person running for senator, “Vote for me... I promise I will try to go to at least half of the meetings.”
