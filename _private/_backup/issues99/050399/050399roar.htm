ROAR!

Dear Editor,

I would like to discuss an issue that should be important to all of us - the doctors at the health clinic. Recently I became sick and had to visit the health clinic. I saw Dr. Lewis and after about a two minute exam he prescribed medicine for me and sent me on my merry way. Two days later I was back in the doctor’s office feeling worse than before. This time I saw Dr. Sexton. Again after a two minute exam, I received my medication and left. Now one week later, having gotten progressively worse, I visited Dr. Sexton again. After describing my symptoms and again taking two minutes to exam me, he decided I had allergies and gave me medicine. This time I was not convinced. I know I had symptoms that did not go along with allergies - mainly chills and body aches. So I drove to my parents house, 1 1/2 hours from Columbus, to see my family doctor. After a very thorough examination, my doctor diagnosed me with the flu and said I was very contagious. The flu is a very recognizable ailment and one that any qualified doctor should be able to recognize. I’m sure our doctors are good physicians, but they do not take enough time. They rush through the examination, listen to your symptoms, and within five minutes you are done. These doctors do not know us. They cannot decide what we have that quickly. I say we deserve the best care possible. If these doctors are not willing to take some time with their patients, then let’s get some new ones!!

CSU Student

Where did this term, ‘college idiots’ come from? It came, as a result of people getting a college degree and even a master’s degree, and still not knowing much about the field they majored in. This result comes from several reasons.

First of all, it comes from too much cramming for tests so you can try and make a passing grade. Then it is so much material that even if you’re fortunate enough to pass the test, you’re surely not going to retain it. I’m talking three to six chapter tests. I mean, get real! If a person is lucky enough to graduate, they’re only going to retain approximately ten percent of the material or even less than that.

The solution? Teachers, and the Board of Regents need to wake up and give a two chapter test bi-weekly, discuss former chapters along with latter chapters and then give a general comprehensive final test on the most important points. This is the one and only solution to the problem. And, until this problem is dealt with properly, it will continue to exist and plague our future graduates. I, for one, think this is a very serious problem, not to be taken lightly. I know, if I was a teacher, I would hate to be partly responsible for graduating a ‘college idiot’.

I, as a student am very concerned about all this cramming. I’m not talking about waiting until the last minute to study, but I’m referring to cramming three to six chapters into one test. That’s, on the average, only about five-hundred pages one is required to remember, just to get a good grade on the test. I really hope this gets published and, further, yet, I pray that every teacher will take a copy of this to their meeting with the Board of Regents. You know, I really would hate to be classified, after finishing college, with a statement like “Oh well; just another ‘college idiot’.”

CSU Student
