The Insider's Edge
Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor

First off, wrestling fans received sad news two weeks ago. “Ravishing” Rick Rude passed away earlier this month, and the cause of death appears to be a heart attack. Rude was best known for his stint in the WWF where he defeated The Ultimate Warrior for the Intercontinental Championship. Rude also had a reign in WCW as the International Champion - a title formerly known as the NWA World title. His last role in wrestling was as a manager for longtime friend Curt Henning.

R.I.P. Rick Rude 1958-1999. Rude was rumored to be looking for a return to active wrestling with his former employer, the WWF.

It is rumored that Scott Hall was ready to make his comeback last Monday night. However, friend and booker Kevin Nash decided to hold off Hall’s return for a little while longer, because Hall’s return right now will not fit into the storyline leading up to this next pay-per-view fight. When Hall does return he will be working Pay Per View and Monday Nitro shows only. Hall’s working agreement with WCW has him off for all house shows. Hall recently got back together with his wife Dana and he is putting family before wrestling. Good for you Scott Hall.

Word on the net is that Triple H is expressing his disagreement in the way the WWF has been using him lately. HHH was against the heel turn when the idea was first presented to him. HHH wants DX to stay around and he might be pushing for the WWF to form a heel DX group that will be allied with the Corporation and will feud with the face DX group. Sound familiar? Remember WCW had the NWO split and feud last year? Well, it was a good story then, and I guess the revamped version will be ok too.

There is not much happening in wrestling right now that is all that exciting. Expect Kevin Nash to destroy DDP for the title at the next Pay-Per-View fight. Degeneration X should be hot in the WWF for awhile. The Hardy Boyz, Shawn Michaels, and maybe even Test or Val Venis may get involved with DX. The Austin/ McMahon being friends angle is interesting. WCW should be following the same trend, and in the near future new friendships and stables should be formed, probably involving Randy Savage, DDp, Nash, Scott Hall, Sting, Ric Flair and Goldberg as the centerpieces.

Let me tack this on the the end of this spill of useless ‘wrasllin’ information... Several weeks ago I did one of the most retarded things I have ever. I did this with a friend of mine, Brennen Knight. We drove into the middle of Nowhere, Georgia and trained to be professional wrestlers.

Well, I trained once. Brennen has been back and is aiming to start working in the ring sometime around June. I, on the other hand still have trouble typing, walking and thinking straight because I tried to “wrestle” a guy named RazorBlade. I put “wrestle” in quotes because it has an alternative definition when it applies to that particular day in the ring. (see bottom for alternative definition ). Check this out ... the ring is in the guy’s back yard, and is made out of plywood and hose pipe for ring ropes. Talk about hardcore wrestling. I learned my lesson ... I love wrestling, and I now have the highest level of respect for those guys. The matches might be staged, and the outcome is predetermined, but it is very very real. Good luck Brennen.

* “wrestle” - to get smacked around like a sissy girl
