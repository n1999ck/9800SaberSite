Public Safety Means YOUR Safety
Sgt. Jim Boling / Public Safety Officer

Well, here we are at the end of the academic year and beginning of that long-awaited summer break! Some of our number will be heading home, while others buckle down to “blitz” courses, cramming a semester’s worth of study into one or two weeks. Many others, year-round members of the campus community, will continue in their routine work pattern. Unfortunately, this last group includes Sneaky Sam and his cohorts, the thieves and vandals who prey on all the other groups.

Sam and his pals will continue to steal bookbags (unless YOU keep them under your physical control), continue to rob and assault folks (unless YOU take the proper precautions, travel in groups, etc.), and continue to steal university and personal property which isn’t safe-guarded (unless YOU take the time to secure desks, rooms, labs, and offices). Sam knows there will befewer people on campus for the next couple of months, which means fewer witnesses and less likelihood of getting caught in his nefarious activities.

Sooooo, let’s all keep our eyes open especially wide and help put Sneaky Sam and his partners on notice that everyone is playing their part in crime prevention and detection, not merely coasting through the summer period as blissful victims-to-be. Watch YOUR property and vehicle; be responsible for YOUR living area and workspace; guard YOUR neighborhood against strangers and suspicious persons; and, above all, protect YOURSELF by exercising caution and restraint in your summertime activities.

If you do these things, either here in the University community or at home in your own neighborhood, it will make for a safe and pleasant summer. The officers of Public Safety will always be there for you, but we can’t be everywhere at once, so please take care and help us help you by following these common sense guidelines.

Have a wonderful time and come back safe and sound in the Fall. Or, for you graduates, congratulations, and good luck in whatever endeavor you choose to pursue.
