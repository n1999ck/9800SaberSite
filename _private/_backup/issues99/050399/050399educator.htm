Educator of the Year announced
Press Release / Office of Public Relations, CSU

Columbus State University has named Shawn Cruzen, assistant professor of astronomy, as Educator of the Year. The announcement highlighted the April 30 Scholastic Honors Convocation in Fine Arts Hall. Other top honorees included Bill Chappell (Faculty Service Award), Mary Lindquist (Faculty Research and Scholarship Award), student Anna Mion (Faculty Cup) and Emily Nerness (Phi Kappa Phi and Academic Recognition Awards).

In addition to teaching physics, Cruzen directs the Mead Observatory at CSU’s Coca-Cola Space Science Center. He joined the CSU faculty in 1997, and has published three papers, one investigating the nature of active galaxies and two describing a sample of galaxies found within a giant void. He is currently working on another paper describing these void galaxies.

Primarily through the space science center, Cruzen’s community involvement has included directing astronomy workshops for teachers, quarterly public seminars on astronomy, distance learning projects using the GSAMS (Georgia Statewide Academic and Medical System) network and developing summer camps in space sciences. He holds a philosophy doctorate in Physics from the University of Nevada-Las Vegas.

Chappell, a CSU faculty member since 1976, serves as associate dean of the College of Arts and Letters and professor of Political Science. He is recognized for leading the development of CSU’s Master of Public Administration program as the program’s former director. He is also widely perceived as a mentor to faculty and department chairpersons, and his service on prominent campus committees is extensive. A university liaison to the community, his expertise has been consistently sought by off-campus organizations including the news media.

Lindquist is the Fuller E. Callaway Professor of Mathematics Education in CSU’s College of Education. Since joining CSU in 1984, she has authored or co-authored 19 books or monographs, written 17 chapters, published 22 professional papers and has made several presentations at national and international conferences. She also has been awarded approximately $1.7 million in research grants. She holds a doctorate in Mathematics Education from the University of Wisconsin.

In receiving the Faculty Cup, Anna Mion earned the highest CSU-student honor, signifying superior academic achievement, participation in university organizations, leadership and service.

Also honored as the outstanding 1998-99 history major, Mion (3.97 GPA) received less than an A-grade only once (a “B”) in four years of CSU course work. Other academic achievements include Phi Kappa Phi induction (spring ‘98), Board of Regents Study Abroad Scholarship (France) recipient (summer ‘97), Dean’s list 1995-99, Outstanding Junior History Major (spring ‘98), and she represented CSU as a Hope Scholar for the University System’s Appreciation Banquet for Governor Zell Miller.

Communication major Emily Nerness (4.0 GPA) received the Academic Recognition and Phi Kappa Phi Senior awards. Previous honors include the WRBL Communication Scholarship (1997 and 1999) and a listing in Who’s Who Among Students in American Colleges and Universities. She is president of the CSU Communication Club and a member of Phi Kappa Phi.
