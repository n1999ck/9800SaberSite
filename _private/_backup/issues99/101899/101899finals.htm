You’ve worked hard all semester, studying, doing the assigned work, and taking enough notes to write a book. You think that your suffering is over, then you find there is one step left, finals. Finals are designed to measure the students mastery of the course and its work. Most of the time, they are scheduled during the weekdays, and in some cases, several on the same day. 

What would you say if some of them where scheduled for certain times on Saturdays? The subject was discussed in a meeting with Dr. Carl Wallman, Ph.D., Dean of Enrollment Services and Registrar. 

According to Dr. Wallman, weekday finals being offered on Saturdays is not a new thing, they have been done this way before. This arrangement comes into play primarily when there is not enough time during the school week to schedule all of the finals--the length of time for most finals is about two hours. Therefore, Saturday finals can provide more available time so the student will not be bombarded at one time with so many tests. This also works out for those students taking evening classes. 

There are some that would like to have most of their finals on the same day, and others that don’t; probably because of a more important engagement that cannot be delayed. According to Dr. Wallman, since the times for finals are announced long before the actual dates of testing, he feels that “the schedule should not be a problem, and ample arrangements should be able to be made to take the test on the date it is given.” 

Concerned members of CSU’s student body had different opinions on the subject. 

When asked if weekend finals seem like a good way to loosen the testing load, Candace Washington said, “It would depend on what I have to do. If I do not have anything more important to do, then I will come and take the test on the day it is. If I do have something else to do, then I will take it later.” She also feels that if students were given a choice to take finals on a Saturday or at the original time, it would be very convenient. 

When another student, Michalla Quartimon, was asked about her view on the topic, she responded, “Yes, Saturday finals for weekday classes would be good, but only in the morning, not at night. For classes that only meet one day, they are not needed.”
