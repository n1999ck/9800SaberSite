White stucco and gray cement are the color schemes on the first floor of the Turner Center these days. Just take a trip over there and see all the work that is being done on the building. Take the elevator to the 2nd floor, and you’ll be amazed at the changes. New carpet, and wall paneling makes the 2nd floor look like a new building. 

Hal Gibson, CSU’s director of plant operations says that the renovations and the building itself are for the community. The Center is a facility that provides the community with a spectrum of services ranging from continuing education classes to facilities for conferences to art and music lessons for children. 

Gibson says that the Turner Center is one of the areas of CSU that the community interacts with the most. He believes that the changes and additions will allow CSU to better serve the community.

A “face-lift to the campus” is what Gibson calls the $1.7 million renovation. The project includes renovating both floors of the building, remodeling the lobby and adding on a new banquet hall. With 3,200 square feet of additional space for the new banquet hall and kitchen, the old banquet hall will be separated into smaller sections. The room will become part of the new kitchen, storage space for the kitchen and for the conference room. 

Spirits are high in the Turner Center these days. Just talk to some of the employees there. An employee of the Regional Law Enforcement Training Center is excited about the academy changing locations from the Turner to the Turner Annex (just across the parking lot), which has also been renovated.

Claudia Bently, a registration specialist at the Turner Center, is enthusiastic about the changes in the Turner Center as well. And she says that the staff at the Turner Center are always open to suggestions as to how they might better serve the university and community.
Hal Gibson says that the renovations to the Turner Center will completely change the appearance of the building. Gibson is optimistic about the project and states, “The changes will take [CSU] into the new millennium with a strong presence in the business community.”
