 Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor

 

I am writing to express my disgust with teachers whose lesson plans apparently consist of a barrage of rude, ignorant, biased, or offensive comments.

Today, during a lecture on Aristotle’s theory of psyche (in a course that will remain anonymous), we as a class were informed that people who own expensive cars are displaying their ignorance, AIDS in America can be contributed to homosexuality, and that dogs are dumb, grotesque, and could easily “be eliminated” as an entire species at no cost to mankind. 

I am entirely fed up with having to endure these kind of ludicrous, offensive, and, most of all IRRELEVANT comments while I am supposedly “learning.”

I was willing to be mature and try to overlook this teacher’s constant condemnation of all the citizens of Columbus and students at CSU as somehow intellectually inferior and his labeling of anyone who watches television as “idiots” and “barbarians”. 

However, when his ignorant commentary turned to berating our nation’s armed forces, he seriously crossed the line. He began to refer to them as our “cry baby armies” who (and I directly quote) “run around saying, ‘I want better housing. I want better medical coverage,’ but don’t send me to war because I might get hurt.”

As the daughter of a man who has faithfully served his country as a member of the Air Force for nearly 20 years and who VOLUNTEERED to go to war when the crisis in Saudi Arabia occurred, I was beyond enraged by such an ignorant, judgmental, and completely uncalled for statement. 

Some people might call this being “overly sensitive.” However, when I sign up for a course in biology or mathematics or philosophy, I expect the course material to be centered around biology or mathematics or philosophy, not the professor’s closed-minded, judgmental attitude. Classrooms are for teaching and learning, not a forum for the professor to insult and attack anything or anyone that dares to differ from his closed-minded outlook. I certainly do not need to pay enormous matriculation fees to be offended.

In short, some of the professors at this institution need to wake up and realize how greatly they are obstructing the flow of knowledge by building dams out of their own self-righteous attitudes.
