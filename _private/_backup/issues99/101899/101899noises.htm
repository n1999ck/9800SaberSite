CSU’s Department of Theatre presents “Noises Off,” October 21-24 and 28-3 1, Thursdays, Fridays and Saturdays at 8 p.m. and Sundays at 2 p.m. in the Fine Arts Hall Auditorium.

Lloyd, played by Jason Horne, is trying desperately to pull together his production of “Nothing On” before it opens in 24 hours. But the actors have different plans. After observing a rehearsal in the first act, we are given a backstage look at what is going on within this hilarious cast. Between shoestrings tied together and attempted murders, the show still manages to hobble along.

Directed by Hazel Hall, Michael Frayn’s classic English farce with an incredible twist takes life on the CSU Mainstage in the Fine Arts Hall. Former students Jason Pearce and Horne take on leading roles along with returning students Jason Williams, Jenny Newell, Lewis Powell, and Bart Cunningham. Freshman cast members include Danielle Grantham, Joely Pittman, and Jessi Evans. 

Also appearing are understudies Amber Dupriest and Kyle Mote. This show is sure to leave you wondering what goes on backstage at any performance. After all, sometimes, the real show is backstage.

Admission is $10 adult, $8 senior citizen, military, alumni, $5 children (18 and under), $8 groups of 10-19, and $7 groups of 20 or more. 

For more information or to make reservations call the CSU Box Office at (706) 569-3179.
