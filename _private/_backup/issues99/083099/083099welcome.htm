Events welcome students to CSU
Jennifer Emmert / Staff Writer

On August 25, the Student Programming Council and Student Government sponsored welcome back events for CSU’s student body. Among these events were the Cougar Picnic, held in front of the Clock Tower, from 11:30 a.m.-2 p.m. 

The picnic gave students the chance to win door prizes, listen to music provided by local radio station B101 and socialize with other students. 

There was also a student organization fair set up by campus groups interested in recruiting new members. Some participating organizations were the Counseling Center, CSU Cheerleaders, Baptist Student Union, Georgia Coalition on Donation, CSU Psychology Club, and Freethought Society.

The Student Government also held a welcome back party for the night students in and around Howard Hall. 

There was also a party in Davidson Cafeteria sponsored by SPC. This year’s theme was “Party like it’s 1999.” The cafeteria was decorated with purple balloons and streamers. The party included a DJ, food, and games such as name that tune, name that movie sound bite, and, of course, a dance contest. 
This is the fourth year that this has been done and is only one of several parties that will be held this year by SPC.

The Student Programming Council will also begin a Brown Bag Series and a Coffee House. 
All of the SPC events are free of charge to CSU students. 

The Student Government Association will also be holding campus events this year as well. SGA will be holding its first forum at 12:30 p.m. on September 9. Policies and events relating to campus will be discussed. Student organizations will be represented and the meeting is open to all students.
