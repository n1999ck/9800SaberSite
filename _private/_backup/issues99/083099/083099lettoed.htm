Letter to the Editor

Ms. Walters,

Your editorial would be more appropriately titled “A reckless thought”. I really don’t appreciate you encouraging students to disregard the advice of their parents, teachers, preachers, etc. I certainly hope that nobody’s “little voice” tells them to act out violent fantasies such as those carried out at Columbine. I guess the parents of the perpetrators at Columbine should not have been criticized so harshly. After all, those boys were only listening to the voice inside their heads. The results were not very happy.

As people such as yourself continue to encourage the blatant disregard of authority, society will continue to become more unruly and lawless. Isn’t it amazing that there was much less school violence back in the 1950’s at a time when kids had much more access to guns than they do now? Of course, that was before we took all mention of God out of schools in order to avoid offending someone. The last thing we want to do is to offend anybody, even if it means telling them the truth. (By the way, the Ten Commandments come from Jewish Law, so I doubt very seriously that you will find any Jew offended by them as you suggested in your previous commentary!)

Sincerely,
David J. Murphy
