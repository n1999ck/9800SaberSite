Far away friends
Amy Walters / Editor in Chief

I think that this week I have suddenly discovered how important the Internet is in this day and age. And it’s not that I’ve used it for my classes or even for writing this paper. As of this week my little colstate email account is the only link I have with friends all over the southeast. 

One of my best friends from high school left two weeks ago to attend a college in Florida. Now I know that Florida is not as far away as California or New York, but for me it’s too far away for her to be. When you are used to talking to someone at least every other day it’s odd not being able to pick up the phone and call them or just drive over to their house to visit. Another friend of mine also moved away this year to attend another college in Georgia. Of course, they are closer than Florida, but still to far away for many visits, especially with the schedule I keep. This was someone I talked to nearly every day of my life last year, so without them to laugh, cry, or just be stupid with it’s almost like they moved to another side of the world. 

With that being said I have become obsessive about checking email. At least four or five times a day I hit the pegasus mail icon on my desktop hoping to have a message from an old friend. I’m like a kid in a grocery store- you know what I mean, when you and your parents went into a store and you couldn’t wait to get to the counter so perhaps you could beg and get a piece of candy or gum. It’s that type of anticipation as I sit down at my desk. Of course, many times I am sorely disappointed (like the child in the store) when I open up my mail and nothing is there (except maybe a forward from my old roommate). But when I do receive a message I sit and read it from beginning to end, hoping to catch a glimpse into what my friends are doing, how their lives are going in their new cities. And as I absorb what they have said I try to think how I’m going to it all that I want to say back into email form (those are the times I really wish they were here for me to talk to).

If you have friends that are far away, drop them a letter every now and then. It may brighten their day and, if they get as excited as I do, they’ll probably respond quickly and tell you what’s going on with them. Even if you are having a bad day, tell them about it. They probably want to be there for you and may even have some good advice for you.

P.S. If anyone wants to send me an email, I’ll be very happy.
