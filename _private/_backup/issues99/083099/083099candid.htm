Campus Candid: Jason Ezzell
Clarissa Andrews / Features Editor

Business major Jason Ezzell, who informed me that he will be 21 in September, is an intelligent and fascinating person; he plays at least five instruments, including drums, guitar, piano, mandolin, and bass, and is currently the guitarist for Digital Cricket. He listens to jazz and blues as well as bands such as Widespread Panic, Phish, and the Grateful Dead. Leigh Daniel, Jason’s girlfriend, swears that the Dixie Chicks is his favorite group, but he denies this, saying he prefers the Allman Brothers and Pink Floyd. Jason does, however, admit to admiring the “great harmony and the songwriting ability” of the Dixie Chicks.

When he’s not in school, Jason works at Art’s Music Shop and enjoys “hanging out with my sweetie, Leigh. I’m pretty low-key,” he says. Jason is an animal lover who owns a rabbit and shares a dog with Leigh. His favorite movie is The Shawshank Redemption (Leigh says it’s really Pretty Woman), and his favorite book is Adrift by Steven Callahan. “It’s a true story about a man who is lost at sea for 76 days and survives,” he explains. Jason is no stranger to the sea himself-over the summer, he visited Atlantic Beach, where he entertained himself by surfing.

Out of all the characters on Cartoon Network, Jason most identifies with Space Ghost. This is because, “I like the way he talks.” His favorite Victoria’s Secret model is Laetitia Casta, which he couldn’t spell without the assistance of Sports Illustrated. If he could go to Europe and visit only one place, Jason says it would be Stonehenge-he thinks the rock arrangements are “unbelievable because no one knows how they got there. A lot of people think it was set up to be a calendar for planting, or maybe a temple, but no one is sure.” Jason is an avid guitar and guitar pick collector; he has ten guitars and about three hundred guitar picks. One of his favorite picks came from a Kenny Wayne Sheppard concert in Athens - “He threw it out into the audience, and I scrounged it up off the floor.” 

Jason attended Pacelli High School before leaving Columbus to attend the University of Georgia for a year and a half. Right now, he’s “five hours a way from being a junior.”
