Summer Concert Fun
Jennifer Kump / Columnist

 

 

After five years at CSU I am finally graduating this summer. For the past few weeks I have been reflecting on my time here and what lessons to take with me to graduate school.

When entering college we think that this is going to be the best time of our lives. For the most part it is, but it is also the hardest time of our lives.
Everything comes with checks and balances. You lose your innocence in college but you gain maturity.

The hardest initial loss is the loss of high school friends. In high school we all think that we will stay in touch, but unfortunately not everyone’s path is the same. 

Some friends wonder off to UGA and others to Auburn. Some even leave the state. We wish them luck and happiness but at the same time we are angry that they are leaving us behind.
The balance to this loss comes in the form of new friends and bonds we make in college. These friends are different than the ones we have in high school. They have been there during the most confusing yet exhilarating times of our young lives. I believe this is what helps form a lasting friendship that goes beyond graduation. 

College teaches you to become a more productive person, a more intelligent person, and helps prepare you for the world.

All these things are important attributes, but I think the most important lessons in college are on a more personal level.

To say that you discover yourself is an understatement. When I think about how much I have changed over the past five years I am amazed.
To go from a shy, innocent girl who never really had many friends to a strong, proud women who speaks her mind, is a triumph in my book.
Because it all comes down to you. What have you done over the past four or in my case five years will last throughout time. Ten years from now it is doubtful that the lasting memory of CSU will be a class you took or a paper you wrote.

You will reflect on how lost and scared you were in the beginning. Then you will remember how you made it through. 

It wasn’t the parties or socials, and it wasn’t what’s his name. It was simply you, wonderful you.

All that you accomplish...everything that is to come is because of your talent and ambition.

So, as I begin a new journey in my life I will take with me the valuable lessons of college. I will take the triumphs and defeats.

I will remember all that you my friends have taught me, and I will look forward to the lessons to come.

Sadness is the final lesson I have learned from this experience. To leave the one place that you finally began to feel comfortable in is heartbreaking. 

I cherish every memory I have of my time here and I hope you do the same.

I will close with a portion of my sorority’s creed. This may not be appropriate but I think that it will summarize my message.
“To those whom my life may touch in slight measure may I give graciously of what is mine...to my friends, understanding and appreciation...to those closer ones, love that is ever steadfast...”
