CSU presents Humor in Music
Jessica Macon / Staff Writer

The CSU Music department hosted a wonderful program entitled Humor in Music at 7:00 p.m. April 6, 2000.  The program was consisted of four multi-part instrumental pieces that were performed by CSU faculty and students. 

The featured works were: Lip My Reeds by Peter Schnickele, Aesop’s Fables narrated and set to music, Serenda for Three by Peter Schnickle and La Revue de Cuisine by Bohuslav Martinu. 

Each piece was performed by a combination of students and faculty.
The first piece, Lip My Reeds, was performed by four bassoonists. 
These CSU students were Jennifer Brinson, Megan Green, Jessica Haynes and Heath Peters.

The piece was a series of variations in notes of the music and sounds. 
On occasion certain notes were exaggerated, making it a very light-heart performance.  

Adding to the humor of the show was the choice of dress the bassoonists chose to wear. Outfits ranged from an oversized tuxedo worn by Jessica Haynes to the Hawaiian-style shirt and denim short worn by Megan Green. 

The second piece, Aesop’s Fables, was narrated by William Bullock and accompanied by Kristen Hansen (french horn) and Janie Lee Bullock (piano). 

The third piece featured was Serenade for Three and was performed by Edwin Riley (clarinet), Patricio Cobos (violin) and Kimberly Borom (piano). 

This piece was divided into three parts. The first part was a happy toned bouncy section, the second was a slow, peaceful section, and the final section was a very fast, at times almost country-like section. 
All three parts were brilliantly performed by the three instrumentalists. However, the very last section is what stood out so vividly. 

Mr. Patricio Cobos performed the fast-paced country/western characteristic of the piece well.

The final piece, La Revue de Cuisine (The Kitchen Revue) was performed by a blend of instruments such as the trumpet (Richard Rulli), the cello (Ning Tien Scialla), the piano (Sue Ruby), the clarinet (Edwin Riley), and the bassoon (Ronald Wirt). 

This piece again featured Patricio Cobos playing the violin. 
The sections of this work ranged from solemn to the swing type music of the roaring 20s. Again, it was performed very beautifully.

The audience seemed to enjoy the experience of the concert. Ellen Najjar, a studying musician at CSU, said “I thought it was very entertaining. The students had a lot of fun with it.” 

Ms. Najjar also stated, “I liked Aesop’s Fables the most because it was just really neat how the story, told by Dr. Bullock, went along with the music.”

Ms. Jessica Haynes, a featured bassoonist, said, “I liked the clarinet, violin and piano trio the most out of the whole concert because it was just so different.”  Haynes has been studying music since the sixth grade.  Along with other students, Haynes was asked to play in this concert after working on the piece in private. 

Megan Green, who was also featured in the first piece with Jessica Haynes expressed favor of Aesop’s Fables.  She has been playing the bassoon for six years although she first started out playing the clarinet.  Aside from school concerts she plays in New York every summer, in churches and different places in the Columbus community.

The violinist of the concert, Patricio Cobos, expressed no certain favorite among the pieces performed.  However, he did offer this statement, “It is very hard for someone like me to say who [my] favorite composer is or what [my] favorite piece was. Usually, when you are playing that piece by that composer, in that moment, that is your favorite.”

Mr. Cobos has played the violin for many years starting out a the age of five.  He gained interest in the instrument through his father who was also a very talented musician. Mr. Cobos was also asked to play in this special concert by some of his fellow colleagues.

Everyone seemed to enjoy the performance very much. It was an honor to be able to attend.
