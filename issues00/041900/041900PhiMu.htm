Phi Mu Fraternity installed at CSU
Robin Remich / Phi Mu Sister


On Friday, March 31 St, the sisters of Phi Mu Kappa obtained the goal that the founding sisters had set just two and a half years ago. 
They were installed as the Theta Theta Colony of Phi Mu Fraternity. The colonies installation took place in the Columbus Room on Friday evening. 

Katie Starrett, the colony director, and the LaGrange Chapter of Phi Mu came down to conduct the installation ceremony which was attended by over 115 family and friends of the sisters. 
The 27 girls that were inducted will now be hard at work as Phi's (Phi Mu' term for New Members) to accomplish the ultimate achievement of receiving a National Charter. 

The Phi's would like to thank our founding sisters, for it was their dream that inspired us all, and our current President Stephanie Swartz, for all that she did to help this goal become a reality. 
The Theta Theta Colony would also like to thank the rest of the Greek community for the tremendous support that they gave us. 

Congratulations to all of the Theta Theta Colony: Christina Barron, Erin Burns, April Buterbaugh, Eli Cain, Angela Cook, Courtney Hughes, Katie Hunt, Emily Jones, Jacquelynne Jordan, Molly Leckrone, Jessica Marsh, Hillary McLendon, Amber Miller, Leslie Minor, Jennifer Morse, Ellen Najjar, Tracee Patrick, Lei Paulding, Angie Pilkington, Tracie Price, Jenny Reeves, Robin Remich, Libby Smith, Stephanie Swartz, Jessica Trenchik, Kristi Watson, Melissa Weatherholtz, Jessica Wilborn, Tiffany Williams. 

The Alumni Advisors are Tracie Price, and Lei Paulding who will be taking this journey with the the sisters. A special thank you goes to Kelly Carden a Phi Mu from Auburn who will be leading the newly initiated women in their Phi training.
