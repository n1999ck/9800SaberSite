CSU Art Department holds annual student exhibit
Jessica Trenchik / Features Editor

Every spring the CSU art department holds it’s annual student art exhibition. The exhibition is a chance for CSU’s art majors to display their work, as well as to be honored with awards and scholarships.
Each student can enter as many pieces as they choose. A guest juror is then brought in to make the final decision as to which pieces go on exhibit. A past CSU guest artist from Indiana served as juror for this year’s exhibit.

This year, around two hundred pieces were entered. Of the pieces entered, about 60 pieces by 40 different artists were selected. 
Besides the honor of having their piece selected, the students are also eligible for several awards and scholarships that are presented in conjunction with the exhibit.

These awards include the Faculty Cup Award, which is chosen by a panel of CSU faculty members. This year’s Faculty Cup Award went to Tony Mion.

Another award given is the Columbus Artist’s Guild Award. Miguel Godoy, who works primarily with canvas and spray paint, was given the Columbus Artist’s Guild Award this year.

Other awards include the Outstanding Art Student award and the Purchase Awards.

The Purchase Awards are particularly exciting because they involve a CSU student having his or her piece purchased by the school and hung on display in the art department. About ten purchase awards were given this year.

All of the awards were announced at a reception on April 11. The pieces are still on exhibit in the Fine Arts Hall. The exhibit is free and open to the public.
