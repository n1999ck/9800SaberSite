Cottonmouths blaze past Blazers
Amy Walters / Editor in Chief

On Tuesday, February 22, I had the opportunity to attend a home game of the Columbus Cottonmouths. That night they were playing the Oklahoma City Blazers. I must say that I thoroughly enjoyed the experience, as I am an avid hockey fan and enjoy watching the game frequently. Anyway here is a quick game recap.

1st Period
This was the most interesting period in the whole game since this is when the Snakes scored three goals, all of which were done by #10 Derek Crimin, one of which was a power play goal that happened to be a hat trick. Also in this period, the Snakes came out playing real strong on the offensive end, keeping the puck away from goal tender Frankie Ouellette, who played a heck of a game.

2nd Period
The second period was pretty much dominated by the Blazers offense who kept extreme pressure in the Snakes end. For about 3/4 of the period, the puck rarely crossed over center ice. However, in this period, the few times that the puck did manage to cross center ice, the Snakes really capitalized by scoring another power play goal, by # 7 Mick Kempffer. Also in this period, we had a fight that broke out at the 6:58 mark.

3rd Period
Completely dominated by the Snakes, Oklahoma really knew that they had been beat and just played meaningless hockey in my opinion not trying to attack the zone. However, it is in this period that the Blazers managed to sneak a shot past Ouellette and get their only goal of the night, ending his shutout.
