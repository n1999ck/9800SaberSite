Athelete Spotlight: Amy Rogers
Johnny Luse / Sports Editor

Second Base * Sophomore * Auburn, Alabama

A versatile athlete, she brings good softball instincts to act as a steadying influence on the young Columbus State squad. She will again be used primarily at second base this season. Holds the single season record with 23 base-on-balls.

1999: Started 49 games as a freshman for the Cougars last season. She led the squad in at bats and base-on-balls last season. She had a .963 fielding percentage. She scored five runs in a game against Ft. Valley State while scoring four runs in two contests. Had a career best three hits on four occasions. Set a career high with four runs batted in vs. Fort Valley State. She had 15 multi-hit games for CSU while she strung together an 11-game hitting streak along with an eight-game and a seven-game streak. 

High School: Played for Coach Ed Crum at Auburn High School. She was an All-State softball selection her last two seasons while also being named a member of the All-Area squad her last three seasons. A participate in the Alabama North-South All-Star contest. She was AHS’s Most Valuable Player in softball and basketball after junior and senior campaigns. Selected as the Most Valuable Player in the Opelika Invitational as a senior. She holds Auburn High’s single season (18) and career (52) home run marks while driving in 87 runs as a senior for another season record. Worked hard off the field as well, she was a National Honor Society member and graduated with a 3.79 g.p.a..

Personal: “A.J.” is the daughter of Thad and Diane Rogers. She has one brother. She is an exercise science major at Columbus State. In her spare time, she enjoys watching movies, cooking, fishing and playing tennis.
