Outdoor World’s big move
Chad Wayne / News Editor

By May 15 Outdoor World will have moved from their current location in Crosscountry Plaza, to their new sight at 6002 Veterans Parkway - about two blocks south of the Houghston Clinic in the building that used to be Alexander’s catering.

This isn’t just a move according to Colby Cardin the Manager at Outdoor World. They are also expanding their space and services. While their current sight consists of about 3,500 square feet, the new sight will be about 4,000 square feet. 

They will be expanding their rock-climbing department and will provide a rock-climbing wall. Admission to the wall will be free and climbing shoes and harnesses will be available for rent. The new facility will also have a classroom and better parking.

Outdoor World is a dealer of many top brands including North Face, Woolrich, Sierra Designs, MSR (Mountain Safety Research), Eureka, Kelty and Perception. And they will be expanding their product lines to include, Mountain Hardware, Golite, Gregory, Arc’teryx, Montrail, Lowa, Eagle Creek, Kavu, Obe, and Hot Chiles.

“We’re trying to provide brands and products that no one else in Columbus provides,” says Colby.

Take for instance the Golite products. Outdoor World will provide a demo set of Golite gear for you to try out for free. Golite is the absolute lightest equipment on the market. A full set of gear including sleeping bag, ground pad, wind jacket and pants, rain jacket and pants, pile cap, umbrella, stuff sacks and tent weigh in at a total of eight pounds.

The new Hot Chiles that Outdoor World will carry “blows the doors off Capilene,” says Colby. Hot Chiles are undergarments and T-shirts that are made of an anti-microbial material. 

So what does that mean to you and me? Basically it means that Hot Chiles won’t keep the aroma of a hiker. It “sheds” the stench so that people at shelters and campgrounds will still want to be with you after you’ve been on a hike.

They will also be carrying sleeping bags by Mountain Hardware that are made with Conduit SL. Conduit SL is a waterproofing element that will keep your down bag dry when it gets dowsed by rain or a spill at suppertime.

Outdoor World sports a wide range of prices and technical sophistication in their product lines. They seem to have something for everyone. For the once-in-a-while camper, there is more cost-effective and less sophisticated equipment. For the avid mountaineer there is high-quality technical gear.

Colby admits that, “You can get some items cheaper from the internet but there’s no guarantee, advice or service. Advice here is free.”

It will be difficult to beat Outdoor World’s prices for the next few weeks though. Between now and their move to Veteran’s Parkway, everything will go on sale at 30 - 75% off. Right now, all Woolrich clothing is 50% off.

Outdoor World doesn’t just provide equipment and clothing. They also provide services like classes and guided trips. They will guide kayaking, canoeing, rock climbing and backpacking trips for as few as one person, or for large groups like fraternities or sororities or church groups.

Outdoor World provides qualified guides for the trips. Joe McClure, owner of Outdoor World, is certified by the American Canoe Association as a kayaking instructor. Colby Cardin is NOLS certified as an outdoor educator and was an Eagle Scout. Scott Anderson is NOLS certified in rock climbing, sea kayaking and backpacking and Robert has 25 years experience in canoeing. Colby and Joe are also First Responder certified.

The classes that Outdoor World provides include: intro to rock climbing, intro to backpacking, kayaking, canoeing, CPR and wilderness first aide. They will also be having a free seminar on May 11 at 6:30p.m.m on how to reduce your pack weight on backpacking trips.

If you have any questions about products or services, call Outdoor World at 563-2113.
