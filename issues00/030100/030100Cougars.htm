CSU Cougars pound West Alabama
Amy Walters / Editor in Chief

The Columbus State Cougars exploded for a school record - tying 11 runs in the bottom of the third inning and went on to defeat West Alabama 14-5 at Cougar Field on Thursday afternoon. 

The Cougars seemed to be flat in the first two innings, and UWA hurler Jim Goble was in control, sitting down the first seven Cougar hitters. Then the game seemed to turn 180 degrees after an throwing error by third baseman Scott Kappler. 

The next eight hitters reached base safely by either hit or walk, blowing the game open. 

The big blows of the inning were a three-run homer by Jay Bayer, and a pair of run-scoring doubles by Rodney Kennedy.

Andrew Ginther also plated three runs in the inning with a double and a two-run single. 

Cougar hurler Jeremy Cook, rejuvenated after the 11-run outburst, gave head coach Greg Appleton eight good innings in his first start. He scattered 12 hits and fanned three in gaining the win. Tom Capallo pitched the ninth for CSU. 

On the day, Ginther had three hits and four RBI to pace the Cougar attack. Kennedy extended his hitting streak to 19 games with his two doubles and added three RBI. Josh Maner, Jeremy Bullington, and Patrick Murphy each had two hits on the day. 

Brad Bouras drove home a pair of runs, while Murphy scored four times on the afternoon. 

For West Alabama, six players had two hits on the day. Ryan Gernard had a long home run in the fifth for the Tigers (8-5). Michael Friedlein added a pair of RBI. 

The Cougars will now prepare for Peach Belt rival Lander University who will visit Cougar Field over the weekend. Saturday’s twinbill gets underway at 1:30, while Sundays series finale will start at 1 PM.
