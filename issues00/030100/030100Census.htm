Census 2000: Stand up and be counted or lose federal money
Jessica Macon / Staff Writer

Attention Student Body of Columbus State University! One of the most important times in our country is upon us. If you need a hint as to what it is, this time only comes around once every ten years. Give up?

The year 2000 marks the next preparation year of the Census. Yes, it’s that time again. Most people know people who have participated in the census but still don’t know what it is for. Some people don’t even know what the Census is and why it is so important. 

The Census is much more than a long questionnaire used to keep track of the population. 

The Census can help officials make the decision about whether more money will be given to things like welfare, education, and healthcare, or whether these much needed programs will be left out in the cold. 
By filling out the Census you are telling the Federal Government that you care where the money goes and that you want your own area to feel the benefits of the funding too.

You are probably wondering why college students need to know about the Census 2000. 

In a memo issued by Dr. Barry A. Fullerton, Vice Chancellor of Student Services, it was stated that Georgia was seriously undercounted the 1990 census, resulting in a loss of about $2 billion in federal money. 

That’s right, TWO BILLION DOLLARS!! And even worse, another seat for Georgia in the House of Representatives. 

People, the more of a presence we have in the higher levels of government, the more likely we are to be allowed the voice we need to get things done. 

If Georgia had gotten the money, think of how the money could be spent: better roads, better programs for financial aid, and other things that we desperately need. 

Now there are some people that realize that the Census is like a lifeline for any institution, people like Dr. Frank Brown, President of Columbus State University. 

According, Dr Brown, “If we don’t count all of our people, then we’re likely to be underfunded for the programs that we’re trying to get develop in our country. It very important for every one to be counted.” 
It is very important for you to take this take this seriously. 

Think of the census as your pay check. If you delaying cashing it, you will soon find yourself down to your last dollar. 

If you don’t fill out the census you and everyone else in the nation that shares your view will be left to survive in society with next to no resources.

Is that how you want it? Schools that are falling apart with no money to fix them, doing without those special services that could help you live a full, healthy life in your golden years, and so on.

In this society, voting, for some people, is considered a way to help out the nation in some way. 

However, do you think citizens should take it that much to heart? 
Dr. Brown says, “Yes, we should.” 

In response to this, CSU is gearing up to make sure that we, as a state, do not miss out on those 2 billion dollars again.

At the moment, CSU is making special Census accommdations for those you living in the dorms and off-campus dwelling, but not those at home with your parents or legal guardians. Because of these accomodations, you will not be left out. 

According to Dr. Bagley, Vice President of Student Affairs, “[We] are making sure some information and forms get out to all the housing students. Then [we] will follow that up with a deadline of when it actually needs to be turned back in and indivdual e-mails to those people who have not turned them back in.” 

Attacking the issue in such a vigorous manner, he feels that “we will get a good turn out and a good return because of that.” 

He assures us that messages will be posted on the student bulletin board as well as sending out an individual e-mail to every student on campus with the help of the Computer Center. 

As you can see, CSU is trying to get you to see the importance of the Census and encouraging you to make your presence count. 

By filling out Census 2000, you and the nation as a whole can get what is most needed. 

If you live in on-campus housing and would like to know more about the times that the Census counters will be around, please contact the Director of Housing.

In mid-March, census forms will mailed to every residence in the U.S. Froms should be mailed back by April 1, Census Day.
