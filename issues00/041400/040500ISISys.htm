Intergrated Student Information System
Submitted by Carl Wallman / Dean of Enrollment Services and University Registrar

It's been a long time coming, but students will finally have access to their student record information through the Web. 

Effective with the advising and registration periods for summer term, students will be able to check their admission and financial aid status, view their grades and advising information, update their address and telephone numbers, and, most importantly, register for classes.

There were two primary reasons for waiting until this time to provide students with this opportunity. 

During previous registration periods, stress on the system resulted in several delays and frustrations for students and faculty. 

With the addition of more memory to the operating system late in the fall, no significant delays in the registration process have occurred. 
In addition, faculty have insisted that the system check for prerequisites before allowing students to register themselves. 

The latest Banner upgrade provided a fix to this process that seemed to work fairly well during the last two registration periods.

The new procedure separates registration from the advising process. To insure that all students meet with their advisors before being allowed to register, advising holds will be placed on the records of all undergraduate students eligible to register. 

Advisors will release the hold during the advising session. Keep in mind that this feature will not release other holds students may have on their records. Students must have their holds removed in the appropriate office before they will be permitted to register.

Students who have a Regents' Test hold must continue to register in the Dean's Office, College of Arts and Letters. 

In addition, all students assigned to the Department of Basic Studies must register with an advisor in University College.

The next goal for student records is completing the degree progress program (CAPP - Curriculum Advising and Planning Program). Current plans are to have reports covering all areas available in printed form before academic advising begins for the fall semester. 

The next step will be a Web version of CAPP, which we hope to have in production for the 2001-2002 academic year.
