Columbus State Career Expo 2000 huge success for jobhunters
Heather Paulk / Staff Writer

Tuesday, April 4 the Careers Expo 2000 was held in the Gym Annex and the Woodruff Gym from 12:30-3:00 pm. 

Before the career fair, a “J.O.B.S” workshop was held which taught students about joint ventures, options of how to balance family and careers, benefits, and employment trends in the new millennium. 
The entire event was free and open to the public. The Career Center sponsored the event and expected between 1,000 and 1,100 students and community members to attend. 

The Career Expo has become an annual event at Columbus State University.

In an interview with Gina Sheeks, the brains and enthusiasm behind the day, she disclosed that close to one hundred employers were expected to attend the expo. 

The employers came from Macon, Atlanta, Albany, Montgomery, and right here in Columbus. 

All of these companies were looking for bright, fresh talent. They were also there to assist people in finding out what employment trends are coming up in the future, how to prepare for them, and answer and questions about their company. 

And of the course the most promising part of the day was the chance to set up interviews with possible employees.

“This is a really unique opportunity for anyone considering job searching because it brings together all kinds of employers.” explained Gina Sheeks. 

Among the possible employers were both public and private companies, various small businesses, and school districts. 
Do the names Coca-Cola, AFLAC, or Peachtree Malls sound familiar? 
When asked who would benefit from a Career Expo like this Mrs. Sheeks stated, “anyone could benefit.”

For those already in the career of their choice, the workshops provide team building skills, explained the financial importance of benefits and how to best use them, and how to keep up with work and a personal life. 

For underclassmen, the workshop about market trends assisted in choosing a major. Discussions with possible future employers always provided tips on what they are looking for when they hire. 

Andy Mac, a student at Columbus Tech, gained some helpful advice from several of the company representatives.

“A few of them (potential employers) said that I have very good communications skills and that lots of companies look for that now, especially in the higher paying positions. So, I guess I’m gonna try to get into some communication classes or something,” stated Andy.
“For those adults considering a career change, an event like this can provide current and specific information to help them get started again,” Gina Sheeks pointed out.

What good is this article after the Career Expo 2000 has come and gone? Well, there are always career fairs in Atlanta, Montgomery, and Birmingham, and there is always next year. 

For future reference, Mrs. Sheeks has provided some guidelines on how to best prepare for such an event. 

First of all “don’t expect a formal interview the day of the expo,” she suggests. 

More than likely, it is going to be a busy day for the representatives and there just isn’t that kind of time. They will usually get your name and phone number and set an appointment over the phone with you. 
Ashley Schwartz is a nurse who took some time off to stay at home with her baby and wants to get back to work. 

Tuesday, she spoke with the contact from St. Francis and has an interview next week! 

Ashley stated, “I was worried it would take a long time to get back in the market but…we’ll see!” 

The next suggestion Mrs. Sheeks had to offer was to come prepared with questions if you already know a targeted company is going to be there. 

Plan ahead so that if your mind goes blank while talking with the representative, you can just look at your notes. If you’re an undergraduate or new to a field, you might want to discuss with them the trends for their particular job market. 

Finally, it is always a good idea to get a list of the companies attending the fair before hand.

Once you are actually at the expo, Mrs. Sheeks recommends making a game plan. 

Try to find a map or just walk around and decide who you want to speak with first. Keep in mind any workshops of interest and make a tentative schedule. 

The rest is sheer charm and foot power; try to be seen by as many people as possible (in a positive way…this is not the time to show why your friends call you “Spit Master”).

The Career Expo 2000 was a huge success! Everyone who attended thanks the Career Center for all their hard work, especially Gina Sheeks. 

So, now that you know what those flyers in the bathrooms were talking about, you have no excuse to miss out on it next year. 
And of course between now and then there will be many opportunities throughout the state to attend career fairs. 

With all the information you have now on how to work the career fair, employers will be filling up your voice mail with offers in no time! So GET A JOB ALREADY! Sheesh!
