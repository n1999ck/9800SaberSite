VOTE NOW!

With the SGA elections coming up next week, I have begun to think about the importance of voting.
Sure we all say that we vote . . . but do we really take the time to explore the various candidates?

I for one am proud of the way this year’s candidates have taken the time to let the student body know where they stand on important views.

However, how many of the students are listening? I wonder how many of our fellow cougars took the time to go to the debate that was held this past Thursday.

I have an advantage over the average student. Since the Saber office is located right next to the SGA office I have an opportunity to see SGA at its best and at its worst.

What’s so important about a silly little student government election anyway? Hello . . . as a student you need to worry about who is speaking for you.

This year’s SGA has accomplished many things for the students. An example...they have helped make it possible for the Computer Lab to stay open 24 hours a day next year. 

They also helped bring about a new Fall Graduation.

You have no idea how much red tape this campus makes you go through to get anything done. 

We have been trying to get the air turned down in the Saber office all semester. You would think that a simple phone call would do the job.
Oh no, let us freeze to death. My fingers are going numb as I type this.

But I digress, the importance of voting is the topic at hand. 

Many of you probably expect me to come out and say who I think you should vote for. Well, I am not going to do that. 

You need to learn to be responsible for yourself. Last year there was a record turn out for the SGA elections. 

I wonder what the voter turn out was in the last Presidential election for our age group?

What is it about becoming informed that is so hard?

Are we worried that by paying attention to the world around us that we are growing up too quickly?

Why should we vote...isn’t that our parents job? Well, think about it this way. What are they voting for?

The things that are being voted on know (and I mean nationally) will affect us for the rest of our lives.

This isn’t important!? I remember when I turned 18 and registered to vote. I am now 22 and have never voted.

Why should this bother me? Simply put, I am ignorant about the world around me, and I mean the immediate world. 

I allow people who I do not know to make decisions that will affect me and my children.

So, I think that it is time for me and you to join the land of the responsible. 

Why not begin with the SGA elections? Let it be a practice run for the real thing.

And I don’t mean just go vote for the person who you think is most popular. Hell, I have a tone of friends but I don’t think you would want me to be the President of the United States!

Being a responsible voter doesn’t make you a geek. Actually, it’s those jerks who knock people for taking concern in political issues that will be wishing they had paid better attention.

Do you want fries with that?

It is amazing how people change over time. I remember when I was a freshman sitting around with my friends. All we talked about was boys and parties. 

Now, we talk about careers and families. Does this mean that I am turning into my parents?

I certainly wouldn’t go that far. I think that it simply means that I have moved passed the frivolous things in life and have chosen to become and adult.

This doesn’t mean that I don’t enjoy a good frivolous gossip session with my best girl pal every now and then.

My point is simple...wake up and smell the coffee. You aren’t getting any younger and the day is coming soon when you will have to take of yourself. Start by taking care of your responsibility to vote.
