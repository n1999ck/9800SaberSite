Celebration held in honor of University sports teams
Chad Wayne / News Editor

The Victors - our own CSU Women’s Basketball team rolled onto the CSU campus on Friday, March 24, to meet a homecoming like they’ve never seen before. Banners were hanging from buildings, people lined the street and each player received a rose as she exited the bus.

As you probably already know, the Women’s Basketball team is now 6th in the nation. This victory, as Coach Jay Sparks put it, “put the CSU women’s basket ball team on the map.” 

Sparks said that of the eight teams at the tournament, the Cougars were the only ones not nationally rated. However, regardless of official credentials, our Cougars went on to show a nationally rated team what basketball is all about - beating Emporia 76 - 67.

Coach Sparks said, “The Cinderella analogy kept coming up over and over again.” 

Coach Greene didn’t mind the analogy. He stated, “Let them keep calling us Cinderella, because as long as they do, we keep winning.”
When CSU found out how the women’s basketball team had done at the South Atlantic Regional tournament, Dr. Brown, President of CSU appointed Nancy Marino to chair a “Welcome Home” committee.
In turn, Nancy formed a committee consisting of: Lougene Brown - accountant in the business office, Francis Parker - administrative secretary in the University Advancement office, Sharon Wiley - Volunteer Coordinator in the University Advancement office, Kathy Carlisle - Director of Human Resources, Chris Russell - Publications Specialist in Public Relations and Peggy Payton - Senior Secretary in the Athletics department.

Together, the members of the committee created a reception for the women’s basketball team like no other that CSU has seen. They provided a police escort for the team’s bus into Columbus, placed a banner up for the bus to drive through, gave a rose to each player and gave a present to each of the coaches.

Lougene Brown said that, “We just wanted to make the girls feel real special.” And they did. Lougene says that some of the players were crying as they exited the bus to receive their rose.

Nancy Marino stated “There was an amazing turnout for the event.” The turnout was amazing because there were no classes on that Friday and students are normally not around campus on Friday nights anyway. Despite the timing, many students came out to support the Lady Cougars.

Nancy Marino was also grateful for the many other organizations that helped with the welcome home reception. Archie Rainy of the Alumni Center arranged for the police escort, Child Resource Center donated supplies and some sororities and fraternities made banners and helped in the decoration of the campus. 

Among the organizations that helped with the reception are the CSU Cheerleaders, Business Office, University Advancement, Student Affairs, Athletics, Public Relations and the Athletic Department.

On Tuesday, March 28, 2000 a combination of the CSU administrative offices held a celebration for all the CSU sports teams that have given a hundred percent and who have brought a lot of recognition to CSU. The celebration was held at the Clocktower, and there were hot dogs, chips and soft drinks free to all CSU students, faculty and staff.

The celebration was to show our support of all the CSU sports teams that have done an excellent job this year, which include the golf team (currently second in the nation), the cheerleading team (which recently won the PBAC cheerleading competition), the baseball team (17th in the nation), the softball team (28-13) so far this season, the men’s basketball team (won the PBAC tournament), the dance team (came in 4th in the Peach Belt competition), the men’s tennis (6-3 in the singles and 4-2 in the doubles) and the women’s tennis team (9-2 in the singles and 8-1 in the doubles).

If you’re wondering about upcoming CSU sports events, just check out the CSU web site. Go to “Student Life” then go to “Athletics.” Just click on a team to get their complete statistics and game schedule.
