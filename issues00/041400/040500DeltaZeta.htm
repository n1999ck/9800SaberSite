Delta Zeta...making a difference
Ashley Johnson / Philanthropy Chair

Philanthropy has been important to Delta Zetas since 1914. 
Sisters of the Lambda Psi Chapter of Delta Zeta have done numerous philanthropy and service projects throughout this past year. In the fall, the women worked at the Glover Food Show which benefited local charities. 

Help the Hooch, the campus-wide Fall Carnival for children, and Cram the Van were just a few of the other activities participated in. 
For Christmas, the women adopted a family and delivered many presents to the children of this family along with Christmas cheer.
This spring, members of Delta Zeta have visited a local fire station, Damascus Way, and Pine Manor Nursing Home. 

Donations have also been made to the Delta Zeta Foundation, Special Olympics, the Ronald McDonald House, and the Valley Rescue Mission. 

Each year the Lambda Psi chapter does a project in which the majority of the proceeds benefit the speech and hearing impaired, specifically the House Ear Institute and Galludet University in Washington, D.C. 

Delta Zeta’s involvement in fundraising for the speech and hearing impaired has a long and rich history. 

This year the women have decided to present a golf tournament. 
The 6th Annual Delta Zeta Golf Tournament will be held at Bull Creek Golf Course on Wednesday, May 24, 2000. 

This is a great way for Columbus State University students and staff to have a fun, exciting afternoon on the golf course while giving back to the community. 

Lunch and a t-shirt will be provided. 

Also, great prizes will be given out from local sponsors such as AC Fitness and Rock 103. 

If you are interested in participating in this event or want to know how you can get involved in other activities contact: Ashley Johnson, Delta Zeta Philanthropist: (706)568-1862 or johnson_ ashley@colstate.edu.
