Students last chance to “get counted”
Jessica Trenchik / Feature Editor

What comes only once every ten years, is absolutely crucial to Georgia and Georgia schools receiving appropriate government funding, and is a responsibility which many college students disregard altogether? 

The answer to all of the above is the national census.
“Census Day,” which was April 1st, has long since come and gone, but students can and should still fill out and return their census forms as soon as possible.

Larry Kees, dean of students, wants to remind CSU students of the importance of their being counted in this year’s census.

The school has already taken care of those students living in housing, but CSU students who live by themselves in off-campus housing need to make sure they register as well.

Basically, if you were not living with your parents on April 1st, then you are responsible for filling out a census from.

Everyone should have received a census form in the mail, but if you did not or you lost yours, then you need to go by one of Columbus’s many “Be Counted” sites and pick one up.

According to Gary Smith, who handles public relations for Columbus’s local census bureau, forms can be picked up at any local fire department or library.

Kees strongly encourages each and every CSU student to take the time to fill out a form because, “It sounds like a small thing, but it’s not.”

“If Georgia doesn’t get [the money], someone else is going to.”
Anyone with a basic understanding of American government knows that the more residents Georgia has, the more legislators it will be allotted.

“The more people you can have in Washington D.C. Speaking for Georgia, the better off Georgia will be,” says Kees.

While perhaps some CSU students do not particularly care about fulfilling their civic duty or even about the state of Georgia receiving adequate funding, there is still one more reason to get your census form returned. 

If you return your census form by April 22nd, you will not be listed on the workload of non-responses and therefore will not be interrupted by a government employee knocking on your door while you are trying to watch the WWF Smackdown.

According to Smith, anyone who returns his or her census forms before April 22nd will show up as having registered. This means that your name will not be put on the non-response workload, which is a list of the names of all the people who have not returned their census forms.

Consequently, you can get through your genetics homework or this week’s episode of Buffy interruption-free.

Even if you can not have your census form completed
and returned by April 22nd, you can and are encouraged to return the form at a later date and still be counted.

“The bottom line is, get counted,” says Kees.
