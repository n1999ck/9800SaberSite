Mummy burial found - Hello
Amy Walters / Editor in Chief

The recent announcement of archaeologists’ findings in Egypt may shed new light on the culture of people who lived in the Roman-Egyptian empire over 2,000 years ago.

What archaeologists have found is an expansive mass grave that may contain the mummified bodies of as many as 10,000 people, including children and complete families, buried together. 

The graves were found in the Bahariya Oasis, 230 miles southeast of Cairo. A man riding through the desert stumbled upon the site when his donkey’s foot fell through a hole into one of the tombs. 

Zahi Hawass, director of the excavation, stated last week that they were aware of the tombs three years ago but that the site was kept secret so that it would not be of interest to looters. The excavation was begun five months ago but only now are pictures being released.

As of now there have been 105 mummies unearthed in the underground tomb that is estimated to stretch under a two square mile section of desert. 

This is the first Egyptian tomb found this century that has not been previously looted before it was discovered. Subsequently, the bodies are well preserved due to the lack of human contact and the dry desert air. 

The fact that the tombs have not been looted has allowed researchers to see the mummies as they were originally buried. From the first data that has been released it is thought that the people were from wealthy, but not overly rich, families. They did, however, have enough money to be buried with some extravagant materials.

The bodies are elaborately decorated with gold masks and adorned with jewels. The tombs are also decorated with cartonnages, linen and plaster-like casket coverings.

Hawass described one cartonnage as depicting a man wearing a funeral crown surrounded by nature scenes and goddesses shielding him from danger with their wings.

With this recent discovery, archaeologists hope to learn about the people and culture of the period including what they ate, how they died, what illnesses they were affected by, and how many children the women had. 

Excavation will resume in November and soon the tomb will be open to the public.

SGA vice president resigns
Amy Walters / Editor in Chief

Sam Holmes, the vice president of scholastic affairs for the Columbus State Student Government Association, submitted his resignation last Thursday. 

Holmes ran against two others for this position in the spring semester of last year. He replaced Grant Salter who served in this position for the previous two terms.

In his resignation letter, Holmes stated that there were a variety of reasons for this sudden decision. He cited that his expected graduation date in May 2000 put excessive pressure on his class schedule. He also stated that the “demands of the music department, as well as music activities outside the school” made him “less visible in Student Government.”

Holmes also made the point that if he continued with student government that the “organization would not benefit as it should, making it unfair for the student body as a whole.” He wrote that he thanked all those that supported him while he was active in SGA either as a representative, senator or executive officer and said that the experience in the organization would “benefit him in future career endeavors.”

The vice president of scholastic affairs handles events and issues of an academic nature within student government. Responsibilities that are included in this office are the SGA sponsored Halloween carnival, selection of educator of the year, planning of the advisor of the year luncheon, and the assignment of senators and representatives to committees. 

According to SGA President Eddie Gordon, applications are being accepted for the vacancy. He said that interviews will begin next week and a new vice president should be presented at the first forum meeting on September 9.

Tryouts for dance and cheerleading
Clarissa Andrews / Staff Writer

If you’re looking for a way to make friends and be part of a team while being actively involved in one of the many extracurricular activities CSU offers, the dance team or the cheerleading squad could be just what you’re looking for. 

Jimbo Davis, the coach for both teams, will be holding a meeting for those interested in dance team tryouts on Thursday, September 2, at 12:30 p.m. in Woodruff Gym room 001. The purpose of the meeting is to discuss what the team is looking for at the tryouts. The girls auditioning will be asked to perform a dance of their choice, lasting about one minute, to display any particular talents they have. The audition will also include a one-minute dance taught that will include the basics the dancers should know.

According to Davis the CSU dance team was started last year, “in order for [the university] to grow, we need to provide a diverse set of [extracurricular] activities.” Last year’s dance team had six girls, but Davis is hoping the team will have at least 16 members. No prior experience is needed to tryout-the first few weeks of practice will be for “cleaning up skills” so that the team members will have a similar style of dancing. A panel of judges will have score sheets to measure the skills, and anyone who gets a score of at least 70 percent will make the team. 

Davis says that extracurricular activities make for a greater success rate in the workforce after graduation; just like in a workplace, the girls on the dance team will need to work together as a team, understanding each others’ strengths and weaknesses. His goal for the team is to “put all of our individual talents out there and get a better product.” The dance team will compete at least once, in the Peachbowl Athletic Conference, and Davis hopes to make more public appearances this year. Last year, the team danced at all of the home basketball games, participated in the lighting of the SGA Christmas tree, and danced for CSU’s 40th anniversary celebration.

Cheerleading tryouts will be done on a private basis, because the main tryouts are held in April. “There are nine guys and nine girls on the team now,” says Davis, but he is hoping to have 10 or 11 of each. If you are interested in trying out for the open spaces on the squad, contact Jimbo Davis at 568-2204 in the Turner Center, room 107. You must be able to do a standing back handspring, standing back tuck, and a tumbling pass with at least two different tricks. After talking with Davis, if you meet all of his requirements, he will invite you to practice with the team for about a week and make his decision after that. 

This year’s cheerleading squad is the “closest group of kids I’ve ever had.” The team bonding is extraordinary-”these are 18 people who are together 99 per cent of the time,” says Davis. The CSU cheerleaders placed third in the country last year in Division II of the UCA College Nationals, and are on ESPN2 three times a month. “Students from all over the country come here because it’s a good program and stay because it’s clean.”

You must have a 2.0 GPA to try out for either the dance or cheerleading teams, and no previous team members are guaranteed a place on the team. “Everyone has to try out and we put the best kids out there,” Davis says. After making the team, students sign an agreement-”they know they’re ambassadors to the school, are constantly in the public eye. We have a clean image. That’s how we’ve gotten so good.” 

Waco investigation reopened
Amy Walters / Editor in Chief

[caption] Students gather outside Davidson Center for refreshments offered at the Back to School Bash on Wednesday, August 25. This was one of the many welcome back events that was held last week.

The FBI and the Justice Department announced last week that they plan to open a new investigation into the 1993 standoff with the Branch Davidians at their compound in Waco, Texas. The siege culminated in a fire that killed leader, David Koresh, and over 80 of his followers.

The investigation was ordered by Attorney General, Janet Reno, and FBI Director, Louis Freeh, a day after the FBI confirmed that they fired what may have been potentially flammable tear gas canisters on the final day of the 51 day siege. 

The Bureau insists that they have no evidence that the devices they fired had any cause in starting the fire. 

Former Deputy Assistant FBI Director, Danny Coulson, stated that two pyrotechnic projectiles were fired onto the grounds approximately four hours before the fire began. Coulson said that during the time between the firing of the devices and the start of the fire the devices could not have stayed hot enough to ignite a fire.

This new information has come to light recently after former Branch Davidians and family members of victims filed a wrongful death suit, charging that the FBI used incendiary devices that started the fire at the main building of the compound.

Reno and Freeh have appointed a team of 40 FBI agents to re-interview each person who was at the scene on the final day of the siege.

The FBI is denying claims that they kept this information secret from the public and is pushing the blame towards the Justice Department, which completed the final investigation of the incident.
Reno stated earlier in the week that she was “very, very troubled” at the recent release of information and pledged to “do everything humanly possible to learn all the facts as accurately as possible and make them available to the Congress and public.”

Lately, it seems as if I can’t get away from one thing ... obscenity. So I thought that I would complain about it a little bit. 

I am all for free speech and free artistic expression, but some of the things I am seeing and hearing are getting on my nerves. Whether it is obscene language or naked people cavorting on prime time television, obscenity is everywhere these days, and the problem is that most of it is absolutely pointless. 

According to one definition, obscenity is something that is created to arouse sexual desire or anger, and it has no redeeming literary, artistic, or scientific value. For instance, no one (except a very disturbed person) is aroused by the statue Venus or by scantily-clad tribal people going about their business on the Discovery Channel. And furthermore, no one gets really hysterical when Rhett Butler says “Frankly, Scarlett, I don’t give a damn.”

On the other hand, some things are entirely unnecessary. A few weeks ago, I went to the movies with some friends. The movie we wanted to see wasn’t playing at the late showing, so we were left with a choice between Run Away Bride and The Thomas Crown Affair. I’m no great fan of “chick-flicks” so we opted for Crown Affair. For those of you who haven’t seen the movie, it is another high-tech thief flick like Entrapment, but not nearly as good. Along with a lot of bad acting and a predictable plot, this movie featured Rene Russo and Pierce Brosnan in one of the longest, most bizarrely filmed sex scenes that I have ever seen. My friends and I were looking at each other and saying things like, “Yuck! When is this torture going to end?” Then, later in the movie, Rene Russo spends lots of time walking around topless for no apparent reason. Someone in the theater might have enjoyed that, but I certainly did not see the point. 

Can’t two people have a conversation fully clothed? I hope this isn’t a weird social phenomena developing - I would hate to have to take off half my clothes in order to have a meaningful conversation with someone. 

Then, of course, there is the subject of verbal obscenity. I have a friend who makes me cringe on a regular basis with his colorful and rather limited vocabulary. For example, he would say something like this: “I beep went to the beep store and they wouldn’t beepin’ sell me any beepin’ cigarettes ‘cause I beepin’ forgot my I.-beepin’-D. Beep it!” (censored version). What is the point of that many curse words? That sentence definitely doesn’t have any artistic, literary, or scientific value. 

The function of curse words, I suppose, is to provide emphasis, but isn’t that function undermined by overuse? For instance, if I say, “I like chocolate very, very, very, very, very, very, very much!” Isn’t that sort of inane? Don’t you get the point after the first “very,” and don’t I sound stupid after the second one? That sentence sounds like it either came from the mouth of a very enthusiastic three-year old or from some one who has nothing between his or her ears but air. No one demonstrates much intelligence by mouthing a sentence that contains more vulgarity than sense. In the words of my foul mouthed friend, “Cuss words are so unin-beepin-ventive!” 

Georgia DUI policy gets tougher 
Georgia Zero Tolerance DUI Information

OCGA 40-6-391-Driving Under the Influence.

The most significant changes to the 40-6-391 were zero tolerance (.02) for drivers under 21 and sentencing in Young Driver DUI convictions.

A person under the age of 21 shall not drive or be in actual physical control of any moving vehicle while the person’s alcohol concentration is .02 grams or more at any time within three hours after such driving or being in physical control from alcohol consumed before such driving or being in actual physical control of the vehicle ended.

Punishment for a DUI conviction Under the Age of 21 include:

First Offense - .02% to .07% BAC
— Revocation of license for six months
— No nolo contendre plea
— Attend DUI school
— Re-take driver’s tests
— No re-instatement prior to end of the six month revocation period
— At least 20 hours of community service, to be served within 60 days of disposition of sentence
— Loss of ability to obtain next level license for 12 months

Second Offense - .02% to .07% BAC
— Same as First Offense except the following:
— Revocation of license for 12 months
— No re-instatement of license prior to 12 month’s revocation period
— At least 40 hours community service, to be served within 60 days of disposition of sentence

First Offense - .08% and above BAC
— Same as first offense above except the following:
— Serve a mandatory 24 hour jail term upon conviction
— Revocation of driver’s license for 12 months

Second Offense - .08% and above BAC
— Same as first offense except that the community service is not less than 40 hours

Other provisions:
— The nolo contendre plea for DUI charges will be treated as a conviction for all purposes for both adults and teenagers.
— Upon first conviction for DUI, a person will have to undergo a DUI Alcohol or Drug Risk Reduction Program.
— Any person over 21 convicted of DUI may be ordered by the Judge to serve a period of imprisonment of up to 12 months, and 24 hours of that sentence may not be suspended or probated. Those persons will also continue to be subject to a $1000 fine for the first offense.

Public Safety Means YOUR Safety
Sgt Jim Boling / Public Safety Officer

Ah, yes! The sun is shining and the birds are singing, and it’s time to start another year here at CSU! This is a great time of year, and lots of things are new and different, so there’s a measure of excitement, even for the “old hands” who have been here for two or three years.

Most noticeable to you as you drive/walk around campus is all the new construction and building going on. This has several effects, not the least of which is a reduction in available parking for commuters (students, faculty and staff are all affected).

The key to finding the parking space you want is to arrive sufficiently early to allow for the “ebb and flow,” as classes dismiss and others depart. The wrong approach is to rush into the campus at the last minute and park somewhere you know is off limits. That includes parking lots designated for others, grass or a seeded area, yellow-striped zones, etc. All that does is get everyone’s attention — those who planned ahead and parked legally, those who come in later and are tempted to do the same as you have, and folks dressed like me, who are distributing little yellow “gift-certificates” on the windshields of your car and others similarly situated. These little citations are a double-value this year; the fine schedule has increased to $20.00 for routine goof-ups and $50.00 for taking a marked handicapped space from someone who really needs it.

The bottom line to all this is to plan ahead, take into account the fact that the university is growing, which limits some facilities during the construction process, and don’t do what you know you shouldn’t. The old expression “Cooperate and Graduate” comes to mind. Have a great semester, and DRIVE SMART!

But supporters of the ordinance said officials can and must legislate morality.

Tracy said the ban is needed to make Ely a better place for families. 

Prostitution is legal in 10 rural Nevada counties, where about 30 brothels are operate. 

It would take four votes to override the mayor, and the two members who voted against the ban said they won’t change their minds.

Far away friends
Amy Walters / Editor in Chief

I think that this week I have suddenly discovered how important the Internet is in this day and age. And it’s not that I’ve used it for my classes or even for writing this paper. As of this week my little colstate email account is the only link I have with friends all over the southeast. 

One of my best friends from high school left two weeks ago to attend a college in Florida. Now I know that Florida is not as far away as California or New York, but for me it’s too far away for her to be. When you are used to talking to someone at least every other day it’s odd not being able to pick up the phone and call them or just drive over to their house to visit. Another friend of mine also moved away this year to attend another college in Georgia. Of course, they are closer than Florida, but still to far away for many visits, especially with the schedule I keep. This was someone I talked to nearly every day of my life last year, so without them to laugh, cry, or just be stupid with it’s almost like they moved to another side of the world. 

With that being said I have become obsessive about checking email. At least four or five times a day I hit the pegasus mail icon on my desktop hoping to have a message from an old friend. I’m like a kid in a grocery store- you know what I mean, when you and your parents went into a store and you couldn’t wait to get to the counter so perhaps you could beg and get a piece of candy or gum. It’s that type of anticipation as I sit down at my desk. Of course, many times I am sorely disappointed (like the child in the store) when I open up my mail and nothing is there (except maybe a forward from my old roommate). But when I do receive a message I sit and read it from beginning to end, hoping to catch a glimpse into what my friends are doing, how their lives are going in their new cities. And as I absorb what they have said I try to think how I’m going to it all that I want to say back into email form (those are the times I really wish they were here for me to talk to).

If you have friends that are far away, drop them a letter every now and then. It may brighten their day and, if they get as excited as I do, they’ll probably respond quickly and tell you what’s going on with them. Even if you are having a bad day, tell them about it. They probably want to be there for you and may even have some good advice for you.

P.S. If anyone wants to send me an email, I’ll be very happy.

WCW does spring cleaning
Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor

It has been a long time rumor that many wrestlers in WCW have been unhappy with the working conditions that have been presented lately. The main problem that they have is the constant push of the older stars, while the younger talent is buried in the middle of the card, with less important roles to play. 

The trouble actually started brewing when, within several months of each other, WCW stars Jeff Jarrett and Paul “The Giant” Wight jumped ship and went to work for the rival federation, the WWF. Then, in late July, Chris Jericho did the same and immediately went from mid-card to main event stature. Now there are more rumblings about moves being made in WCW. The problem came to a head before last Monday’s Nitro. WCW President Eric Bischoff, in what is considered a gutsy move, offered an OUT in contracts to anyone who was unhappy with the company. Bischoff is said to be fed up with constant complaining by his group of wrestlers. It was reported on WCW Live that at that point only Raven (Scott Levy) had taken Bischoff up on his offer. 

According to Internet reporter Bob Ryder, there were several wrestlers singled out for their constant complaining in recent months. Among them were Konan, Raven, and The Public Enemy, with only Raven making the move, or so we thought. It was also reported by Ryder that Billy Kidman later approached Eric Bischoff with regard to being released and was initially declined. However, Kidman later recanted and Bischoff agreed to release him when he got back to Atlanta. 

To further add to the possible defects from WCW, a source close to this situation states that Buff Bagwell has likewise asked for an early release from the company. Bagwell has only five months left on his contract and up until that point hadn’t been approached by WCW with regard to renewing his contract. An unidentifiable source says that it is likely that Bagwell will be granted his release from the company. Ryder reports that after the meeting several wrestlers headed for pay phones. A source states that the extent of said calls are unknown and would be pure speculation at this point, but there just may be more defects or attempts to defect from WCW over the next few weeks. It is not believed by this source that all who are requesting releases will have them granted. 

So what does this mean for wrestling? It means that Bagwell, Raven, Kidman, Randy Savage, and any other unhappy WCW star could end up in ECW or WWF any time after their release is granted. This is indeed a gutsy move by WCW. The company will go much farther and be a better product with stars that want to be there. The downside to the move is that WCW could lose a great number of the younger talent like Chris Benoit, Buff Bagwell and Dean Malenko, while older stars like Hulk Hogan, Sting, and Kevin Nash dominate the headlines. The problem with that is the older stars is just that – they are older and closer to retirement. Where will this leave WCW if the fallout of the last week is a large defection of talent? We can only wait to see, but I would be happy if I were a talent booker for ECW or the WWF.

Top ten things to remember for Rush
Stephanie Adams / Greek Editor

All Greek organizations are wonderful and have many things to offer everyone, but you may find one a little more to your liking than the others. The following steps will help you make that decision: 

1.) Attempt to be as neat as possible in appearance. Dress for Success! 
2.) Ask each fraternity or sorority what they expect of a pledge—time, grades, etc. 
3.) Familiarize yourself with fraternity names and their pronunciations. 
4.) Find out what the financial obligations are. How much does it cost monthly, etc. 
5.) Remember you are a guest in each fraternity or sorority house—act accordingly. 
6.) Do yourself a favor by looking at all fraternities or sororities prior to pledging. There are great people in all. 
7.) Remember that choosing a Greek organization is YOUR decision! Friends can be helpful, but don't let them influence your decision. 
8.) Try to keep an open mind; we know that all the names involved with Greek life can be confusing, but remember we are all here to help you.
9.) Be objective about your decision. Remember, it is the members that make the organization. Find a place where you are the most comfortable, you can obtain the best college education, and can contribute as well as benefit the most. 
10.) And most of all, just sit back, relax, and enjoy yourself; after all, these are the best years of your life!

Delta Sigma Theta Sorority Inc. 
Theta Phi Chapter

GREETINGS
Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., is a group of college women dedicated to making the community more productive. The Columbus, Fort Benning, and Phenix City communities and the campuses of Columbus State University and Troy State University at Phenix City and Fort Benning are the communities our chapter works with diligently to improve. This task is accomplished through presenting a number of programs focusing on the concerns of today’s youth, college students and the community as a whole. Our remarkable Founders developed a “five point thrust” to encompass all of the sorority’s activities. Continuing to carry out those founding principles, programs on educational development, political awareness and involvement, international awareness and involvement, mental and physical health and economic development are presented on an annual basis.

Sisterhood is our being.

Public Service is what sisters do together.

HISTORY 
Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., the nations leading black sorority was founded in 1913 at Howard University by 22 dynamic young women who were members of Alpha Kappa Alpha sorority. In 1912, there was a split between those who preferred the more socially oriented program to those who wanted to create one of service, community involvement and political actions. Our founders were determined to establish a sisterhood with Greek roots as well as one dedicated to public service. On January 13, 1913, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority was founded. Today that determination has blossomed into a vast organization of over 190,000 doctors, lawyers, congresswomen, actresses, school teachers, corporate executives, and countless other professions, diligently working to continue the legacy.
Theta Phi chapter received our national charter on June 3, 1971. The charter line consisted of 20 members. Theta Phi chapter involvement include: Thanksgiving baskets, volunteering for CCSC, hospice, and T.E.A.R.S, fund raisers for the UNCF scholarship, sponsoring programs like the Infectious Disease seminar and numerous other community service projects and involvement.
