ROAR!

Letters to the Editor

 

Freedom
of Speech!!!

Of all the freedoms we take for granted, in my opinion, the freedom of speech has the most affect on people. We don’t want people to criticize what we say yet we put down so many things we hear, read and see. I myself am a highly opinionated person not afraid to voice my thoughts, be they frivolous or serious.

When does this freedom start to hurt others? Maybe it is when we, as individuals, become overly sensitive to the issues and become so blinded by our own views that we don’t consider or empathize with another’s perspective.

You might be asking yourself, Stephanie…honey…where are you going with this? Well, I guess this is a result of all the negative reactions I have witnessed about last weeks roar column. 
I am not putting down any one person’s reaction. My point is very simple. We have a right as individuals to voice our opinion in whatever venue we see fit. Whether I agree with that or not is a different story. I am disappointed in the way that several students have criticized not only the person who wrote the roar article, but also the Saber for printing the article.

Let’s get something straight…in my opinion the Saber has an obligation to publish any article (positive or negative) that is submitted. To criticize is to also say, yea…I want the right to censure others…but don’t you dare censure me when my turn comes around.

Well censure this!!!! People are going to have different opinions whether you like it or not. What separates the mature from the immature is their ability to handle negativity. I am not saying you can’t disagree with what is written in newspapers, on billboards, on television, etc. But, stick to the heart of the material at hand. Dispute the content, not who wrote it. When you attack someone personally your voice gets lost in the embarrassment you cause that person. This is why I was always told never to discuss politics or religion with close friends. You can’t help but take it personally.

I love a good argument, but I try to stick to the facts. The easiest way to lose a battle is to get emotionally involved. I commend the person who wrote the roar article for their courage to stand up and say, “Hey guys…I think something is wrong here!” I also would like to thank all of the people who submitted their opinions to the Saber, specifically those of you who voiced your opinion without criticism and ridicule. 

Finally, no one person can ever be right or wrong in any given situation. We are all entitled to our opinions and we all have the right to voice them. If you disagree with something then don’t ever be afraid to speak up. However, be careful how you do it…your voice might get lost in the negative air you create around it. If you focus on the issue at hand then you will always be heard.
