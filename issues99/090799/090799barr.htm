Letters to the Editor

 

Republican Congressman Bob Barr visited the CSU campus last Monday to answer questions and raise awareness about his upcoming re-election campaign. 

Barr spoke in the Fine Arts Hall Auditorium to a small group of republican supporters from the surrounding area. The speech included a moderated question and answer period in which Barr spoke about some of the problems that he sees facing the Congress and the American people in the next millennium. 

In an interview before the speech the Congressman answered questions in reference to his recent stance on religious practices on military bases. In response to the military allowing enlisted personnel to practice Wicca on bases, Barr said that it was “absolute nonsense to allow the practice of witchcraft on military bases.” This supports an earlier comment he made this year in which he stated that allowing this practice to continue would set a “dangerous precedent that could easily result in the practice of all sorts of bizarre practices being supported by the military under the rubric of religion.”

Barr also spoke to the audience about what he thought America’s weaknesses were going into the next century which include the continuing decline in the strength of the military and the fall of the country’s morality. He said that he thought the United States military was “not equipped to handle problems.” Barr also spoke about Y2K problems, stating that he was worried about the way the present administration was dealing with problems, but was glad that the Congress forced the issue of compliance. 

Barr is the representative for the seventh district of Georgia, which covers LaGrange, Carollton, Rome, and part of Marietta. He is an assistant majority whip and serves on a multitude of House committees including the House Judiciary Committee. Barr is a life member of the National Rifle Association and serves on its board of directors. He has also been awarded a number of awards during his term including the Friend of the Family award from the Christian Coalition.
