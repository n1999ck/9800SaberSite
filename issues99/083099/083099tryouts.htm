Tryouts for dance and cheerleading
Clarissa Andrews / Staff Writer

If you’re looking for a way to make friends and be part of a team while being actively involved in one of the many extracurricular activities CSU offers, the dance team or the cheerleading squad could be just what you’re looking for. 

Jimbo Davis, the coach for both teams, will be holding a meeting for those interested in dance team tryouts on Thursday, September 2, at 12:30 p.m. in Woodruff Gym room 001. The purpose of the meeting is to discuss what the team is looking for at the tryouts. The girls auditioning will be asked to perform a dance of their choice, lasting about one minute, to display any particular talents they have. The audition will also include a one-minute dance taught that will include the basics the dancers should know.

According to Davis the CSU dance team was started last year, “in order for [the university] to grow, we need to provide a diverse set of [extracurricular] activities.” Last year’s dance team had six girls, but Davis is hoping the team will have at least 16 members. No prior experience is needed to tryout-the first few weeks of practice will be for “cleaning up skills” so that the team members will have a similar style of dancing. A panel of judges will have score sheets to measure the skills, and anyone who gets a score of at least 70 percent will make the team. 

Davis says that extracurricular activities make for a greater success rate in the workforce after graduation; just like in a workplace, the girls on the dance team will need to work together as a team, understanding each others’ strengths and weaknesses. His goal for the team is to “put all of our individual talents out there and get a better product.” The dance team will compete at least once, in the Peachbowl Athletic Conference, and Davis hopes to make more public appearances this year. Last year, the team danced at all of the home basketball games, participated in the lighting of the SGA Christmas tree, and danced for CSU’s 40th anniversary celebration.

Cheerleading tryouts will be done on a private basis, because the main tryouts are held in April. “There are nine guys and nine girls on the team now,” says Davis, but he is hoping to have 10 or 11 of each. If you are interested in trying out for the open spaces on the squad, contact Jimbo Davis at 568-2204 in the Turner Center, room 107. You must be able to do a standing back handspring, standing back tuck, and a tumbling pass with at least two different tricks. After talking with Davis, if you meet all of his requirements, he will invite you to practice with the team for about a week and make his decision after that. 

This year’s cheerleading squad is the “closest group of kids I’ve ever had.” The team bonding is extraordinary-”these are 18 people who are together 99 per cent of the time,” says Davis. The CSU cheerleaders placed third in the country last year in Division II of the UCA College Nationals, and are on ESPN2 three times a month. “Students from all over the country come here because it’s a good program and stay because it’s clean.”

You must have a 2.0 GPA to try out for either the dance or cheerleading teams, and no previous team members are guaranteed a place on the team. “Everyone has to try out and we put the best kids out there,” Davis says. After making the team, students sign an agreement-”they know they’re ambassadors to the school, are constantly in the public eye. We have a clean image. That’s how we’ve gotten so good.”
