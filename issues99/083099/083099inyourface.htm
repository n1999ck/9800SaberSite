IN YOUR FACE!
with Stephanie Adams

Alright sports fans...let’s get ready to rumble!!! This week we look at the world of wrestling, and oh what a twisted world it is. For those of you who are die hard fans of the masters of bulk, you may not like what I have to say. So strap on your weight belt and hold on to the ropes - let’s talk some trash!

First of all, if it is not already apparent, I am not a fan of wrestling. However, I believe in looking at both sides of the issue. Let’s start with some of the things that people love and hate about the “sport.” After talking to several students on campus I discovered that many like and dislike wrestling for the same reason. And what is that reason you may ask - they say it is because wrestling is stupid and fake!!! 

Many watchers of the world’s most over dramatized “sport” expressed that wrestling's stupidity and childishness appeals to them. They also find it very entertaining. I guess I could agree that it is pretty funny when grown men strap on leotards and prance around the stage yelling your mama jokes at each other. 

Some female students told me that they like wrestling because of the hard bodies! I have to agree with that one also... But could I make a suggestion: some of those costumes need to be de-drag-queened if you know what I mean. Talk about tacky.

So, what don’t people like about wrestling? Have I already mentioned that it is a sport that requires little intellegence on the viewer’s part? If you have a brain you can keep up with this “sport.” There is no strategy involved. And sugar, I hate to break it to you, but it is entirely fake. Think of professional wrestling as a male soap opera. There is a story line and a well rehearsed series of maneuvers. I have been told by some fans that there is actually some improvisation in wrestling matches, but the bottom line people is that it is not real. Maybe that is why it is so popular. It is an opportunity to escape from reality.

Not to get too serious on you but let’s examine the fact that professional wrestling promotes violence. Funny or not, this “sport” is about hand to hand physical competition. Combine that with constant trash talking and an increasing use of vulgarity and you have what should be considered a situation that is not suitable for children. 

I actually worked a concession stand at a wrestling match at the Civic Center last year. I saw many parents (an alarming number who where under the influence) letting there children run around and pretend to beat each other up. Oh, but you could probably blame that on irresponsible parenting, right? 
Let’s not blame only the parents for letting their children be a part of this increasingly violent display of testosterone. Let’s blame those who continue to support the violence in media - but that is a totally different can that can be opened at another time. 

Bottom line - I am not knocking the hard work and countless hours that wrestlers spend perfecting their bodies. I know that takes incredible amounts of stamina and discipline, but professional wrestling is not a true “sport.” Darlings, it is fake! Just think of it as a giant ballet. It is a performance and nothing more. 

Oh, and did I mention that the wrestlers themselves don’t help by acting like they have a brain the size of a pea? 

“Hey, my name is Big Stan... but you can call me B.S. If you don’t... do... what I say... I am going to beat you with a spatula... sideways!”
