ROAR!

I am a freshman here at CSU. After enrolling I was excited to learn that this campus had an active Student Government Association and I had hoped to get involved this semester. At my high school I was very active in our Student Government and I gained invaluable experience. However, I am very disappointed to report that I will not be getting involved in the SGA this year or any other year until some things are improved. 

After speaking casually to one of the senators, they encouraged me to stop by the office and talk to the president. What I witnessed convinced me that I have no place in SGA. Several members of our SGA executive board were having a petty argument, might I say a very loud petty argument. After observing this argument for several minutes, I decided to leave. I had no desire to become involved in an organization that aired its personal problems to the public.

Having been involved in Student Government for the last two years of my high school career, I found this to be highly unprofessional. If our executive board does not have the tact to contain their problems then I have no desire to work among them. I recommend that whoever is in charge of Student Government investigate to ensure that no other students experience what I did. 

How am I to have faith in the leaders of my school if my initial impression is one of pettiness and childishness? I have heard very positive things about the persons involved in SGA and I was really looking forward to having the opportunity to work with them. I may go back another time and see if things have changed. Until then, I encourage the officers of SGA to try and contain their problems and continue to portray a positive image of Student Government.
