Group offers new theatre opportunities
Bridgette Brumbaugh / Staff Writer

A new cultural activity for students has arrived on campus. The new Studio Theatre group will provide students with the opportunity to produce plays that cannot be produced by the theater department.

According to Molly Henderson, the managing director, the group’s mission is to, “Challenge student views on the world around them, broaden students prospects on what theater is and how it can be produced, and present works by contemporary playwrights and students.“

This organization will be able to produce plays that may be too controversial for the school to endorse, but will still be of benefit to the student body.

The group has access to any theater space on campus and will also produce outdoor plays. Tickets will range from $5-$8 so that shows will be cheap enough for all to enjoy.

The organization was around in the early 1980s and has been revived by Gerald Gibson, the artistic director. This is an opportunity for students with busy schedules to participate in theatre and learn about production.

It is the hope of the founders that this group will offer an outlet for not only theatre majors, but for those who have not chosen theatre as their course of study as well. In this way, they plan to include people from all aspects of life here at CSU.

Any student who has a play idea, wants to act, or has written a play and wants it produced can e-mail Studio-Theatre@excite.com.
