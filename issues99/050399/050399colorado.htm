CSU students react to Colorado tragedy
Clarissa Andrews / Staff Writer

Just before noon on Tuesday, April 20, two students from the “Trenchcoat Mafia” at Columbine High School in Littleton, Colorado, began an attack that left fifteen dead, including themselves. By now, everyone has seen the reports of the sawed-off shotguns, pipe bombs, and the many other weapons used in this killing spree. Across the country, students and parents are terrified at the idea of the same thing happening in their towns. Everyone is asking, “What could have caused those young men to kill thirteen innocent people?” The students at CSU are no exception.

Jean Landreau says, “People are wanting to place blame for this incident. Perhaps some of them should look inward.” Although Landreau does not condone the activities of Eric Harris and Dylan Klebold, she says they were “certainly exhibiting signs of anger long before this tragedy. The wearing of Nazi insignia and uniforms, speaking German, and the Nazi salutes were attempts to get attention... the ultimate attention-getter for these two became a notoriety worthy only of the likes of their idol, Adolf Hitler.”

Another student, Jason Ezzell, feels that the events in Littleton prove “a couple of things. Guns should be a more regulated item and schools should take more safety precautions than they do now.” Ezzell recalled that, during his years at Pacelli High School, many of the outer doors were locked, which meant students had to walk around the building to enter if they got locked out. While some students complained about this, Ezzell says that “it’s better than letting a crazed maniac with a gun walk into the hallway.”

One of the many explanations offered for the killings has been the music the killers listened to-bands such as Marilyn Manson and KMFDM. Adam Brown says that rather than blame the music, people should blame the availability of guns. Many people are angry with the NRA who scheduled a conference in Denver for just a few days after the shootings, and then refused to cancel. Instead, they have agreed to limit it to one day.

According to Brown, “The NRA should be shut down, and the guns should be stored in arsenals somewhere. The Constitution gives us the right to bear arms, but that was when American soil was infiltrated by the enemy. If that happened again, the military could open its arsenals and we could ‘bear arms’ once more. Until then, there is no reason for us to own semi-automatic weapons. It’s outrageous that anyone can get hold of a TEC 9 semi-automatic, much less a minor without his parents knowing about it.”

MerriAnne McCall says that it is “amazing to me what some young people are capable of. I don’t understand what circumstances could make them have that much hatred.”

Landreau doesn’t understand either, but says that “one has to wonder if these young men had received some of the attention they craved, even being told that their behavior was unacceptable, maybe things would have been very different. There is a lesson to be learned from this tragedy for everyone.”

No one will forget the tragedy that took place in Littleton, Colorado, nor will anyone ever be able to give definite answers to the many questions being asked all over the United States. Will people continue blaming Marilyn Manson’s music, especially now that he has cancelled the rest of his tour out of respect to the victims? Will people blame the availability of weapons on the NRA? Or will the blame be placed on the internet, the boys’ parents, cliques in high school, or the thousands of other reasons the young men had for killing thirteen people? Did Klebold and Harris have help making and placing the thirty bombs found around the school? These are questions everyone wants answered...

Whatever the answers may be, the one resoundingly unanimous opinion is that this tragedy is one of the worst this nation has ever experienced.
