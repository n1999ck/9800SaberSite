Guns don't kill people, westerns kill people
Wes Carroll / Guest Writer

Well, it’s happened again. A pair of upper-middle class high school kids in a small “close-knit” town have decided that their peers didn’t treat them with respect so they decided to get together a giant pile of automatic weapons and grenades and head to school seeking revenge. And once again, America is forced to ask the question, “Why?”

Well, I have my theories about why this is happening and I just want to get them out there in the open. The sooner we understand why this is happening, the sooner we can do something about it. There are four basic causes for this phenomenon.

Cause number one is movies set in the old west. Now I know that you are thinking, “Huh?” and I understand that, but hear me out. Think about the plot of every Western ever filmed. A guy or group of guys who are normally peaceful are provoked by some bad guys who inevitably kill or kidnap someone very close to the “good guy”. This may be his mom, daughter, sister, son, wife, girlfriend, sister-in-law, best pal, dog, the town sheriff, the town deputy, the town drunk (Otis), or worst of all, his horse. This causes a complete rage to overtake our pacifist hero and he gets together a posse and they go out and kill all the bad guys for the pure satisfaction of revenge.

Next we can blame rap music. Now I know that rap music gets blamed for everything that goes wrong. But I think it definitely deserves a slice of this blame pie. Where else is this gang-type, mafia-style union glorified but in Westerns and rap music? It’s all about who you “hang with” and “who’s got your back.”

Next, we blame Marilyn Manson. These kids from Colorado (the Trench-coat Mafia) looked up to Manson and, let’s be realistic here for a second, the guy is evil. Okay? I know that people like his “music” and think he’s misunderstood, but in the midst of what is going on in America, let’s just see him, for a minute, as he is: EVIL. There.

Lastly, I blame Keanu Reeves. These kids really looked up to Reeves’ character in “The Matrix” (now in theaters) and wanted to be like him when they dressed in black and shot everyone. So, he must be evil, too. That would explain his success as an actor despite the handicap of not having any acting ability whatsoever.

So, what do we do about this? Simple. We send, Keanu Reeves and Marilyn Manson to Iraq. While the Iraqis try to figure out what Manson is, Keanu can peacefully lull them to sleep with a scene from any of his movies. Thus, less hostility.

Next, we send all the rappers to Kosovo and let them handle things there. And Puff Daddy can beat Slobodan like he was a record executive. Problem solved. No more “heroes” for the high school kids and no more wars in Europe and the Middle East.

Of course, maybe I’m wrong. Maybe I just don’t like rap music, Westerns, Marilyn Manson, or Keanu Reeves.
