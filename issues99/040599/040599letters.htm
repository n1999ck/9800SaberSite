Letters to the Editor

Dear Editor,

This letter is in response to a student’s complaint regarding the pricing structure at the Howard Deli.

The pricing structure at the deli was based on a campus wide survey in which over 700 responses were received. The summary of those responses, which included a statistical sample of students, staff and faculty, showed that customers were willing to pay a little more for a superior sandwich.

The sandwiches made at the Deli are made on Fresh Baked Bread and include Healthy Choice Deli meats. In comparison to other delis, such as Subway, you will find that we put 33% more meat on each sandwich. In addition, we offer a choice of “Real Cheeses” such as cheddar, mozzarella and provolone. Subway sandwiches include processed cheeses in order to keep their food cost down.

In response to your plea, we will work on the development of both breakfast and lunch value meals. In the meantime, you may want to consider purchasing our Turkey, Chicken or Ham “Deli Sandwiches” and a Large Drink, which will cost only $3.68. Our French Bread Pizza is another great buy at only $2.98 for a Pizza and a Large Drink.

Columbus State University Dining Services are striving to please our customers. We are in the process of developing a Customer Comment Board, where comments can be posted along with responses from the Food Service Director. In addition, we are in the process of developing both a “Dine with the Director” Program and a “Student Advisory Committee”. The purpose of these programs is to open the lines of communications between Dining Services and the students, faculty and staff and propel our services to new heights. Please look for the advertisements regarding our upcoming programs and participate, because YOUR OPINION COUNTS.

Dan Silver,
Food Service Director

Dear Editor,

Since October, 1998, there has been a Self-defense Club on campus. As the president of that club, I attempted to make a small splash in the water by offering information on rape and assault prevention, self-protection skills, and so forth. To my surprise, I received very little interest, with only about five CSU students and a couple of outsiders in regular attendance. After talking with Dr. Ivy Mallisham in the Counseling Center about being involved in rape awareness, and after discussing a possible joining of forces with the officers at Public Safety, I was also surprised to find that no one in these offices wanted to include the Self-defense Club in their activities. Interestingly, one of the CSU deputies told me that he would stop by one of the self-defense classes only if I offered free food!

When the Sexual Assault Prevention Week hit campus, I thought for sure that some new students would partake in the self-defense classes. After all, campus police was offering their own self-defense class, so people would probably be interested in learning more. I tried to get my foot in the door by talking to Rape Crisis of Columbus, Inc., and Dr. Robin Lee, but to no avail. At the self-defense class offered by public safety, we were told that it was not self-defense but “survival skills” that would be taught. I still cannot figure out what’s the difference.

As many of you probably saw, the Self-defense Club entered the display contest for Sexual Assault Prevention Week. Many of you told me personally that you couldn’t understand why the Self-defense Club didn’t place first, second, or third. Many people on campus have told me that it’s politics. The Self-defense Club is a little fish in a big pond, and it hasn’t made a big enough splash to get noticed, so everyone’s just looking the other way. What’s funny is that I was even interviewed by the Saber about three weeks ago about the club, and the article has yet to be published. You know, I’ve trained for some years in camouflage techniques; I didn’t realize how good I was.

Dan Hall
President,
Self-defense Club
(706) 565-4281

Dear Editor:

President Bill Clinton has authorized the U.S. military to engage in air strikes against a country with whom we are not at war, to end a centuries old ethnic conflict which does not threaten the United States, in a part of Europe in which we have no national self-interest. Why? Why are American soldiers being sent off to die in this Balkan wasteland?

Bill Clinton has answered: “It’s a moral imperative.” The President’s “moral imperative” is motivated entirely by the desire to sacrifice American soldiers as global policemen. The essence of his policy is, “We have a duty to prevent any more atrocities from occurring precisely because we don’t have a national interest in Kosovo.”

Yet, a foreign policy that abandons our self interest is doomed to fail and can only result in tragedy. Already one U.S. plane has gone down and despite hundreds of sorties by U.S. and British fighters, Slobodan Milosevic has increased his assaults on ethnic Albanians. Milosevic knows he has nothing to fear from us. When our legitimate interests have been at stake, such as with Iraq and Osama bin Laden, America has demonstrated that it is only a paper tiger. Given America’s past failures to defend its interests and the inherent sacrifices of the current action, the bombing of Serbia is doomed to fail.

What is the solution? We must reject the sacrifice of American lives and declare that the United States exists for its own sake. We must return to the idea that our military exists to protect America - not Kosovar autonomy, oppressed Albanians, Kurdish rebels, or starving Somalians. We must immediately withdraw from this conflict, announce that our military will be used when only U.S. interests are at risk, and uphold this policy consistently. This is the only way to regain our credibility, protect America, and prevent such deadly mistakes in the future.

Sincerely,
Don Thompson
Boston College Law School
Class of 2000
