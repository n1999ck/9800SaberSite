IN YOUR FACE!
with Stephanie Adams

No butts about it!!

Smoking - it’s nasty, right?! It makes you unattractive and smelly. &127;Smoking can kill you in numerous ways. Non-smokers have to endure second hand smoke and cigarette butts littering their world everyday.

GET OVER YOURSELVES!!!!

Human beings are flawed. There are worse things we could be doing besides smoking. What makes non-smokers so superior anyway? Do they not have any other vices to speak of? Are smokers morally inferior to non-smokers? I say NO!

Tobacco has been a part of America’s economy from the very beginning. We all put the stereotype on the South of only growing cotton. Tobacco is a large part of the South’s economy today, as it was a hundred years ago. Oh, but those were the days before we realized that tobacco was bad for us.

Well, realize this, one of the reasons there has been a rise in tobacco related deaths is because of all of the man-made junk that has been put in tobacco products. People used to rip it out of the ground, roll it up, strike two rocks together and enjoy a smoke.

People are so sensitive these days. Everywhere you turn someone is being harassed or oppressed or their rights are being violated. We are becoming a society of paranoid tattle tales whose sole purpose is to create so many rules that eventually you won’t be able to scratch your nose without permission. (Someone might interpret a threatening meaning from a gesture such as scratching your nose.)

We should all walk with our eyes tilted down, and do not even think of violating someone else’s personal space. By the way, that grass you are walking on has feelings too! I digress, back to the issue at hand.

If you don’t want to smoke - don’t. If you don’t want to be around cigarette smoke - don’t associate with smokers. Sit in the non-smoking section, don’t go to places where smoking is allowed throughout the establishment (bars, etc.), and most importantly - if you don’t want your children to smoke then teach them about the dangers involved.

Until we can all learn to get along, I have contrived a few rules that non-smokers can follow so they can get along better with smokers. And, just to prove that this issue is not one-sided, I have included some rules that smokers can follow to be more courteous to non-smokers.

Ways to make a smoker happy:
1) Don’t ride in a smoker’s car and complain about the smoke.
2) Don’t say it is ok to sit in the smoking section and then complain.
3) Don’t get drunk and ask me for a cigarette.
4) Never lie and say you are only a social smoker.

Ways to make a non-smoker happy:
1) Don’t smoke right next to an entrance to a building.
2) Don’t smoke directly underneath a non-smoking sign.
3) Don’t blow smoke directly in a non-smoker’s face while carrying on a conversation.
4) Don’t ask to smoke in a non-smoker’s car or house.
5) Leave the decision on where to sit in a restaurant up to the non-smoker.
