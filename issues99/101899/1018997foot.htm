Friday, October 15, the first night of Uptown Jam weekend, only a short while after Blue Oyster Cult left the Riverfront Stage, the crowd had dwindled down to around 30 people. The handful of people who were still around were sitting on bales of hay, watching the security guards look for a lost set of car keys. 

When 7 Foot Politic walked out on the stage, there was a mild round of applause, which turned to a wild yell when lead singer and guitarist Jonathan Callicutt asked the crowd to get up off the hay and get close to the stage. 

For the few people who stuck around, the show was definitely worthwhile. Forty-five minutes of loud swing/rockabilly music cheered everyone up. By the time the last song rolled around, the fans were screaming and dancing their hearts out. 

While the band members did seem to feel as if they got “the bum time slot” (due to the fact that most people left after Blue Oyster Cult finally played “(Don’t Fear) The Reaper”), they felt that those who stuck around really enjoyed it. Jonathan said, “The people who wanted to see us were up front. And it’s better to play to twenty people who are enjoying themselves than 200 who don’t want to be there.”

7 Foot Politic’s other members are Jason Sneath, trumpet and background vocals, David Parker, drums, and Griffin Campbell, bass. 
The group has been together almost six years and got its start in Athens, GA. With its widespread fame, however, the band only gets to play Athens once every school semester. 

“We’re a national touring group,” says Jonathan. “We were on a tour over the summer that went to Ohio, Michigan, Nebraska . . . we played in a lot of college towns.”

The band cites a wide variety of influences. “Everything from Bill Haley and the Comets to Cheap Trick, AC/DC, and Louie Jordan,” says Jason.

Although 7 Foot Politic was born in Athens, the band members weren’t. Jonathan, Griffin, and David were born in South Carolina, and Jason was born in Chicago. “He’s our transplant Yankee,” laughs Jonathan.
