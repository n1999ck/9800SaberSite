Well, we are now in the second week of Crime Prevention Month, and so far, so good. I mean y’all are preventing so much crime out there that we aren’t getting any e-mails on the SILENT WITNESS hotline.

Remember Silent Witness? That’s the program which allows you to anonymously e-mail in your observations or suspicions about criminal activity around the campus and dorms. I KNOW if you saw anything, you WOULD send us that information, RIGHT? I mean, if you don’t let us know what’s happening, we’re only going to be able to act on what we see — and we can’t be everywhere at once.

Maybe you need an incentive to use this program. I can’t offer big rewards like Crimestoppers, but how’s this — if you tell us soand-so’s stealing, we’ll arrest him or her, and then YOUR stuff won’t get stolen. Or, if you tell us someone is using drugs or alcohol illegally, then YOU won’t get implicated or suspected by possibly being associated with that person.

In other words, you can only help yourself by using this valuable tool to reduce criminal behavior around our community. That should be reward enough — knowing that a potential source of trouble for you and your friends has been removed from the campus (and that is only the beginning - those we catch involved in criminal acts are processed through the whole gamut of state and local courts, jails, etc.).

So, I guess what it all boils down to is: help us help you. SILENT WITNESS, and just being willing to “get involved,” even if it’s anonymously, is the best way you can do that. When you do, you’ll have put the real “teeth” into Crime Prevention Month, and will help, as old McGruff says, “Take a bite out of crime.”

Okay, okay, I know I said the column a couple of weeks ago was my “annual” alcohol message. But, this is Alcohol Awareness Week, and smack-dab in the middle of Crime Prevention Month, so here goes a little blending of the two...

Preventing crime, as we’ve been saying all month, is EVERYONE’S responsibility. That goes for doing all those things to secure ourselves and our property, and equally, to making sure our friends and others around us don’t get involved in criminal conduct. So, what does this have to do with alcohol? Very simply, it means playing the “brake” to your friend’s “alcohol accellerator.”

When you are out and about, whether it’s a patio get-together, a frat party, or just hangin’ with your buds at Rio Bravo, YOU have a share in what the others in the group might do. By this, I mean if someone underage is drinking, or if someone who’s been imbibing heads for their car, or if there’s a member of the party who can’t be a “happy” drunk, but wants to fight, YOU should be ready to act to stop what is actually a CRIME. 

Being aware that alcohol is often at the root of illegal acts (by its consumption or by the effect it has), YOU have to be prepared to deal with a little crime prevention if you place yourself in an environment where alcohol is being used.

If you don’t do what I’ve described here, I hope you realize that you’re letting yourself and your friends down. If you turn a blind eye toward alcohol-related offenses, you’re contributing to the behavior. If you make excuses for the miscreant, you’re actually aiding and abetting his/her criminal behavior. Don’t get caught in this trick-bag — do the right thing, even if it’s unpopular at the moment. Anyone who holds it against you either doesn’t understand the problem of alcohol-related criminality, or they’re making mental excuses for themselves.
