News from the world of rock: Foo Fighters, Black Sabbath, Phish, Counting Crows
 Brian Hale / Entertainment Editor

 

Right before Christmas is a great time for music lovers to grab some new tunes. Every year, it is almost guaranteed that at least one good album will come out. In fact, in most cases, Christmas and the summer months are the only times the much anticipated “album of the year” comes out. 

Well, this year, we have two new good releases to keep you rocking through the holiday season and right into the new millennium.
The first of the two albums appearing on the shelves this holiday season is the unbelievably delayed Rage Against the Machine. These guys haven’t had a full album out since Evil Empire. And have the fans been anxious? You bet. 

The new CD, though still untitled, is due out November 2. The album will contain 12 brand spanking new tracks, hopefully calming Rage fans after their ridiculously long wait. Some of the new tracks like “Guerilla Radio”, which is set to be the first released single, “Testify”, “Calm Like a Bomb,” and “Mic Check (Once Hunting, Now Hunted)” promise the same style that we have come to know and love. There will be one twist, however, that might have Rage fans screaming, “Sell out!”
Unlike the traditional style of Rage Against the Machine, the band has decided to post a few songs on the web. Not only will they post a few songs on the web, but will give you free songs to download after buying the new album. Forcing fans to buy the new album, log on to the web page, and enter in some secret code found on the CD to hear tracks that undoubtedly should have been released on the album. If you’re interested in hearing some of the new tracks before they hit the stores, then get online and log onto www.realguide.com/ratm.

The next big album is due out on November 3. One day after the brand new Rage album the Counting Crows will release their album. Coincidence? I think not. 

The Counting Crows new album also promises to be one of this year’s greatest albums. The new album, entitled “This Desert Life,” is not going to be like the first album, nor the second. It will be something totally new. So expect something new, and run out and get it.

To support this new album the Counting Crows are launching a fall tour. Some of the dates are as follows:

  • 10/26 - Atlanta, GA , Tabernacle
  • 10/28 and 10/29 - Washington, DC - 9:30 Club
  • 10/31 - Boston, MA - Orpheum Theater
  • 11/03 - Philadelphia, PA - Tower Theater

The Tabernacle show is probably sold out by now. However, grabbing a scalped ticket might be worth the extra money. Forget I said that.
