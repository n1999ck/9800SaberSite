Shannon Sando: an outstanding member
Jaimie Kirby / Publicity Chair, Xi Theta

 

 

 

 

 

 


Not long ago the sisters of Xi Theta sorority were asked to choose a sister who they feel has done an outstanding job this semester. This is a difficult task because each sister possesses certain qualities that make her an outstanding member of the organization.

One particular sister has been recognized not only for her work within Xi Theta but also for her work here at CSU and in the community: our President, Shannon Sando.

Shannon is a great leader to her Xi Theta sisters as well as a great leader on campus and in the community. She is Vice President and Treasurer of Panhellenic, as well as an active participant with the Big Brothers and Big Sisters of America Program and she is active with Cougar Paws here at CSU.

Through her one and a half years as president of Xi Theta, Shannon had helped strengthen the bond of friendship and sisterhood within Xi Theta tremendously.

Shannon is dedicated to Xi Theta through and through. She does not try to dictate over the organization. She is always open to suggestions and new things and always makes each member feel that she is equal, no matter what her status might be.

Shannon greets each day with a bright smile -just a short chat with Shannon can definitely brighten your day! Shannon has taught each of the sisters that we are all unique individuals. We all have our strengths as well as our weaknesses. She encourages us to maintain these strengths while improving the weaknesses. Shannon has never ridiculed anyone if things do not turn out as planned. She maintains a close relationship with each sister and provides a healthy environment for each to reach her goals, inside and outside.

Shannon has said, “I joined Xi Theta because of its uniqueness and fearlessness to try to break through the sorority stereotype. Xi Theta means the world to me...

I have seen her go through many changes and hard times yet still pull through. My hopes are that this organization is always filled with strong, courageous, unique, and wonderful women. 

I hope Xi Theta will catch on and people realize what a wonderful thing we are trying to get started.”

Shannon’s unique leadership and social skills make her a wonderful role model for each sister, as well as for other people in the community. 
A warm congratulation goes out to Shannon Sando for a job well done! Keep it up, girl!
