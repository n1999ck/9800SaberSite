Last Wednesday in a less than full, in fact almost empty, meeting room in the Davidson Center, two men debated on a topic which may be one of the most hotly debated issues in Columbus’ history--the renewal of the city’s one cent sales tax.

Dave Wilkins, of the Yes, Yes task force, and Bob Hutchinson, of the Think Twice organization, spoke about the upcoming vote and the views and stands that each organization has taken. 

Yes, Yes has campaigned throughout the community to encourage voters to support the upcoming sales tax renewal vote, which would raise over $200 million dollars for community projects including new roads and streets, new recreation facilities, improvement of police and fire departments, and a new library and community learning arts center.
The Think Twice organization opposes the renewal of the sales tax and using city money to create a public city library. 

Wilkins, when answering questions about the sales tax and its effects, stated, “people are so excited about progress and momentum that they put their own money in it.” 

An anonymous donor has given over $15 million towards the progress of the new city library. According to Wilkins, “the community is blessed with individuals who want to give.”

Hutchinson, in opposition to the new library and questioning the motives of the person who gave the money stated that, “ most people who give have a reason to do it.”

Hutchinson’s skepticism of the government officials dealing with the tax reached as far up as the mayor and the city council, as he said that he believed there was a “veil of silence and lack of candor at the government center.” 

Hutchinson also stated that “city officials should go back to the drawing board and rethink [their ideas].” 

Columbus citizens will go to the polls on Nov. 2 to decide whether or not the city should renew the one cent sales tax that began in 1993. 
The ballot has two sections, the first asking people to vote on the renewal of the sales tax and the second asking citizens to approve the new library proposed at the Columbus Square Mall site. 

Several web sites include information about the upcoming vote. The Columbus Ledger Enquirer has ongoing coverage of the vote and articles dealing with the event can be found on their website at www.l-e-o.com. 

The Yes, Yes task force has a website which can be found at www.voteyesyes.com. 

Think Twice information can be found at www.thinktwice.org.
