Students at Courtyard II may be getting some relief next week from overcrowding in the parking lot that has plagued many for weeks.
By the end of next week, a new gravel parking lot should be completed at Courtyard II, directly behind K building. The lot is designed to hold at least 15 to 20 extra spaces for dorm residents. 

Railroad ties will be used to mark off the perimeter of the lot, and the lot is designed to be similar to the visitor’s parking lot at Courtyard I. In addition, a security light will be placed in the lot for safety.

According to Dana Schraufnagel, Director of the Housing Department, the purpose of creating of the lot was to relieve the overflowing that has occurred in the parking lot at Courtyard II. She also stated that the new lot was created so that “people could be close to their building and not have to walk far from their car to their apartment.”

During this semester many students have had to deal with the problem of not being able to park near their apartments; some students have even had to park in the visitor’s lot if they return very late at night. 
The goal of the new parking spaces is to push some people in the far buildings (near K building) to park in the gravel lot, letting others take up the spaces near their building. The spaces can also be used for students who return home at odd hours.

Many students are pleased that the new lot is being built, but others are foreseeing more problems in the future.

Jessica Heald, a resident at Courtyard II stated, “[I think] it’s a temporary solution to a permanent problem, especially because they are only providing fifteen or twenty extra spaces. What are they going to do next year when they need fifteen or twenty more spaces?”

Heald, who lives in back of K building, was also disappointed that the residents were not informed of the impending construction. According to Heald, “[I] came home from class one day and found two four-foot piles of dirt and gravel outside my back door.”

Heald, as well as others, have also raised the question of more parking for visitors at Courtyard II. Heald even proposed that some of the new spaces be designated for visitor parking. 

According to Schraufnagel and Jasper Cooke, Director of Public Safety, there are plans in the future to create more parking for those visiting the dorm. 

The empty house that now occupies much of the space in the visitor parking lot at Courtyard II is set to be moved by Christmas. When the house is removed, plans are to expand the visitor’s lot.

According to Cooke, the school can, “make improvements we could not do because of the house” once the lot is cleared.

Although students occupy most of the apartments at both Courtyards, students at Courtyard I have not experienced the parking problems of those at Courtyard II. 

This, according to Cooke, is due to the fact that there are an “even number of spaces around each building.” 

The future plans for Courtyard II are to create a more viable situation for parking, similar to that at Courtyard I.
