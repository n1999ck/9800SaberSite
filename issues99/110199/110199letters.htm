Dear Editor,

I would like to take this time to inform you about the recent trip to Atlanta, Georgia that the Association of Information Technology Professionals Student Chapter of Columbus State University participated. The NetWorld+Interop Expo, held at the World Congress Center, provided an excellent opportunity for all students to see new technology, talk to vendors, and experience some of the most outstanding computer networking software or hardware available today. 
The Expo also provided a platform that enabled students to learn in a different environment. By doing this, the students gained an incredible amount of hands on experience. The Expo excellently displayed what a wonderfully challenging and exciting profession we have chosen to work in.

However the day wasn’t filled with work! We were able to collect many items such as t-shirts, bags, software, stress-toys, key chains, ball caps and numerous other items the vendors were more than happy to give to us just for showing up! One student even won $500.00 cash money! Not to mention all of the other contests we enter that where for prizes such as laptop computers, servers, modems, a Ferrari for a day, a Porsche for a year, TV’s, digital cameras, more software and tons of other items. The list could go on for days!

The AITP Chapter learned a great deal from this trip and it was well worth the effort and planning to make it happen. In the future, the organization will continue to attend events such as these and remember all are invited to attend with us! Anyone interested in joining the AITP Student Chapter may contact me at Hightower_Odell@colstate.edu or my work phone, 562-1677.

Sincerely,

O’Dell Hightower
President
AITP
Columbus State University Chapter
