Delta Zeta seeking golfers for charity
by Dana Thrower / Delta Zeta

Delta Zeta is currently seeking players and sponsors for their fifth annual charity golf tournament. A portion of the proceeds from this event will be donated to their national philanthropy, Gallaudet University, for the speech and hearing impaired located in Washington, D.C. This year the tournament is being held Friday May 21st, from 12 p.m. to 5 p.m. at Woodland Hills Golf Course.

The tournament begins with lunch and registration. The players will receive a list of rules, a golf cart, and then the competition will begin. The sisters of Delta Zeta will furnish a variety of beverages (no alcohol) and snacks throughout the tournament. After all the teams have completed the course, prizes for 1st, 2nd , 3rd, closest to the pin, and longest drive will be awarded.

This day of golf, food and fun can be yours for a minimum donation of fifty dollars per player or one hundred dollars per team. Delta Zeta and Gallaudet University will also appreciate any other donations. If you have any questions call Amanda Branson, philanthropy chair, at 570-7246. Hope to see you there!
