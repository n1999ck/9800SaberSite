President

Eddie Gordon

Eddie Gordon is a psychology and criminal justice major. He has been in Student Government for two years, and is currently vice president of finance. While serving as senator, he was awarded “Senator of the Quarter,” as well as being appointed to speaker of the senate. He says because of his involvement with SGA, he has had no time to devote to other organizations. He wants to help students by having an SGA open house for students to get to know their representatives on a personal level. Also, they would be able to ask any questions of the Student Government officials. He says, “If elected, I will make sure all money will be allocated for student use. I will not award funds to non-students or non-student organizations, which has occurred under former presidents.”

Amy Walters

Amy Walters is a music education major who has been involved in Student Government since August, when she was appointed secretary. She has served on various committees through her secretary position, and worked on most events that were sponsored by SGA this year. She is President of Mu Phi Epsilon, a national coed music fraternity. She feels she can benefit students through the connections she has made in other campus offices as secretary, and will know whom to contact to solve students’ problems. If she is elected president, Walters says she will address concerns including improved academic advising and working towards the development of a campus wide excused absence policy. She would also like to see more students, as individuals and as members of organizations, become involved with Student Government. She says “I want all organizations on campus to get involved with SGA, so we can learn from each other.”

 
Vice President of Scholastic Affairs

Heather Elwood

Heather Elwood is majoring in biology. This is her first experience with SGA. She is a member of Tri Beta, a biology honor society. She feels she will benefit students because she listens to the needs of others and works hard to meet those needs. She believes she is well rounded and is very convincing, which she believes is beneficial for students. She says, “I will try to get the student voice heard.”

Sam Holmes

Sam Holmes is a music education major. He has spent 4 years in SGA, 3 as a representative and 1 as a senator. He is also president of CMENC (Collegiate Music Educator’s National Conference), a 77 member group; state vice president of CMENC; a member of Phi Mu Alpha; and resident life assistant at the dorms. He believes he can help the students because of his years of experience on campus. He knows the faculty and students, and knows how things operate. He says, “My future goal is to continue to build on the steady foundation of which the current administration has established.”

Matt Olesin

Matt Olesin is a biology major. He has been involved with SGA for a year working as a senator and speaker of the senate. He is a CSU Cheerleader and is on the Orientation Team. He also works as an EMT for 911 in Phenix City. He feels he can benefit students because he holds the university in high regards and will apply this standard in his work. He lives by the motto, “You can do the impossible if you set your mind to it.”

 
Vice President of Finance

Travis White

Travis White is majoring in pre-engineering. He has been involved with SGA since he was appointed as a senator 2 months ago. He currently works in the registrar’s office. He believes he can help students by implementing an open door policy for students to discuss whatever is on their mind. He comments, “ I want organizations to know what funds they can receive and how.” Travis is running unopposed.

 
Chief Justice

Jason Harrison

Jason Harrison is majoring in biology. He has served in SGA for a year, as chief justice, and has served on several committees. He is also involved in Tri Beta, the biology honor society. He feels he can continue to help students by refunding money paid for unjust parking tickets, $150 worth so far. He says “I will protect students’ rights.”

Shannon Miller

Shannon Miller is a communications major with an emphasis in public relations. She has been involved with Student Government this semester. She is involved in CORE (College Orientation Recruitment Excellence), intramurals, and the Baptist Student Union. She believes she is a strong leader and will be representative of the student body. She added, “I will make sure there is fair justice for the students.”
