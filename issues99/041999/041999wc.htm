Help for writers
by Leslie Maxwell / Staff Writer

Many colleges and universities around the country have writing centers where students can consult with peer tutors in order to improve their writing skills. CSU has now joined these colleges and established a writing center of its own, staffed with trained peer tutors.

At this time, the Writing Center is operating out of the Writing Lab, which is located in Arnold 207. Tutors are available by appointment to help students with their assignments. Appointments are available during a variety of times, and they last 20 minutes.

Dr. Noreen Lape, who has been instrumental in creating the CSU Writing Center (WC), says, “The Writing Center is a great place for writers of all abilities to go, share ideas, and improve their writing skills.”

The peer tutors who staff the WC have been through a semester-long training course. They are trained in a variety of techniques, and they can assist a student in any stage of the writing process. Tutors can help students understand an assignment, brainstorm ideas, or, if the student has a draft of a paper, the tutor can offer suggestions for improving it. In addition, tutors can explain teacher’s comments or answer questions about paper formats, such as MLA style.

Mrs. Cathy Fussell, coordinator of the Writing Lab, says the WC is a “place outside the classroom where students can talk about writing and become immersed in the academic community.”

Many students do not have a chance to engage in academic conversation outside of the classroom, so the WC serves as a place to talk informally about papers and generate ideas.

The WC also gives students a chance to receive feedback from a peer instead of their instructor since many students seem hesitant to approach their instructor with questions that seem “stupid” or “insignificant”.

Dr. Lape, who teaches English 1101 and 1102, says, “Many of my students who have used the WC have shown great improvement in their writing skills, and they are becoming more confident and better writers.”

The English faculty and the WC peer tutors hope that everyone will come and explore the new services that are being offered.
