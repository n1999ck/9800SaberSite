Officers launch this year's Operation ABC Mobilization over Thanksgiving holiday

Spurred by the dramatic national increase in child restraint use and significant drop in child fatalities in just the two years since the Mobilizations began, the Department of Public Safety is joining the next 50-state crackdown on drivers with unbuckled children.

Starting on Nov. 22 and continuing through the Thanksgiving weekend, our officers will be looking for and ticketing deadbeat drivers. These are adults who--regardless of the widespread attention to child passenger safety--are still putting children at deadly risk by failing to properly buckle then in the back seat.

I’ll never forget the first time I arrived at the scene of a crash where the victim was an unbuckled child. That is why I am so committed to these Mobilizations.

Transportation officials credit the Mobilizations with helping to move the nation toward its goal of decreasing child fatalities by 15 percent by the year 2000. Since the crackdowns began two years ago, restraint use by children ages 1-4 has increased from 60 to 87 percent, and child fatalities have dropped by 12.3 percent, according to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration.

“Despite the dramatic increase in child restraint use and subsequent drop in child fatalities, we still have work to do,” said Janet Dewey, Executive Director of the Air Bag & Seat Belt Safety Campaign.

“Deadbeat drivers who fail to take responsibility for protecting their child passengers are still placing millions of children at deadly risk every day.”

Traffic crashes remain the leading cause of death for children of all races ages 5-14. Nearly six out of 10 children who die in crashes are unbuckled. Of those, nearly half would be alive today if an adult had made sure they were buckled up.

Today, we are calling on our entire community to adopt zero tolerance for unbuckled children. No one should look the other way when they see a child at risk. Everyone must make sure that relatives, neighbors, and friends know that letting a child ride unbuckled--no matter how short the ride--is always life threatening and never worth the risk.

Georgia’s child passenger law requires that all front seat passengers and persons under 18 anywhere in the vehicle must use seat belts, and children under three years old must be in approved child car seats.

The agency will also be stepping up enforcement of adult seat belt laws, because it’s a proven fact that most adults who don’t buckle up themselves, don’t buckle up kids. Research has found that buckled drivers are three times more likely to restrain their child passengers than drivers who don’t buckle up.

Motorists who don’t wear seat belts are statistically more likely to be seriously injured or killed in a traffic crash. During this Thanksgiving holiday, they will also be more likely to get a ticket.

In partnership with law enforcement and state highway safety offices, the Operation ABC Mobilization is sponsored by the Air Bag & Seat Belt Safety Campaign and the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, and is a part of Buckle Up America, and ongoing national initiative to increase belt use and save lives of all Americans.

Remember--Public Safety Means Your Safety!
