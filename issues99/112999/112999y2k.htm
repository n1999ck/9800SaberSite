For the past 18+ months, Computer Information and Networking Services (CINS), the campus Y2K Task Force, campus Contingency Planning Teams and many other individuals have been very busy identifying and correcting Y2K related problems, conducting Y2K tests and planning for events that may be beyond our control. 

Fortunately, we are not anticipating any major Y2K problems. However, please be advised that the CSU computer network will be down from noon on December 31, 1999 until 8:00am on January 1, 2000. 

While we DO NOT expect to postpone the start of the semester, decisions regarding the delay of registration will be made by the Registrar's Office at noon on January 4, 2000 and decisions regarding the delay of classes will be made at noon on January 6, 2000. 

The first step in the process of identifying potential Y2K problems was to take a thorough inventory of all computer hardware, software and embedded chip devices. 

Based on the inventory results, eighteen Y2K projects were established. The remediation efforts for each project were closely followed throughout the life of the project.

The second step on the road to preparedness was to verify that our critical vendors are prepared for Y2K. Hundreds of vendors were researched via web sites, letters and telephone calls.

Next, contingency planning teams were established and very detailed contingency plans were created to cover potential failures that may affect the CSU computer network, Registration and Financial Aid, the Business Office, Accounting, Student Services, Public Safety, Plant Operations, and Payroll. 

The Contingency Planning Teams will be on campus on January 1, 2000 to test all systems and prepare the campus for opening on January 3, 2000. The Public Relations Office will be informed of any Y2K situations and will disseminate pertinent information to the public.

Finally, CSU staff tested networking hardware, operating systems and many of the software applications installed in faculty/staff offices and in the student labs. 

Test systems were rolled over to January 1, 2000 and CINS staff and end-users verified that dates were recognized properly and calculating correctly. 

The tests were repeated to ensure the same functionality after the leap-year rollover.
CSU's Y2K activities have been closely monitored by the Board of Regents of the University System of Georgia Year 2000 Project Office and the State of Georgia Year 2000 Project Office. Independent consultants that visited each campus gave CSU a healthy "low-risk" Y2K Risk Assessment rating. 

Feel free to direct any questions or concerns to CSU's Y2K Coordinator at bittinger_jean@colstate.csu. Have a safe and happy New Year!
